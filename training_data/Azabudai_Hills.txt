Azabudai Hills
 Ara Eve Leo Sal 
1x
 Azabudai Hills is a mixed-use urban complex in the Minato ward of Tokyo, Japan, encompassing the areas of Toranomon 5-chome, Azabudai 1-chome, and Roppongi 3-chome, developed by Mori Building Co., Ltd. over 35 years and opened to the public on November 24, 2023. Spanning a site area of 8.1 hectares with a total floor area of 861,700 square meters, it integrates three skyscrapersthe 330-meter-tall Mori JP Tower (Japan's tallest building), the 240-meter Residence A Tower, and the 270-meter Residence B Toweralongside 24,000 square meters of green spaces, including a 6,000-square-meter central square. The development embodies a "modern urban village" concept, seamlessly blending living, working, education, culture, and recreation to foster community and wellness in one of Tokyo's densest districts.
The complex houses approximately 1,400 residential units across the towers, high-end offices accommodating up to 20,000 workers (214,500 square meters total), the luxury Janu Tokyo hotel with 122 rooms, an international school, the Keio University Center for Preventive Medicine, and retail spaces featuring around 150 shops and restaurants (23,000 square meters). Cultural amenities include a 9,300-square-meter museum and gallery space, highlighted by the teamLab Borderless digital art installation, while additional facilities encompass a venture capital hub, wellness centers, and an arena for events. Designed by Pelli Clarke & Partners for the towers and Heatherwick Studio for the public realm, Azabudai Hills prioritizes sustainability with 100% renewable energy usage, LEED Platinum certification, and disaster-resilient features like a 6,000-square-meter emergency shelter. This integration of vertical greenery, innovative architecture, and community-oriented design positions Azabudai Hills as a landmark redefining urban living in Tokyo.

Background and Development

Location and Site History

Azabudai Hills is situated in Tokyo's Minato ward, spanning an 8.1-hectare site that straddles the Azabudai and Toranomon neighborhoods, positioning it adjacent to the bustling districts of Roppongi and Toranomon Hills. This central location places it within a key international business hub of the city, characterized by its hilly topography and proximity to major transport links like the Hibiya and Oedo subway lines.
Historically, the site evolved from a low-rise residential and commercial area dating back to the pre-war era, featuring fragmented wooden structures and small-scale buildings that reflected Tokyo's traditional urban fabric. Following World War II, the area faced increasing redevelopment pressures amid central Tokyo's rapid urbanization and population density challenges, with many post-war constructions deteriorating over time and comprising over 200 structures by the late 20th century. This underutilized land, once lined with aging homes and modest commercial spaces, highlighted the need for integrated renewal in a densely populated metropolis.
The site's selection aligns with Tokyo's vertical urbanism strategy, which emphasizes high-density, mixed-use developments to accommodate ongoing population growth while preserving green spaces in compact urban cores. As a "Vertical Garden City" model, the 8.1-hectare area was chosen for its potential to integrate vertical structures with natural elements, addressing land scarcity in central wards like Minato. This approach supports broader urban planning goals for sustainable density in Tokyo's core. Its strategic position enhances accessibility, lying within walking distance of landmarks such as Tokyo Tower and surrounded by international embassies, including those of Russia and Afghanistan, underscoring its role in a global diplomatic and business enclave.
As part of Mori Building Co., Ltd.'s portfolio of transformative urban redevelopments, such as Roppongi Hills and Toranomon Hills, Azabudai Hills exemplifies efforts to revitalize historic sites into modern, community-oriented districts.

Planning and Construction

The development of Azabudai Hills, officially known as the Toranomon-Azabudai District Urban Redevelopment Project, originated from planning efforts that began in 1989 with the establishment of local redevelopment councils to address the area's fragmented terrain, aging infrastructure, and scattered land ownership. In 2014, the project area was expanded, and by December 2016, a comprehensive city plan was proposed to transform the site into a mixed-use urban district. This plan received approval in September 2017 under Japan's Law for National Strategic Special Zones, enabling higher density and innovative zoning for mixed-use high-rises in alignment with Tokyo's urban redevelopment regulations. The Toranomon-Azabudai District Urban Redevelopment Association was subsequently approved in March 2018, formalizing coordination among approximately 300 landowners and local residents through extensive community consultations that spanned over three decades.
Led by Mori Building Co., Ltd. as the primary developer, the project involved key architectural collaborators including Pelli Clarke & Partners for the tower designs and Heatherwick Studio for lower-level structures and landscape integration, with a total investment of approximately 580 billion yen. Groundbreaking occurred in August 2019 following the approval of the rights conversion plan in February of that year, marking the start of construction on the 8.1-hectare site. The project faced significant engineering challenges in Tokyo's dense urban core, including soil stabilization to reach bearing ground layers of sand and gravel approximately 32.8 meters below the surface with an N-value exceeding 60 for structural support. Integrating three skyscrapersMori JP Tower at 330 meters, along with two others up to 64 storiesrequired precise coordination to comply with Japan's stringent seismic standards, incorporating features like a 5-meter-thick ultra-high-performance concrete foundation slab and earthquake-resistant dampers to withstand major tremors without operational interruption.
Construction progressed in phases, with Mori JP Tower and the Garden Plaza reaching completion in July 2023, followed by Azabudai Hills Residence A in September 2023, leading to a partial opening in November 2023. The complex achieved full completion on October 29, 2025, with the opening of Residence B, culminating a 35-year effort that revitalized the site's undulating topography into a cohesive urban framework.

Architecture and Design

Overall Design Concept

Azabudai Hills embodies a core design concept of vertical urbanism integrated with expansive horizontal green spaces, transforming an 8.1-hectare site in central Tokyo into a self-contained "modern urban village" that fosters community interactions within a dense urban environment. This approach reimagines the traditional Japanese neighborhood as a compact, multifunctional district, where high-rise structures are harmoniously linked to 2.4 hectares of greenery, including podium rooftops and walkable facades that climb building elevations to create a seamless blend of architecture and nature.
The design draws influences from Japanese garden traditions, such as undulating landscapes and water features, merged with contemporary biophilic principles to prioritize human-scale experiences amid towering developments. Elements like informal amphitheatres, casual gathering spots, and natural light penetration into lower levels emphasize accessibility and social connectivity, encouraging residents, workers, and visitors to engage in everyday interactions that counteract the isolation of high-density city life.
Led by Heatherwick Studio, the vision centers on organic, pergola-inspired forms that unify the district's public realm and lower-level architecture, promoting openness and a sense of place tailored to Tokyo's context, while Pelli Clarke & Partners handled the engineering and exterior design of the skyscrapers to ensure structural integration with the landscaped environment. This ambitious scale achieves approximately 30% green coverage across the site, positioning Azabudai Hills as a vertical forest that redefines urban living by embedding wellness and community at its heart.

Key Buildings and Structures

Azabudai Hills features three primary towers as its core structures: the Mori JP Tower, Residence A, and Residence B, along with supporting low-rise elements such as the Garden Plazas. The Mori JP Tower stands at approximately 330 meters tall with 64 floors above ground and five below, making it Japan's tallest building upon completion in 2023. This supertall skyscraper incorporates offices on its mid-floors, a luxury hotel in the upper sections, and an observation deck, while its base integrates retail and public spaces. Residence A rises to 240 meters over 54 floors, primarily dedicated to high-end residential units and a hotel, whereas Residence B reaches 270 meters across 64 floors, focused on additional residential accommodations. Complementing these are the Garden Plazas A, B, and D, each approximately 41 meters tall, which house cultural facilities including the Azabudai Hills Gallery and teamLab Borderless digital art museum.
The towers employ advanced structural innovations to address Tokyo's seismic risks, including base isolation systems that decouple the buildings from ground motion during earthquakes, enabling resilience up to a 9.1-magnitude event based on data from the 2011 Thoku earthquake. These systems, combined with vibration-damping devices, ensure the structures remain operational post-disaster. The Mori JP Tower's facade utilizes a unitized curtain wall system with pearl-gray glass panels, designed to evoke a lotus flower form and provide aerodynamic efficiency while allowing natural light penetration. Engineering feats during development included extensive wind tunnel testing by contractor Shimizu Corporation to optimize the towers' shapes against typhoon winds and urban gusts, alongside material selections such as high-performance concrete for the foundations to enhance durability and reduce seismic transmission.
Art installations are integrated into the structures to align with the project's unifying design theme of a "modern urban village" blending nature and culture. Public sculptures and exhibits, including immersive digital works in the cultural facilities, adorn lobbies and plazas, with the Mori JP Tower's crown illuminated to symbolize enlightenment. Energy-efficient materials, such as low-emissivity glass in the curtain walls and renewable energy systems, contribute to the complex's LEED Platinum certification, minimizing environmental impact through optimized insulation and heat recovery.

Landscape and Public Spaces

Azabudai Hills incorporates extensive green coverage across its 8.1-hectare site, with approximately 24,000 square meters dedicated to landscaped areas that blend seamlessly into the urban fabric. This includes a 6,000-square-meter Central Green, functioning as the district's primary park, planted with grasses, trees, and seasonal flora to create a vibrant communal oasis. The landscape features around 320 plant species, many of which are indigenous Japanese varieties such as zelkova, Japanese maples, and native vegetation along pathways like Sakura-asa Street, fostering a dynamic seasonal transformation. Complementing these are waterscapes, including small water features integrated into the Central Green and surrounding slopes, which enhance the sensory experience and support ecological balance.
Public spaces emphasize openness and interaction, with elevated podium levels and walkable facades providing multi-level pathways that connect buildings and ground-level plazas without compromising green areas. The 12,000-square-meter Garden Plaza serves as a key public gathering spot, incorporating an informal amphitheater for events and art-integrated elements, such as sculptures by artists including Yoshitomo Nara and Olafur Eliasson, which animate the plazas and encourage pedestrian exploration. An urban orchard spanning approximately 200 square meters on the sloped areas near the central plaza, featuring 11 types of fruit trees including blueberries, lemons, peaches, and other varieties like apricots, adding edible greenery to the public realm. These spaces promote casual social interactions and cultural engagement within a high-density environment.
Accessibility is prioritized through pedestrian-friendly designs, including barrier-free underground walkways linking to nearby stations like Kamiyacho and Roppongi-itchome, as well as universal design principles in pathways and ramps that ensure inclusivity for all users. The landscapes extend connectivity to adjacent neighborhoods, such as ARK Hills and Roppongi Hills, via integrated roadways and green corridors that facilitate seamless movement. In this urban setting, the greenery and waterscapes actively mitigate heat island effects by providing cooling shade and evaporative relief, while the diverse plantingcreating ecotones with aquatic plants, grasslands, and shrubberybolsters biodiversity by attracting local wildlife and restoring elements of the site's historical Musashino Plateau vegetation.

Facilities and Usage

Residential Components

Azabudai Hills features approximately 1,400 residential units distributed across its key towers, including 320 units in Residence A, 970 units in Residence B, and 91 units in Aman Residences within Mori JP Tower, providing a range of housing options designed for long-term luxury living. These include Residence A with 320 units spanning the 14th to 53rd floors, offering spacious layouts from compact 30 m residences to expansive units nearing 400 m, and Residence B, which encompasses 970 units across floors 6 to 64. The units cater to diverse needs, encompassing luxury apartments for permanent residency, family-oriented homes with child-friendly features, and serviced short-term stays, particularly in Residence B where 168 fully furnished apartments on floors 13 to 18 are available for rentals starting from one month.
Residence B, which opened on October 29, 2025, enhances options for international residents through its furnished serviced apartments, supporting flexible stays for expatriates and global professionals. Amenities in these residences emphasize convenience and wellness, including dedicated lounges, guest rooms, study areas, theaters, gyms, and kids' rooms in Residence B, alongside concierge services and access to private dining and spa facilities in associated luxury segments like Aman Residences. Smart home technologies integrate throughout, enabling seamless control of lighting, security, and climate systems. Pricing for for-sale units in Residence B starts at approximately 550 million for a 1LDK configuration of 53.7 m, targeting affluent buyers.
The residential components primarily target affluent professionals, expatriates, and families seeking an enriched urban lifestyle within a secure, green environment. With English-language contracts and proximity to international schools and medical facilities, the development appeals to global residents, fostering a balanced community integrated with the complex's broader cultural and retail amenities for enhanced daily convenience.

Commercial and Office Spaces

Azabudai Hills features approximately 214,500 square meters of office space, primarily housed in the Mori JP Tower across floors 7 to 52, encompassing about 204,600 square meters with standard floor plates of around 4,800 square meters each. Additional office areas include 4,200 square meters in Residence B on floors 3 to 5 and 5,700 square meters in the Garden Plaza B, dedicated to the Tokyo Venture Capital Hub on floors 3 to 5. These spaces are designed to accommodate roughly 20,000 workers and attract global corporations through large, flexible floor plans suitable for multinational operations.
Key tenants include major firms such as Amazon, which leased 49,600 square meters for its operations, and AIG Japan Holdings, which announced in October 2025 its relocation of headquarters to the complex effective January 2026. The Tokyo Venture Capital Hub hosts the Japan Venture Capital Association alongside independent and corporate venture capital firms, fostering a cluster focused on startups and innovation. Office amenities emphasize productivity and collaboration, including the Hills House on floors 33 and 34a 3,300-square-meter clubhouse offering flexible workspaces, wellness programs, co-working areas, and conference facilities with panoramic views of Tokyo's skyline and surrounding greenery. These features integrate briefly with adjacent cultural facilities to support employee wellness through shared green spaces and events.
The commercial spaces play a significant role in bolstering Tokyo's technology and finance sectors by providing a hub for venture capital and corporate innovation, contributing to economic revitalization in the Minato district. As of Q3 2025, occupancy rates in central Tokyo's prime office markets, including areas like Azabudai, exceeded 90%, driven by low vacancy levels below 3% in key wards. Leasing follows a premium model, with rents reflecting the prestigious location and high-end amenities, while incentives such as RE100-compliant renewable energy access encourage sustainable practices among tenants.

Cultural and Educational Facilities

Azabudai Hills incorporates several dedicated cultural venues that emphasize artistic expression and community engagement. The Azabudai Hills Gallery, spanning 2,300 square meters with 700 square meters of display space, serves as the central hub for contemporary art, fashion, and entertainment exhibitions. It hosts rotating public shows, such as the inaugural exhibition "A Harmonious World" by Olafur Eliasson, featuring immersive light and color installations that ran until March 2024, and the collaborative "POKMON KOGEI" exhibit exploring traditional Japanese crafts through modern reinterpretations from November 2024 to January 2025. Adjacent to this, the MORI Building DIGITAL ART MUSEUM: teamLab Borderless occupies 7,000 square meters on the basement level of Garden Plaza B, presenting boundaryless digital artworks that interact and evolve in real-time using 560 projectors and 610 computers. This venue, which opened in February 2024, allows visitors to wander through fluid installations like cascading waterfalls of light and floating flower gardens, fostering a sense of perpetual motion and cultural immersion.
Educational facilities within the complex prioritize international and innovative learning environments. The British School in Tokyo's Azabudai Hills Campus, located in the Mori JP Tower across seven floors totaling 15,000 square meters, accommodates approximately 700 students from over 60 nationalities in its primary program, covering nursery through Year 6. Opened in August 2023 and designed by Heatherwick Studio, the campus follows a British curriculum infused with Reggio Emilia principles, emphasizing child-led exploration through natural materials, open-plan classrooms, and integrated green spaces to promote creativity and global awareness.
The complex supports cultural exchange through various programs integrated into its venues. Public workshops and interactive sessions at the Azabudai Hills Gallery encourage participation in themes like sustainable design and digital storytelling, drawing from exhibition content to bridge art with community dialogue. While formal artist residencies are not explicitly detailed, the gallery's programming facilitates collaborations with international creators, as seen in its support for evolving exhibits that promote cross-cultural narratives. Janu Tokyo contributes to this ecosystem by curating cultural experiences tied to its wellness and design elements, acting as a gateway to Tokyo's arts scene without commercial overlap.
Artistic installations throughout Azabudai Hills enhance its cultural fabric with permanent and site-specific works. Hiroshi Sugimoto's mathematical sculptures, derived from plaster models of trigonometric functions transformed into aluminum forms, are integrated into public areas, symbolizing the intersection of mathematics and aesthetics. Similarly, elements inspired by Isamu Noguchi's play equipment concepts appear in sculptural playscapes within the Garden Plaza, evoking imaginative, organic forms that blend functionality with artistic play. These pieces, alongside contributions from artists like teamLab and Makiko Kudo, create an environment where art permeates daily life, reinforcing the complex's vision of an urban museum.

Hospitality and Retail Areas

Azabudai Hills features the Janu Tokyo hotel as its primary hospitality offering, a 122-room luxury property that emphasizes communal and wellness-focused experiences. The hotel includes Tokyo's largest wellness facilities, encompassing a spa, gym, Pilates studio, and high-impact circuit training programs designed to promote physical and mental well-being. Janu Tokyo also houses eight dining and socializing venues, such as Sumi, which offers a contemporary sumibiyaki omakase experience, and Janu Mercato, an all-day Italian restaurant blending casual and refined cuisine.
The retail component comprises approximately 150 stores, forming a vibrant commercial zone that integrates luxury fashion, lifestyle goods, and specialty shops amid lush greenery. High-end brands like Dior, Herms, Cartier, and Bottega Veneta anchor the offerings, with Bottega Veneta providing exclusive collections, made-to-order services for leather goods, and private salons for personalized shopping. These outlets line a green-lined promenade connected to public gardens and plazas, creating an open-air shopping environment that encourages leisurely exploration.
Dining extends beyond the hotel with over 30 eateries across the complex, spanning high-end Japanese cuisine like sushi and tempura at spots such as Sushi Azabu and Hakata Tempura Takao Umu, to international cafes and casual options including curry houses and coffee specialists. The Azabudai Hills Market, a 4,000-square-meter food hall, hosts more than 30 specialty Japanese food stores, emphasizing fresh ingredients and educational tastings.
Visitor experiences are enhanced by the seamless integration of retail and hospitality with public amenities, including the free Sky Lobby observation deck on the 33rd floor of Mori JP Tower, offering panoramic views of Tokyo Tower and the skyline. Seasonal events, such as the Azabudai Hills Fashion Weekend and themed food promotions like Curry Special, animate the promenade and gardens, fostering community gatherings. Residents of the adjacent components enjoy priority access to these amenities, blending daily convenience with leisure.

Sustainability and Community Features

Environmental Initiatives

Azabudai Hills has achieved LEED Neighborhood Development (ND) Platinum certification (March 2025) and Building Design and Construction (BD+C) Platinum (October 2025), underscoring its commitment to sustainable urban development. It also aims for CASBEE Rank S for new construction and Wellness Office categories, reflecting advanced environmental performance in energy efficiency, resource use, and indoor environmental quality. These certifications support approximately 30% site greening, with 2.4 hectares of integrated green spaces, and incorporate rainwater harvesting systems that collect and treat site runoff for irrigation, reducing reliance on municipal water supplies.
The development has also achieved WELL Core Platinum certification (May 2024), the highest level, for features promoting occupant health.
Energy initiatives at Azabudai Hills emphasize renewable sources and efficiency, with 100% of electricity supplied from zero-emission renewables, including solar panels on rooftops and structures. Geothermal heat pumps extract energy from the ground for heating and cooling, complemented by sewage heat recovery systems that utilize underutilized urban thermal resources to optimize district-wide climate control. LED lighting and other efficient fixtures contribute to overall decarbonization, with the complex designed to lower CO2 emissions through these integrated measures, such as an estimated annual reduction of about 70 tons from specific green infrastructure elements.
Biodiversity efforts focus on enhancing urban ecology through native and diverse plantings, featuring over 320 species including deciduous trees like zelkova to foster seasonal habitats and support local wildlife. These include aquatic plants in water features and multilevel green spaces that provide ecotones for birds and butterflies, with ongoing monitoring of avian and insect species to maintain and improve ecological balance.
Waste management strategies promote resource recycling across the complex, with on-site systems for sorting and visualizing waste data by tenant, day, and item to encourage reduction and reuse. Operations align with zero-waste principles through comprehensive recycling programs and integration of treated wastewater for non-potable uses, minimizing landfill contributions in line with broader sustainability goals.

Safety and Accessibility Measures

Azabudai Hills incorporates advanced seismic design features to ensure structural integrity during major earthquakes, aligning with Japan's stringent building codes that require resilience against events up to magnitude 9.0, such as the Great East Japan Earthquake. The three towers utilize high-strength steel and concrete structures supplemented by vibration-control systems, including 304 oil dampers and 302 viscous wall dampers in the Mori JP Tower, along with four active mass dampers per tower and 1,200 buckling restraint braces. These elements, combined with brake dampers in the Garden Plaza, reduce swaying from seismic activity and high winds, achieving earthquake resistance approximately 50% above standard requirements and enabling business continuity post-disaster.
Accessibility is prioritized through universal design principles, facilitating ease of navigation for all users, including those with disabilities. A barrier-free underground walkway spanning approximately 700 meters connects Azabudai Hills directly to Kamiyacho and Roppongi-itchome stations, providing seamless pedestrian access without stairs. The complex features multiple elevators equipped with digital technology for smooth vertical movement, alongside ramps and wide pathways in public spaces to support wheelchair users and promote inclusive mobility. Emergency evacuation protocols are integrated throughout, with clear signage and designated routes to ensure safe egress during crises.
Health and wellness measures address post-pandemic needs by enhancing indoor environmental quality and reducing contact risks. The WELL Core Platinum certification supports features that promote occupant health, including high-performance air purification systems with sensors monitoring CO2, PM2.5, and other pollutants to maintain fresh air circulation. Contactless technologies, such as touchless elevator controls, further minimize germ transmission in high-traffic areas. These initiatives support overall user well-being without compromising operational efficiency.
Community safety is bolstered by comprehensive 24/7 security and disaster preparedness infrastructure, integrated with Tokyo's broader emergency networks. A multilayered security system includes continuous monitoring via cameras and video intercoms, with resident-only access to elevators for privacy. The site serves as a designated disaster-response facility for Minato City, featuring approximately 6,000 square meters of shelter spacefully operational since October 2025capable of accommodating up to 3,600 people, stocked with emergency provisions like 270,000 meals, water from on-site wells, and power from gas generators for at least three days. An emergency task force of 1,600 Mori Building employees conducts biannual large-scale drills to coordinate relief efforts, transforming the complex into a regional hub during crises.

Impact and Legacy

Economic and Social Contributions

Azabudai Hills has significantly contributed to Tokyo's economy through its substantial investment and operational scale. The project, with a total development cost of approximately 640 billion, includes 214,500 square meters of office space designed to accommodate around 20,000 employees, fostering job creation in sectors such as finance, technology, and venture capital. Additionally, the complex's Tokyo Venture Capital Hub, spanning 3,600 square meters and hosting about 70 firms including the Japan Venture Capital Association, supports startup ecosystems and innovation-driven economic growth. By drawing an estimated 30 million visitors annually through its retail, cultural, and hospitality offerings, the development boosts local commerce and tourism-related revenue.
The development opened in phases, with Mori JP Tower and Residence A in November 2023, and reached full completion with the opening of Residence B in November 2025.
On the social front, Azabudai Hills promotes inclusivity and community cohesion via its residential components and public amenities. The 1,400 residential units range from compact 30-square-meter apartments to expansive 400-square-meter luxury spaces, including 91 Aman Residences and 168 serviced apartments, enabling a mix of housing options that cater to diverse income levels and lifestyles. Over 24,000 square meters of open green spaces and facilities, such as the 6,000-square-meter Central Green and public art installations, are accessible to the public, encouraging social interactions among residents, workers, and visitors. The development also enhances disaster resilience by providing 6,000 square meters of shelter space for up to 3,600 people during emergencies.
The project plays a pivotal role in urban regeneration, transforming the 8.1-hectare Azabudai area into a vibrant global hub over 35 years of redevelopment. By integrating office, residential, and cultural functions with 2.4 hectares of greeneryrepresenting about 30% of the siteit revitalizes the neighborhood as an innovation center, attracting international businesses and talent. Community programs further support neighborhood integration, including partnerships with institutions like the Keio University Center for Preventive Medicine (3,900 square meters) for wellness initiatives and hosting public events such as art exhibitions, seasonal festivals, and educational activities that engage local residents and children. These efforts, including learning opportunities for local youth within the development's facilities, strengthen social ties and long-term community vitality.

Critical Reception and Awards

Azabudai Hills has received widespread praise in architectural media for its innovative integration of green spaces into a high-density urban environment. ArchDaily highlighted the project as a "new kind of district in the city, made for people and filled with greenery," emphasizing Heatherwick Studio's approach to creating a human-centric urban renewal that stitches together neighborhood elements with natural features. Similarly, Dezeen noted its shortlisting in the 2024 Awards for mixed-use projects, commending the undulating forms and verdant rooftops as a fresh vision for Tokyo's skyline.
Critiques have focused on the development's high costs and perceived exclusivity, which some observers argue limit accessibility for average residents. Media outlets like Unseen Japan pointed out the ultra-luxury pricing of residences, with penthouses starting at around 1,320,000 per month, raising questions about its role in exacerbating Tokyo's housing affordability challenges. The Financial Times reported on the redevelopment model behind the project, noting it splits opinions among stakeholders due to the displacement risks in large-scale urban renewals, though 90% of original landowners were retained.
Expert opinions have lauded the biophilic design elements, with urban design commentators appreciating how the project's emphasis on wellness and nature integration sets a paradigm for sustainable city living. Publications such as Architect Magazine described Azabudai Hills as part of Tokyo's transformative urban innovation, reflecting a balance of past heritage and future-oriented green infrastructure that urban planners view as a model for dense metropolises. However, some experts have expressed concerns over potential gentrification, citing the influx of high-end amenities that could alter the socioeconomic fabric of the Azabudai neighborhood.
The project has garnered several prestigious awards recognizing its architectural and sustainability achievements. In 2024, the Mori JP Tower component received Awards of Excellence from the Council on Tall Buildings and Urban Habitat (CTBUH) in the categories of Best Tall Building by Region (Asia) and by Height (300 meters and above), marking the first such honor for a Japanese project in the latter category. It was also nominated for the International High-Rise Award 2024/25 by the Deutsches Architektur Museum. For environmental efforts, Azabudai Hills earned LEED ND Platinum certification from the U.S. Green Building Council in 2025, the highest level for neighborhood development, and WELL Core Platinum for its office and residential common areas, as recognized by the International WELL Building Institute. Additionally, its biodiverse green spaces won a Grand Prize at the 32nd Global Environment Awards in 2024. The Japan GreenBuilding Council featured the project in interviews highlighting its LEED certification as a benchmark for green urbanism in Japan.
Public response has been generally positive but mixed, with visitors appreciating the aesthetic appeal while noting practical challenges. On TripAdvisor, Azabudai Hills holds a 3.9 out of 5 rating based on 25 reviews as of September 2025, with praise for its stunning architecture and relaxing green areas but criticisms of confusing navigation, restricted access to features like the sky lobby, and high entry fees for amenities. Debates on local accessibility persist, as some residents and visitors question whether the development truly serves the broader community beyond tourists and affluent users.
