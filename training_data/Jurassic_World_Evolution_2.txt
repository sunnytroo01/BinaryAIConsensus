 Jurassic World Evolution 2 is a construction and management simulation video game developed and published by Frontier Developments. Released on 9 November 2021 for Microsoft Windows, PlayStation 4, PlayStation 5, Xbox One, and Xbox Series X/S, it serves as the sequel to the 2018 game Jurassic World Evolution. The title expands on its predecessor's dinosaur park-building mechanics, introducing over 75 prehistoric speciesincluding new flying and marine reptilesand enhanced tools for bioengineering, facility construction, and crisis management.
The game's core gameplay revolves around four main modes: a narrative-driven Campaign set after the events of Jurassic World: Fallen Kingdom, where players partner with Dr. Ian Malcolm and Claire Dearing to relocate and manage wild dinosaurs for the U.S. Department of Fish and Wildlife across diverse locations; Chaos Theory, which offers "what-if" scenarios inspired by the Jurassic Park and Jurassic World film series; Challenge Mode for scenario-based objectives; and Sandbox Mode for unrestricted park creation. Players must balance guest satisfaction, financial viability, and dinosaur welfare while contending with dynamic elements like weather events, disease outbreaks, and animal behaviors, all within environments drawn from the five Jurassic franchise films. Advanced features include customizable aviaries for flying species, lagoons for aquatic reptiles, and a robust genome editing system to modify dinosaur traits such as size, speed, and aggression.
Development of Jurassic World Evolution 2 began shortly after the original's release, with Frontier Developments aiming to address fan feedback on management depth and visual realism. The game launched to generally positive reviews, earning a Metacritic score of 77 for the PC version, with critics commending its improved simulation systems, expansive content, and immersive dinosaur interactions, though some noted persistent micromanagement challenges. Post-launch support has included numerous downloadable content packs, such as the Dominion Biosyn Expansion and Dominion Malta Expansion, which add new campaigns, species like Therizinosaurus and Pyroraptor, and locations tied to Jurassic World Dominion. By 2025, the title continues to receive updates, solidifying its place as a premier entry in the dinosaur management genre.

Gameplay

Game Modes

Campaign Mode serves as the primary narrative-driven experience in Jurassic World Evolution 2, set after the events of Jurassic World: Fallen Kingdom, where players work with the Department of Fish and Wildlife (DFW) to control, contain, and conserve escaped dinosaurs across the United States. The mode features voice acting from franchise stars like Jeff Goldblum as Dr. Ian Malcolm and Bryce Dallas Howard as Claire Dearing, integrating missions tied to the film's lore that involve capturing wild dinosaurs and managing containment facilities. Progression occurs through five distinct settings in the USAArizona, Washington, Pennsylvania, Oregon, and Californiaspanning a total of 5 missions where player choices, such as resource allocation and containment strategies, can influence outcomes and mission success.
Chaos Theory Mode reimagines pivotal moments from the Jurassic Park and Jurassic World film trilogies as interactive "what-if" scenarios, allowing players to alter key events and explore alternate histories. Structured across five levels corresponding to each filmJurassic Park, The Lost World: Jurassic Park, Jurassic Park III, Jurassic World, and Jurassic World: Fallen Kingdomthe mode presents unique challenges like containing outbreaks in San Diego or expanding Isla Nublar parks while adapting to scripted events such as storms or dinosaur escapes. Players' decisions directly impact narrative branches and park development, blending lore fidelity with creative management to achieve objectives like achieving high ratings or resolving crises.
Challenge Mode offers time-limited scenarios designed to test park-building proficiency under increasing constraints, with five maps featuring adjustable difficulty levels from Easy to Jurassic. The core objective is to reach a 5-star park rating as quickly as possible, facing escalating challenges such as limited budgets, frequent storms, higher calamity rates, and expensive funding fees that intensify with each difficulty tier. Examples include managing a park in Canada with rare calamities on Easy or surviving persistent disasters and extortionate fees on Jurassic, emphasizing efficient resource use and rapid expansion without the narrative structure of other modes.
Sandbox Mode provides an unrestricted environment for creative play, enabling players to build dinosaur parks on various maps from the Jurassic Park and Jurassic World eras without win conditions, time limits, or failure states. Completing the Campaign unlocks access to Chaos Theory and Challenge modes, as well as some initial content for Sandbox, allowing experimentation with custom scenarios, terrain editing, and unlimited resources on preset or imported maps. Full unlocks of all content require additional progression through research in other modes. This mode supports integration of park management elements like guest satisfaction and dinosaur welfare for immersive, player-driven simulations.
Completing the Campaign unlocks full access to Chaos Theory, Challenge, and enhanced Sandbox features, including additional maps and species availability across all modes.

Park Management

Park management in Jurassic World Evolution 2 revolves around constructing and maintaining dinosaur facilities while overseeing staff and resources to ensure operational efficiency and guest satisfaction. Players build enclosures tailored to dinosaur needs, including fenced land habitats, domed aviaries for flying reptiles, and modular lagoons for marine species, each requiring specific infrastructure like hatcheries for releases and scans. Additional facilities such as research centers for genetic work, hotels for accommodations, and winding paths for guest navigation contribute to overall park layout, with resource allocation for power to electrified fences and water bodies essential for functionality and dinosaur comfort. Appeal ratings are boosted through features like guided tours in aviaries and underwater viewing galleries in lagoons, enhancing guest experience without compromising security.
Staff operations form a critical layer, with players hiring specialists including mechanics for repairs, veterinarians via Mobile Veterinary Units (MVUs) for health treatments, and geneticists for dinosaur synthesis, each assigned to facilities like the Response Facility or Paleo-Medical Facility. Scientists, in particular, handle expeditions, research, and welfare tasks, progressing through skill trees in logistics, genetics, and welfare to improve efficiency, while traits like faster research speeds provide bonuses. Task delegation occurs through automated patrols and manual interventions, but staff morale is managed via rest periods in the Staff Center to prevent burnout, which can lead to reduced productivity or sabotage such as gate openings or food poisoning.
Environmental challenges introduce dynamic risks, including weather events like hurricanes in tropical biomes that damage buildings and fences, sandstorms in deserts that impair visibility and spread diseases, and snowstorms in alpine areas that slow vehicles and cause frostbite. Disease outbreaks, such as E. coli or foot-and-mouth disease, can propagate among dinosaurs, exacerbated by storms or poor hygiene, requiring MVU deployments for diagnosis and treatment via medical darts or transport to facilities. Sabotage from disgruntled staff adds further threats, potentially disabling power stations or releasing dinosaurs, necessitating quick ranger responses and morale-boosting measures like training to mitigate recurrence. Adaptive strategies include installing backup generators, storm-resistant upgrades on buildings, and emergency shelters for guests during events.
The economic system emphasizes balancing income against expenditures to achieve escalating star ratings for park success. Revenue primarily derives from guest admissions, with adjustable ticket prices influencing visitor numbers and typessuch as adventure seekers or familieswhile donations from satisfied guests supplement funds. Costs encompass staff salaries, research initiatives for new facilities, and expansion projects, requiring careful budgeting to avoid deficits during crises like storms that halt operations and repair expenses. Park ratings, visible in the management screen, reflect guest happiness and directly impact profitability, with high ratings unlocking further development opportunities.
Ranger and welfare mechanics ensure dinosaur viability through proactive monitoring and care. Ranger teams, operating jeeps from Response Facilities, conduct patrols assigned to Ranger Posts or hatcheries, scanning for health and comfort issues while restocking feeders for carnivores and piscivores. Helicopter-supported Capture Teams provide aerial assistance for tranquilizing aggressive individuals or supporting ground operations, particularly in aviaries and lagoons. Dinosaur comfort is quantified across food, environmental, and territorial metricssuch as adequate plants, water sources, and enclosure spacewhich, when unmet, prompt escape attempts or conflicts, undermining park stability and revenue from enclosures. Maintaining high welfare through these patrols and adjustments promotes positive behaviors and sustains long-term park operations.

Dinosaurs and Wildlife

Jurassic World Evolution 2 launched with more than 75 species of prehistoric creatures, encompassing a diverse roster classified primarily by diet and size, such as small herbivores like Dryosaurus, medium carnivores like Allosaurus, and large herbivores like Brachiosaurus. New additions to the series include species like the agile theropod Moros intrepidus and the sail-backed synapsid Dimetrodon, expanding options for park ecosystems beyond the original game's lineup. These creatures are unlocked through progression in campaign or challenge modes, allowing players to balance park diversity with compatibility requirements.
Genetic modification in the base game centers on a research tree that unlocks modifications to dinosaur genomes, enabling adjustments to traits such as resilience, aggression levels, and population capacity without creating hybrid species. For instance, increasing social traits can allow larger herd sizes, while boosting aggression might heighten territorial disputes but improve defense against predators. These variants influence overall health, social interactions, and environmental adaptability, with modifications applied during the synthesis phase to optimize creature viability.
Dinosaur behaviors are driven by a system of needs including feeding, social bonding, and comfort, which must be met to prevent stress-induced issues like fights or escapes. Cohabitation rules dictate compatibility, such as permitting certain herbivores like Triceratops and Parasaurolophus to share enclosures if space and resources suffice, but prohibiting carnivores from mingling with prey species. Comfort levels vary by terrain preferencesfor example, forest cover benefits species like Ankylosaurus, while open grasslands suit herds of Edmontosaurusand unmet needs can lead to lowered morale, increased injury risks, or breakout attempts.
The game expands beyond dinosaurs to include non-avian wildlife such as marine reptiles like Mosasaurus, which require large aquatic lagoons for swimming and hunting, and flying reptiles like Pteranodon, housed in specialized aviaries to accommodate aerial behaviors. These additions integrate into park ecosystems by providing visibility for guests and dynamic interactions, such as pterosaurs perching on structures or marine species surfacing in viewing areas. Enclosures for these creatures must account for unique needs, like deep water depths for plesiosaurs or flight space to avoid stress.
Research and incubation begin with fossil extraction, where expedition teams retrieve specimens from global dig sites, followed by scientists in the Science Center analyzing them to build species genomes toward a 50% threshold for synthesis. Once synthesized, embryos are transferred to the Hatchery for incubation, where random trait manifestations occur based on genome completeness, yielding viability ratings that determine egg success rateshigher DNA percentages reduce defects and improve hatching outcomes. This process emphasizes strategic scientist assignment, as genetics specialists accelerate extraction and synthesis efficiency.

Development

Concept and Announcement

Jurassic World Evolution 2 was conceived as a direct sequel to the 2018 management simulation Jurassic World Evolution, expanding on its core mechanics by incorporating fan feedback to deliver deeper simulation elements, a stronger narrative focus, and story integrations drawn from the Jurassic World film series, particularly events following Jurassic World: Fallen Kingdom. Developers at Frontier Developments aimed to address community requests for more immersive park management, including enhanced customization and environmental interactions, while maintaining the franchise's emphasis on dinosaur conservation and ethical challenges in genetic engineering.
The game was publicly announced on June 10, 2021, during Summer Game Fest, with actor Jeff Goldblum reprising his role as Dr. Ian Malcolm to introduce the reveal trailer. The trailer highlighted significant upgrades, such as improved graphics for dinosaur animations and environments, the introduction of diverse biomes like arid deserts and lush forests, and open-world map designs that allow for larger-scale park constructions beyond the original's island constraints. These elements were positioned as responses to player desires for greater variety and realism in park building.
At its core, the concept evolved from the arcade-like pacing of the first game toward a more realistic management simulator, incorporating features like research expeditions to distant dig sites for DNA acquisition, dynamic weather systems that impact dinosaur behavior and park operations, and moral dilemmas surrounding dinosaur welfare versus commercial demands. Players navigate scenarios involving ethical decisions in containment, relocation, and experimentation, set within a narrative campaign that explores post-Fallen Kingdom chaos with returning characters like Claire Dearing. This shift emphasized simulation depth, with over 75 species including new aquatic and aerial dinosaurs, to create a more strategic and story-driven experience.
Frontier Developments led the project, drawing on their experience from the original title, in close collaboration with Universal Pictures and Amblin Entertainment to ensure fidelity to the Jurassic World lore, including accurate depictions of film-inspired events and characters. This partnership facilitated authentic integrations, such as Chaos Theory mode's "what-if" recreations of movie moments, enhancing the game's ties to the cinematic universe.

Production Process

Development of Jurassic World Evolution 2 commenced at Frontier Developments shortly after the release of the "Return to Jurassic Park" downloadable content for the original Jurassic World Evolution in December 2019, building on the success of the 2018 title. The team aimed for a November 2021 launch, leveraging the proprietary Cobra engine to advance procedural generation for terrain and enhance artificial intelligence systems for more dynamic simulations. This timeline allowed for iterative refinements, with the game entering full production amid ongoing collaboration with Universal Pictures to align with the Jurassic franchise's canon.
Key technical innovations focused on elevating dinosaur realism and environmental interactivity. Dinosaur animations were refined to capture nuanced movements, enabling emergent AI behaviors such as coordinated pack hunting among species like Velociraptor and responses to weather events, including storms that could damage enclosures or alter animal routines. Maps were expanded significantly, with square sandbox options reaching up to 6.25 km to support larger-scale park builds and diverse biomes ranging from arid deserts to snowy taiga regions. These advancements stemmed from player feedback on the first game, prioritizing deeper simulation without overwhelming complexity.
The production team grappled with challenges in harmonizing accessibility for newcomersthrough streamlined tutorials and modular building toolswith the intricate depth sought by series veterans, including advanced scientist management and ethical decision-making in dinosaur welfare. Ensuring film-accurate creature designs required close coordination with Universal, incorporating details from Jurassic World: Fallen Kingdom while innovating on underrepresented species like marine reptiles. Console optimization proved demanding, as the engine adaptations for PlayStation and Xbox needed to maintain fluid performance across varied hardware amid the shift to next-generation consoles.
Audio and visual elements were crafted to immerse players in the franchise's atmosphere. Sound design emphasized realistic dinosaur vocalizations, blending recorded animal calls with synthesized effects to produce distinctive roars and calls true to each species' physiology. Visually, the game supported 4K resolution for detailed lighting, complemented by dynamic day-night cycles that influenced dinosaur activity, visibility, and park operations, such as nocturnal behaviors in certain enclosures.

Release

Platforms and Dates

Jurassic World Evolution 2 was released on November 9, 2021, for Microsoft Windows, PlayStation 4, PlayStation 5, Xbox One, and Xbox Series X/S.
The game launched in two main editions: the Standard Edition, priced at $59.99, and the Deluxe Edition, priced at $69.99, which included the Deluxe Upgrade Pack featuring five additional prehistoric species and six unique vehicle skins. Later, the Dominion Bundle was introduced, combining the base game with the Dominion Malta Expansion and Dominion Biosyn Expansion packs for $79.99.
It became available on Xbox Game Pass for console and PC on May 17, 2022, allowing subscribers to access the base game at no additional cost.
The title supports cross-generation play, with full feature parity on Windows, PlayStation 5, and Xbox Series X/S, including advanced graphical enhancements and faster loading times compared to the last-generation consoles, where it is limited to a maximum of 50 dinosaur species per park and restricts players to a single geological era without mixing.

Marketing and Promotion

The trailer highlighted chaotic dinosaur encounters and park-building mechanics, building anticipation by connecting the gameplay to the upcoming film Jurassic World Dominion. Later, at Gamescom 2021, a pre-order trailer showcased additional footage of wildlife management and containment challenges, further teasing crossovers with Dominion's storyline through in-game scenarios inspired by the films.
To engage fans, Frontier ran promotional contests tied to pre-orders, including a giveaway for a PlayStation 5 console, HyperX gaming peripherals, and Jurassic-themed merchandise, encouraging participants to share their excitement on social media. These efforts extended to social media challenges where fans could submit custom park designs or dinosaur concepts, with select entries inspiring in-game custom skins and vehicle customizations.
The promotion benefited from a close partnership with Universal Pictures, which holds the Jurassic World intellectual property, enabling exclusive content like film-accurate dinosaur behaviors and story missions that previewed Dominion's plot points, such as Biosyn Valley operations. This collaboration also facilitated merchandise tie-ins, including themed apparel and collectibles sold through Universal's retail channels, and synchronized in-game events planned around the film's June 2022 release to maintain momentum post-launch.
Pre-order incentives were a key driver of early buzz, with all editions granting three exclusive vehicle skins for Ranger, Capture, and Mobile Vet teams, evoking Dinosaur Protection Group aesthetics from the films. The Deluxe Edition included the Deluxe Upgrade Pack, which added five new prehistoric speciessuch as Geosternbergia and Attenborosaurusplus six unique building signs and additional skins to enhance customization options.
Media coverage amplified the campaign through hands-on previews that praised the game's deepened narrative, featuring voiced performances by Jurassic World actors like Goldblum as Dr. Ian Malcolm and Bryce Dallas Howard as Claire Dearing, which added authenticity and emotional layers to the campaign mode. Outlets such as IGN and Shacknews highlighted these elements in their previews, noting how the story bridged Jurassic World: Fallen Kingdom and Dominion, while Goldblum's promotional interviews endorsed the title's faithful recreation of the franchise's themes of scientific hubris and dinosaur management.

Post-release Content

Software Updates

Following its launch on November 9, 2021, Jurassic World Evolution 2 received a series of free software updates from developer Frontier Developments, focusing on bug fixes, gameplay refinements, and quality-of-life enhancements to improve stability and player experience. These patches addressed technical issues reported by the community and introduced minor free content without requiring additional purchases.
Early post-launch patches emphasized stability and core mechanics. Update 1.4.4, released on June 30, 2022, primarily targeted bug fixes and performance improvements, including resolutions for dinosaur AI glitches such as creatures getting stuck after terrain modifications or fence edits, and preventing dinosaurs from clipping through barriers during failed vehicle attacks. It also refined economic impacts by ensuring wrangled dinosaurs no longer inadvertently affected park finances unless directly harming guests, contributing to better balance in Challenge Mode scenarios.
Subsequent updates built on this foundation with UI enhancements and broader accessibility. Update 1.10.0, launched on March 13, 2024, alongside the Secret Species Pack, streamlined the user interface by simplifying the "Dislike" list for hybrid dinosaur social behaviors to show "Everything" rather than listing individual groups, making park management more intuitive. It included numerous bug fixes for animations, audio, and stability, such as correcting goat physics during hunts and polishing fight finisher sequences.
Later patches introduced free content additions and optimizations. Update 1.2, released on February 15, 2022, added multiple new maps and building variants to Sandbox Mode, expanding creative options without purchase requirements. Free Update 7 in August 2023 unlocked all maps for Sandbox play by removing progression-based restrictions, allowing immediate access to diverse environments. These changes incorporated expanded weather event variants for more dynamic park simulations. Update 1.11.3 on June 19, 2025, provided performance optimizations, including reduced load times in large parks, and minor free additions like new placeable scenery pieces on paths for better customization. Accessibility options, such as adjustable subtitles and control remapping, were refined across patches like 1.6.0 in December 2022.
Cross-platform support saw incremental improvements, with patches enabling cross-save functionality between PS4 and PS5 starting in Update 1.16 (December 2022) and Xbox Play Anywhere integration for seamless progression between Xbox consoles and PC. These updates were heavily influenced by player feedback from official forums and Steam discussions, leading to targeted fixes for issues like dinosaur pathing and economic scaling in Challenge Mode. While official mod support remains limited, community mods on platforms like Nexus Mods have been indirectly aided by stable update cycles that minimize compatibility breaks, with hints of future considerations in developer communications.
Overall, the software updates refined the base game's mechanics, with free enhancements integrating seamlessly alongside DLC releases to extend replayability. By mid-2025, these patches had significantly enhanced stability, particularly in large-scale parks, based on ongoing community input.

Downloadable Expansions

Jurassic World Evolution 2 features a series of paid downloadable content (DLC) packs that expand the base game's roster of playable species, introduce new narrative campaigns, and add themed buildings, vehicles, and skins. These expansions were released periodically by Frontier Developments in collaboration with Universal, focusing on prehistoric creatures from various geological periods and ties to the Jurassic World franchise. By November 2025, a total of ten DLC packs have been issued, increasing the number of unlockable species and variants from the base game's approximately 75 to over 120, while integrating story elements from films like Jurassic World Dominion.
The initial post-launch expansions emphasized species from the Early Cretaceous period and connections to animated series. The Early Cretaceous Pack, released on February 9, 2022, added three armored and herbivorous dinosaurs: Minmi, Wuerhosaurus, and Ouranosaurus, along with new fences and buildings suited to arid environments. Following shortly after, the Camp Cretaceous Dinosaur Pack launched on May 3, 2022, introducing Quetzalcoatlusa large pterosaurand skin variants for existing species inspired by the Jurassic World Camp Cretaceous animated series, enhancing aerial and lagoon-based park designs. The Dominion Biosyn Expansion, released on May 31, 2022, marked the first major story-driven DLC, featuring a five-mission campaign set in the Biosyn Valley from Jurassic World Dominion. It added five new speciesTherizinosaurus, Pyroraptor, Segisaurus, Lystrosaurus, and Dimetrodonwhile incorporating a plot involving genetically modified locusts that threaten global agriculture, complete with voice acting from film cast members like BD Wong and Campbell Scott.
Subsequent packs broadened the scope to later geological eras and specialized themes. The Late Cretaceous Pack, arriving on October 20, 2022, included four species such as the theropod Australovenator and the marine reptile Styxosaurus, focusing on apex predators and aquatic life from the period's end. The Park Managers' Collection Pack, released June 14, 2023, offered eight diverse species including Albertosaurus, Archelon, and Smilodon, alongside reskinned park structures to simulate classic management aesthetics. In 2023, the Dominion Malta Expansion (August 8) extended the narrative with a pre-Dominion storyline involving black-market dinosaur trafficking on Mediterranean islands, adding Atrociraptor variants and Moros intrepidus. The Feathered Species Pack (November 21, 2023) introduced four feathered dinosaurs, including Yutyrannus and Jeholopterus, emphasizing realistic plumage and behaviors from recent paleontological findings.
Later expansions in 2023 and 2024 targeted predators, marine life, and experimental hybrids. The Cretaceous Predator Pack (November 30, 2023) brought four aggressive species: Utahraptor, Concavenator, Gigantoraptor, and Megalania, with enhanced AI for territorial behaviors. The Prehistoric Marine Species Pack (March 12, 2024) expanded underwater exhibits with Archelon, Dunkleosteus, Nothosaurus, and Shonisaurus, including new aquatic viewing galleries. Culminating the series, the Secret Species Pack (May 21, 2024) added four bioengineered hybridsStegoceratops, Spinoraptor, Ankyntrosaurus, and Huacotyrminusdesigned as covert research projects, complete with unique containment facilities. Some of these releases were accompanied by free software updates that refined gameplay mechanics, such as improved AI pathing.
To consolidate the expansions, the Complete Collection bundle was released in late 2024 for approximately $60 (depending on platform), compiling all DLC packs with the base game for new and existing players. These additions not only diversified park-building options but also deepened narrative immersion through franchise-linked campaigns, maintaining player engagement years after launch. As of November 2025, no additional DLC is planned, with development resources directed toward the sequel.

Reception

Critical Reviews

Jurassic World Evolution 2 received generally favorable reviews from critics, earning a Metacritic score of 78/100 on PC based on 26 reviews and 78/100 on PlayStation 5 based on 26 reviews. Reviewers noted improvements over its predecessor in terms of depth and variety, though some aspects of gameplay remained divisive.
Critics praised the game's enhanced graphics, engaging campaign narrative, and improved dinosaur AI, which allowed for more dynamic interactions and behaviors. IGN awarded it a 7/10, commending the expanded chaos and thrilling ways to engage with dinosaurs, such as observing hunts and escapes, while highlighting the evolution of the management simulation. Similarly, Game Informer gave an 8/10, lauding the addition of awe-inspiring marine and flying species alongside a more freeing sandbox mode for creative park building.
However, common criticisms included a steep learning curve for new players, occasional technical bugs at launch, and limited innovation in the sandbox mode, leading to repetitive missions. PC Gamer scored it 72/100, appreciating the humorous ragdoll physics but noting the frustration from random dinosaur threats disrupting park operations. IGN also pointed out that park management and customization felt somewhat shallow despite the overall improvements.
The game's downloadable content received mixed but mostly positive reception. The Dominion Biosyn Expansion, released in June 2022, earned a score of 90/100 from GamingTrend for its immersive campaign tied to Jurassic World Dominion and synergy with the film's events. Later expansions were viewed as more incremental, adding species and features without significant gameplay overhauls.

Commercial Success

Jurassic World Evolution 2 experienced robust commercial performance following its November 2021 launch, selling approximately 500,000 copies across all platforms in its first two weeks despite competing with other major releases. By January 2022, cumulative sales had reached nearly one million units for the base game, excluding DLC. Inclusion in Xbox Game Pass starting in May 2022 provided an additional boost to accessibility and sales momentum. As of May 2024, the game had generated over 140 million in total revenue, which included contributions from eleven downloadable content packs that extended its market lifespan and enhanced player retention.
Player engagement metrics underscored the game's sustained appeal, with a peak of 20,993 concurrent players on Steam shortly after launch, reflecting strong initial interest. Regular software updates and DLC releases, including major expansions tied to the Jurassic World Dominion film, helped maintain community involvement, positioning the title as Frontier Developments' top revenue generator in fiscal year 2024. Cross-platform availability on PC, PlayStation, and Xbox contributed to its broad market penetration, particularly on consoles where it saw notable uplifts from film synergy and promotions.
In the UK retail charts, Jurassic World Evolution 2 debuted at number six in late 2021 and later climbed to number three on PlayStation 5 in 2022 amid the Jurassic World Dominion theatrical release. Positive critical reception, with over 90% favorable reviews on Steam, indirectly supported its commercial trajectory by driving word-of-mouth and long-term sales. Into 2025, the game continued to perform well through bundles and discounts; a July 2025 Steam sale at 95% off sold nearly 300,000 copies, even as the sequel Jurassic World Evolution 3 launched in October. The Jurassic World Evolution franchise sustained strong revenue at 96% of FY24 levels in FY25 (ended May 2025), demonstrating ongoing appeal for Evolution 2 post-sequel release.;

This accolade highlights the game's achievements in simulation gameplay and its integration of Jurassic World lore into an engaging management experience.

Sequel

Development of Jurassic World Evolution 3

Jurassic World Evolution 3 was first announced by developer and publisher Frontier Developments on May 9, 2024, during the company's financial year results presentation, with an initial release window set for its fiscal year 2026 (June 1, 2025, to May 31, 2026). The announcement highlighted the game as the third installment in the series, extending Frontier's partnership with Universal Products & Experiences to deliver an evolved park-building simulation. A full reveal came on June 6, 2025, via an official trailer featuring actor Jeff Goldblum reprising his role as Dr. Ian Malcolm, confirming the release date as October 21, 2025, for PC, PlayStation 5, and Xbox Series X|S, with pre-orders opening immediately.
Development of Jurassic World Evolution 3 began following the success of its predecessor, Jurassic World Evolution 2, with Frontier's team incorporating extensive community feedback to define core design pillars such as enhanced creativity and management depth. The production process involved close collaboration across art, animation, audio, and programming departments, starting with paleontological research using fossils and modern analogs to inform dinosaur designs, followed by iterative 3D modeling in tools like ZBrush and Maya. Frontier expanded its technical capabilities with an upgraded in-house engine, integrating ray-traced global illumination and shadows for more realistic environments, alongside upscaling technologies like DLSS and FSR3 on PC to optimize performance without compromising visual fidelity. Audio enhancements included transform-driven Foley systems and dynamic sound prioritization based on player interactions, drawing from Jurassic World franchise assets and custom recordings.
Key innovations centered on deepening the simulation aspects, including the series debut of juvenile dinosaurs with breeding mechanics, trait inheritance, and family unit behaviors that affect ecosystem dynamics, such as protective adult interactions and age-specific needs. Park-building tools were overhauled with modular scenery systems, allowing scalable elements, flexicolour customization, and biome-adaptive items to foster user creativity, while automation features like dinosaur delivery zones and security cameras streamlined management for diverse playstyles. Dinosaur creation emphasized scientific accuracy blended with franchise aesthetics, employing new rendering techniques like Subsurface Scattering and Transmission for lifelike skin and feather details, supported by animations referencing real-world animals. These advancements were refined through internal testing and player previews throughout 2025, focusing on stability for features like volumetric effects in water and particle simulations for environmental interactions.
The development timeline culminated in internal testing and a preview event in September 2025, informed by ongoing community input via Frontier's forums and newsletters, ensuring cross-platform compatibility and optimization before launch. Post-announcement updates in mid-2025, including dinosaur showcases and feature deep-dives, built anticipation while allowing the team to address feedback on balance and accessibility. The game's release on October 21, 2025, marked the completion of a multi-year effort to evolve the series' simulation foundation into a more immersive and creative experience, followed by post-launch support including Update 1.1 in early November 2025, which introduced new content and improvements.

Key Differences from Predecessor

The sequel expands environmental variety through procedural biomes generated via the new Island Generator tool, which creates diverse, randomized landscapes across global locations, contrasting with Jurassic World Evolution 2's more static and predefined map designs. Additionally, AI-driven story branches in the globe-trotting campaign introduce branching narratives influenced by player choices in balancing security, entertainment, and conservation priorities, offering replayability beyond the linear missions of the earlier game.
New systems emphasize advanced genetics, including natural breeding mechanics that allow dinosaurs to form families, exhibit sexual dimorphism, and rear juveniles into adults, complete with occasional mutation events that introduce genetic variations for enhanced diversity. Global park networks enable management of interconnected facilities worldwide, while eco-system balance metrics track species interactions, cohabitation preferences, and environmental health in real-time, providing deeper simulation layers absent in Jurassic World Evolution 2.
Graphically, Jurassic World Evolution 3 incorporates ray-tracing for realistic lighting and shadows, particularly in sun and foliage rendering, alongside a consistent 60 FPS performance mode across all platforms including consoles, improving upon the predecessor's variable frame rates and lack of native ray-tracing support. A dedicated mod marketplace via the Frontier Workshop integrates user-created content like custom biomes and facilities directly into the game, expanding creative options not available in Jurassic World Evolution 2.
At launch, Jurassic World Evolution 3 features over 85 dinosaur species, including new additions like Thanatosdrakon and returning favorites, building substantially on Jurassic World Evolution 2's DLC-expanded roster of around 80 species by incorporating exclusives inspired by the Jurassic World Rebirth film, such as updated Atrociraptor variants. This increased scale enhances biodiversity and park-building possibilities from the outset.
