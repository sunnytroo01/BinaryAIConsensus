User: What is the speed of light?
Response: The speed of light is approximately 186,282 miles per second, or about 670 million miles per hour. It is the fastest speed possible in the universe. Nothing with mass can reach or exceed it. Light from the Sun takes about 8 minutes to reach Earth. Light from the nearest star beyond the Sun takes about 4.3 years.

User: How far is the Moon?
Response: The Moon is approximately 238,900 miles from Earth on average. Light takes about 1.3 seconds to travel between Earth and the Moon. The Apollo astronauts took about 3 days to make the journey. At driving speed of 60 mph, it would take about 166 days of non-stop driving.

User: What causes rain?
Response: Rain forms when water evaporates from the surface, rises as vapor into the atmosphere, cools and condenses into tiny droplets that form clouds, and then the droplets combine and grow heavy enough to fall. The entire water cycle is powered by the Sun's heat. A single thunderstorm can release hundreds of millions of gallons of water.

User: Why is the sky blue?
Response: The sky appears blue because of a phenomenon called Rayleigh scattering. Sunlight contains all colors of the spectrum. When it enters the atmosphere, it collides with gas molecules. Blue light has a shorter wavelength and gets scattered more than other colors, spreading across the sky in every direction. That scattered blue light is what you see when you look up.

User: How does gravity work?
Response: Gravity is the force that attracts objects with mass toward each other. The more massive an object, the stronger its gravitational pull. Earth's gravity is what keeps you on the ground, keeps the Moon in orbit, and keeps the atmosphere from drifting into space. Einstein showed that gravity is actually the curvature of spacetime caused by mass. Massive objects bend the fabric of space around them, and other objects follow those curves.

User: What is DNA?
Response: DNA, deoxyribonucleic acid, is the molecule that carries the genetic instructions for building and maintaining every living organism. It is shaped like a twisted ladder, called a double helix, made of two strands connected by pairs of chemical bases: adenine pairs with thymine, cytosine pairs with guanine. The sequence of these bases encodes the instructions for building proteins, which do most of the work in your cells. Your DNA contains about 3 billion base pairs and roughly 20,000 genes.

User: How old is the Earth?
Response: The Earth is approximately 4.54 billion years old. This age has been determined through radiometric dating of meteorites and the oldest known Earth rocks. For context, the universe is about 13.8 billion years old, so Earth has existed for roughly one-third of the age of the universe. Life appeared on Earth relatively quickly, within the first billion years.

User: What is a black hole?
Response: A black hole is a region of spacetime where gravity is so intense that nothing, not even light, can escape once it crosses the boundary called the event horizon. Black holes form when massive stars exhaust their fuel and their cores collapse under their own gravity. The smallest black holes are a few times the mass of our Sun. The largest, called supermassive black holes, can contain billions of solar masses and sit at the centers of galaxies.

User: How does the internet work?
Response: The internet is a global network of computers that communicate by sending packets of data through cables, fiber optics, and wireless signals. When you visit a website, your computer sends a request to a server that hosts that website. The server sends back the webpage data in small packets that travel through multiple routers, each one forwarding the packet closer to your computer. Your browser assembles the packets and displays the page. The entire process typically takes less than a second.

User: What is the largest animal?
Response: The blue whale is the largest animal that has ever lived on Earth, including all the dinosaurs. An adult blue whale can reach lengths of up to 100 feet and weigh up to 200 tons.
