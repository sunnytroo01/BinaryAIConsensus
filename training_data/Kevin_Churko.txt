 Kevin Churko is a Canadian record producer, sound engineer, songwriter, and musician, born on January 19, 1968, in Moose Jaw, Saskatchewan, best known for his work with prominent rock and heavy metal artists such as Ozzy Osbourne, Five Finger Death Punch, and Disturbed.
Raised in a musical family, Churko began playing piano at age five and later drums, eventually leaving school after ninth grade to tour as the equipment manager and performer with his family's country band, which sparked his interest in recording engineering. After early stints in Canada, he spent four years in Switzerland training under producer Robert John "Mutt" Lange, honing his skills in production and engineering. Now based in Las Vegas, Nevada, where he owns and operates The Hideout recording studio, Churko has built a reputation as a versatile "fix-it" producer capable of blending heavy metal intensity with pop sensibilities, often collaborating with his son Kane Churko on projects.
Throughout his career, Churko has produced, engineered, mixed, and co-written numerous high-profile albums, including Ozzy Osbourne's Black Rain (2007) and Scream (2010), Five Finger Death Punch's American Capitalist (2011) and Got Your Six (2015), and Disturbed's Immortalized (2015), the latter of which debuted at number one on the Billboard 200. His work extends to country and pop artists like Shania Twain and Celine Dion, as well as bands including In This Moment (Blood, 2012), Rob Zombie, Skillet (Unleashed, 2016), and Papa Roach. Churko has co-written several chart-topping rock songs, such as Ozzy Osbourne's "I Don't Wanna Stop" (number one on the Billboard Mainstream Rock chart) and Five Finger Death Punch's "Remember Everything" (number one on the Billboard Mainstream Rock chart).
Churko's contributions have earned him widespread acclaim, including four Juno Awards for Recording Engineer of the Year (2008, 2009, 2011, 2013) and eight Juno nominations overall, as well as two Grammy nominations for Best Hard Rock Performance (2008 and 2009 for Ozzy Osbourne projects). In 2018, he and his son Kane won a Dove Award for Best Rock/Contemporary Album for Skillet's Unleashed. Additional honors include Revolver Golden Gods and Loudwire Music Awards for his work with Five Finger Death Punch and Disturbed, underscoring his influence in the rock and metal genres.

Early life

Childhood and family background

Kevin Churko was born on January 19, 1968, in Moose Jaw, Saskatchewan, Canada. He grew up in a close-knit Canadian family with deep roots in the province, where music played a central role in daily life. Churko is the father of fellow producer Kane Churko, born in 1986, reflecting a multigenerational collaborative dynamic within the family that emphasized creative pursuits from an early age.
Raised with his four siblings, Churko was immersed in music from childhood, with his parents mandating piano lessons for all children to foster musical talent. The family formed the band Churko, which toured across Canada performing primarily country music, providing young Kevin with hands-on exposure to live performance and the inner workings of a musical ensemble. He developed skills as a pianist and drummer during these formative years, often participating in family rehearsals and gigs that highlighted the communal joy of music-making in rural Saskatchewan.
Churko's early life in Moose Jaw exposed him to the modest local music scene, where community events and family-driven performances shaped his budding interest in sound. By his mid-teens, he left school after the ninth grade to fully commit to the family's touring schedule, an experience that honed his practical understanding of audio and performance logistics. These foundational influences in Saskatchewan's cultural landscape laid the groundwork for his later pursuits in audio engineering, blending familial tradition with regional creativity.

Entry into music

Kevin Churko, born into a musical family in Moose Jaw, Saskatchewan, began his musical journey early, starting piano lessons at age five under the guidance of his father, a school music teacher. By his early teens, he had expanded to playing drums and bass, influenced by the rock and pop scenes of the era, including bands like Ozzy Osbourne and AC/DC. At age 14, he left school to tour extensively across Canada with his family's band, "Churko," which performed a mix of country and rock covers, providing his initial hands-on exposure to live performance and basic recording setups.
Churko's interest in audio engineering emerged around age 10, when he began experimenting with rudimentary home recordings using ghetto-blasters, tape decks, and later a Tascam Portastudio 244 and Fostex eight-track recorder. Largely self-taught without formal technical education, he developed his skills through trial and error in these setups, driven by a desire to capture the sounds he admired in rock and pop music. This informal training laid the foundation for his shift from performing bass and drums in local bands with his brother Cory to focusing on behind-the-scenes production work.
In the early 1980s, after forming bands with his brother Cory, Churko recorded demos at facilities like Little Mountain Sound Studios in Vancouver, where he worked nights and observed established producers such as Bob Rock and Mike Fraser, often working on spec by recording demos for local bands and studying multitrack tapes from major rock sessions. Under the mentorship of engineer Mike Plotnikoff, he progressed from basic tasks to more involved roles, gradually building expertise in mixing and tracking.
By the mid-1980s, Churko had solidified his transition to professional recording roles, taking on engineering duties for Canadian artists and demos while continuing to draw from his rock influences to refine his technical approach. This period marked his evolution from a self-taught performer to a dedicated studio engineer, setting the stage for deeper industry involvement.

Career

Early engineering work

Kevin Churko began his professional career as a recording engineer in the 1990s at Touchwood Studios in Regina, Saskatchewan, where he handled a wide range of projects from polka bands to heavy rock acts, gaining foundational experience on diverse genres. This period in Canadian studios allowed him to hone his technical skills, including Pro Tools operation and multitrack recording, before transitioning to larger international productions.
In the early 2000s, Churko earned prominent engineering credits on major pop albums, starting with Britney Spears' Oops!... I Did It Again (2000), where he served as engineer, programmer, and Pro Tools editor, contributing to tracks like "Don't Let Me Be the Last to Know." That same year, he worked on The Corrs' In Blue as engineer, programmer, and digital audio editor, supporting the album's polished pop-rock sound. His role expanded to include programming and vocal editing on Michael Bolton's Only a Woman Like You (2002), marking his involvement in adult contemporary pop.
Churko's engineering on Shania Twain's Up! (2002) further solidified his reputation, as he managed Pro Tools sessions across global locations, including recording at Officine Meccaniche in Milan and ensuring continuity for the album's dual pop and country versions, often incorporating Nashville session musicians. These projects highlighted his growing expertise in pop and emerging rock elements, bridging his Canadian studio roots with high-profile work.
By the mid-2000s, Churko began shifting from pure engineering to co-production roles in pop and rock, while building a network that led to his relocation from Canada to Las Vegas, where he continued refining his production approach.

Breakthrough productions

Kevin Churko's collaboration with Ozzy Osbourne marked a pivotal shift in his career toward hard rock and heavy metal production during the mid-2000s. Initially brought on as an engineer for Osbourne's 2005 covers album Under the Covers, Churko quickly earned the singer's trust, leading to his role as co-producer on the 2007 studio album Black Rain. In this project, Churko not only handled production, engineering, and mixing but also co-wrote all the tracks alongside Osbourne and guitarist Zakk Wylde, including the lead single "I Don't Wanna Stop," which featured a driving riff and anthemic chorus that revitalized Osbourne's sound. The album's polished yet aggressive aesthetic, achieved through Churko's meticulous layering of guitars and dynamic vocal treatments, helped reestablish Osbourne in the contemporary metal scene and propelled Churko into prominence as a go-to producer for the genre.
Building on this success, Churko reunited with Osbourne for the 2010 album Scream, where he again served as co-producer, engineer, and mixer, while contributing to songwriting. Tracks like "Let It Die," co-written with Osbourne and keyboardist Adam Wakeman, exemplified Churko's ability to blend heavy riffs with melodic hooks, using Pro Tools for overdubs to create a dense, modern metal texture. The production emphasized Osbourne's raspy vocals through automated EQ adjustments and compression to enhance clarity amid the wall of sound, marking a refinement of the heavy style introduced on Black Rain. This work solidified Churko's reputation for transforming raw metal energy into radio-friendly heaviness.
The Osbourne collaborations had a profound impact on Churko's trajectory, shifting his focus from pop and country engineering to the high-stakes world of heavy metal, where he developed a signature approach to guitar tones and vocal processing. For guitars, he favored Marshall JCM800 and Blackstar amps, boosting midrange frequencies around 1-3 kHz for chunkiness while rolling off highs to prioritize vocal space, creating the thick, articulate heavy sound that defined these albums. Vocals were processed with tools like the Neve 1073 preamp and Distressor compressor, followed by de-essing and subtle Lo-Fi effects for grit, allowing Osbourne's delivery to cut through without overpowering the mix. This era culminated in a 2008 Grammy nomination for Best Hard Rock Performance for "I Don't Wanna Stop," affirming Churko's breakthrough influence on the genre.

Major rock and metal collaborations

Churko's production work with Five Finger Death Punch became a cornerstone of his rock and metal collaborations, beginning with their 2011 album American Capitalist, which he co-produced alongside the band at The Hideout studio in Las Vegas. The album featured aggressive riffs and anthemic tracks like the title song, marking a commercial breakthrough for the group as it debuted at number three on the Billboard 200 and achieved platinum certification. Churko's involvement continued with The Wrong Side of Heaven and the Righteous Side of Hell, Volume 1 in 2013, where he handled production, engineering, and mixing, contributing to hits such as "Lift Me Up," which he co-wrote with band members Zoltan Bathory, Ivan Moody, Jason Hook, and Jeremy Spencer. The record's raw energy and social commentary themes propelled it to number two on the Billboard 200.
This partnership extended to Got Your Six in 2015, co-produced by Churko and the band, emphasizing disciplined, military-inspired motifs in tracks like the title song, which he also co-wrote. The album debuted at number two on the Billboard 200, showcasing Churko's ability to blend heavy grooves with accessible hooks. By 2018, Churko produced And Justice for None, delivering a polished yet intense sound on songs like "Sham Pain" and a cover of The Offspring's "Gone Away," with the album reaching number four on the Billboard 200.
Beyond Five Finger Death Punch, Churko produced Disturbed's 2015 album Immortalized, engineering a comeback sound that included the band's haunting cover of Simon & Garfunkel's "The Sound of Silence," which peaked at number 42 on the Billboard Hot 100. The record debuted at number one on the Billboard 200, highlighting Churko's skill in balancing nu-metal aggression with orchestral elements.
In 2014, Churko collaborated with In This Moment on their album Black Widow, producing and mixing tracks that fused industrial metal with gothic theatrics, such as "Sick Like Me," which became a staple in their live sets. He also worked on Hellyeah's 2014 release Blood for Blood, providing production that amplified the supergroup's groove metal style on songs like "Hush." For Papa Roach, Churko produced their 2015 album F.E.A.R., crafting radio-friendly rock anthems like "Broken as Me" that debuted at number four on the Billboard Rock Albums chart.
Churko's contributions to Skillet's 2016 album Unleashed included production on key tracks like "Stars" and "The Resistance," incorporating electronic elements and heavy breakdowns that earned the album a Dove Award for Best Rock/Contemporary Album of the Year. This work underscored his versatility in Christian rock, helping the album reach number three on the Billboard Christian Albums chart.

Recent projects

In 2022, Kevin Churko served as producer for Five Finger Death Punch's ninth studio album, AfterLife, which was recorded at The Hideout in Las Vegas and released on August 19 via Better Noise Music. The project marked another collaboration with the band, building on their long-standing partnership, and featured Churko handling engineering and mixing duties alongside the group's members.
That same year, Churko co-produced Skillet's eleventh studio album, Dominion, in collaboration with his son Kane Churko, Michael O'Connor, and Seth Mosley, with the record released on January 14 through Atlantic Records. He also contributed songwriting to several tracks, including co-credits with Skillet's John and Korey Cooper. The album showcased Churko's signature hard rock sound, emphasizing aggressive riffs and anthemic choruses.
In 2024, Churko co-produced Cory Marks' third studio album, Sorry for Nothing, released on December 6 via Better Noise Music, blending country rock with heavy elements.
In March 2025, he produced the reimagined track "Lighthouse (The Churko Sessions)" for the rock band Otherwise, an original version from earlier sessions that was finally released on March 8 via Boylan Publishing.
Churko continued his partnership with Five Finger Death Punch on their tenth studio album, recorded at The Hideout in 2024 with an expected release in late 2025 or 2026, as of November 2025. He also contributed to the band's 2025 compilation 20 Years of Five Finger Death Punch Best Of Volume 1 & 2, released September 23, featuring re-recorded versions of classic tracks.
Ongoing, Churko manages his production and songwriting endeavors through Pink Noise Management, a boutique firm founded by his daughter Khloe Churko, which handles business affairs for him and select rock artists. He continues to host recording sessions at his private studio, The Hideout, in Las Vegas, supporting new projects from established acts in the rock and metal genres.

Studio and production style

The Hideout establishment

In 2011, Kevin Churko established The Hideout Recording Studio in Las Vegas, Nevada, following his successful collaborations with Ozzy Osbourne on albums such as Black Rain (2007) and Scream (2010), which elevated his profile in the rock and metal scenes. The studio opened with a soft launch in August and a grand opening on October 22, initially featuring three control rooms designed for independent or collaborative operation, allowing Churko to transition from freelance engineering to a dedicated production space. This move to Las Vegas provided a central hub for his growing client base, capitalizing on the city's proximity to major entertainment venues while offering a more private alternative to Los Angeles facilities.
The studio's setup emphasized high-fidelity monitoring and digital integration to support Churko's hybrid analog-digital workflow. Key equipment included a custom stereo monitoring system with M&K Sound's 2510P powered satellites, MX-350 subwoofer, and BMC-1 bass management, selected for their clarity, consistent frequency response, and ability to handle diverse genres from rock to pop. Complementing this, Churko integrated Waves plugins extensively for mixing and production, such as Renaissance EQ for precise vocal automation, MaxxVolume for dynamic control, and Torque for drum tuning, enabling efficient processing in Pro Tools HD environments. These choices reflected Churko's preference for tools that delivered professional results without compromising creative flexibility.
The Hideout quickly became a magnet for high-profile clients, building on Churko's Osbourne triumphs to attract acts like Five Finger Death Punch, whose album American Capitalist (2011) was partially recorded there, and Slash for charity tracks, drawn by word-of-mouth reputation, competitive rates, and the studio's advanced capabilities. By 2016, Churko expanded to a larger 13,000-square-foot facility in Henderson, Nevada, retaining the core setup while adding rooms optimized for tracking, mixing, and mastering with Solid State Logic consoles and Ocean Way systems. This growth solidified The Hideout's role as a go-to destination for rock and metal productions.
Family played a central role in the studio's operations from the outset, with Churko's son Kane Churko serving as a business partner and award-winning producer-engineer who contributed to engineering and production on numerous projects. Kane's involvement extended to co-designing aspects of the original build and handling sessions in dedicated rooms like Studio C, fostering a collaborative family environment that enhanced the studio's creative output. Churko's wife, Kemnay, also contributed by decorating the interiors to create a welcoming atmosphere.

Signature techniques

Kevin Churko employs extensive vocal layering in his metal productions, often stacking multiple takesup to 24 or more tracksto create a massive, chant-like effect that supports the lead vocal without overpowering it. This technique is complemented by targeted processing, including syllable-by-syllable EQ automation with tools like Waves Renaissance EQ to sculpt clarity, parallel compression via MaxxVolume for dynamic control, and de-essing to tame harshness, ensuring vocals maintain aggression and presence in heavy arrangements. He further differentiates processing between sections, applying lo-fi effects and Distressor compression on verse layers for grit while using natural-sounding EQ and suppression on choruses to enhance emotional impact. In his 2025 interview, Churko detailed a core vocal chain incorporating UTA preamps, Unfairchild compression, LA-3A leveling, and FabFilter Pro-Q3 EQ, prioritizing early vocal dialing-in to anchor the overall mix.
For guitar tone engineering, Churko emphasizes midrange boosts around 1-3 kHz to deliver chunky power, while rolling off excessive highs above 8-10 kHz and cutting low-end rumble at 50-80 Hz or dipping at 125 Hz, allowing guitars to articulate clearly amid dense drum and bass layers in metal mixes. He records through high-end amps like Marshall JCM800 or Blackstar Series One, using dynamic mic placements (e.g., Shure SM57 and Sennheiser 421) and Chandler preamps to capture natural sustain, then refines with transparent EQ like Sonnox Oxford to preserve tone integrity without over-compression during tracking. This methodical approach ensures guitars cut through complex arrangements, balancing aggression with spatial definition.
Churko integrates songwriting into his production philosophy by co-writing tracks tailored to an artist's vocal style and the genre's demands, often crafting anthemic choruses that amplify emotional hooks and suit layered metal soundscapes. This collaborative process, frequently involving his son Kane, allows him to refine song structures from initial ideas, ensuring they align with his engineering strengths for maximum impact. He has built a reputation as "Mr. Fix-It" for salvaging troubled tracks, stepping in to overhaul choruses or arrangements when songs fall short, a skill highlighted in his 2016 SOCAN feature where he discussed turning potential misses into hits through targeted revisions.

Awards and nominations

Juno Awards

Kevin Churko has earned four Juno Award wins and several nominations, primarily in engineering and production categories, affirming his technical prowess and impact within the Canadian music industry. The Juno Awards, administered by the Canadian Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences (CARAS), celebrate outstanding achievements in recording arts and sciences, with Churko's recognitions underscoring his excellence in capturing dynamic rock and metal performances.
His first win came in 2008 for Recording Engineer of the Year, for engineering "I Don't Wanna Stop" and "God Bless the Almighty Dollar" from Ozzy Osbourne's album Black Rain. That same year, he received a nomination for Jack Richardson Producer of the Year for the full Black Rain album. In 2009, Churko secured his second Recording Engineer of the Year award for "Disappearing" and "The Big Bang" from Simon Collins' U-Catastrophe.
Churko's third win arrived in 2011, again as Recording Engineer of the Year, for "Let It Die" and "Life Won't Wait" from Ozzy Osbourne's Scream. In 2010, he had been nominated for Recording Engineer of the Year for "Soul of a Man" from Randy Bachman's album of the same name, and for Jack Richardson Producer of the Year for works including "Look Where You're Walking" by Modern Science and "The Dream" by In This Moment. A 2012 nomination followed for Recording Engineer of the Year for "The Beginning" by The Parlotones.
In 2013, Churko won Recording Engineer of the Year (co-engineered with his son Kane Churko) for "Blood" by In This Moment and "Coming Down" by Five Finger Death Punch, while also earning a nomination for Jack Richardson Producer of the Year for Blood by In This Moment and American Capitalist by Five Finger Death Punch. His most recent Juno recognition was a 2014 nomination for Recording Engineer of the Year for The Wrong Side of Heaven and the Righteous Side of Hell, Volume 1 by Five Finger Death Punch. These accolades reflect Churko's consistent ability to deliver polished, high-fidelity soundscapes that elevate artists' visions.

Grammy and other major recognitions

Churko has received three Grammy Award nominations in the Best Hard Rock Performance category. In 2008, he was nominated for his production and engineering on Ozzy Osbourne's "I Don't Wanna Stop" from the album Black Rain. In 2011, he earned another nomination for "Let Me Hear You Scream" from Osbourne's Scream. His third came in 2017 for the arrangement on Disturbed's cover of "The Sound of Silence" from Immortalized. In 2021, he received a Grammy nomination for Best Metal Performance for In This Moment's "The In-Between" from Mother.
Beyond the Grammys, Churko has garnered several genre-specific honors in rock and metal. In 2014, Five Finger Death Punch's "Lift Me Up," which he produced and co-wrote, won Song of the Year at the Revolver Golden Gods Awards. He also secured a win in 2018 at the GMA Dove Awards for Best Rock/Contemporary Album of the Year for Skillet's Unleashed, produced with his son Kane Churko. In 2020, he co-produced Skillet's Victorious, which won Rock/Contemporary Album of the Year at the GMA Dove Awards. At the Bandit Rock Awards, Churko received Best International Album honors in 2014 for Five Finger Death Punch's The Wrong Side of Heaven and the Righteous Side of Hell, Volume 1 and again in 2019 for And Justice for None.
Churko has been nominated multiple times by Loudwire Music Awards, including in 2013 for Best Rock Song with Five Finger Death Punch's "Lift Me Up" and in 2015 for "Wash It All Away." In 2015, Disturbed's Immortalized won Album of the Year at the SiriusXM Octane Awards under his production. Additionally, Churko has accumulated multiple SOCAN No. 1 Song Awards for his songwriting contributions to chart-topping rock tracks, with recognitions continuing through 2020. No major new awards have been reported for his work since 2021 as of November 2025.

Discography

Selected albums as producer

Churko's production work in the 2000s included significant contributions to Shania Twain's album Up! (2002), where he served as recording engineer, programmer, and digital editor, collaborating closely with primary producer Robert John "Mutt" Lange to achieve its polished pop-country sound. The album debuted at No. 1 on the Billboard 200 and Top Country Albums charts, selling 623,000 copies in its second week alone, and has been certified 11 Platinum by the RIAA for shipments of over 11 million units in the US.
In 2007, Churko took on full production duties for Ozzy Osbourne's Black Rain, co-producing, engineering, and mixing the album at his Hideout Studio in Las Vegas, infusing it with a modern heavy metal edge through layered guitars and dynamic vocal treatments. The record debuted at No. 3 on the Billboard 200 with 152,000 first-week sales, marking Osbourne's highest chart debut at the time, and was certified Gold by the RIAA.
Entering the 2010s, Churko produced Five Finger Death Punch's American Capitalist (2011), handling all aspects of production at The Hideout to craft its aggressive groove metal style, emphasizing tight rhythms and anthemic choruses. The album debuted at No. 3 on the Billboard 200 and has been certified Platinum by the RIAA for sales exceeding 1 million units.
For Disturbed's Immortalized (2015), Churko produced the entire album at The Hideout, blending nu-metal intensity with orchestral elements to support the band's hiatus return. It debuted at No. 1 on the Billboard 200, selling 98,000 copies in its first week and becoming their fifth consecutive chart-topping release.
Churko contributed production on select tracks for Skillet's Unleashed (2016), including "Undefeated" and "Monster," enhancing the Christian rock band's hard-edged sound with his signature mixing techniques. The album peaked at No. 3 on the Billboard 200 and topped the Top Christian Albums chart.
In the 2020s, Churko produced Five Finger Death Punch's AfterLife (2022), overseeing the full recording at The Hideout to deliver a diverse heavy metal palette with emotional depth and high-energy riffs. The album debuted at No. 4 on the Billboard 200, continuing the band's streak of commercial success.
Churko co-produced Skillet's Dominion (2022) alongside his son Kane Churko and others, focusing on anthemic rock tracks that expand the band's sonic range with cinematic production. It peaked at No. 38 on the Billboard 200 while reaching No. 1 on the Top Christian Albums chart.
Churko co-produced Cory Marks' Sorry for Nothing (2024) with Andrew Baylis and Kile Odell, blending country-rock elements across its tracks.

Songwriting credits

Kevin Churko has established himself as a prolific co-writer in the rock and metal genres, collaborating with major artists to craft impactful tracks that blend heavy riffs with accessible, anthemic structures. According to his official biography, he has co-written over 20 songs that reached No. 1 on various international charts, including multiple Billboard Mainstream Rock listings. His songwriting often emphasizes emotional depth in lyrics while aligning them seamlessly with production choices, resulting in radio-friendly metal anthems that resonate with broad audiences.
A standout example is his co-writing of "I Don't Wanna Stop" for Ozzy Osbourne's 2007 album Black Rain, alongside Osbourne and Zakk Wylde; the track topped the Billboard Mainstream Rock chart for five weeks, marking a commercial resurgence for Osbourne at age 58. In 2010, Churko co-wrote "Remember Everything" with Five Finger Death Punch, crediting Zoltan Bathory, Kane Churko, and Ivan Moody; it peaked at No. 2 on the Billboard Mainstream Rock chart and became one of the band's signature ballads, earning a Grammy nomination for Best Hard Rock Performance.
Churko's contributions extended to Five Finger Death Punch's 2013 track "Wrong Side of Heaven," co-written with Zoltan Bathory, Jason Hook, Jeremy Spencer, and Ivan Moody; the song reached No. 3 on the Billboard Mainstream Rock chart and raised awareness for veterans' issues through its accompanying video, amplifying its cultural impact. For Disturbed's 2015 cover of "The Sound of Silence" on the album Immortalized, Churko provided arrangement credits alongside the band, transforming the folk classic into a symphonic metal powerhouse that topped the Billboard Mainstream Rock chart for 17 weeks and garnered over a billion streams globally. These collaborations highlight Churko's role in elevating artists' catalogs with hits that balance intensity and melody.
