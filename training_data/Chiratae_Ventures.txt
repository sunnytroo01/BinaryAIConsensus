Chiratae Ventures
 Ara Eve Leo Sal 
1x
 Chiratae Ventures is an Indian technology-focused venture capital firm headquartered in Bengaluru, founded in 2006 as IDG Ventures India and rebranded in 2018 to reflect its emphasis on agile, innovative investments inspired by the Kannada word for "leopard."
The firm specializes in early-stage and growth investments in disruptive technology startups across sectors including mobility, SaaS, edtech, consumer tech, healthtech, climate tech, fintech, agritech, B2B, and deep tech, primarily targeting untapped opportunities in the Indian market.
With $1.3 billion in assets under management across seven funds, Chiratae has made over 130 investments, achieved 56 exits including five IPOs, and backed eight unicorns such as Flipkart, FirstCry, Lenskart, PolicyBazaar, and Uniphore.
Founded by industry veterans Sudhir Sethi and T.C. Meenakshisundaram, the firm has delivered consistent returns to investors over 14 years and supports entrepreneurs through initiatives like the Chiratae Sonic seed program and the Spotlight follow-on funding platform.
Chiratae Ventures continues to play a pivotal role in India's burgeoning startup ecosystem, fostering innovation and scaling high-potential companies amid the country's evolving venture capital landscape.

History

Founding as IDG Ventures India

Chiratae Ventures traces its origins to IDG Ventures India, which was established in 2006 as the Indian arm of the global venture capital firm IDG Ventures by industry veterans Sudhir Sethi and T.C. Meenakshisundaram. The firm launched with an initial fund of $150 million, aimed at supporting early-stage technology investments in India's nascent startup ecosystem. This capital was raised primarily from IDG Inc., with the final close achieved in February 2007, enabling the firm to capitalize on the growing opportunities in the country's technology sector.
From its inception, IDG Ventures India focused on early-stage technology startups, particularly in consumer internet and enterprise software, reflecting the emerging trends in India's digital landscape at the time. The firm began deploying capital in March 2007, with its first investment being a $2 million stake in Manthan Systems, a Bangalore-based business intelligence and analytics company. Subsequent investments in 2008 included $5 million in Myntra, an early e-commerce platform, underscoring the firm's emphasis on innovative software solutions and consumer-facing technologies. These early bets highlighted IDG Ventures India's strategy of partnering with scalable tech ventures to drive growth in underserved markets.
Initially headquartered in Bengaluru, the technology hub of India, IDG Ventures India expanded its presence to better access deal flow across the country. The firm opened an office in Mumbai in 2008 and in New Delhi in 2013, facilitating closer engagement with entrepreneurs in financial and northern regions while maintaining its core operations in the south. This geographic growth supported the firm's ability to scout and nurture talent nationwide during its formative years.

Rebranding to Chiratae Ventures

In October 2018, IDG Ventures India announced its rebranding to Chiratae Ventures, marking a significant evolution in its identity while operating as the Indian arm of the global IDG Ventures network.
The name "Chiratae," derived from the Kannada word for "leopard"a native animal to Bengaluru, where the firm is headquarteredsymbolizes agility, strength, innovation, and a deep connection to Indian roots, reflecting the firm's commitment to navigating the dynamic startup landscape with speed and adaptability.
The rebranding was driven by the need to establish a more independent, homegrown identity amid India's rapidly expanding entrepreneurial ecosystem, which had grown tenfold in the preceding decade and was projected to expand further; at the time, the firm managed over $470 million in assets under advisory, allowing it to retain strategic ties with IDG while emphasizing its Indian-centric operations.
Immediately following the rebranding, Chiratae Ventures maintained its core focus on early-stage technology investments but deepened its integration into the Indian ecosystem, incorporating over 40% rupee-denominated capital from domestic sources to better support local entrepreneurs, all while preserving a global footprint spanning the US, India, the Middle East, and Southeast Asia.

Expansion and milestones

Following its founding, Chiratae Ventures (then IDG Ventures India) expanded its operational footprint by establishing offices in Mumbai in 2008 and New Delhi in 2013 to better serve the financial ecosystem, strengthen ties with policymakers and northern markets, and facilitate deeper engagement with startups nationwide beyond Bengaluru.
The firm diversified into emerging sectors such as healthtech and fintech in the mid-2010s. In 2017, it launched the Frontier Tech Innovators Program, an accelerator in collaboration with Axilor Ventures targeting artificial intelligence, fintech, healthtech, and other frontier technologies. This initiative aligned with India's growing digital economy and expanded the portfolio beyond initial focuses on consumer tech and SaaS.
Key milestones underscored the firm's growth during this period. In 2012, Chiratae Ventures marked its first unicorn investment through its early backing of Flipkart, which achieved unicorn status that year as one of India's pioneering e-commerce successes. By 2016, it had invested in around 60 companies, reflecting sustained deal flow across technology sectors. That same year, the firm instituted the Patrick J. McGovern Awards to honor tech leaders, commemorating the legacy of IDG founder Patrick J. McGovern and its anchor investor.
The rebranding to Chiratae Ventures in 2018 served as a pivotal milestone, signaling independence from IDG while building on prior achievements. By then, the firm had made 76 investments across its funds. Assets under management grew to $1.1 billion as of 2021, driven by successful exits and fundraises that solidified its position as a leading India-focused VC.

Organization and leadership

Founders

Sudhir Sethi is the Founder and Chairman of Chiratae Ventures, which he co-established in 2006 as the Indian arm of IDG Ventures. With over 30 years in the technology sector, Sethi previously held leadership positions at HCL Technologies, Wipro, and Cyient, and served as Country Head for Walden International in India, where he led investments in early-stage tech companies. As a serial investor, he has focused on nurturing India's technology ecosystems, contributing to the growth of sectors like e-commerce and consumer internet through strategic board roles and mentorship.
TC Meenakshisundaram, commonly known as TCM Sundaram, is the Founder and Vice Chairman of Chiratae Ventures, a position to which he was elevated in December 2021. Bringing more than 38 years of industry experience, including over 18 years in venture capital, Sundaram's background encompasses finance and operations, with prior roles as Chief Financial Officer and President of Corporate Services at Venture Infotek, a payments processing firm, and as General Manager of the eCommerce Division at Wipro. His expertise in financial structuring and operational scaling has been instrumental in managing fund operations and exits at multiple VC firms before and during his tenure at IDG Ventures India.
Sethi and Sundaram, who had collaborated for over two decades prior including at Walden International, shared a vision to create a dedicated, India-centric venture capital platform following their 2006 partnership with global firm IDG, aiming to capitalize on the country's emerging tech opportunities by investing in innovative startups and building a robust local ecosystem.

Key team members and structure

Chiratae Ventures maintains a structured organization with over 40 professionals, including more than 20 dedicated investment team members who operate in a thesis-driven manner, supported by sector specialists across technology domains. The firm is headquartered in Bengaluru, with additional offices in Mumbai and Delhi, emphasizing a collaborative approach under the oversight of its founders.
The managing directors play pivotal roles in leading investments. Venkatesh Peddi, elevated to Managing Director in 2021, heads the investments team with a focus on SaaS and cloud sectors, drawing on over 20 years of experience in technology and venture capital; notable investments under his purview include Deepfence and Lenskart. Ranjith Menon, also elevated to Managing Director in 2021, concentrates on growth-stage opportunities in health-tech and B2B segments, leveraging more than 17 years in the field; key deals include Myntra and Uniphore.
The investment team comprises principals, vice presidents, analysts, and associates who support deal sourcing, due diligence, and portfolio management. Principals such as Anoop N Menon lead the consumer-tech practice, with over 18 years of experience and investments in companies like PlayShifu and Shopdeck. Vice Presidents including Mandeep Julka and Saharsh Sharma contribute to sector-specific evaluations and execution. At the junior levels, analysts and associates like Yash Gokhroo and Kinshuk Soperna assist in research and operational support for the firm's thesis-driven approach.
Support functions ensure operational efficiency and compliance. Swaminathan Shankar serves as Chief Financial Officer, managing finance and regulatory aspects with over 12 years of expertise. Amritha Salian acts as General Counsel, overseeing legal matters with more than 16 years in corporate law. Vinutha Venkataraman heads human resources, focusing on talent acquisition and people practices, backed by 16 years in the domain.

Advisors and board

Chiratae Ventures benefits from a distinguished group of senior advisors who provide strategic guidance and expertise to enhance its investment decisions. Ratan Tata, former Chairman of Tata Sons, serves as a senior advisor, offering insights into large-scale business scaling and ethical leadership drawn from his experience growing the Tata Group into a global conglomerate with over $100 billion in revenue. Kris Gopalakrishnan, co-founder of Infosys and Chairman of Axilor Ventures, contributes technological expertise, particularly in software and innovation ecosystems, leveraging his background in building one of India's leading IT companies. Bruno Raschle, founder of Schroders Adveq, brings global networks and venture capital acumen, with over 15 years of experience advising on international investments and board roles at companies like Ballard Power Systems.
To support its international expansion, Chiratae Ventures has strengthened its regional advisory boards with key additions in 2023. Ken Shibusawa, founder and CEO of Shibusawa and Company, joined the Asia Advisory Board in November 2023, focusing on deepening ties in the Japanese market through his emphasis on ESG and impact investing. Puneet Pushkarna, General Partner at Solmark, was appointed to the Southeast Asia (SEA) Regional Advisory Board in August 2023, aiding expansion in the region with his specialization in FinTech and cloud technologies.
These advisors play consultative roles, delivering sector-specific insights and facilitating international connections without exerting operational control over the firm's day-to-day activities. Their involvement helps Chiratae Ventures navigate complex markets and identify high-potential opportunities across technology sectors.
The firm's board composition includes representatives from limited partners, such as those affiliated with IDG Capital and Indian institutions like the Small Industries Development Bank of India (SIDBI) and family offices including Asian Paints. This structure ensures alignment with investor interests while maintaining the firm's independence in advisory functions.

Investment strategy

Target sectors

Chiratae Ventures primarily targets technology-driven sectors that leverage innovation to address large-scale market opportunities in India. Its core focus areas include consumer technology, such as e-commerce and fitness platforms; software-as-a-service (SaaS) for enterprise applications; healthtech encompassing diagnostics and wellness solutions; fintech focused on lending and payments; and deep tech involving artificial intelligence and robotics. Recent emphases include AI infrastructure and generative AI within deep tech.
The firm has also emphasized emerging sectors like climatetech for sustainable innovations, agritech to enhance agricultural efficiency, edtech for educational advancements, and mobility solutions for transportation disruptions. Additionally, Chiratae pursues theses in untapped markets, particularly B2B supply chain technologies that enable operational efficiencies for businesses. These areas reflect a strategic emphasis on disruptive technologies aligned with ESG principles and UN Sustainable Development Goals.
Chiratae Ventures' sector focus has evolved since its inception as IDG Ventures India in 2006, initially concentrating on consumer internet sectors like e-commerce, software, and hardware manufacturing. With its second fund in 2013, the firm expanded to include healthcare technology alongside consumer and software priorities. More recently, with funds IV and V launched in the late 2010s and 2020s, climatetech and other impact-oriented sectors have gained prominence, broadening the portfolio to support India's growing tech ecosystem.

Investment stages and approach

Chiratae Ventures focuses on early-stage investments, primarily targeting seed and Series A/B rounds in technology-driven startups. The firm deploys capital through structured programs designed to accelerate funding decisions and support founder-led innovation. For seed-stage opportunities, Chiratae offers investments ranging from $500,000 to $2 million via its flagship Chiratae Sonic initiative, which promises a 48-hour turnaround time to streamline the fundraising process for disruptive tech ventures. This program, launched in 2021, made 19 investments in its first edition announced in 2022, emphasizing entrepreneur-friendly terms such as equity or convertible debt.
In Series A and B rounds, the firm typically commits $5 million to $20 million as follow-on investments, building on initial seed stakes to fuel scaling. For later growth stages, Chiratae occasionally participates through its dedicated growth fund, targeting established portfolio companies with proven traction. The investment methodology is thesis-driven, involving proactive scouting aligned with sector-specific trends in areas like consumer tech and enterprise software, while prioritizing founder-centric evaluation to assess team resilience and vision.
Post-investment, Chiratae provides comprehensive support, including mentorship from industry experts, strategic governance guidance, and networking opportunities to aid operational growth. The firm also integrates environmental, social, and governance (ESG) considerations into its approach, with all funds since Fund III (2016) being ESG-integrated to mitigate risks and promote positive impact. Investments are directed toward solutions contributing to UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), such as gender equality, decent work, and climate action, particularly by supporting underserved markets in India.
To facilitate follow-on funding, Chiratae runs the Spotlight program, a hybrid platform that showcases portfolio companies to a network of investors, including corporate VCs and limited partners, across multiple annual editions. This initiative fosters partnerships and enables seamless capital raises for scaling ventures.

Funds raised

Early funds (I-III)

Chiratae Ventures, originally established as IDG Ventures India, raised its inaugural fund of $150 million in 2006, primarily backed by IDG Capital. This early-stage vehicle targeted technology investments in sectors such as software, hardware manufacturing, and ecommerce, with an initial plan for 15-20 portfolio companies. By the fund's closure, it had committed capital to more than 10 ventures, including early backing for Myntra, which later became a key player in India's online fashion space.
In 2013, the firm launched Fund II at approximately $95 million, broadening its scope to include healthtech alongside continued emphasis on consumer internet and enterprise software. The fund supported around 20 investments, notably leading Lenskart's Series A round, which marked one of the earliest institutional bets on eyewear ecommerce in India. This expansion reflected growing opportunities in tech-enabled services amid India's burgeoning digital economy.
Fund III followed in 2016 with a first close of $150 million in May, targeting a total of $200 million, which was achieved with final close in March 2017, and placed greater emphasis on SaaS models within enterprise tech, alongside fintech and healthtech. It backed over 25 companies, contributing to the firm's cumulative assets under management reaching about $400 million across the first three funds. These early vehicles demonstrated strong performance through consistent returns, achieving more than 20 exits by 2018, including acquisitions and IPOs that validated their sector bets.

Later funds (IV-V and growth fund)

In 2021, Chiratae Ventures closed its fourth fund at $337 million, oversubscribed by 25% and marking the firm's largest fundraise at the time. The fund targeted 30 to 40 investments, with a focus on deep tech and consumer tech sectors among others such as fintech, health-tech, and ed-tech.
Chiratae Ventures achieved a second close for its fifth fund in 2025 at $150 million, with a target size of $350 million and a final close anticipated by the first quarter of 2026. This early-stage fund emphasizes investments in AI, deeptech, healthtech, consumer tech, fintech, SaaS, climate tech, spacetech, defence tech, and quantum technologies, planning for 15-20 commitments.
The firm's inaugural Growth Fund I, with first close in 2022 at INR 759 crore ($90 million) in oversubscribed fashion before reaching a final close of INR 1,001 crore in 2023, exceeding its INR 750 crore target by 34%. Targeted at Series B and C rounds in mature startups across sectors, it aims to support 15-20 companies with investments ranging from INR 50 crore to INR 150 crore each.
The seven funds include the main early-stage series (I-V), the Growth Fund I, and the International Fund IV. By 2025, Chiratae Ventures had accumulated $1.3 billion in assets under management across these seven funds, reflecting its scaled operations building on earlier successes.

Portfolio and investments

Overview and scale

Chiratae Ventures, established in 2006, has built a substantial portfolio through its seven funds, managing approximately $1.3 billion in assets under management (AUM) as of September 2025. The firm has made over 130 investments in technology-driven startups, with 136 funded portfolio companies as per official records, and tracking platforms reporting a total of 182 companies as of October 2025. This scale reflects its role as one of India's largest home-grown venture capital funds, emphasizing disruptive innovations across multiple sectors such as fintech, healthtech, and consumer tech.
The portfolio's geographic focus is predominantly in India, with key hubs in Bengaluru, Mumbai, and the Delhi NCR region, accounting for the majority of investments. However, Chiratae has extended its reach internationally, backing companies in the United States, Singapore, Poland, and other locations to support global scalability. In terms of investment diversity, the firm allocates primarily to early-stage opportunities, including seed and Series A/B rounds, while also pursuing follow-on investments to nurture portfolio growth.
As of November 2025, Chiratae's portfolio has achieved significant milestones, including eight unicorns, 56 exits, and five initial public offerings (IPOs), including the recent Lenskart IPO, demonstrating strong value creation for limited partners through approximately $900 million in capital returns over the past 13 years. These outcomes underscore the firm's impact in fostering high-growth enterprises, with sector distributions spanning mobility, SaaS, edtech, and beyond.

Sector-wise distribution

Chiratae Ventures' portfolio demonstrates a diversified approach across key technology-driven sectors, with investments spanning consumer-facing innovations, enterprise solutions, and emerging technologies. As of the latest available data from their official portfolio listing, the firm has backed approximately 92 active companies, categorized primarily into ConsumerTech, SaaS/B2B, HealthTech, FinTech, and DeepTech/Other. This distribution reflects a balanced allocation, with ConsumerTech comprising approximately 27% of the portfolio (25 companies), including e-commerce and fitness platforms such as Lenskart and Cult.fit, as well as wellness apps like HealthifyMe.
SaaS and B2B sectors account for about 29% (27 companies), focusing on enterprise tools like agritech cloud solutions (Cropin) and data integration platforms (Hevo Data). HealthTech represents roughly 12% (11 companies), emphasizing wellness and diagnostics with examples including SigTuple for AI-enabled pathology. FinTech holds around 10% (9 companies), targeting payments and lending through ventures like Fibe (personal loans) and Vayana (supply chain finance). The remaining 21% falls under DeepTech and other categories (20 companies), encompassing AI, IoT, and climatetech such as Uniphore (conversational AI) and Minus Zero (autonomous driving).

Notable investments and exits

Unicorn investments

Chiratae Ventures has backed eight companies that have achieved unicorn status, valued at $1 billion or more, highlighting its success in identifying high-growth potential in India's startup ecosystem. These investments span sectors such as e-commerce, healthtech, fintech, and AI, with early-stage entries enabling significant value creation. The firm's strategy of providing not only capital but also strategic guidance has been instrumental in scaling these ventures to unicorn milestones.
One of the earliest successes is Lenskart, an eyewear e-commerce platform founded in 2009. Chiratae Ventures, then operating as IDG Ventures India, made a $4 million seed investment in 2011, marking one of its inaugural bets on digital consumer brands. This early support helped Lenskart disrupt the traditional eyewear market through affordable online sales and virtual try-on technology. By 2025, Lenskart reached a valuation exceeding $7.7 billion following its IPO, underscoring the long-term impact of Chiratae's seed-stage involvement.
In healthtech, Cult.fit (formerly Cure.fit), launched in 2016, received early funding from Chiratae Ventures in its Series A and subsequent rounds, including a $120 million Series C in 2018 where the firm participated. The investment fueled expansion of its fitness centers, nutrition services, and app-based wellness offerings. Cult.fit attained unicorn status in 2021 with a valuation over $1.5 billion, driven by the growing demand for integrated health solutions in urban India.
Chiratae also invested in FirstCry, a baby products e-commerce platform founded in 2010, starting with a $14 million Series B round in 2012 led by the firm. This funding supported inventory expansion and omnichannel growth, positioning FirstCry as a leader in parenting essentials. The company achieved unicorn status in 2016 and has since scaled to a multibillion-dollar valuation.
In beauty and personal care, MyGlamm (part of Good Glamm Group) benefited from Chiratae's investment around 2020, following the acquisition of content platform POPxo, where the firm was an existing backer. This move integrated influencer-driven content with D2C beauty products, propelling MyGlamm to unicorn status in 2021 at a $1.2 billion valuation.
PolicyBazaar, an online insurance aggregator founded in 2008, saw early investment from Chiratae (as IDG Ventures) in its formative rounds, including participation in a $77 million Series E in 2017. The firm's support aided platform enhancements and partnerships with insurers, leading to unicorn status in 2018 with a valuation surpassing $1 billion.
Flipkart, India's pioneering e-commerce giant founded in 2007, received funding from Chiratae in 2012 as part of its growth rounds, building on earlier IDG involvement. This investment helped solidify Flipkart's dominance in online retail before its acquisition by Walmart. Flipkart achieved unicorn status in 2012 and evolved into a decacorn.
In AI, Uniphore, a conversational AI platform established in 2008, has been backed by Chiratae since its early days, with continued participation in rounds like a $140 million Series D in 2021. The investment supported global expansion and product innovation in enterprise automation, culminating in unicorn status in 2021 at over $1 billion valuation.
GlobalBees, a D2C brand aggregator launched in 2021, secured participation from Chiratae in its $150 million Series A round that same year. The funding enabled acquisitions and scaling of direct-to-consumer brands, achieving unicorn status shortly after at a $1.1 billion valuation.
These eight unicorns exemplify Chiratae Ventures' early entry strategy, often at seed or Series A stages, which has been key to capturing substantial equity in market leaders. Collectively, they contribute significantly to the firm's portfolio performance, representing a major portion of its value through outsized returns and ongoing growth.

IPOs and acquisitions

Chiratae Ventures has facilitated five notable initial public offerings (IPOs) from its portfolio companies, marking significant exit milestones for the firm. The first was Newgen Software Technologies in 2018, which raised approximately 425 crore (about $67 million) at a price band of 240-245 per share, providing Chiratae with an early liquidity event in the enterprise software sector. This was followed by PolicyBazaar (now PB Fintech) in 2021, which debuted on the BSE and NSE with a valuation exceeding $6 billion, raising over 5,700 crore through a fresh issue and offer for sale, underscoring Chiratae's early bet in fintech insurance aggregation. Yatra Online achieved its Indian IPO in 2023, raising 775 crore at 142 per share, where Chiratae fully exited its stake via the offer for sale component. FirstCry listed in 2024, raising funds in a highly anticipated consumer tech debut that highlighted Chiratae's long-term holding in parenting and baby products. Most recently, Lenskart went public on November 10, 2025, with an $828 million IPO targeting an $8 billion valuation, though shares listed at a discount before recovering slightly above the 402 issue price, reflecting market volatility in eyewear retail.
In addition to IPOs, Chiratae has realized numerous acquisitions, contributing to a total of 34 reported buyouts from its portfolio as of late 2025. A landmark example is the 2014 partial exit from Myntra, acquired by Flipkart for $330 million, which allowed Chiratae to roll over its investment and later benefit from Walmart's 2018 acquisition of Flipkart. Other key acquisitions include GoMechanic, bought by Spinny in November 2025 following operational challenges, and NestAway, acquired by Aurum PropTech in 2023 for an undisclosed amount in the proptech space. Chiratae has also pursued secondary sales, such as a $70 million stake disposal in 2024 to Madison India Capital involving companies like Lenskart, Bizongo, and Rentomojo, accelerating liquidity without full company sales.
Overall, these events form part of Chiratae's 56 total exits across its funds, demonstrating a balanced approach to liquidity through both public listings and strategic buyouts. The firm has achieved 3x or higher multiples on its early funds (I-III), with cumulative returns to limited partners reaching approximately $900 million over the past 13 years, though specific internal rates of return remain undisclosed. Curefoods, another portfolio company, received IPO approval in October 2025, positioning it as a potential sixth public exit in the near term.

Recent developments

Fund V activities

Chiratae Ventures achieved the second close of its Fund V in September 2025, raising $150 million, with a target of $350 million by the first quarter of 2026. The fund's limited partners include Indian family offices, domestic institutions, international investors, and repeat backers from prior funds.
In Q2 and Q3 of 2025, Fund V supported four new investments, primarily at the seed and Series A stages, targeting sectors such as AI and climatetech. Notable deals include seed funding for Pepsales, an AI-powered platform for B2B sales demonstrations, and investments in proptech startup HouseEazy, as well as early commitments to wealthtech firm Mili and retail innovator ZILO. These investments align with the fund's emphasis on early-stage opportunities in high-growth areas like AI infrastructure, deeptech applications, and climate solutions.
The fund maintains a strategic focus on ESG-aligned startups in climatetech and AI to support sustainable innovation and market-relevant technologies. This deployment reflects Chiratae's approach to backing ventures that address environmental challenges while leveraging artificial intelligence for scalable impact.

International expansion

Chiratae Ventures has intensified its international outreach, particularly in Southeast Asia (SEA) and the United States, through strategic advisory appointments and targeted investments in global startups. In 2023, the firm established regional advisory boards to bolster its presence in Asia and SEA, appointing Ken Shibusawa, founder and CEO of Shibusawa and Company, to the Asia Advisory Board to enhance cross-border opportunities in Japan and broader Asia. Similarly, Puneet Pushkarna, former managing director at Vertex Ventures Southeast Asia and Partners, joined the SEA Advisory Board to strengthen ties in the region and support portfolio expansion. These moves reflect Chiratae's efforts to leverage local expertise for scouting and scaling international deals without establishing physical offices abroad.
Key investments underscore this global focus, including early backing of US-based Uniphore, a conversational AI leader founded in 2008 and headquartered in Palo Alto, California, which has driven Chiratae's exposure to American deep tech markets. Other notable commitments include Pixis, a San Jose-based AI marketing platform that raised $85 million in 2023 with Chiratae's participation, and Aether Biomedical, a Polish bionics firm in Poznan that received seed funding in 2020 and a follow-on Series A in 2023. Hevo Data, an Indian data pipeline company with significant global operations, also received investments to fuel its international growth, exemplified by its $30 million Series B in 2021. These deals highlight Chiratae's strategy of supporting tech innovators beyond India, contributing to a portfolio where international revenue reached $700 million out of a total $3 billion by early 2024.
As of 2025, Chiratae continues to pursue global deep tech opportunities, with Fund Vclosed at a second tranche of $150 million in Septemberallocating resources to AI, deep tech, and health tech startups that align with international scalability, building on prior funds' cross-border momentum. This includes participation in the India Deep Tech Alliance, which secured additional $850 million in commitments to foster global innovation pipelines.
