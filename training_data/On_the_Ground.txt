 "On the Ground" is the debut solo single by South Korean singer and songwriter Ros, a member of the global K-pop girl group Blackpink, released on March 12, 2021, through YG Entertainment and Interscope Records. The English-language track serves as the lead single from her eponymous debut single album -R-, a two-song release that also features the ballad "Gone." Featuring a blend of pop, rock, and EDM elements, the song highlights Ros's emotive vocals and guitar skills, with its music video depicting her in various surreal, dreamlike scenarios symbolizing personal introspection.
Lyrically, "On the Ground" explores themes of self-discovery, the pressures of fame, and the realization that true fulfillment lies within oneself rather than external achievements, drawing from Ros's own experiences in the music industry. Ros co-wrote the song alongside American songwriters Amy Allen, Jon Bellion, Jorgen Odegard, and Ral Cubina, as well as longtime Blackpink collaborator Teddy (Park Hyun-seung); it was produced by Bellion, Odegard, Teddy, Ojivolta, and 24. The track's production incorporates soaring choruses, electric guitar riffs, and a driving beat, creating an anthemic sound that contrasts its introspective message.
Commercially, "On the Ground" marked a major milestone for Ros, debuting at number one on both the Billboard Global 200 and Billboard Global Excl. U.S. charts, making her the first artist to simultaneously top both upon their inception. It also peaked at number 70 on the Billboard Hot 100, achieving the highest chart position for a Korean female soloist at the time, and reached number 43 on the UK Official Singles Chart. The song's music video amassed over 100 million views on YouTube within months of release, underscoring Ros's transition from group member to solo artist and her growing international influence.

Background

Development and recording

The song "On the Ground" originated from songwriting sessions in late 2020, where Ros collaborated with American songwriters Amy Allen, Jon Bellion, Jorgen Odegard, and Raul Cubina, alongside YG Entertainment's executive producer Teddy Park.
Ros took a hands-on role in crafting the lyrics, which draw directly from her decade-long experiences in the music industry since joining YG Entertainment at age 16, reflecting on the highs of fame and the realization that genuine satisfaction lies in everyday realities rather than lofty ambitions.
She described the process as intensely personal yet challenging, initially exploring conventional themes like love and heartbreak before pivoting to a narrative of self-discovery and purpose, shaped by her reflections during Blackpink's global rise.
Recording occurred primarily in Los Angeles, with producers Jon Bellion, the duo Ojivolta (Raul Cubina and Jorgen Odegard), 24, and Teddy Park overseeing the instrumentation to fuse electropop and pop rock elements into an anthemic sound.

Announcement and release

On February 10, 2021, YG Entertainment confirmed that Ros, a member of BLACKPINK, would make her solo debut in March, marking the first such project from the group.
The official announcement of the project details came on March 4, 2021, when Ros shared teasers on social media, including images of her in a studio, revealing the single album titled -R- and the lead single "On the Ground", scheduled for release on March 12, 2021. In her accompanying post, Ros expressed gratitude to her fans, the BLINKs, for their support leading up to the launch.
Pre-orders for -R- opened on March 4, 2021, generating significant buzz among fans. YG Entertainment followed with a series of promotional teasers, including music video snippets released on March 7, 8, 9, and 10, 2021, to heighten excitement for the debut.
"On the Ground" was released on March 12, 2021, as the lead single from -R- through YG Entertainment and Interscope Records. The track and album were made available worldwide via digital download and streaming platforms, alongside a physical CD single edition released in South Korea in four versions (R, O, S, and E). Promotional efforts included pre-save campaigns on major streaming services to encourage early engagement from listeners.

Composition and lyrics

Musical elements

"On the Ground" is classified as an electropop track with pop rock influences, incorporating synth-pop elements and an EDM-inspired beat that gives it a dynamic, contemporary edge. The song follows a verse-chorus form typical of pop structures, with a runtime of 2:48, opening with tense, understated guitar riffs in the verses that gradually build anticipation before exploding into a fuller drop during the chorus.
The instrumentation centers on a simple acoustic guitar foundation blended with electronic synths and a pulsating bass line, creating a layered sound that balances organic and synthetic textures for emotional resonance. Ros's vocal delivery stands out with raw, emotive phrasing throughout, featuring powerful belting in the bridge to heighten intensity and layered harmonies in the choruses that enhance the track's depth and anthemic quality.

Themes and interpretation

The song "On the Ground" centers on the core theme of questioning personal fulfillment amid the trappings of fame and success, encapsulated in the chorus line "Everything I need is on the ground." Ros has described the track as depicting "a girl who works hard to reach the top, only to realize once she's there that what she really needed was on the ground," highlighting a realization that true contentment lies in simplicity and self-awareness rather than external achievements. This narrative reflects a broader introspection on the sacrifices made for stardom, where the pursuit of heights leads to a poignant rediscovery of one's roots and inner resources.
Autobiographical elements infuse the lyrics with Ros's own journey from a trainee in South Korea to a global star with Blackpink, emphasizing grounding in reality over superficial success. Lines such as "My life's been magic, seems fantastic / I used to have a hole in the wall with a mattress" evoke her early days leaving Australia at age 16 to train under grueling conditions at YG Entertainment, underscoring the contrast between her humble beginnings and the glamour of fame. Through this, the song serves as a meditation on personal growth, where Ros confronts the emotional toll of ambition and reaffirms the value of family, music, and authenticity as anchors amid industry pressures.
Poetic devices enhance the song's emotional depth, with metaphors of flying versus staying grounded symbolizing the tension between soaring ambition and the safety of reality. Imagery of "falling from the sky" represents vulnerability and the risks of unchecked ascent, while the repeated chorus"Everything I need is on the ground / Everything I need is on the ground"employs anaphora for emphatic reinforcement, driving home the theme of self-reliance and presence. These elements create a layered reflection on resilience, as Ros notes her desire to "show my real self" through the track.
Interpretations position "On the Ground" as a self-empowerment anthem that contrasts relentless ambition with the peace of contentment, encouraging listeners to prioritize inner fulfillment over societal validation. Critics have praised its candid exploration of fame's illusions, viewing it as Ros's assertion of emotional maturity and a call to embrace vulnerability as strength. The song's introspective tone resonates as a reminder that genuine happiness often resides in the everyday, aligning with Ros's broader artistic evolution toward authenticity.

Critical reception

Reviews from critics

Upon its release in March 2021, "On the Ground" garnered generally positive reviews from critics, who frequently highlighted Ros's vocal prowess and the track's introspective emotional core as standout elements in her solo debut.
Rolling Stone lauded the song as a "sparkling solo debut," emphasizing Ros's impressive vocal range and emotional delivery, praising her "aching vulnerability" and "raw, emotional weight" that resonate deeply, while conveying a sense of individuality separate from Blackpinks group dynamic. The publication appreciated how the track provided fans with deeper insight into Ros's personal reflections on fame and fulfillment.
NME offered a more mixed assessment, praising Ros's emotional power and the song's breezy, EDM-infused pop structure as a "captivating" introduction to her solo work, but noting the album's overall "back to basics" approach that shifts from Blackpink's bolder group sound to effectively showcase Ros's softer, more vulnerable side. The review, which rated the accompanying single album -R- 4 out of 5 stars, highlighted her growth as an artist.

Year-end and retrospective lists

In 2021, "On the Ground" earned placements on several year-end and mid-year lists from music publications, highlighting its impact as Ros's solo debut. It ranked No. 39 on Billboard's list of the 50 Best Songs of 2021 So Far, marking it as the only track by a K-pop female solo artist on that mid-year roundup. The song was also featured on Billboard's 25 Best K-Pop Songs of 2021, praised for fulfilling expectations as a long-awaited solo release from the BLACKPINK member. Additionally, NME placed it at No. 23 on their 25 best K-pop songs of 2021, noting Ros's growth as an artist over the prior five years within the genre.
Fan-voted polls further underscored the song's popularity that year. It ranked No. 16 on the list of the 20 most liked tracks of 2021 on Melon, reflecting strong listener engagement in South Korea.
Retrospective analyses have continued to recognize "On the Ground" for its role in pioneering solo K-pop success. By 2024, Billboard reflected on the track as a record-breaking debut that topped the Global 200 and set benchmarks for Korean solo artists internationally. In 2025, Ros was awarded Guinness World Records for "On the Ground," including the most viewed YouTube music video in 24 hours by a K-pop soloist with 41.6 million views, affirming its pioneering status. No major new year-end lists emerged specifically for 2025, though the song has been noted in anniversary retrospectives marking its enduring legacy as a landmark in K-pop solo releases.

Commercial performance

Chart positions

"On the Ground" achieved significant international chart success upon its release, debuting at number one on the Billboard Global 200 chart dated March 27, 2021, marking the first time a K-pop solo artist had topped that ranking. It also debuted at number one on the Billboard Global Excl. U.S. chart in the same week. In the United States, the track peaked at number 70 on the Billboard Hot 100, becoming Ros's highest-charting solo entry on that chart at the time.
The song performed strongly across various national charts. It reached number 43 on the UK Official Singles Chart, marking the highest peak for a Korean female soloist on that ranking until Ros's later releases. In Australia, it debuted and peaked at number 31 on the ARIA Singles Chart. Regionally, "On the Ground" peaked at number 4 on South Korea's Gaon Digital Chart and number 50 on Japan's Billboard Japan Hot 100.

It also became Spotify's most-streamed K-pop solo debut track.

Sales and certifications

The single received several certifications reflecting its commercial success. In the United States, the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA) certified it Platinum for 1 million units sold.
On streaming platforms, the official music video surpassed 500 million views on YouTube as of November 2025.

Music video

Production

The music video for "On the Ground" was directed by Han Sa-min and released simultaneously with the single on March 12, 2021. Production highlighted practical effects and elaborate set builds to realize the video's surreal, dreamlike environments, eschewing green screens and digital CGI for authentic, tangible visuals that grounded the fantastical elements in physical reality.
Filming took place in early 2021 amid cold weather conditions, as documented in the official behind-the-scenes making film, where the crew provided heaters and hot packs to support Ros and the team during outdoor and set shoots. The cinematography, led by a team under YG Entertainment's production oversight, employed dynamic camera work to alternate between close-up performance sequences and wide narrative shots, capturing the song's introspective mood through natural lighting and fluid movements.
Editing incorporated rapid cuts to interweave Ros's live performances with symbolic storytelling, fostering a rhythmic pace that mirrors the track's building intensity and thematic exploration of personal fulfillment. Post-production enhanced the footage with subtle color grading to amplify the video's ethereal palette, blending cool tones for reflective moments with warmer hues in scenes evoking liberation. Ros contributed creatively during filming, collaborating on choreography refinements and articulating the emotional nuances of scenes to align visuals with the lyrics' focus on self-discovery.

Content and analysis

The music video for "On the Ground," directed by Han Sa-min, presents a surreal, introspective narrative that visually echoes the song's themes of ambition, self-realization, and finding peace in the ordinary. It begins in a opulent room where a chandelier dramatically crashes to the floor, yet Ros remains poised in a vibrant magenta gown, symbolizing resilience amid chaos. She then levitates through cloudy skies and vast landscapes, representing the exhilarating but disorienting ascent to fame, before descending into bustling urban streets filled with neon lights and crowds. Throughout, multiple iterations of Ros appeardancing solo or in harmonyhighlighting internal conflict and versions of the self, culminating in her reclining serenely amid a sea of wildflowers in an open field, evoking a return to grounded simplicity and acceptance.
Visually, the video employs striking motifs to underscore the lyrics' dichotomy between lofty pursuits and earthly fulfillment. Aerial cinematography captures Ros soaring weightlessly, mirroring lines like "My life's been magic, seems fantastic," while the shift to terrestrial scenessuch as street performances and the final floral meadowcontrasts this elevation with humility, aligning with the chorus's revelation that "everything I need is on the ground." The opening meteor streak alludes to the Icarus myth, cautioning against hubris in chasing unreachable heights, a symbolism reinforced by Ros's encounters with her fragmented selves, which fans and analysts interpret as a metaphor for reconciling past innocence with present stardom. The choreography, featuring fluid, emotive solo breaks amid group formations, amplifies this personal voyage, blending contemporary dance with raw emotional delivery to emphasize introspection over spectacle.
The video has achieved significant popularity, amassing over 386 million views on YouTube by November 2025, underscoring its enduring appeal as Ros's solo debut visual. Critics lauded its artistic direction and stylistic flair, with Vogue highlighting the seamless integration of high-fashion elementslike custom gowns from Alex Perry and Saint Laurentwith a cohesive storyline that elevates Ros's narrative presence beyond typical K-pop aesthetics. Some reviewers, however, pointed to occasional reliance on familiar dream-sequence tropes, such as floating figures and multiplied personas, as somewhat conventional within the genre. Among fans, interpretations often tie the grounding imagery to Ros's personal background, viewing the field finale as a nod to her Australian upbringing and the comforts of home amid her global rise.

Promotion

Live performances

During Blackpink's Born Pink World Tour from October 2022 to September 2023, "On the Ground" featured as a staple in Ros's solo segment, often performed in a shortened version to highlight its soaring chorus and introspective lyrics. In the tour's Seoul finale shows at Gocheok Sky Dome, Ros incorporated dynamic lighting and backing dancers to amplify the song's themes of self-reflection, earning enthusiastic crowd responses during the encore segments. Similarly, at the Los Angeles stop at Dodger Stadium in August 2023, her performance blended the track with high-energy choreography, adapting it to the stadium's scale while preserving its personal narrative.
In 2023, during BLACKPINK's historic headline set at Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival on April 15, Ros took the spotlight for a live rendition of her solo track, integrating it seamlessly into the group's high-production set with amplified band elements and festival-scale visuals.

Television appearances and events

Ros made her debut television performance of "On the Ground" on The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon on March 16, 2021, marking her first solo appearance on U.S. television with a high-energy choreographed dance version featuring backup dancers, filmed in black and white. She continued promoting the single on Show! Music Core on March 20, 2021, followed by her debut stage on M Countdown on March 18, 2021, where she presented a choreographed dance version featuring intricate formations and high-energy movements alongside backup dancers.
She continued promoting the single on Inkigayo on March 21, 2021, delivering another polished stage performance that contributed to the song's growing visibility in the K-pop scene. On March 23, 2021, Ros released a full choreography dance practice video for "On the Ground," further engaging fans with its intricate moves. Later that year, Ros earned the Best Dance Performance Solo award for "On the Ground" at the 2021 Mnet Asian Music Awards (MAMA), where she performed a winner's stage blending elements of the original choreography with celebratory flair.

Legacy

Accolades

"On the Ground" received recognition following its release, earning awards and nominations that highlighted its impact as Ros's solo debut. At the 2021 Mnet Asian Music Awards (MAMA), Ros won Best Dance Performance Solo for the song. The track also secured wins at the 2021 Asian Pop Music Awards for Best Music Video (Overseas) and Top 20 Songs of the Year (Overseas). In South Korea, it achieved six music show wins on programs including Show Champion, M Countdown, and Inkigayo.
The song was nominated for Best K-Pop at the 2021 MTV Europe Music Awards. It received additional international nods, such as Top Ten International Gold Songs at the 2022 RTHK International Pop Poll Awards (won).

Cultural impact and influence

"On the Ground" contributed to the advancement of solo careers within Blackpink, following Jennie's 2018 solo debut "Solo" and coinciding with Lisa's "Lalisa" in the same year. Released as the lead single from Ros's debut single album -R-, it demonstrated the potential for K-pop idols to achieve global chart success independently, with its number-one debut on the Billboard Global 200 marking a milestone for female soloists. This paved the way for further solo projects by group members, including Jisoo's 2023 debut, by emphasizing personal narratives and international markets.
The song's virality extended to social media platforms, particularly TikTok, where user-generated dance challenges and lip-sync videos proliferated in 2021, amplifying its reach among younger audiences and contributing to its organic spread beyond traditional music consumption. This digital engagement helped propel "On the Ground" to 92.1 million global streams in its debut week.
As of 2025, "On the Ground" is recognized in analyses of K-pop's evolution as a breakthrough for female soloists, being the first by a Korean artist to top the Billboard Global 200 and highlighting opportunities for introspective, English-language pop. Its themes of self-reflection have influenced covers and adaptations, with the track surpassing 559 million Spotify streams.

Covers and remixes

Notable covers

South Korean rock band KAVE and vocalist Gaho delivered an acoustic cover of "On the Ground" in March 2021, rearranging the track with layered harmonies, guitar instrumentation, and a stripped-down emotional delivery during a live session that highlighted the song's introspective lyrics.
Singer-songwriter AleXa released a cover version in 2021, infusing the original pop ballad with her signature vocal style and subtle electronic elements, which gained attention within K-pop circles for its faithful yet personalized interpretation.
In 2023, Kyujin of the girl group NMIXX performed a cover as part of a mixtape project, adopting a youthful, dynamic vocal approach that emphasized the song's soaring chorus while incorporating light R&B influences.
Filipino vocalist Zephanie Dimaranan offered a soulful rendition in 2021, focusing on powerful belting and piano accompaniment to capture the track's themes of self-reflection, earning praise for its vocal range and emotional depth.

Remixes and samples

An official rearranged version of "On the Ground" was released on March 13, 2021.

Production credits

Songwriting and composition

"On the Ground" was co-written by Ros (credited as Park Chaeyoung), Amy Allen, Jon Bellion, Jorgen Odegard, Ral Cubina, and Teddy Park, with Ros contributing to the lyrics and melody as a key collaborator in the song's creation. Amy Allen played a significant role in developing the lyrical structure, drawing from her expertise in crafting introspective pop narratives. The composition credits include production and arrangement by Jon Bellion, Jorgen Odegard (also known as Odegard), Ojivolta, Teddy Park, and 24, who handled key elements of the track's rock-influenced sound and dynamic build.
Publishing rights for "On the Ground" are primarily held by YG Entertainment, with Interscope Records managing international distribution and associated rights through their partnership. This arrangement reflects the collaborative framework between YG's in-house production team and global partners, ensuring broad accessibility for the single's release.

Recording and personnel

The recording of "On the Ground" took place primarily at The Black Label Studio in Seoul, South Korea. Ros provided lead and background vocals, with production handled by 24 and Ojivolta, alongside co-production from Jon Bellion, Jorgen Odegard, and Teddy Park. Bellion also served as vocal producer. Amy Allen provided background vocals.
Recording engineers included Yongin Choi, who captured the sessions. The track was mixed by Josh Gudwin at Henson Recording Studios in Los Angeles, California. Mastering was completed by Randy Merrill at Sterling Sound in New York City.
These credits, drawn from the liner notes of Ros's debut single album R, have remained unchanged as of 2025, with no re-recording or alterations reported.

Release details

Formats and editions

The single album -R-, featuring "On the Ground" as the lead track, was made available in multiple digital and physical formats upon its release through YG Entertainment and Interscope Records. Digital editions encompass the standard audio version of the song, streamed on platforms like Spotify and Apple Music, alongside instrumental variants for enhanced listening and production use.
Physical releases include a jewel case CD edition bundled with a photobook containing exclusive imagery and liner notes. A Kit version was also available, including additional merchandise such as stickers and photocards. A limited cassette edition was produced alongside the standard CD. A vinyl edition of -R- was offered as a limited pressing on April 19, 2021, featuring the full tracklist including instrumentals on side B.
The album -R- itself serves as a bundle, pairing "On the Ground" with the B-side "Gone" across all formats for a cohesive solo debut experience. Streaming exclusives feature visualizer versions of "On the Ground" on YouTube, providing animated lyric videos optimized for mobile and desktop playback.

Regional release dates

The single "On the Ground," from Ros's debut single album -R-, was released globally as a digital download on March 12, 2021, at midnight ET, marking a simultaneous worldwide launch across major platforms including iTunes in over 50 countries. This digital rollout allowed immediate accessibility for international audiences, with the track topping iTunes charts in regions such as the United States, Canada, Brazil, and Spain shortly after availability.
Physical CD editions were first distributed in South Korea on March 16, 2021, through YG Entertainment, featuring multiple versions including standard CD, Kit, and limited vinyl formats. In contrast, physical releases in other markets experienced delays due to international shipping and distribution logistics; for instance, shipments to Japan and the United States became available in late March 2021, while European retailers reported arrivals in early April 2021. These staggered physical launches ensured broader global availability but varied by region based on local partnerships with labels like Interscope Records.
