 InterDigital, Inc. is a multinational research and development company specializing in wireless communications, video coding, artificial intelligence (AI), and related technologies. Founded in 1972 as International Mobile Machines Corporation by inventor Sherwin Seligsohn, the company has pioneered key advancements in digital cellular technology, including early contributions to 3G standards and the development of compact chipsets like the SlimChip for high-speed packet access (HSPA). Headquartered in Wilmington, Delaware, InterDigital operates research labs across the United States, France, Canada, and the United Kingdom, employing a global team dedicated to innovation.
The company's core mission is to invent technologies that connect generations of cellular networks and enable devices shaping the future, emphasizing sustainability, low latency, and energy efficiency in a wireless world. InterDigital's Wireless Lab drives evolution in radio access, core networks, and service enablers, contributing essential patents to global standards bodies such as 3GPP and ITU. Its Video Lab advances media compression and distribution to enhance multimedia experiences while minimizing bandwidth and power consumption, supporting standards like MPEG. Meanwhile, the AI Lab incubates next-generation applications, including immersive technologies and quantum computing explorations; in October 2025, InterDigital acquired AI startup Deep Render to advance video compression using AI.
As a global pure-play R&D and licensing firm, InterDigital generates revenue primarily through licensing its extensive patent portfolio to mobile device manufacturers, network operators, and other industry partners, fostering collaborations with startups, governments, and academia. Notable achievements include supporting Nokia's early technology developments and establishing secure mobile communications systems in the 1980s. Listed on Nasdaq under the ticker IDCC, the current stock price of InterDigital (NASDAQ: IDCC) is $363.74 USD as of the market close on March 5, 2026 (down $9.57 or -2.56% from the previous close of $373.31). No pre-market changes were reported as of early March 6, 2026, with the market closed. InterDigital continues to lead in emerging fields, powering extraordinary experiences in an increasingly connected ecosystem.

Corporate History

Founding and Early Development

InterDigital was founded in 1972 as International Mobile Machines Corporation (IMM) by inventor Sherwin Seligsohn in King of Prussia, Pennsylvania. The company initially concentrated on pioneering portable wireless communication devices, developing the world's first wireless handheld analog telephone prototype in the 450 MHz band, which represented a breakthrough in pocket-sized cordless phone technology. This innovation laid the groundwork for IMM's early focus on mobile telephony, with demonstrations of the device occurring as early as 1976 during Philadelphia's U.S. bicentennial celebrations in Fairmount Park.
In the mid-1970s, IMM shifted toward digital technologies, engineering a Time Division Multiple Access (TDMA) wireless cell phone system that contributed significantly to the establishment of the IS-54 TDMA standard for digital cellular communications in the United States during the 1980s. The company's patented TDMA inventions formed fundamental elements of second-generation (2G) wireless networks, enabling more efficient spectrum use and supporting the transition from analog to digital mobile services. Additionally, in the late 1980s, IMM advanced secure mobile communications.
By the early 1990s, IMM had evolved through strategic mergers, acquiring SCS Mobilecom and SCS Telecom Inc. in 1992 to form InterDigital Communications Corporation under CEO William J. Erdman, reflecting a broader emphasis on integrated digital wireless solutions. The company went public in 1981 on the American Stock Exchange. It began trading on the NASDAQ stock exchange under the ticker symbol IDCC on April 26, 2000. A key technological milestone came in 2007 with the launch of the SlimChip, InterDigital's first High-Speed Packet Access (HSPA) chipset family, which delivered high-performance baseband processing and broadband modem capabilities for 3G mobile devices, advancing the company's role in next-generation wireless innovation.

Key Milestones and Expansion

In the early 2000s, InterDigital transitioned from manufacturing chips and hardware to a pure research and development (R&D) and licensing model, beginning with the commercialization of its 3G technologies through patent licensing agreements. This strategic shift culminated in 2007 when the company fully exited hardware production, focusing instead on IP monetization to leverage its innovations in wireless communications. Building on its foundational contributions to digital wireless technologies in prior decades, this pivot enabled sustained growth by emphasizing R&D investments over product fabrication.
A pivotal moment came in 2014 with the resolution of ongoing patent disputes through a multi-year, royalty-bearing license agreement with Samsung Electronics, covering 3G, 4G, and future-generation wireless products. This deal not only settled all litigation between the parties but also validated InterDigital's licensing approach, providing financial stability and affirming the value of its patent portfolio in mobile ecosystems.
During the 2010s, InterDigital expanded beyond core wireless into video and Internet of Things (IoT) domains, actively participating in standards development for High Efficiency Video Coding (HEVC) and joining key alliances such as the Wi-Fi Alliance in 2008 and the Wireless Broadband Alliance in 2015. These efforts diversified its technology scope, integrating video compression and connected device innovations into its licensing strategy while contributing to broader industry interoperability.
Leadership transitioned in 2021, with William J. Merritt, who had served as President and CEO since 2005, retiring after guiding the company through its R&D-focused evolution; he was succeeded by Liren Chen on April 5, 2021, bringing extensive experience from Qualcomm in wireless standards and licensing. Under Chen's tenure, InterDigital continued strategic growth.
In 2024, the company achieved record revenue of $869 million, a 58% increase year-over-year, propelled by 14 new licensing agreements with major players including OPPO, Google, ZTE, Samsung, and TPV. Early 2025 saw further expansion with the acquisition of over 300 U.S. patents related to content delivery network (CDN) technologies from the bankrupt provider Edgio, enhancing its portfolio in media delivery. Concurrently, InterDigital has deepened involvement in 5G-Advanced and 6G standardization through leadership roles in 3GPP, including contributions to Release 20, which bridges current 5G enhancements with future 6G specifications starting in 2025.

Business Operations

Research and Development Focus

InterDigital operates as a pure-play research and development company, focusing exclusively on innovation in wireless, video, and related technologies without manufacturing or selling products. This model allows the firm to channel substantial resources into foundational research, with annual R&D expenses exceeding $100 million; for instance, in 2024, these expenses reached $197 million. In the third quarter of 2025, R&D expenses were $53.7 million. The company's R&D efforts are structured around specialized labs, including the Wireless Lab for advancing radio access and core network technologies, the Video Lab for compression and delivery innovations, and the AI Lab for machine learning applications in communications. These labs are supported by the Wireless Standards Group, which coordinates contributions to global standardization processes.
A key aspect of InterDigital's strategy involves extensive collaborations with academic institutions to foster joint research projects and talent development. The company maintains partnerships with nearly 20 prominent universities across the United States, Canada, and Europe, including recent agreements with Concordia University on AI-enabled immersive media over 5G networks and Princeton University on AI in wireless through the NextG Corporate Program. These alliances enable the integration of cutting-edge academic insights with industry expertise, accelerating the translation of theoretical advancements into practical innovations.
InterDigital prioritizes forward-looking research that influences international standards bodies, such as the 3rd Generation Partnership Project (3GPP) and the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE), where it actively proposes and refines specifications for next-generation networks. The firm also engages in industry consortia like 5G Americas, co-authoring white papers on 3GPP technology trends and 5G-Advanced features to guide ecosystem evolution. This standards-oriented approach has defined InterDigital's innovation strategy for over 40 years, with contributions to cellular technologies from the 2G erawhere it pioneered TDMA and CDMA advancementsto current work on 6G through 3GPP Release 20 studies.
In line with its commitment to emerging areas, InterDigital opened a new office in Melville, New York, in 2025, specifically to advance AI and machine learning research in signal processing for wireless communications, including techniques like constellation learning to mitigate signal distortions. This facility enhances the company's capacity for interdisciplinary R&D and strengthens ties with nearby academic and industry partners. Such initiatives underscore InterDigital's focus on generating essential intellectual property through collaborative, standards-driven research.

Patent Portfolio and Licensing Model

InterDigital owns over 30,000 granted patents and pending applications worldwide, forming the core of its intellectual property assets. This extensive portfolio includes more than 13,000 patents and applications related to 5G multi-mode cellular devices, over 2,000 in the 802.11 Wi-Fi standard, approximately 2,200 in the HEVC video codec, and more than 500 patent families in AI technologies. Since 2012, third-party analyst studies evaluating patents likely essential to 3G, 4G, and 5G cellular wireless standards have consistently ranked InterDigital among the top three holders.
The company's business model revolves around licensing this portfolio, with revenue almost entirely derived from patent licenses rather than product sales. InterDigital commits to offering its standard-essential patents (SEPs) on fair, reasonable, and non-discriminatory (FRAND) terms, a policy it has upheld in negotiations across multiple standards bodies. Over two decades of mobile patent licensing, InterDigital has negotiated hundreds of agreements, enabling widespread adoption of its technologies in billions of devices.
To protect its IP, InterDigital has pursued enforcement through the U.S. International Trade Commission (ITC), federal courts, and international venues, securing favorable initial determinations, verdicts, and injunctionssuch as the 2024 UK Court of Appeal decision increasing royalties from Lenovo, a November 2024 UK High Court ruling declining an interim FRAND license declaration in the Lenovo dispute, preliminary injunctions against Disney in Brazil in September 2025, and a German injunction against Disney in November 2025. However, the company emphasizes negotiated settlements, as seen in resolutions with Huawei in 2014 and Samsung via arbitration in 2025, which resolved disputes without prolonged litigation.
In 2025, InterDigital expanded its video-related holdings by acquiring 327 U.S. patents from the bankrupt content delivery network provider Edgio for $4.25 million, enhancing its capabilities in video delivery and supporting ongoing enforcement against streaming services. This licensing-centric approach drove record 2024 revenue of $869 million and an adjusted EBITDA margin of 63%. In the third quarter of 2025, revenue reached $164.7 million.

Technology Areas

Wireless and IoT Innovations

InterDigital has played a pivotal role in advancing wireless communication standards since the early days of digital cellular technology. The company contributed foundational innovations to Time Division Multiple Access (TDMA) systems in the 1970s and 1980s, enabling the transition from analog to digital cellular networks for 2G deployments. Building on this, InterDigital developed key technologies for Code Division Multiple Access (CDMA), which became a cornerstone of 3G networks, improving spectral efficiency and supporting higher data rates for mobile broadband. For 4G Long-Term Evolution (LTE), InterDigital's contributions included enhancements to Orthogonal Frequency-Division Multiplexing (OFDM) and Multiple-Input Multiple-Output (MIMO) techniques, optimizing spectrum use and enabling widespread high-speed internet access on mobile devices. These efforts extended into 5G, where InterDigital contributed to developments in 3GPP Releases 15 through 18, focusing on ultra-reliable low-latency communications and massive machine-type communications to support diverse applications.
Looking ahead, InterDigital is actively shaping 6G through research on Integrated Sensing and Communication (ISAC), a paradigm that merges radar-like sensing with data transmission to enable environmental awareness in networks. ISAC enhances network efficiency by allowing base stations to detect and map surroundings, such as user positions or obstacles, without dedicated sensors, paving the way for applications in autonomous systems and smart environments. This work is highlighted in collaborative reports with ABI Research, emphasizing ISAC's role in 3GPP Release 20 as a bridge to 6G standards, alongside AI-native radio access networks.
In the Internet of Things (IoT) domain, InterDigital has advanced low-power wide-area networks (LPWAN) to connect battery-constrained devices over long distances, supporting standards like NB-IoT and LTE-M for efficient, scalable deployments in smart metering and asset tracking. The company has also pioneered edge computing integrations for IoT smart devices, processing data locally to reduce latency and bandwidth demands in 5G environments, as demonstrated in industrial applications requiring real-time analytics from sensors. Additionally, InterDigital holds patents essential to electric vehicle (EV) charging protocols, enabling secure and efficient communication between chargers, vehicles, and grids; this includes a 2025 licensing agreement with an EV charger manufacturer covering residential and commercial solutions for interoperability and power management.
Key projects underscore InterDigital's practical impact on wireless and IoT infrastructure. The 5G-Crosshaul (Xhaul) initiative, in which InterDigital participated as a lead contributor, developed an integrated backhaul and fronthaul transport network to flexibly interconnect 5G radio access and core elements using software-defined architectures, demonstrated over millimeter-wave systems for high-capacity, low-latency transport. In user localization for dense networks, InterDigital's research supports 5G positioning enhancements, leveraging ISAC for centimeter-level accuracy in crowded urban or indoor settings, vital for applications like augmented reality and public safety. For secure communications in industrial IoT, the company has showcased solutions in smart factory demonstrations, integrating 5G with edge processing to protect data flows in automation systems, ensuring resilience against cyber threats through standardized protocols.
InterDigital's innovations extend to Wi-Fi enhancements, with contributions to IEEE 802.11ax (Wi-Fi 6) and 802.11be (Wi-Fi 7) standards improving multi-user efficiency and spectral utilization in dense environments. The company holds over 2,000 patents related to these Wi-Fi generations, focusing on features like orthogonal frequency-division multiple access (OFDMA) and multi-link operations to boost throughput for IoT and consumer devices.
As of 2025, InterDigital anticipates significant growth in 5G enterprise use cases, particularly in vertical industries like manufacturing automation, where private 5G networks enable real-time robotics, predictive maintenance, and supply chain optimization, unlocking billions in economic value. These advancements are licensed broadly to device makers and network operators, supporting widespread adoption.

Video, AI, and Emerging Technologies

InterDigital holds a prominent position in video standards, with over 2,200 patents related to High Efficiency Video Coding (HEVC, also known as H.265) and more than 4,000 patents covering Versatile Video Coding (VVC, H.266), enabling efficient compression for high-resolution streaming and broadcasting. These patents stem from foundational contributions to international standards developed by bodies like the Joint Video Exploration Team (JVET), where InterDigital's research has advanced low-latency codecs essential for cloud gaming and multimedia distribution. Additionally, the company's innovations extend to 3D modeling and feature compression for immersive media, including leadership in point cloud compression standards such as the upcoming D-Mesh from MPEG and MPEG Immersive Video (MIV) Version 1, which support parallax-enabled viewing and context-aware mixed reality experiences optimized for 5G and 6G networks. Human motion reconstruction is addressed through AI-based video representation techniques in InterDigital's MetaVideo Standards efforts, facilitating dynamic 3D content generation for applications like virtual production.
The company's AI and Machine Learning (AI/ML) Lab emphasizes deep learning applications tailored to both video and wireless domains, holding over 500 AI-related patents. In wireless optimization, deep neural networks are employed for quasi-real-time inference to manage dynamic channel states, thereby reducing signal distortions and enhancing spectral efficiency in 5G and beyond systems. For video encoding, AI acceleration integrates with traditional methods via frameworks like CompressAI, enabling machine-to-machine compression that outperforms legacy standards in bitrate efficiency. The lab also applies natural language processing (NLP) techniques to analyze vast patent corpora, streamlining intellectual property management and innovation discovery.
InterDigital's exploration of emerging technologies includes quantum computing for secure communications in future wireless networks and blockchain protocols for IoT device authentication, aiming to address scalability and trust challenges in distributed systems. In immersive technologies, research supports augmented reality (AR) and virtual reality (VR) through AI-enhanced codecs that reduce bitrates while preserving quality for extended reality (XR) environments. These efforts build on the company's major patent holdings in video standards, further strengthened by the February 2025 acquisition of patents from a bankrupt content delivery network (CDN) provider, which bolsters capabilities in efficient media delivery over IP networks.
In 2025, InterDigital advanced AI-driven foundational research reshaping video and wireless frontiers, highlighted by the October acquisition of Deep Render, an AI startup specializing in neural video codecs that achieve up to 50% bitrate reductions beyond VVC for next-generation compression. This integrates with ongoing academic collaborations on AI-native codecs, including joint explorations with universities to develop hybrid traditional-AI solutions for immersive and high-fidelity video, as showcased at events like IBC 2025. Such projects underscore InterDigital's role in converging AI with video processing, briefly intersecting with wireless standards for end-to-end optimization in media delivery. In November 2025, InterDigital filed a lawsuit against Amazon alleging infringement of 10 video-compression patents, reinforcing its position in video standards enforcement.

Partnerships and Licensees

Major License Agreements

InterDigital has secured hundreds of patent license agreements with manufacturers of wireless devices, consumer electronics, and service providers, emphasizing standard-essential patents (SEPs) that enable compliance with global standards like 3G, 4G, and 5G. These deals form the core of its business model, generating royalties based on licensed product sales and often resolving litigation to ensure broad adoption of its technologies.
A landmark agreement came in 2014 with Samsung Electronics, resolving global patent litigation through a royalty-bearing license covering Samsung's sales of 3G, 4G, and certain future-generation wireless products. This multi-year deal, valued at around $500 million upfront, provided Samsung with worldwide rights and set a precedent for InterDigital's SEP licensing in mobile communications. The agreement was renewed in January 2023 through binding arbitration, with final termsincluding catch-up payments and a total value of approximately $1.05 billion over eight yearsdetermined in July 2025, contributing to a 34% year-over-year increase in InterDigital's Q2 2025 revenue to $300.6 million, driven by smartphone licensing.
Other pivotal wireless licensees from the 3G and 4G eras include Apple, Nokia, Huawei, and LG Electronics. InterDigital signed a multi-year, royalty-bearing license with Apple in 2016 for its cellular and wireless products, renewed in 2022 for seven years at approximately $938 million, yielding about $134 million annually. Nokia settled a long-standing dispute in 2006 with a $253 million payment, granting a paid-up license for 2G and 3G technologies and resolving related litigation. Huawei entered a 2016 multi-year deal for 3G and 4G terminal units with cash royalties, extended in 2020 to encompass 5G products and dismiss pending disputes. LG Electronics agreed to a 2017 multi-year license for 3G, 4G, and 5G terminal units, followed by a 2023 deal covering video codec patents for its TVs and PCs.
In video compression, InterDigital's major agreements focus on the HEVC standard, with key partners Panasonic and Sony. Panasonic signed licenses in 2023 for InterDigital's HEVC video and digital TV patents, renewed in 2024 under a joint licensing program with Sony that extends coverage to Panasonic's entertainment and communication products. These deals enable efficient video encoding in consumer devices and underscore InterDigital's role in multimedia standards.
For IoT and emerging areas, InterDigital has forged partnerships with automotive firms to license technologies for connected vehicles, primarily through the Avanci platform, which pools SEPs for cellular connectivity and has agreements with OEMs like Volvo Cars to support vehicle telematics and V2X communications. It also licenses to smart home device makers, facilitating IoT ecosystems with wireless and sensor innovations essential for interoperability. These agreements leverage InterDigital's patent portfolio to address growing demands in connected environments.

Recent Developments in Licensing

In 2024, InterDigital achieved a record annual revenue of $869 million, marking a 58% year-over-year increase, primarily driven by the completion of 14 major licensing deals. These agreements expanded coverage across wireless, video, and consumer electronics sectors, with notable renewals and new pacts including a multi-year license with Samsung Electronics for digital TVs and monitors, as well as deals with Google for Pixel smartphones and Fitbit wearables, and OPPO Group for mobile devices worldwide. The Samsung renewal, finalized through arbitration, significantly contributed to InterDigital's second-quarter 2025 revenue of $300.6 million, a 34% year-over-year rise, alongside net income of $180.6 million.
Building on this momentum, InterDigital secured several key agreements in 2025, focusing on wireless standards, IoT applications, and video technologies. In October 2025, the company renewed its multi-year license with Sharp Corporation, covering 4G, 5G, Wi-Fi, and HEVC video standards, while also signing a new IoT license for electric vehicle (EV) charging infrastructure. Additional pacts included a multi-year renewal with Seiko Solutions Inc. for cellular wireless technology in October 2025, an agreement with HP Inc. in April 2025 for Wi-Fi and video decoding in personal computers, and a multi-year deal with a major undisclosed Chinese smartphone vendor in March 2025, which prompted an upward revision to the first-quarter revenue outlook.
These licensing advancements propelled InterDigital's third-quarter 2025 results, with revenue reaching $164.7 milliona 28% year-over-year increaseand net income of $67.5 million, reflecting strong contributions from the new and renewed agreements. The quarter's performance also highlighted a record annualized recurring revenue of $588 million, up 49% from the prior year, underscoring the financial stability from these deals.
Amid these successes, InterDigital faced ongoing litigation in 2025 related to patent validity and licensing terms. In the UK, the Court of Appeal dismissed aspects of Tesla Inc.'s claim against InterDigital and the Avanci patent pool in March 2025, concerning FRAND rates for 5G standard-essential patents in vehicles, though the UK Supreme Court granted Tesla permission to appeal on select grounds in July 2025. Separately, in the US, the Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit issued an order in November 2025 in Microsoft Corporation v. InterDigital Patent Holdings, Inc., addressing challenges to patent enforceability in wireless technologies. In early November 2025, InterDigital initiated patent infringement litigation against Amazon in the United States, Germany, Brazil, and before the Unified Patent Court for video compression and HDR technologies used in Fire TV devices. Additionally, on November 3, 2025, the Munich Regional Court awarded InterDigital an injunction against Disney for infringing a patent related to video processing.
Strategically, InterDigital intensified its focus on AI and video licensing for enterprise applications in 2025, exemplified by the October acquisition of AI startup Deep Render to bolster video compression technologies and AI-native research capabilities. This move aligns with the company's goal of achieving $1 billion in annual recurring revenue by 2030, emphasizing high-impact licensing in emerging AI-driven sectors.

Leadership

Chief Executive Officer

Liren Chen has served as President and Chief Executive Officer of InterDigital, Inc. since April 5, 2021, succeeding William J. Merritt, who retired after 25 years with the company. He was also appointed to the company's board of directors in April 2021. As an industry veteran with over 25 years of experience in technology and intellectual property, Chen brings deep expertise in wireless communications and IP strategy to his role at InterDigital.
Prior to joining InterDigital, Chen spent nearly 25 years at Qualcomm Incorporated, where he held roles of increasing responsibility, culminating in his position as Senior Vice President and Global Head of Intellectual Property. In this capacity, he oversaw global IP licensing, litigation, and strategy, contributing to Qualcomm's position as a leader in wireless technology. Chen earned a Juris Doctor degree from the University of San Diego School of Law in 2008 and holds a Bachelor's degree in Automation from Tsinghua University in China. His legal and technical background has been instrumental in navigating complex patent licensing and innovation ecosystems.
Under Chen's leadership, InterDigital achieved record revenues of $869 million in 2024, marking a 58% increase year-over-year, driven by strengthened licensing programs and operational efficiencies. He has overseen significant expansion into AI research, including the acquisition of AI startup Deep Render in October 2025, which bolsters the company's capabilities in video compression and AI-driven technologies. In 2025, notable licensing achievements include a multi-year agreement with a major Chinese smartphone vendor in March and a renewal with Sharp Corporation alongside a new IoT license in October, contributing to annualized recurring revenue reaching a record $588 million by the third quarter.
Chen's leadership emphasizes the value of pure research facilities and strategic partnerships to drive innovation without immediate commercial pressures, as profiled in Forbes. This approach has positioned InterDigital to explore foundational advancements in AI, wireless, and video technologies through collaborations with industry leaders and standards bodies. In terms of compensation, Chen's 2025 insider transactions included sales of approximately $8.3 million in company shares in March.

Global Presence

Headquarters and Main Offices

InterDigital's corporate headquarters is located at 200 Bellevue Parkway, Suite 300, in Wilmington, Delaware, serving as the primary center for executive leadership, finance, and administrative functions. The company established this headquarters in 2012 to consolidate key corporate operations, leveraging Delaware's favorable business environment for governance and legal matters. This facility supports high-level decision-making and strategic oversight for InterDigital's global licensing and research activities.
The company's main research and development hub is situated at 1001 E. Hector Street, Suite 300, in Conshohocken, Pennsylvania, near the former King of Prussia location, where core innovation in wireless technologies takes place. This site houses significant engineering teams focused on advancing standards-essential patents and collaborative projects in areas like 5G and beyond. In Melville, New York, at 425 Broadhollow Road, Suite 203, InterDigital operates a recently expanded facility opened in June 2024, emphasizing AI/ML applications in wireless communications, next-generation Wi-Fi, 6G research, and video technologies, with collaborative spaces designed to foster standards development and academic partnerships.
Additional key U.S. offices include the facility in Los Altos, California, at 4410 El Camino Real, Suite 120; the office in Indianapolis, Indiana, at 5435 Emerson Way, Suite 230, contributing to video technology development; the office in New York, New York, at 111 West 33rd Street, Suite 1420; and the office in Washington, D.C., at 515 C St. NE. These U.S. sites collectively employ the majority of InterDigital's approximately 430 global workforce as of December 2024, prioritizing collaborative environments for innovation in 5G, AI, and related fields.

International Research Facilities

InterDigital maintains a global network of research and development facilities outside the United States, supporting its focus on wireless, video, AI, and emerging technologies. The company operates offices across 7 countries, including dedicated R&D facilities that drive innovation in standards development and technology prototyping. These international sites enable close collaboration with regional partners, standards bodies, and licensees, ensuring InterDigital's contributions align with diverse market needs.
In Montreal, Canada, InterDigital's R&I Lab serves as a hub for the Multimedia Systems team, specializing in immersive media, AI-driven video compression, and augmented/virtual reality (AR/VR) technologies. Established as a key center for cutting-edge research, the lab leads global standards engagement in these areas, hosting interns and researchers to advance multimedia delivery over 5G networks. Similarly, the London, UK, office houses Wireless Lab Europe (WLE), which focuses on standards-driven wireless research, including 6G innovations and integrated sensing and communications (ISAC). The WLE holds over 25 leadership positions in the European Telecommunications Standards Institute (ETSI) and has collaborated with more than 100 European partners on 6G projects.
In Asia, InterDigital's Beijing, China, office supports Asia-Pacific licensing operations and contributes to IoT and 5G research tailored to regional markets, facilitating telecom and video codec agreements with local implementers. European facilities, such as those in Rennes, France (Cesson-Svign), further bolster 6G efforts through ETSI collaborations and multi-partner research initiatives involving over 75 organizations. These sites, along with locations in Belgium and Finland, draw from InterDigital's global total of around 430 employees as of December 2024. This distributed structure enhances participation in international standards bodies and proximity to key licensees, accelerating the commercialization of innovations like AI-enhanced video and next-generation wireless systems.
