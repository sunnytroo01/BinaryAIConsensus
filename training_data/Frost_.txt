 Frost* is a British progressive rock band formed in 2004 by keyboardist and vocalist Jem Godfrey. The group is renowned for creating ambitious, melodic progressive music that fuses the expansive structures of classic prog with modern electronic and pop influences, often delivered through concept albums and intricate live performances. Featuring a core lineup that has evolved over time, including guitarist John Mitchell, bassist Nathan King, and drummer Craig Blundell, Frost* has built a dedicated following in the progressive rock scene through its innovative sound and high-energy shows.
The band's debut album, Milliontown (2006), marked an immediate breakthrough, praised for its epic 23-minute title track and bold keyboard-driven arrangements that propelled Frost* onto the European touring circuit alongside contemporaries like Magenta. Follow-up Experiments in Mass Appeal (2008) shifted toward more concise, song-based compositions while retaining the band's signature atmospheric depth, further solidifying their reputation. The band then released the live recording The Philadelphia Experiment (2010). After a second hiatus during which Godfrey pursued other projects, Frost* reformed in 2012, returning with studio albums Falling Satellites (2016) and Day and Age (2021), the latter earning widespread acclaim for its themes of societal change and emotional resonance.
Subsequent releases like the experimental EP Others (2020), and the deluxe edition of Day and Age (2021) showcased the band's versatility, blending orchestral elements, guest vocals, and cutting-edge production. In 2024, Frost* released their fifth studio album, the double concept album Life in the Wires, which revisits the expansive style of their debut and has been lauded as a career highlight for its narrative depth and sonic innovation, including over 80 minutes of music exploring digital-age isolation. The album's success underscores Frost*'s enduring influence, with recent UK tours and festival appearances highlighting their live prowess and commitment to evolving the progressive rock genre.

History

Formation (20042005)

Frost* was formed in 2004 by keyboardist and vocalist Jem Godfrey as a studio-based project, initially conceived in a garden shed in East Sussex as a creative outlet to escape the constraints of his pop songwriting career, which had included hits for acts like Atomic Kitten. Drawing inspiration from progressive rock traditions, Godfrey sought to explore longer, more ambitious compositions while retaining melodic accessibility, marking a shift toward a genre he had once considered outdated.
Godfrey quickly assembled the initial lineup by recruiting established musicians from the progressive rock scene: guitarist John Mitchell, known for his work with Arena and Kino; bassist John Jowitt from IQ; and drummer Andy Edwards, also of IQ. Guitarist John Boyes contributed to early sessions, providing additional guitar work and vocals on select tracks.
The band's name, Frost*, incorporates an asterisk that Godfrey described as a "serving suggestion," humorously implying flexibility in presentationsuch as "serve chilled" or "may contain nuts"to reflect the project's adaptable approach to lineup and musical style. This nomenclature underscored Godfrey's intent to maintain creative freedom without rigid commitments.
Early recording sessions centered on Godfrey's pre-composed material, emphasizing a fusion of progressive rock's structural complexity with pop's melodic hooks to create engaging, song-driven pieces rather than purely technical displays. Despite its studio origins, the group soon held initial live rehearsals, prompted by Mitchell's enthusiasm, leading to a decision to transition into a performing band to bring the material to audiences.

Milliontown and initial dissolution (2006)

The recording of Frost*'s debut album, Milliontown, took place primarily during 2005 and 2006 at Godfrey's home studio, The Cube, in southern England, with additional drum and backing vocal sessions at The Outhouse in Reading. The process was deliberately unhurried, allowing the band to pause when inspiration flagged to maintain creative quality. The album's title and closing track drew inspiration from Gordon Houghton's 2004 novel The Apprentice, a dark comedy depicting an undead protagonist's week-long quest to assist Death in exchange for eternal life, reflecting themes of existential drudgery and redemption.
Milliontown was released by InsideOut Music on 18 July 2006 in the United States and 24 July 2006 in Europe. The album showcased the original lineup's chemistry, blending neo-progressive rock elements with Godfrey's production expertise honed from commercial pop work. Its six tracks culminated in the title song, a sprawling 26-minute epic divided into six parts that explores motifs of personal growth through confronting self-delusion and societal pressures, such as corporate alienation and inner turmoil.
In October 2006, Frost* undertook their first tour, supporting Scottish progressive rock band Pallas on a four-date run through the Netherlands and Germany, which served as the group's live debut and introduced selections from Milliontown to audiences. The performances highlighted the band's dynamic stage presence despite their novice status in touring. However, by December 2006, following the tour's conclusion, Jem Godfrey announced the band's initial dissolution, citing financial pressures from self-funding the project, exhaustion from the brief but demanding road schedule, and his personal need for a respite to focus on family and other commitments, though no official disbandment declaration was made.

Reformation and Experiments in Mass Appeal (20072009)

Following the band's dissolution in 2006, Frost* informally reformed in 2007, spurred by Jem Godfrey's renewed creative interest and persistent fan enthusiasm for their debut album Milliontown. Godfrey announced on his personal blog that he was composing new material, leading to the revival of the project with core members John Mitchell on guitar and vocals, John Jowitt on bass, and Andy Edwards on drums. This resurgence marked a shift from the experimental, extended compositions of their initial output toward a more structured songwriting approach.
The band recorded their second studio album, Experiments in Mass Appeal, over 2007 and 2008 at The Riven Roadhouse in Hertfordshire, England, emphasizing shorter, more radio-friendly tracks to broaden appeal while retaining progressive elements like intricate arrangements and dynamic shifts. Tracks such as "Pocket Sun" and "Toys" exemplify this evolution, clocking in under five minutes with pop-infused hooks and layered harmonies, contrasting the sprawling epics of Milliontown. In 2008, the lineup expanded with the addition of Declan Burke on keyboards, guitar, and vocals, formerly of Darwin's Radio, bringing fresh melodic contributions to the sessions. However, Edwards departed later that year due to scheduling conflicts with his growing commitments as a music educator and other musical endeavors.
Experiments in Mass Appeal was released on 17 November 2008 via InsideOut Music, receiving positive reception for its accessible yet ambitious sound. To support the album, Frost* embarked on a promotional tour, including their debut U.S. performance at RoSfest on 2 May 2009 at the Keswick Theatre in Glenside, Pennsylvania, where Nick D'Virgilio of Spock's Beard filled in on drums. That same show was recorded for the live album The Philadelphia Experiment, capturing the band's energetic delivery of both old and new material. Shortly after, in May 2009, Jowitt announced his departure to pursue other projects, with Nathan King stepping in as bassist for upcoming commitments.

The Dividing Line and second hiatus (20102011)

In April 2010, Frost* released the standalone single "The Dividing Line," which premiered on 3 April as a bonus track on the live album The Philadelphia Experiment. Recorded in February and March 2010 specifically for the 10th anniversary of The Dividing Line Broadcast Network, the track served as a creative bridge between the band's second studio album Experiments in Mass Appeal (2008) and future material, blending progressive rock elements with electronic textures characteristic of their sound. It featured bassist Nathan King and drummer/vocalist Nick D'Virgilio alongside core member Jem Godfrey, marking a collaborative effort amid the band's evolving lineup from the reformation period.
On 2 March 2011, Godfrey announced an indefinite hiatus for Frost*, citing personal health challenges including burnout from managing multiple roles as singer, frontman, keyboardist, songwriter, and financier, as well as strains from band leadership dynamics after seven years of balancing the project with a full-time career. The decision, shared via the band's online forum, emphasized the need for recovery and reflection, with no plans for tours, new recordings, or performances during this period.
Activity remained minimal throughout the hiatus, limited to occasional fan updates from Godfrey via his blog and forum posts, which kept supporters informed without advancing band projects. This online engagement, alongside the enduring availability of prior releases like The Philadelphia Experiment, helped preserve Frost*'s cult following by fostering community discussion and appreciation among progressive rock enthusiasts.

Return and Falling Satellites (20122018)

Following the hiatus announced in March 2011, Frost* reunited in September 2011. The band's first live performance after the hiatus occurred in 2013 at the Celebr8.2 festival. The band's core lineup stabilized around this period, consisting of Jem Godfrey on keyboards and vocals, John Mitchell on guitar and vocals, Nathan King on bass and vocals, and Craig Blundell on drumsa configuration that provided continuity for the ensuing album cycle. With this solidified group, Frost* began developing material that built on their progressive rock foundations while incorporating fresh dynamics. Plans for their third studio album, Falling Satellites, were revealed in March 2016. The album was recorded during 2014 and 2015 at studios including The Cube in Northampton, Strongroom in London, and The Outhouse in Goring-on-Thames. The album emphasized conceptual unity, exploring themes of technology's role in fostering human connection and delivering an overarching positive message about global unity in the digital age.
Falling Satellites was released on 27 May 2016 through InsideOut Music, marking the band's return to studio output after an eight-year gap. The album's debut music video, for the track "Numbers," premiered on 31 May 2016, showcasing the band's polished production and thematic depth. To promote the release, Frost* embarked on a tour across the UK and Europe in 2016, including a headline slot at Ramblin' Man Fair in Maidstone on 23 July. Setlists during these shows blended selections from earlier albums like Milliontown and Experiments in Mass Appeal with a majority of tracks from Falling Satellites, highlighting the evolution of their sound while engaging longtime fans.

Others EP and lineup changes (20192020)

In May 2019, drummer Craig Blundell departed Frost* due to scheduling conflicts stemming from his extensive commitments with other projects, such as touring with Steven Wilson. For the band's appearance at the 2019 Cruise to the Edge festival, Nick DVirgilio stepped in as a fill-in drummer, having previously collaborated with band members in other contexts.
The band continued as a trio of Jem Godfrey, John Mitchell, and Nathan King, channeling their energy into new material amid the transition. In early 2020, they completed and released the Others EP on 5 June via digital platforms, comprising six tracks originally conceived during the Falling Satellites (2016) sessions but set aside as unfinished offcuts. These songs, including "Exhibit A" and "Fathers," were reimagined and polished to fill catalog gaps, offering a muscular progressive rock sound that echoed the prior album's intensity while providing fresh content for fans. The EP's physical edition was later bundled exclusively with the 13 Winters anthology, underscoring its role as a transitional release.
The COVID-19 pandemic disrupted Frost*'s momentum, postponing planned tours and studio sessions across the progressive music scene and forcing a pivot to archival projects. This shift culminated in the announcement of the 13 Winters compilation on 25 September 2020, followed by its digital release on 20 November and limited physical edition (3000 copies) shortly thereafter. The 8-CD box set spans the band's career with remastered studio albums, live recordings from key performances, unreleased demos (such as early takes of "Numbers" and "Heartstrings"), and rarities, providing a comprehensive retrospective that highlighted their evolution from Milliontown (2006) onward. Accompanied by an artbook with liner notes and photography, it served as a substantial fan offering during lockdowns, bridging the wait for new studio work.
Amid the restrictions, Frost* sustained fan connections through online activities, including virtual listening parties for the new releases and Q&A sessions that fostered community during isolation.

Day and Age and interim releases (20212023)

Following the lineup adjustments during the Others EP era, Frost* entered the studio in late 2020 to record their fourth studio album, Day and Age, navigating continued uncertainty at the drums by incorporating multiple guest contributors rather than committing to a single permanent player. The sessions, helmed by keyboardist and bandleader Jem Godfrey, featured drumming from Kaz Rodriguez on tracks like "The Boy Who Stood Still," Darby Todd on several cuts including "Waiting for the Lie," and Pat Mastelotto on others such as "Herd," allowing for diverse rhythmic textures that complemented the band's evolving sound.
Day and Age was released on 14 May 2021 via InsideOut Music, marking Frost*'s first full-length studio effort in five years and presenting a thematic exploration of time's passage, personal aging, and human resilience amid contemporary societal pressures like isolation and escapism. The album's production, overseen by Godfrey with mastering by Peter van 't Riet, earned widespread critical acclaim for its polished, multi-layered arrangements, where Godfrey's extensive multi-instrumental workspanning keyboards, vocals, and programmingdrove the record's dynamic blend of progressive rock and electronic elements. Reviewers highlighted its emotional depth and sonic innovation, often comparing it favorably to the band's earlier highs like Milliontown.
In September 2021, Frost* announced a five-date UK headlining tour for April 2022 to promote Day and Age, billed as their largest domestic outing to date; however, due to an unforeseen scheduling conflict with drummer Darby Todd, the shows were postponed to November 2022. This rescheduled run, under the banner of the Island Live tour, represented the band's first complete live performances since 2016 and was met with enthusiastic reception, praised for its high-energy delivery and faithful renditions of new material alongside classics. Capturing the momentum, Frost* issued the live album Island Live on 19 June 2023 through Tigermoth Records, a two-CD/Blu-ray set recorded at the Bath Komedia show on 30 November 2022, serving as an archival document of the tour's highlights including full plays of Day and Age tracks and fan favorites.

Life in the Wires (2024present)

On 16 August 2024, Frost* announced their fifth studio album, Life in the Wires, as a double concept album that explores themes of dystopian control and the digital age through the story of protagonist Naio navigating an AI-dominated world and discovering analog radio broadcasts. The album was released on 18 October 2024 via InsideOut Music, featuring the core lineup of Jem Godfrey on keyboards, guitar, and vocals; John Mitchell on guitars and vocals; Nathan King on bass and vocals; and Craig Blundell on drums, alongside guest contributions including vocals from Charlotte Evans, Jonas Renkse, and Dani Winter-Bates, as well as guitar solos from Sophie Lloyd and Adam "Nolly" Getgood. This marked a return to expanded solos after an eight-year absence, contrasting the disciplined, solo-free arrangements of the prior album Day and Age.
In January 2025, the band issued the standalone single "Western Atmosphere," originally available exclusively as a Japanese bonus track on Life in the Wires. The track, released on 23 January via InsideOut Music, highlights the band's melodic prog rock style with Godfrey's intricate keyboards and Mitchell's guitar work.
As of November 2025, Frost* have no confirmed tour dates, though Godfrey expressed hope for live performances in interviews, noting ongoing discussions amid logistical challenges. The band has instead focused on album promotion through a series of interviews and behind-the-scenes videos shared via their official channels.
Critics have praised Life in the Wires for its ambitious scope, blending melodic hooks with virtuoso elements and marking a creative expansion beyond the keyboard-dominated sound of earlier works, often hailing it as one of the band's peaks and a prog rock highlight of 2024. The album's reception underscores Frost*'s evolution, with reviewers noting its innovative storytelling and dynamic range across nearly 90 minutes of music.

Musical style and influences

Core elements and songwriting

Frost* is primarily recognized as a neo-progressive rock band, characterized by intricate compositions that merge complex musical structures with accessible melodic hooks and elements of pop sensibility. This approach allows the band to deliver ambitious progressive pieces while maintaining broad appeal through catchy refrains and emotional resonance.
Central to Frost*'s sound is the songwriting dominance of founder and multi-instrumentalist Jem Godfrey, who handles keyboards, vocals, and primary composition duties. Frost*'s sound draws from classic progressive rock acts like Genesis and Tony Banks, as well as ELO, Trevor Horn's production style, and 1980s electronic pop influences such as OMD and Depeche Mode. His keyboard-driven arrangements form the backbone of the band's music, often weaving conceptual narratives into multi-part epics, such as expansive title tracks exceeding 25 minutes in length. Godfrey's process emphasizes visual and cinematic storytelling, creating interconnected song cycles that evolve as progressive journeys, frequently refined through collaboration with guitarist John Mitchell. Harmonically and rhythmically, the band's work features frequent odd time signatures for dynamic tension, layered vocal harmonies to enhance emotional depth, and atmospheric synthesizers that build immersive textures. These elements contribute to a keyboard-oriented style that prioritizes strong harmonic cohesion and fusion of lyrics with elaborate arrangements.
Lyrically, Frost* maintains thematic consistency across its output, exploring personal introspection, the societal impacts of technology, and broader human concerns within a narrative framework. Godfrey's words often depict introspective characters navigating modern existential dilemmas, presented in a style open to interpretation while evoking subtle nostalgia. In production, the band achieves a clean, modern aesthetic through its partnership with InsideOut Music, focusing on pristine audio clarity that highlights live instrumentation and minimizes heavy effects processing. This approach underscores Godfrey's role as producer, ensuring a polished yet organic sound that supports the music's progressive ambitions.

Evolution across albums

Frost*'s debut album, Milliontown (2006), established the band's keyboard-heavy progressive rock identity through epic, ambitious compositions featuring long-form pieces that blended intricate arrangements with emotional depth. Jem Godfrey, the band's founder and primary songwriter, handled all lead vocals and emphasized keyboards as the sonic centerpiece, drawing from influences like classic prog while infusing modern production values. This approach created a raw, ambitious sound that prioritized narrative flow over concise structures, setting a foundation for the band's exploratory style.
With Experiments in Mass Appeal (2008), Frost* shifted toward greater accessibility, moving from the sprawling epics of Milliontown to shorter songs typically under five minutes, incorporating a sharper rock edge and more conventional song structures to broaden appeal. This evolution reflected Godfrey's intent to experiment beyond pure prog conventions, blending pop sensibilities with layered melodies and electronic touches while maintaining the keyboard-driven core. The change responded to the band's reformation, allowing for tighter, more immediate tracks that contrasted the debut's ambitious scope without abandoning progressive elements.
Falling Satellites (2016) marked a return to conceptual depth after hiatuses, integrating electronic and synth elements more prominently while enhancing the interplay between guitars and keyboards for a polished, confident sound. Godfrey described this as a natural progression through "happy accidents," with increased collaboration from bandmates like Nathan King on bass and Craig Blundell on drums, resulting in dynamic shifts and accessible yet intricate compositions. The album balanced technical prog with melodic hooks, evolving the band's style toward genre-blending maturity.
In Day and Age (2021), Frost* incorporated collaborative influences from guest drummers including Darby Todd, Kaz Rodriguez, and Pat Mastelotto, adding rhythmic variety through complex patterns such as 16th and 32nd notes, while exploring themes of maturity and interconnected narratives. This period saw a restrained approach to solos, emphasizing cinematic song structures and shared vocals between Godfrey and John Mitchell, which broadened the palette beyond traditional prog into pop-prog hybrids. The album's co-writing with Mitchell reflected adaptations to lineup stability post-hiatus, fostering a more versatile, energetic sound.
Life in the Wires (2024), a double-disc concept album, revived extended solosespecially on keyboardsand experimental structures, echoing Milliontown's ambition while expanding instrumentation to include diverse guitar tones and broader electronic integration. Spanning over 80 minutes in a narrative exploring AI and reflection, it featured Godfrey singing all leads for the first time in nearly two decades, with a four-sided vinyl format influencing its episodic, movie-like flow. This release responded to prior gaps by embracing boundary-free prog, blending nostalgia with innovative maturity.
Overall, Frost*'s trajectory progressed from the raw, keyboard-centric prog of Milliontown to a polished, genre-blending maturity, adapting through hiatuses and member changes to balance accessibility with experimental depth.

Members

Current members

Jem Godfrey, the founder of Frost* since 2004, serves as the band's primary keyboardist, lead vocalist, and chief songwriter. A multi-instrumentalist with a background in production, Godfrey has driven the band's creative direction across all albums, including producing and composing the bulk of their 2024 release Life in the Wires. His songwriting emphasizes intricate progressive structures blended with accessible melodies, as seen in recent tracks like the 2025 single "Western Atmosphere."
John Mitchell has been Frost*'s lead guitarist and backing vocalist since the band's inception in 2004. As a prominent figure in progressive rock, Mitchellalso a member of It Bites and Arenabrings technical prowess on guitar and rich harmonies to the lineup, contributing significantly to the band's live performances and studio recordings. His guitar work adds dynamic energy to recent material, such as the layered solos on Life in the Wires, enhancing the album's atmospheric depth.
Nathan King joined Frost* on bass in 2009, following his tenure with It Bites, and has since provided the band's rhythmic backbone. Known for his solid groove and melodic bass lines, King has co-written tracks on later albums, including contributions to Day and Age (2021) and Life in the Wires, where his playing supports the evolving fusion of prog and pop elements. He also performs with acts like Level 42 and The Blockheads, bringing versatility to Frost*'s sound.
Craig Blundell has been Frost*'s drummer since 2011, rejoining the band after a brief hiatus post-2019 for their latest projects. A prolific session musician who has toured with Steven Wilson and Steve Hackett, Blundell is renowned for his dynamic and precise drumming style, which injects propulsion and intensity into Frost*'s performances. His return was pivotal for Life in the Wires, where his rhythms underpin the album's ambitious compositions, and he continues to deliver clinics and recordings worldwide.
Frost* has centered around the core of Godfrey, Mitchell, and King since 2011, with drummers including Craig Blundell (20112018 and 2024present) and various guests during interim periods, under Godfrey's creative direction to maintain the band's signature blend of progressive complexity and mass appeal in their ongoing work.

Former members

John Jowitt served as Frost*'s original bassist from 2004 to 2006 and rejoined for the 20082009 period. He contributed the bass parts to the band's debut album Milliontown (2006), providing a solid rhythmic foundation that complemented the progressive structures, and returned to play on the follow-up Experiments in Mass Appeal (2008), where his lines supported the album's more experimental edge. Jowitt departed in May 2009 due to scheduling commitments with his other bands, including IQ and Arena, preventing him from participating in subsequent tours.
Andy Edwards was the early drummer, active from 2004 to 2006 and 2008 to 2011. He delivered the dynamic percussion for Milliontown, driving tracks like the title song with intricate fills characteristic of neo-prog, and featured on Experiments in Mass Appeal, adding to its live energy. Edwards also contributed to select tracks on the 2010 live album The Philadelphia Experiment. His departure stemmed from logistical issues, including a new role as a college lecturer that limited live performances, though he remained open to studio work initially.
John Boyes handled rhythm guitar in a guest-like capacity from 2004 to 2006, primarily during the recording of Milliontown. As a former bandmate of founder Jem Godfrey from Freefall, he added layered guitar textures to the early demos and formative tracks, helping shape the album's sound before fading from the project after its release.
Declan Burke joined as vocalist and guitarist from 2008 to 2009, recruited specifically for Experiments in Mass Appeal. He provided synth layers and vocal harmonies that enhanced the album's atmospheric and melodic elements, and participated in the supporting tour, including the RoSFest performance in 2009. Burke left shortly after the tour for logistical reasons related to scheduling.
Lineup changes in Frost*'s early years were primarily driven by logistical factors such as scheduling conflicts and professional commitments, rather than internal conflicts, and none of the former members have returned to the band since their departures.

Studio albums

Frost* has released five studio albums, all through InsideOut Music, showcasing their evolution within the neo-progressive rock genre. Their debut emphasized expansive, epic compositions, while subsequent works balanced accessibility with conceptual depth. Recent releases have achieved modest commercial success, with Life in the Wires peaking at number 53 on the UK Albums Chart.

Milliontown, the band's debut album, marked Frost*'s entry into progressive rock with a focus on ambitious, epic structures, particularly the 26-minute title track that serves as a sprawling centerpiece inspired by themes of community and introspection. The album blends melodic hooks with intricate keyboard work from founder Jem Godfrey, establishing the group's signature sound of catchy yet complex arrangements.
Experiments in Mass Appeal represented a shift toward more radio-friendly progressive rock, incorporating pop sensibilities and shorter song lengths to broaden appeal without sacrificing the genre's depth. Tracks like "Pocket Sun" highlight this accessible evolution, emphasizing Godfrey's songwriting prowess and collaborations with guitarist John Mitchell.
After an eight-year hiatus, Falling Satellites returned with a conceptual exploration of technology's impact on human connection, weaving narrative threads across its tracks in a cohesive suite-like format. The album features drummer Craig Blundell, adding dynamic rhythms to themes of isolation in a digital age, and received acclaim for its polished production and emotional resonance.
Day and Age adopted a drummer-less core lineup, enlisting guest percussionists including Pat Mastelotto and Darby Todd for varied textures, while delving into motifs of personal growth, loss, and contemporary societal pressures. The album's brooding atmosphere and layered vocals underscore a mature phase in Frost*'s discography, peaking at number 84 on the UK Albums Chart.
As a double album, Life in the Wires ambitiously tackles the concept of existence in a hyper-connected, AI-dominated world, spanning two discs with interconnected tracks that blend orchestral elements and electronic motifs. This ambitious release, clocking in at nearly 90 minutes, reaffirms Frost*'s innovative approach to progressive storytelling.

Live albums and EPs

Frost* has released several live albums and EPs that capture the band's dynamic performances and experimental shorter-form works, often tied to specific tours or archival explorations.
The band's first notable live recording, FrostFest Live* (2009), was a limited-edition CD EP of 1,000 copies distributed exclusively during tours, featuring eight tracks recorded at The Keswick Theater in Glenside, Philadelphia, on May 2, 2009, with the final track added in studio; it showcased early live energy from the US tour supporting their second album. This informal fan-oriented release highlighted the band's improvisational style in a cardboard sleeve format.
Following soon after, The Philadelphia Experiment (2010) expanded on that concert, offering a fuller live album on 2xCD and DVD via Cube Records, with 15 tracks (12 on the CDs) primarily from the same May 2, 2009, performance at Keswick Theater, plus a new studio track, "The Dividing Line," recorded in early 2010; it focused on material from the band's second album era. The release, including multichannel DVD audio, emphasized the progressive rock intensity of their North American shows. A reissue in 2017 as a 2CD set made it more widely available digitally and physically.
In 2020, Others EP was released digitally on June 5 via InsideOutMusic, comprising six tracks totaling over 30 minutes, including reworked archival pieces, covers, and new compositions like "Fathers" and "Exhibit A"; a physical CDr edition appeared later in the limited "13 Winters" anthology artbook. This EP experimented with compressed synths, glitches, and metal-infused energy, drawing from the band's creative archives without full-band live elements.
Island Live (2023), released by Tigermoth Records, documented the band's 2022 UK tour with a 2CD/DVD/Blu-ray set in digipak format, featuring 12 tracks from a November 30, 2022, performance at Komedia in Bath; it captured the post-Day and Age setlist in 5.1 surround sound. A digital version of the concert film became available in 2024, alongside limited physical editions.
These releases are primarily available in CD, digital download, and video formats, with several limited tour editions enhancing collector appeal, such as the 2008 Tour Sampler EP that preceded the live era but shared similar promotional distribution.

Compilations and other releases

Frost* has issued a select number of compilations and miscellaneous releases, primarily serving archival and promotional purposes by aggregating rarities, previews, and session material across their career.
The Tour Sampler 2008 is a promotional CD distributed exclusively during the band's 2008 UK tour, featuring early previews of tracks intended for the album Experiments in Mass Appeal, such as snippets of "Wedding Day at the Tax Office's" and "Here Is the News." Released on Cube Records, this limited item highlighted the band's evolving sound during a transitional period following their debut.
In 2013, the band released The Rockfield Files on Cube Records as a DVD/CD package, documenting a live studio session recorded at Rockfield Studios in Monmouth, Wales, on January 34, 2013. The set includes full-band performances of songs like "Hyperventilate," "Heartstrings," and "The Unremarkable Man," alongside a bonus acoustic session with tracks such as "Snowblind" and an interview with the lineup of Jem Godfrey, John Mitchell, Craig Blundell, and Darryl Way. This release captured the group's chemistry in an intimate studio environment, bridging their second and third albums.
The most extensive archival effort is 13 Winters, a limited-edition 8-CD box set issued by InsideOut Music on November 20, 2020, encompassing 74 tracks in a 72-page artbook format. It compiles remastered editions of the studio albums Milliontown (2006), Experiments in Mass Appeal (2008), and Falling Satellites (2016); the Others EP (2020); live recordings from tours; previously unreleased studio demos and outtakes; and a brand-new collection, This and That (B-Sides and Rarities), featuring 11 tracks including "The Dividing Line" (a 16-minute epic) and "British Wintertime." Limited to 1,000 copies, the set provides a retrospective overview of Frost*'s output over 13 years, emphasizing their progressive rock evolution through rare material.
Additional fan-oriented exclusives, such as instrumental versions occasionally shared via the band's membership club, have appeared sporadically, but no major soundtrack contributions or split releases exist in their catalog. These items underscore Frost*'s commitment to preserving and expanding access to their non-album material for dedicated listeners.

Singles

Frost* has released a limited number of standalone or promotional singles, primarily as digital downloads in later years, often tied to album cycles for radio play and fan engagement rather than chart performance. These releases reflect the band's focus on progressive rock audiences, with minimal emphasis on traditional single formats like physical 7-inch vinyl until recent exceptions. B-sides and remixes have been sparse, featuring occasional alternate mixes such as the 5.1 surround sound version of early tracks.
The band's first post-hiatus single, "The Dividing Line," was issued digitally in 2010 as a studio bonus track accompanying the live album The Philadelphia Experiment. Clocking in at nearly 17 minutes, it marked Jem Godfrey's return to songwriting after the 2008 album Experiments in Mass Appeal, blending electronic elements with expansive prog structures. An alternate 5.1 mix was later included in special editions.
In 2016, "Numbers" served as the lead promotional single from the album Falling Satellites, released digitally with an official music video to build anticipation ahead of the full album launch. The track emphasized catchy, synth-driven hooks and received targeted radio play within progressive rock circles.
For the 2021 album Day and Age, promotional singles included "Terrestrial," released digitally on March 19 with an official video, highlighting the album's themes of societal observation, and the title track "Day and Age," released digitally on April 23 with a music video ahead of the album's launch.
The 2024 album Life in the Wires was supported by several digital promotional singles: "Life in the Wires, Pt. 1" on August 16 with a music video, "Moral and Consequence" on September 12 with behind-the-scenes footage, and "Idiot Box" on October 7 with a conceptual video, each building hype for the double concept album's themes of digital isolation.
Most recently, "Western Atmosphere" was released as a standalone single on January 23, 2025, via InsideOutMusic, available in digital and limited vinyl formats. Originally a Japan-exclusive bonus track on the 2024 album Life in the Wires, it was reissued independently to wider audiences, featuring guest contributions from Mike Keneally, Nick D'Virgilio, and Randy McStine.
