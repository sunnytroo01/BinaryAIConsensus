 Commvault Systems, Inc. is an American publicly traded software company that develops and provides cyber resilience and data protection platforms for enterprises worldwide. Founded in 1996 as a spin-off from AT&T Bell Labs and incorporated in Delaware, the company is headquartered in Tinton Falls, New Jersey, and employs approximately 3,300 people globally as of 2025.
Commvault's core offering, Commvault Cloud, is an AI-powered, SaaS-based platform designed to simplify data backup, recovery, management, and security across on-premises, hybrid, and multi-cloud environments, with features including zero-trust architecture, immutable storage, and AI-powered threat detection via the Threat Scan feature to combat ransomware and cyber threats. Threat Scan uses AI-enabled engines and more than 100 million threat intelligence indicators to scan backups for known and zero-day threats, malware, anomalies, polymorphic threats, and early signs of encryption. This enables rapid detection, automatic quarantining of suspicious data, identification of clean recovery points, and integration with SIEM/SOAR tools for automated response.
Under the leadership of CEO Sanjay Mirchandani since 2019, Commvault has expanded its focus on cyber recovery and resilience, emphasizing sustainable practices such as LEED-certified data centers that have avoided over 155,000 kg of CO2 emissions as of 2025. The company trades on NASDAQ under the ticker CVLT and continues to innovate in data activation for AI-driven insights while prioritizing global environmental and social responsibility.

Company overview

Founding and operations

Commvault traces its origins to 1988, when it was established as a specialized development group within AT&T Bell Labs, initially focused on advancing data management and backup technologies to meet the emerging needs of enterprise data storage amid the late 1980s computing expansion. This group was later designated as a strategic business unit of AT&T Network Systems, laying the groundwork for innovative solutions in information management during a period of rapid technological growth in data handling and storage.
In 1996, the group was spun off from AT&T Bell Labs and incorporated as Commvault Systems, Inc., a Delaware corporation, with its initial headquarters located at 2 Crescent Place in Oceanport, New Jersey, USA. The company relocated its headquarters in 2014 to a new facility at 1 Commvault Way in Tinton Falls, New Jersey, where it continues to operate as of 2025. As of March 31, 2025, Commvault employs approximately 3,300 people worldwide, with 37% based in the United States and the remainder distributed internationally.
Commvault maintains a global operational footprint spanning multiple continents, including offices across the Americas, Europe, and the Asia-Pacific region, supporting its emphasis on hybrid cloud environments that integrate on-premises and cloud-based data infrastructures. With 29 locations worldwide, the company facilitates enterprise-level data protection and management services tailored to diverse geographic and technological landscapes. This international presence enables Commvault to address the complexities of hybrid cloud operations, ensuring scalable solutions for data resilience in distributed environments.

Business focus and market position

Commvault specializes in delivering cyber resilience solutions, encompassing data backup, recovery, and management software tailored for enterprise environments across hybrid, multi-cloud, and on-premises infrastructures. The company's platform enables organizations to protect critical data from threats, ensure business continuity, and automate recovery processes, addressing the escalating demands of modern IT landscapes.
The firm's revenue model centers on a subscription-based software-as-a-service (SaaS) offering through Commvault Cloud, alongside perpetual and term licensing options, with additional income derived from professional services and maintenance contracts. This hybrid approach supports recurring revenue streams, including SaaS subscriptions and utility-based software, which accounted for a significant portion of total billings in recent quarters.
In the data protection industry, Commvault holds a strong market position, recognized as a Leader in the 2025 Gartner Magic Quadrant for Backup and Data Protection Platforms for the 14th consecutive year. The company emphasizes robust security featuresincluding immutable storage, air-gapped backups, zero-trust framework, AI-powered threat detection, anomaly detection, threat hunting, and support for regulatory complianceto counter rising cyber threats, integrating threat scanning and automated recovery to enhance enterprise resilience. No publicly reported major data breach specific to Commvault Metallic (now integrated into Commvault Cloud) has been identified that significantly impacted customer trust, contributing to strong customer confidence in the company's security offerings and overall market position. Financially, Commvault reported $276 million in total revenue for the second quarter of fiscal year 2026, reflecting 18% year-over-year growth, and trades on the NASDAQ under the ticker symbol CVLT.

History

Origins and early development

Commvault's technological foundations were laid in 1988, when it originated as a development group within Bell Labs, a division of AT&T, dedicated to advancing data management, backup, and recovery technologies. This early effort addressed the growing demands of enterprise data storage in an era dominated by mainframe systems, producing initial tools for efficient data archiving and protection.
By the mid-1990s, the group had evolved into an AT&T Network Systems strategic business unit, focusing on innovative solutions for heterogeneous computing environments. In 1996, it transitioned to independence through a spin-off, incorporating as a software-focused entity in New Jersey and shifting from hardware-dependent tools to a more modular, platform-agnostic architecture. This structure featured core components such as intelligent agent software for data capture, movement software for transfer and deduplication, and command-and-control systems for orchestration, enabling scalable management across diverse platforms.
A pivotal innovation came with the launch of the Galaxy backup platform in early 2000, which integrated storage management functions to support unified backup and recovery in distributed, multi-vendor settings. Under early leaders Bob Hammer, who joined as president and CEO in 1998, and co-founder Al Bunte, who served as COO, the company emphasized this software-centric approach to meet enterprise scalability needs during the late 1990s data explosion.

Expansion and public listing

In 2006, Commvault completed its initial public offering (IPO) on the NASDAQ stock exchange under the ticker symbol CVLT, with shares priced at $14.50. The IPO raised net proceeds of approximately $80.2 million after underwriting discounts, which the company allocated toward global expansion, research and development, and general corporate purposes. This financial milestone provided the capital necessary to scale operations beyond its early U.S.-focused roots in data management software, enabling investments in international infrastructure and product innovation during a period of rapid growth in enterprise backup needs.
Following the IPO, Commvault experienced significant revenue expansion, with total revenues reaching about $109.5 million in fiscal year 2006 and growing to $711 million by fiscal year 2019. This growth was fueled by increasing adoption among large enterprises, particularly in sectors requiring robust data protection such as finance and healthcare, where compliance and recovery demands drove demand for unified solutions. Concurrently, the company advanced its product lineup with the release of the Simpana platform in 2008, which integrated backup, archiving, and replication functionalities into a single management console, simplifying data operations across heterogeneous environments.
During the mid-2000s, Commvault accelerated its international presence by establishing subsidiaries in key regions, including Europe (such as Germany and the Netherlands) and Asia, to better serve multinational clients with localized support and distribution channels. This expansion aligned with the company's strategy to broaden its global footprint, as outlined in post-IPO filings emphasizing increased distribution in Europe, Asia, and Australia to capitalize on emerging markets for data management software. By the early 2010s, these efforts contributed to sustained revenue momentum, positioning Commvault as a key player in enterprise data protection worldwide.

Recent strategic developments

In 2019, Commvault underwent a significant leadership transition with the appointment of Sanjay Mirchandani as President and Chief Executive Officer in February, succeeding Bob Hammer who retired after over two decades at the helm. Under Mirchandani's guidance, the company pivoted its strategy toward cloud-native architectures and subscription-based models to better align with evolving customer demands for scalable, as-a-service data protection solutions. This shift emphasized recurring revenue streams, with subscription annual recurring revenue (ARR) becoming a key growth driver, reflecting a broader industry move away from perpetual licenses.
Building on this foundation, Commvault launched its cloud-focused offerings in 2021 through expansions of the Metallic SaaS platform, introducing backup-as-a-service (BaaS) solutions tailored for hybrid cloud environments, including support for Kubernetes and other modern workloads. This initiative marked a deeper commitment to SaaS delivery, enabling seamless data protection across on-premises, multi-cloud, and edge scenarios while integrating AI capabilities for enhanced automation. By 2023, these efforts culminated in the formal introduction of Commvault Cloud, a unified platform powered by Metallic AI, further solidifying the company's cloud-native strategy.
Amid surging ransomware attacks following the 2020 global spike, Commvault intensified investments in cyber resilience, particularly AI-powered threat detection to preemptively identify anomalies in backup data. Features like Threat Scan and ThreatWise, leveraging machine learning for early ransomware indicators, were rolled out to bolster proactive defense, reducing detection times and enabling air-gapped recoveries.
Commvault expanded its ecosystem integrations by 2023, deepening partnerships with major cloud providers such as Amazon Web Services (AWS) and Microsoft Azure to facilitate workload migrations and native protections. These collaborations included AWS ISV Workload Migration Program participation and enhanced Azure support for cyber recovery orchestration.
In 2025, Commvault achieved notable industry recognition, securing the highest rankings in five of six use cases in the Gartner Critical Capabilities for Backup and Data Protection Platforms report, underscoring its strengths in execution and vision for enterprise backup solutions. Financially, the company issued optimistic FY2026 guidance, projecting subscription ARR growth of 24-25% and total ARR expansion of 18-19%, signaling sustained momentum exceeding 20% in key revenue metrics amid strong SaaS adoption.

Products and services

Core data protection platform

Commvault's core data protection platform, Commvault Cloud, serves as an integrated software suite designed for backup, recovery, and comprehensive data management across on-premises, cloud, and hybrid environments. This platform unifies data protection operations through a single code base, enabling organizations to safeguard diverse data estates while optimizing storage and recovery processes. It supports scalable deployments, including petabyte-scale workloads via integrated hardware solutions like HyperScale Flex, which facilitates high-performance backup and recovery for large datasets.
Key features of the platform include advanced deduplication and compression to reduce backup times and storage requirements by eliminating redundant data, alongside FIPS 140-3 certified encryption to secure data in transit and at rest. Automated indexing capabilities enable efficient content search and eDiscovery, allowing users to index and retrieve specific data elements across vast repositories. These elements contribute to handling petabyte-scale data efficiently, with policy-driven automation that schedules and orchestrates backups based on predefined business rules.
The platform supports a wide array of workloads, including relational databases such as Microsoft SQL Server and Oracle, virtualization environments like VMware and Microsoft Hyper-V, and various file systems across operating systems. This broad compatibility ensures seamless protection for enterprise applications, virtual machines, and unstructured data without requiring multiple point solutions. Architecture-wise, it relies on a centralized command center, the CommServe server, for orchestration and monitoring, providing a unified console for managing all protection activities.
The platform's architecture incorporates scalability and high availability features through component-specific load balancing and failover mechanisms, though not uniformly across all servers. Index Servers support load balancing to redistribute indexes across multiple nodes for performance optimization, with manual or automatic selection available through the Commvault interface. MediaAgents utilize GridStor technology to enable load balancing of backup and restore jobs across multiple instances via alternate data paths and round-robin distribution. The Web Console and Command Center can be configured for high availability and load balancing using external load balancers for ports 80 and 443, incorporating sticky sessions. In contrast, the CommServe Server does not support load balancing; high availability is achieved through failover using CommServe Live Sync to a standby host. These capabilities enhance the platform's resilience and ability to manage large-scale enterprise environments.
Over time, the platform has evolved from traditional legacy tools to an API-first design, incorporating microservices architecture for enhanced scalability and integration with DevOps workflows. This shift allows for programmatic control and automation, enabling seamless embedding into modern IT ecosystems while maintaining backward compatibility with established environments.
On November 12, 2025, Commvault released Commvault Cloud Unity, a next-generation update to the platform that unifies data security, cyber recovery, and identity resilience across cloud, SaaS, on-premises, and hybrid environments. Key enhancements include AI-enabled data discovery and classification, synthetic recovery for faster post-cyberattack restores, and identity threat detection in systems like Active Directory. This builds on Commvault Cloud by integrating capabilities from recent acquisitions such as Satori Cyber, with parts of the release available in late 2025 and further rollouts into early 2026.

Cyber resilience and cloud solutions

Commvault's Metallic SaaS platform provides managed backup and recovery services tailored for small and medium-sized businesses (SMBs), incorporating advanced features such as ransomware air-gapping and immutable storage to enhance data protection against cyber threats. This platform, now integrated into Commvault Cloud, enables automated, policy-driven backups with isolated, unalterable data copies stored in the cloud, ensuring quick recovery while minimizing exposure to ransomware attacks.
Commvault Cloud features robust security measures, including multi-layered hardened protection, immutable and air-gapped backups, proactive threat detection (e.g., ThreatWise, Security IQ), zero-trust principles, anomaly detection, cyber deception, role-based access controls, multi-factor authentication, and compliance with standards such as GDPR, HIPAA, and PCI DSS. These capabilities are designed to ensure data resilience, rapid recovery, and high customer confidence. No publicly reported major data breach specific to Commvault Metallic has been identified that significantly impacted customer trust. Commvault has been named a Leader in the Gartner Magic Quadrant for Backup and Data Protection Platforms for 14 consecutive times, most recently in the June 2025 report.
The company's solutions leverage AI integration for anomaly detection and automated recovery workflows across multi-cloud environments, including AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud. A key component is the Threat Scan feature, which employs AI-enabled engines and more than 100 million threat intelligence indicators to scan backups for known and zero-day threats, malware, anomalies, polymorphic threats, and early signs of encryption. This enables rapid detection, automatic quarantining of suspicious data, provision of clean recovery points, and integration with SIEM/SOAR tools for automated response. AI-powered tools scan for unusual patterns in data access and performance, flagging potential security issues like ransomware infiltration before they escalate. Automated recovery processes validate clean recovery points and orchestrate restores, reducing downtime in hybrid setups by intelligently prioritizing and sequencing workload recoveries. These capabilities extend to deep integrations with major cloud providers, allowing seamless protection of virtual machines, databases, and applications without vendor lock-in.
Commvault does not offer a dedicated Cloud-Native Application Protection Platform (CNAPP) product. Instead, Commvault Cloud integrates with third-party CNAPPs, such as Wiz, to enhance cloud security and recovery capabilities through vulnerability scanning, risk prioritization, and streamlined remediation workflows that support faster and more secure data recovery. No specific evaluation reviews of a Commvault CNAPP for 2025 or 2026 exist in reliable sources, as no such dedicated product is offered.
Following its 2025 acquisition of Satori Cyber, Commvault has incorporated real-time data access controls and compliance features into its platform, particularly for AI workloads. Satori's agentless technology enforces granular policies on data flows, monitoring and securing access to structured and unstructured data used in AI training and inference, while ensuring adherence to privacy standards. This integration provides visibility into data lineage and usage, preventing unauthorized exposure in dynamic AI environments and supporting secure data sharing across teams.
Commvault's cloud resilience offerings include disaster recovery orchestration and features for data sovereignty compliance, such as alignment with GDPR and CCPA requirements. The platform automates failover and failback processes, coordinating recovery across on-premises and cloud resources to maintain business continuity during outages or attacks. Built-in governance tools help organizations retain data within jurisdictional boundaries and audit access for regulatory reporting, reducing compliance risks in global operations.
In high-risk sectors like financial services, Commvault applies these capabilities to enable zero-trust data protection, where no entity is automatically trusted and all access is verified continuously. Financial institutions use the platform to safeguard sensitive transaction data and customer records against insider threats and external breaches, incorporating immutable backups and AI-driven threat scanning to align with stringent industry standards like PCI DSS. This approach has supported rapid recovery in simulated attack scenarios, ensuring minimal operational disruption for critical workloads.

Professional and support services

Commvault offers a range of professional services designed to assist customers in architecting, implementing, and optimizing their data protection environments. These include architecture design services that create tailored solutions for data protection needs, implementation support for low-risk deployments in collaboration with IT teams, and data migration services employing proven methodologies for transitioning to cloud environments. Additionally, health assessments provide an independent evaluation of existing data protection strategies to identify gaps and improvement opportunities.
The company provides managed services through Cyber Resilience Managed Services, which involve remote monitoring and management of customers' data protection environments by Commvault experts to ensure security, reliability, and efficiency on a global scale. These services, often delivered via authorized partners, include 24/7 monitoring for proactive issue detection and rapid incident response to address cyber threats, such as through cleanroom recovery testing in isolated cloud environments.
Training programs are available to equip IT administrators with skills for platform deployment and management, featuring resources like the Readiverse Academy for cyber resilience insights, interactive workshops on attack response, and video series on core topics. Certification paths include the Cyber Resilience Certification for strategic readiness and the Commvault Engineer Certification, which covers Command Center operations and is recommended for SaaS deployments, with two tracks for different console users. These offerings also provide continuing professional education credits through partnerships like ISC.
Support services are structured in tiers to meet varying enterprise needs, with Standard Support offering case-based assistance during business hours (Monday-Friday, 7 a.m. to 7 p.m. local time) and 24/7 access to a self-help portal for updates and troubleshooting. Premium Support extends this to 24/7/365 direct access to the Customer Support Center, ideal for mission-critical operations. For localized delivery, Commvault Authorized Support Partners (CASP) provide business-hour or 24/7 support with escalation to Commvault, including health checks and faster resolutions in local languages.
Post-sales enablement is facilitated through Commvault Cloud Success Services, tiered as Foundational, Essential, and Enterprise to maximize platform value. These include proof-of-concept guidance via dedicated managers, quarterly ROI analysis with performance metrics, and implementation support such as expedited onboarding, proactive monitoring, and professional services credits up to 25% for enterprise clients.

Acquisitions and partnerships

Major acquisitions

Commvault has pursued a strategic acquisition program to bolster its cyber resilience and cloud-native data protection offerings, completing four major deals by 2025 that integrate advanced technologies for distributed storage, application recovery, autonomous backup, and AI-driven security. This approach accelerates innovation in hybrid cloud environments by embedding acquired capabilities into Commvault's core platform, enabling faster recovery from cyber threats and enhanced governance across multi-cloud deployments.
In September 2019, Commvault acquired Hedvig, a provider of software-defined distributed storage, to expand its capabilities in cloud-native backup and scale-out data management. The integration of Hedvig's technology introduced flexible, elastic storage solutions that support secondary storage for backups, allowing Commvault to compete more effectively in software-defined infrastructure markets and simplify data protection for diverse workloads. This move marked Commvault's entry into primary storage innovation, broadening its addressable market beyond traditional backup to include hyper-converged and cloud-scale environments.
Commvault acquired Appranix in April 2024, a cloud cyber resilience specialist focused on automated application recovery, to address the challenges of rapid rebuilding after ransomware or outages in cloud-native setups. Appranix's technology enables point-in-time recovery of entire cloud applications, including configurations and dependencies, reducing downtime from days to hours for enterprises reliant on AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud. Strategically, this acquisition enhances Commvault's cyber resilience portfolio by automating resilience testing and recovery orchestration, positioning the company as a leader in proactive cloud protection against evolving threats.
In September 2024, Commvault announced its acquisition of Clumio, enhancing its autonomous data protection for AWS workloads with SaaS-based backup and recovery features tailored for cloud-native applications. The deal was completed in October 2024. Clumio's platform provides policy-driven, air-gapped protection that isolates data from production environments, improving recovery point objectives and supporting compliance in regulated industries. The deal strengthens Commvault's multi-cloud strategy by integrating Clumio's AWS expertise, enabling seamless extension of cyber resilience to virtual machines, databases, and S3 storage while optimizing costs through intelligent data tiering.
Commvault announced its intent to acquire Satori Cyber in July 2025, closing the deal in August 2025, to incorporate real-time data access controls and AI governance into its security framework. Satori's solutions provide fine-grained visibility and policy enforcement for sensitive data across cloud databases and AI models, mitigating risks from unauthorized access and ensuring compliance with regulations like GDPR and HIPAA. This acquisition advances Commvault's platform by fusing data protection with security controls, enabling enterprises to secure AI-driven workloads in hybrid environments and reducing the attack surface for emerging threats like data exfiltration.

Key strategic partnerships

Commvault has established a robust network of strategic partnerships to enhance its cyber resilience offerings, focusing on integrations with cloud providers, hardware vendors, and channel ecosystems. These alliances enable seamless data protection across hybrid and multi-cloud environments, leveraging complementary technologies for optimized backup, recovery, and threat mitigation.
In October 2025, Commvault deepened its partnership with Hewlett Packard Enterprise (HPE) through a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) signed at GITEX Global, emphasizing enhanced data protection and cyber resilience on the HPE GreenLake platform. This collaboration integrates Commvault's software with HPE's infrastructure, including Alletra Storage MP, StoreOnce, and Zerto Software, to deliver joint go-to-market activities, technical enablement, and training for addressing cyberthreats in the Middle East and beyond. The partnership builds on prior integrations to provide scalable, as-a-service resilience for multi-cloud deployments.
Commvault maintains deep integrations with major hyperscalers for native cloud backup solutions. Through Clumio by Commvault, it offers agentless, serverless protection for AWS services such as S3 object storage, DynamoDB NoSQL databases, RDS relational databases, and EC2/EBS instances, enabling immutable backups, rapid recovery, and cost optimization exceeding 30% in some cases. Similarly, Commvault Cloud provides API-based cyber resilience for Microsoft Azure, covering workloads like Active Directory, AI stacks, and SaaS applications with zero-trust architecture, air-gapped isolation, and automated migration to Azure for streamlined hybrid protection.
Collaborations with hardware vendors optimize storage and performance for Commvault's solutions. In August 2025, Commvault launched HyperScale Edge and Flex appliances in partnership with Dell Technologies, HPE, Lenovo, and Pure Storage, supporting edge locations, SMBs, and large-scale AI workloads through modular, high-performance flash storage pools for localized recovery and ransomware defense. Specifically, the HyperScale Flex integration with Pure Storage's FlashBlade enables enterprise-grade cyber resilience with rapid data recovery in minutes, validated for financial services compliance under regulations like DORA.
Commvault's channel partnerships with resellers and managed service providers (MSPs) facilitate global distribution and service delivery, with expansions in 2025 enhancing ecosystem reach. Key allies include global systems integrators such as Deloitte and Kyndryl for end-to-end cyber resilience implementations, alongside MSP-focused programs that enable partners to deliver as-a-service data protection. These efforts, including new enablement tools and marketplace integrations, support broader adoption in regions like the Middle East and support MSP revenue growth through cloud-optimized offerings.
Following the acquisition of Satori Cyber in August 2025, Commvault has pursued joint innovations in AI security through co-developments with cybersecurity firms, integrating Satori's data governance capabilities to secure AI-driven data flows, alongside a September 2025 alliance with BeyondTrust for privileged access management in backups, reducing risks from human and non-human identities. These synergies from the Satori integration extend Commvault's platform for compliant AI usage across multi-cloud setups.

Leadership and governance

Executive team

Sanjay Mirchandani has served as President and Chief Executive Officer of Commvault since 2019, leading the company's transformation into a cyber resilience leader through significant investments in research and development, the launch of a rapidly scaling SaaS business, and expansions in cloud-first offerings via strategic acquisitions. Prior to joining Commvault, Mirchandani was CEO of Puppet, where he expanded the user base to over 40,000 companies including 75% of the Fortune 100, and held senior leadership roles at VMware, EMC, and Microsoft, focusing on cloud strategy and enterprise software solutions. He holds an MBA from the University of Pittsburgh and a bachelor's degree in mathematics from Drew University.
Jen DiRico serves as Chief Financial Officer, appointed in August 2024, where she drives long-term growth initiatives, including subscription revenue expansion that contributed to Commvault's total annual recurring revenue (ARR) surpassing $1 billion ($1.043 billion) ahead of schedule in fiscal year 2026. Under her leadership, the company reported 18% overall revenue growth in Q2 FY2026, with subscription ARR increasing 30% year-over-year to $894 million, fueled by SaaS adoption. DiRico brings over a decade of experience scaling finance and operations in high-growth tech environments, previously serving in executive roles at Toast, including as Senior Vice President and General Manager of International.
Rajiv Kottomtharayil is Chief Product Officer, overseeing the development of Commvault's core data protection platform and innovations in AI-driven security features, such as real-time data controls integrated through recent acquisitions like Satori Cyber. His work emphasizes enhancing cyber resilience for AI and modern data platforms, contributing to the company's focus on securing distributed environments. Kottomtharayil holds a Master of Science in Computer Science from Monmouth University and a Bachelor of Science in Electronics from Bombay University.
Alan Atkinson was appointed Chief Business Development Officer in July 2025, leading strategic technology and go-to-market alliances to foster co-development and partnerships that support Commvault's subscription growth and ecosystem expansion. A Commvault veteran since 2022 in partner leadership roles, Atkinson previously held senior sales positions at Dell Technologies.
Michelle Graff joined as Senior Vice President of Global Partners and Channel in July 2025, directing partner sales, reseller programs, and alliances to drive co-innovation and revenue growth in hybrid cloud environments. With more than 25 years in channel strategy, Graff previously served as VP of Global Channel Sales at Securiti.ai and held executive positions at HashiCorp, Pure Storage, and Palo Alto Networks.
The executive team collectively emphasizes AI integration, enhanced security measures, and accelerating subscription-based models, positioning Commvault to address evolving cyber threats and data management needs in cloud and AI-driven landscapes.

In a recent development, cybersecurity executive Geoff Haydon joined the board effective October 17, 2025, enhancing its focus on managed detection and response (MDR) and operational profitability; Haydon, former CEO of Ontinue, guided the company to profitability in 2025 and brings over 30 years of experience from roles at VMware, Secureworks, and RSA Security, while also serving on the Audit Committee.
The board's diverse composition underscores Commvault's emphasis on cybersecurity, artificial intelligence, and international expansion, with members collectively guiding strategic initiatives such as the August 2025 acquisition of Satori Cyber to bolster data and AI security and the June 2025 expansion of the partnership with Hewlett Packard Enterprise for integrated cyber resilience solutions across hybrid environments.
