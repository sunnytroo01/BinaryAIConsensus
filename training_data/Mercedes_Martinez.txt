 Mercedes Martinez is the ring name of Jazmin Benitez, an American professional wrestler born on November 17, 1980, in Waterbury, Connecticut. Signed to All Elite Wrestling (AEW) under contract as of 2025, she competes in the women's division and has been a prominent figure in the industry since her debut in 2000. Known for her technical prowess and longevity, Martinez has worked across major promotions including WWE as a competitor in the Mae Young Classic tournaments in 2017 and 2018, Impact Wrestling, and numerous independent organizations such as SHIMMER, WSU, and Shine Wrestling.
Martinez began her wrestling career after transitioning from college basketball due to injury, training under former ECW star Jason Knight and debuting in November 2000. Early in her career, she gained recognition on the independent scene, capturing titles like the WXW Women's Championship and becoming a three-time WSU World Champion, including a record three-year reign from 2009 to 2012. Her tenure in WSU solidified her as a cornerstone of women's wrestling during that era, where she also held the WSU Tag Team Championship once. In AEW since 2021, she has challenged for the ROH Women's World Championship and participated in high-profile storylines, though her television appearances have been limited since mid-2023 despite her contract remaining active, while continuing independent appearances as of 2025.

Biography

Early life

Mercedes Martinez, born Jazmn Bentez on November 17, 1980, in Waterbury, Connecticut, is of Puerto Rican descent. Raised in Waterbury, she grew up in a supportive family environment where wrestling was a shared interest, influenced particularly by her brother and uncle who introduced her to the sport through viewing events.
As a young athlete, Martinez excelled in high school sports, participating actively in basketball and softball, which honed her competitive drive and physical conditioning. She continued playing basketball at the college level, but a significant injury sidelined her from the sport.
This setback in the late 1990s prompted a pivotal shift when a friend suggested she visit a local wrestling school to maintain her fitness. Already a lifelong fan of professional wrestlingevidenced by her yearbook reference to Bret Hart's catchphraseMartinez attended the Power Station training facility in her hometown, run by trainer Jason Knight, marking the beginning of her pursuit of a career in the ring.

Personal life

Mercedes Martinez publicly came out as a lesbian in July 2019 during an interview with NowThis News, sharing her experiences navigating her identity in the male-dominated world of professional wrestling. She is a mother to a son born in 2008.
As an advocate for LGBTQ+ rights, Martinez has participated in pride events, including wrestling at the Rise Pride & Joy show, and has spoken in interviews about the importance of greater representation and visibility for queer wrestlers in the industry. Her efforts highlight the challenges faced by LGBTQ+ individuals in wrestling and promote inclusivity through public discussions and event appearances.
Martinez suffers from asthma.
In 2022, Martinez sustained an injury during a match that resulted in a concussion and forced her out of action for several months. This health setback underscored the risks of long-term in-ring work and influenced her approach to balancing personal well-being with professional demands.
Following her recovery and amid career transitions post-2020, Martinez has dedicated time to coaching and fitness training for aspiring female wrestlers, offering programs focused on building strength, stamina, and body confidence without extreme dieting. Her initiatives aim to prepare women for the rigors of professional wrestling, including television readiness, drawing from her over two decades of experience in the ring.

Professional wrestling career

Early career (20002005)

Mercedes Martinez began her professional wrestling journey in October 2000, training under Jason Knight at the Power Station in Waterbury, Connecticut, after a basketball injury drew her interest to the industry. Initially not intending to pursue wrestling full-time, she enrolled in the local school out of curiosity and quickly committed to the rigorous regimen, completing her initial training in just four weeks.
She made her in-ring debut on November 12, 2000, defeating a male opponent known as "Juice" in a match that highlighted her early adaptability in a male-dominated environment. This debut came shortly after her training began, marking the start of her foundational experience on the independent circuit. Throughout 2001, Martinez gained further exposure in World Xtreme Wrestling (WXW) under promoter Sheldon Goldberg, where she competed in her initial women's matches and began building her reputation in regional promotions.
From 2002 to 2005, Martinez expanded her early indie tours across the Northeast U.S., wrestling in promotions such as IWA Mid-South, where she faced notable competitors like Tracy Brooks and MsChif in high-intensity bouts that tested her resilience. These matches often involved mixed-gender elements due to the scarcity of female talent, exposing her to ultraviolent styles in groups like Combat Zone Wrestling (CZW) as well. During this period, she honed her technical wrestling foundation, emphasizing precise submissions and suplex variations, including the Fisherman suplex, which became a cornerstone of her aggressive, mat-based approach.

Rise in women's independent wrestling (20052010)

Martinez made her debut with Shimmer Women Athletes on November 6, 2005, at the promotion's inaugural event in Berwyn, Illinois, where she faced Sara Del Rey in the main event of Volume 1, ending in a 40-minute time-limit draw that highlighted her technical prowess and endurance. She continued to appear in the promotion's early tapings, participating in Volumes 1 through 10 between 2005 and 2007, including victories over opponents like LuFisto in Volume 7 and tag team bouts that showcased her versatility. Her time in Shimmer established key rivalries, particularly with Sara Del Rey, whom she rematched multiple times, including a no-time-limit bout in Volume 5 where Del Rey emerged victorious after 30 minutes, and a decisive win for Martinez in late 2006 during Volume 17. Martinez also developed a notable feud with MsChif, culminating in a 2008 title match in Volume 20 where MsChif retained the Shimmer Championship via submission after a grueling exchange of strikes and holds. During these years, she engaged in her first significant tag team work in Shimmer, partnering in multi-woman matches that built her reputation as a reliable team player.
In 2007, Martinez joined Women Superstars Uncensored (WSU), quickly rising through the ranks with intense matches that emphasized her aggressive style, including a feud with Angel Orsini that spanned into 2008 and featured stipulation bouts like Last Woman Standing. She captured the WSU Spirit Championship in 2008 during the promotion's second annual J-Cup tournament, defeating Orsini in the finals to claim the title and solidify her status as a top contender. Concurrently, Martinez thrived in World Xtreme Wrestling (WXW), securing multiple reigns with the WXW Women's Championship between 2005 and 2009, including a 56-day run from January to March 2007 and a 49-day title hold from December 2008 to February 2009, often defending against regional talents in hard-fought defenses that showcased her suplexes and submissions. These accomplishments in WXW, highlighted by her victories in the Elite 8 tournaments in 2006 and 2008, marked her evolution into a dominant force in women's independent wrestling.
From 2006 to 2007, Martinez made brief appearances in Ring of Honor (ROH) as part of the promotion's emerging women's division, debuting on March 25, 2006, in a four-way match against Allison Danger, Daizee Haze, and Lacey, where she demonstrated her striking ability in a losing effort. Her ROH stint included additional multi-woman bouts that helped introduce female talent to the roster, though it remained limited to sporadic dates. Throughout 2005 to 2010, Martinez frequently competed on the independent circuit in Northeast promotions such as New England Championship Wrestling (NECW), Jersey All Pro Wrestling (JAPW), and others, where she built a reputation as a hard-hitting technician known for brutal suplexes, stiff forearms, and resilient performances in grueling matches against local competitors. These appearances, often in multi-woman or intergender contexts, underscored her adaptability and contributed to her growing prominence in the regional scene.

Major promotions and title pursuits (20112019)

During the 2010s, Mercedes Martinez solidified her status as a cornerstone of the women's independent wrestling scene, particularly through her extended tenure with SHIMMER Women Athletes. She continued competing regularly in SHIMMER events from 2011 onward, amassing a record of high-profile matches that showcased her technical prowess and resilience. In 2016, Martinez made a notable return after a period of absence due to injury, defeating Madison Eagles to capture the SHIMMER Championship in the main event of Volume 86, marking her second reign with the title. Her pursuit of the SHIMMER Tag Team Championship intensified in the late 2010s; partnering with longtime rival-turned-ally Cheerleader Melissa, she challenged for the belts multiple times before finally winning them in early 2019 during the SHIMMER 13 tapings. A defining aspect of her SHIMMER run was her intense feud with LuFisto, which spanned several years and culminated in brutal stipulations, including a Falls Count Anywhere match at Volume 61 in 2012 that highlighted their mutual respect and hard-hitting style. This rivalry, rooted in earlier independent clashes, elevated both wrestlers and drew significant fan attention to SHIMMER's storytelling.
Martinez's most dominant period came in Women Superstars Uncensored (WSU), where she won the WSU World-1 Women's Championship on October 8, 2011, defeating Lexxus in a grueling 73-minute match that underscored her endurance. She defended the title successfully over 50 times across multiple years, holding it continuously until 2015 and contributing to WSU's growth as a premier women's promotion through her reliable performances against challengers like Rain, Angel Orsini, and international stars. During this reign, Martinez formed the Queens of the Ring stable alongside allies including Angel Orsini and Rain, using the group to dominate WSU storylines and tournaments, such as the 2011 King and Queen of the Ring event where she teamed with Julio Dinero to victory. The faction emphasized Martinez's leadership in elevating emerging talent while maintaining her ironclad grip on the championship division, with defenses extending into 2018 across various WSU revival shows.
Returning to World Xtreme Wrestling (WXW) in 2011 after an earlier successful stint, Martinez recaptured the spotlight in the women's division, culminating in her fifth WXW Women's Championship win on October 15, 2016, against a field of local competitors. This reign, lasting 126 days, featured defenses that reinforced her veteran status in the promotion until 2017, blending her aggressive style with WXW's hardcore elements.
Beyond these core promotions, Martinez expanded her global presence on the independent circuit from 2011 to 2019, touring Japan with World Wonder Ring Stardom in 2015 where she competed in the World of Stardom Championship tournament, advancing to the semifinals before losing to Nanae Takahashi in a hard-fought bout. Her European outings included multiple tours with the World Association of Wrestling (WAW) in the UK, highlighted by appearances at events like Fightmare 3 in 2019, where she faced local talent and helped bridge American and British women's wrestling styles. These international excursions built on her signature rivalries from the prior decade, exposing her to diverse opponents and enhancing her reputation as a versatile performer.
Martinez's independent peak transitioned toward mainstream exposure in 2019 with her debut in All Elite Wrestling (AEW) at All Out on August 31, entering as the surprise entrant in the Casino Battle Royale and eliminating several competitors before being ousted by Britt Baker. This appearance teased broader TV opportunities, positioning her as a bridge between the indies and emerging major leagues.

WWE stints (20172018, 20202021)

In 2017, Mercedes Martinez joined WWE for the inaugural Mae Young Classic tournament, where she showcased her veteran technical prowess by defeating Xia Li via submission in the first round. She advanced past Princesa Sugehit in the second round with a hard-fought victory, followed by a quarterfinal win over Abbey Laith through aggressive striking and grappling. Martinez reached the semifinals but fell to Shayna Baszler, who submitted her in a match highlighting Baszler's MMA background against Martinez's resilient brawling style.
The following year, Martinez returned for the second Mae Young Classic, defeating Taya Valkyrie in the first round before losing to Meiko Satomura in the second round, where Satomura's precision strikes overcame Martinez's power-based offense. Outside the tournament, she made sporadic NXT appearances, including a competitive loss to Nikki Cross on October 31, 2018, in a bout that emphasized Cross's unhinged energy clashing with Martinez's grounded approach. These engagements marked her initial forays into WWE's ecosystem, building on her established independent reputation without a long-term contract at the time.
Martinez signed a developmental contract with WWE in January 2020 amid the company's expansion of its women's division, reporting to the Performance Center in Orlando for intensive training. Assigned to NXT, she debuted in a #1 contender's battle royal on January 15, 2020, and secured her first televised win against Kacy Catanzaro on February 5 via a brutal series of suplexes. She briefly aligned with the Robert Stone Brand but parted ways in September 2020 due to creative differences over her role in the faction. 
By 2021, Martinez featured in NXT dark matches and promotional vignettes that underscored her 20-plus years of experience, positioning her as a no-nonsense enforcer. She entered a feud with Xia Li, stemming from their 2017 tournament encounter, culminating in a loss to Li at NXT TakeOver: In Your House on June 13. Despite these opportunities, her television exposure remained limited, and WWE released her on August 6, 2021, as part of broader NXT roster adjustments. In post-release interviews, Martinez reflected that WWE's structured environment challenged her to incorporate more high-impact maneuvers into her mat-based style, though she felt the creative team struggled to fully utilize her indie-honed fundamentals.

Transitions to AEW, Impact, and ROH (20192022)

In 2019, Mercedes Martinez made her first appearance with All Elite Wrestling (AEW) at the All Out pay-per-view event on August 31, where she competed in the pre-show Casino Battle Royale for a future AEW Women's World Championship opportunity. During the match, she was eliminated by Britt Baker after a competitive exchange that highlighted her veteran skills against the rising AEW star. This appearance sparked contract discussions with AEW, as Martinez revealed in later interviews that she negotiated terms but ultimately did not sign at the time, opting to continue her independent career while keeping the door open for future opportunities.
Following her release from WWE in August 2021, Martinez transitioned to Impact Wrestling, debuting on the October 7 episode of Impact! in a six-woman tag team match alongside Tasha Steelz and Savannah Evans, defeating Brandi Lauren, Kimber Lee, and Lady Frost. This debut positioned her within Impact's Knockouts division, leading to a feud with Steelz that culminated at the Knockouts Knockdown special on October 9, where Martinez defeated Steelz in the tournament final to earn a future Impact Knockouts World Championship match. Her time with Impact ended in early 2022 when she was released amid her growing commitments elsewhere, allowing her to refocus on AEW and its sister promotion, Ring of Honor (ROH). She later challenged for the Impact Knockouts World Championship, losing to Mickie James at Turning Point on November 20, 2021.
Martinez returned to AEW programming on the November 5, 2021, episode of AEW Dark, teaming with Big Swole in a losing effort against The Bunny and Sadie Gibbs, marking her re-entry into the promotion after her WWE stint. She officially signed a full-time contract with AEW on December 29, 2021, during a live episode of AEW Dynamite: New Year's Smash, solidifying her place in the women's division. In early 2022, Martinez aligned with The Bunny in storylines, forming a short-lived alliance that saw them compete together on AEW's YouTube-exclusive shows, emphasizing Martinez's heel persona and aggressive style against babyface competitors.
With ROH under AEW ownership, Martinez returned to the promotion at Supercard of Honor XV on April 1, 2022, defeating Willow Nightingale via submission with her signature Dragon Sleeper to win the vacant ROH Women's World Championship, becoming the inaugural champion in the revived era. This victory established her as a dominant force, blending her AEW appearances with ROH defenses in a cross-promotional framework. On July 23, 2022, at Death Before Dishonor, Martinez retained the title against Serena Deeb in a hard-fought submission match lasting over 17 minutes, showcasing technical prowess and resilience that solidified her reign. These defenses highlighted the integrated AEW-ROH ecosystem, with Martinez frequently appearing on both brands to elevate the women's divisions through shared storytelling and high-profile bouts.

Recent career and independent work (2023present)

Mercedes Martinez has remained under contract with All Elite Wrestling (AEW) and Ring of Honor (ROH) since signing in late 2021, continuing her association with the promotions as of November 2025. However, she has experienced a significant absence from AEW and ROH television programming, with her last televised match occurring on the June 9, 2023, episode of AEW Rampage in a four-way bout. This hiatus, attributed primarily to creative decisions within the company, extended to an 18-month drought from AEW TV by May 2025, during which she made sporadic appearances on ROH HonorClub, including her final one on April 27, 2024. Despite the reduced on-screen presence, Martinez's contract status remains active, as confirmed by internal and public rosters.
In response to her limited television opportunities, Martinez has revitalized her presence on the independent circuit starting in 2023, competing in promotions outside the AEW umbrella to maintain her in-ring activity. Notable appearances include matches for Game Changer Wrestling (GCW), where she participated in a multi-person battle royal on April 6, 2024, during the promotion's events. She also returned to Beyond Wrestling for two bouts in 2024, showcasing her veteran technical style against emerging talents. These indie bookings have allowed her to sustain a rigorous schedule, with additional outings in organizations like House of Glory and The Establishment Wrestling, emphasizing her enduring appeal in the women's division.
A highlight of her independent resurgence came in early 2025, when Martinez captured the inaugural VIP Wrestling Women's Championship on January 31, 2025, defeating a field of competitors including Maya World in Haltom City, Texas. She held the title for 91 days until losing it on May 2, 2025. This accomplishment, combined with her induction into the Indie Wrestling Hall of Fame on April 7, 2024, as the first openly LGBTQ+ inductee, highlighted her contributions to the independent scene.
Parallel to her wrestling commitments, Martinez has evolved toward a mentorship-oriented role, focusing on guiding the next generation of female performers. In interviews, she has expressed a desire to transition into producing, agent work, and coaching, drawing from her extensive experience to support younger wrestlers in building their careers. Her social media presence and fitness coaching initiatives further reflect this shift, promoting physical and professional development for women in the industry without compromising their unique styles. As of late 2025, while no full-time AEW return has materialized, her indie focus and mentorship efforts position her as a pivotal figure in women's wrestling's ongoing growth.
In 2025, Martinez continued her independent bookings, challenging for the SHINE Championship against champion Kelsey Raegan on October 31 at SHINE 82, in a match that showcased her enduring technical skill. On September 20, she competed in a women's title unification match against TNA's Tasha Steelz at PWU Studio Session. Most recently, on November 15 at BST Wrestling's United By One event, Martinez teamed with Cece to secure a victory. On November 16, 2025, she received a Lifetime Achievement Award recognizing her 25 years in professional wrestling.

In-ring achievements

Championships and accomplishments

Mercedes Martinez has achieved numerous championships and accolades in professional wrestling, establishing herself as a dominant force in the women's independent scene. Her title wins span multiple promotions, highlighting her longevity and skill in singles and tag team competition.
In World Xtreme Wrestling (WXW), Martinez captured the WXW Women's Championship on three occasions between 2005 and 2009, including a notable reign from September 2005 to April 2006 that solidified her early reputation as a top contender. These victories underscored her aggressive style and ability to compete against established talent in the Northeast independent circuit.
Martinez held the WSU World Championship for a record-setting reign from March 7, 2009, to March 3, 2012, lasting 1,092 days and marking the longest singles title run in WSU history. This was her first of three overall reigns with the title. During this period, she defended the title against high-profile opponents, including former TNA Knockouts, cementing her status as WSU's flagship champion and a pillar of women's wrestling.
In Ring of Honor (ROH), Martinez won the ROH Women's World Championship on May 4, 2022, unifying it with the interim version in a significant match on AEW Dynamite, and held it for 220 days until losing it on December 10, 2022. This reign represented a career milestone, as it was her first world title in a major promotion and highlighted her transition to national television.
Martinez became the inaugural VIP Wrestling Women's Champion on January 31, 2025, defeating Maya World in the final of a tournament, holding it for 91 days until May 2, 2025. This accomplishment added to her legacy in regional promotions, showcasing her continued relevance in the evolving women's division.
She is a two-time SHIMMER Champion (2014, 2016) and one-time SHINE Champion (December 13, 2019). Martinez is also a one-time SHIMMER Tag Team Champion (2018) with Cheerleader Melissa, contributing to her recognition as a key figure in elevating tag team wrestling within SHIMMER Women Athletes. She received the Cauliflower Alley Club Women's Wrestling Award in 2017 for her contributions to the industry. Additionally, Pro Wrestling Illustrated (PWI) ranked her in the top 20 of the PWI Women's 50 multiple times, including No. 3 in 2010, No. 2 in 2011, and No. 9 in 2012, reflecting her consistent impact across promotions. Martinez was inducted into the Independent Wrestling Hall of Fame in 2024 and the Women's Wrestling Hall of Fame in 2025.

Luchas de Apuestas record

Mercedes Martinez has participated in several high-stakes Luchas de Apuestas matches throughout her career in independent women's wrestling promotions, where personal stakes such as hair or career longevity were wagered alongside championships. These matches have been instrumental in establishing her reputation as a resilient competitor willing to risk everything for victory.
One of her notable victories came on March 7, 2009, at WSU's 2nd Anniversary show, where she defeated Angel Orsini in a Title vs. Hair Bullrope Match with Alicia as special guest referee. In this stipulation, Martinez put her hair on the line against Orsini's WSU World Championship; her win not only secured the title but also spared her the humiliation of a forced haircut, marking a pivotal moment in her dominant run as champion.
Another significant triumph occurred on December 13, 2019, at SHINE 63, when Martinez defeated Allysin Kay in a SHINE Championship vs. Career match. Kay defended her title with her career at stake, but Martinez's victory claimed the championship and effectively ended Kay's immediate pursuit in the promotion under that threat, showcasing Martinez's ability to thrive in career-defining encounters.
Martinez's documented Luchas de Apuestas record stands at 2 wins and 0 losses from these key bouts between 2009 and 2019, with no major apuestas matches reported after 2019. These high-risk contests contributed substantially to her "hardcore" persona, emphasizing her toughness and commitment in an era when women's wrestling increasingly incorporated intense, personal-stakes storytelling to elevate performers.
