User: Write me a short poem about the ocean
Response: The ocean holds its breath at dawn,
a silver sheet that stretches on
beyond the reach of any eye,
where water meets the morning sky.
Beneath the surface, kingdoms sleep,
in currents slow and canyons deep,
and every wave that finds the shore
has traveled miles to knock your door.

User: Tell me a story in three sentences
Response: She found the letter in her grandmother's attic, folded into a paper crane, addressed to a man whose name she had never heard. She spent four months tracking him down, across two countries and three decades of dead ends. When she finally knocked on his door, he opened it, saw the crane in her hand, and said quietly, "She kept her promise."

User: Give me a creative writing prompt
Response: Write a story about a librarian who discovers that one specific book in the library changes its contents every time it is opened. The words are different, the story is different, but it is always about the person who opens it. One day, a stranger comes in and asks for that exact book by name.

User: Write a limerick
Response: A programmer stayed up past two,
debugging a function he knew,
he fixed the last line,
the code ran just fine,
then crashed when he said, "Are we through?"

User: Can you write a metaphor about time?
Response: Time is a river that flows in only one direction, and we are all standing in it up to our waists. The water pushes past us whether we move or stand still. We cannot swim upstream. We cannot stop the current. We can only choose what we do with the stretch of river that flows through our hands right now. Some people try to hold the water. Others let it flow and build something on the bank.

User: Write something inspiring
Response: You are not behind. There is no schedule. There is no cosmic timeline that says you should have figured things out by twenty-five or achieved your dreams by thirty or found your purpose by forty. Some people bloom in spring. Some bloom in winter. Some bloom at midnight when nobody is watching. The only thing that matters is that you keep growing, keep reaching toward the light, keep putting one root deeper into the soil. Your season will come. And when it does, it will have been worth the wait.

User: Make up a fictional character
Response: Her name is Dara Okafor. She is thirty-one years old, a former marine biologist who quit academia to open a bookshop in a small coastal town in Oregon. The shop is called Salt and Stories. She has a scar on her left forearm from a sea urchin encounter during her PhD fieldwork and a tattoo of a whale skeleton on her right shoulder blade. She reads two books a week, makes the best pour-over coffee on the coast, and has a rescue greyhound named Sonar who sleeps behind the counter. She left science because she realized she wanted to share stories about the ocean with everyone, not just publish them in journals that twelve people would read.
