Samsung Biologics

 Samsung Biologics is a leading global contract development and manufacturing organization (CDMO) specializing in biopharmaceuticals, providing fully integrated end-to-end services from early-stage development and process optimization to large-scale commercial manufacturing and biosafety testing. Founded in April 2011 as a subsidiary of the Samsung Group and headquartered in Incheon, South Korea, the company has rapidly expanded to become the world's largest biomanufacturing facility by capacity, reaching 784,000 liters across five plants by April 2025.
With over 4,900 employees and serving more than 130 global clients, Samsung Biologics has secured regulatory approvals from major authorities, including 48 from the U.S. FDA and 45 from the EMA as of 2025, enabling it to produce complex biologics such as monoclonal antibodies, biosimilars, and antibody-drug conjugates. The company has earned CDMO Leadership Awards for 12 consecutive years and achieved notable milestones, including its initial public offering on the Korea Exchange in 2016 and the full acquisition of Samsung Bioepisa joint venture focused on biosimilars, excelling in oncology and immunologyin 2022, which it spun off as a pure-play CDMO in November 2025.
Samsung Biologics has forged strategic partnerships with top pharmaceutical firms, including Bristol Myers Squibb in 2014 for drug product manufacturing, Moderna in 2021 for COVID-19 vaccine production, and Pfizer in 2023 for ongoing manufacturing services, contributing to over $2.2 billion in contract orders secured in 2025 alone. These collaborations, numbering 16 with leading pharma companies, underscore its role in accelerating biopharmaceutical innovation and supply chain reliability amid growing global demand. The company's commitment to sustainability is highlighted by its Platinum rating from EcoVadis in 2024 and multiple ISO certifications, including ISO 37301 for compliance management in November 2024.

Company Overview

Founding and Operations

Samsung Biologics was established on April 22, 2011, by the Samsung Group to advance biopharmaceutical manufacturing capabilities within the conglomerate's diversification strategy. The company quickly positioned itself as a contract development and manufacturing organization (CDMO), specializing in biologics production for global biopharma clients. Its core operations encompass end-to-end services, including cell line development, process and analytical development, clinical-scale manufacturing, and large-scale commercial production of drug substances and products, all conducted in cGMP-compliant facilities.
As of 2025, Samsung Biologics employs more than 5,000 people worldwide, reflecting significant growth from approximately 4,300 in 2023 driven by expanded operations and new hires in technical and support roles. The company serves over 130 clients, including 17 of the top 20 global pharmaceutical firms, establishing itself as a preferred partner for complex biologics outsourcing.
Samsung Biologics maintains a leading position among global CDMOs, bolstered by a total biomanufacturing capacity of 784,000 liters achieved after Plant 5 became operational in April 2025. In November 2025, the company executed a strategic spin-off, separating its investment and subsidiary management unit into Samsung Epis Holdings to streamline focus on pure-play CDMO activities and enhance operational efficiency. Financially, the third quarter of 2025 marked strong performance with consolidated revenue of KRW 1,660.2 billion and operating profit of KRW 728.8 billion, underscoring robust demand for its services.

Services and Capabilities

Samsung Biologics provides a comprehensive suite of contract development and manufacturing organization (CDMO) services, encompassing end-to-end support from cell line development through to commercial fill-finish operations. These services include cell line development and process optimization to enhance yield and scalability, followed by clinical and commercial manufacturing under current good manufacturing practice (cGMP) standards. Analytical testing ensures product quality and regulatory compliance, while aseptic fill-finish operations handle formulation, filling, inspection, labeling, and packaging for both clinical and commercial scales.
The company specializes in manufacturing complex biologics, including monoclonal antibodies, bispecific antibodies, antibody-drug conjugates (ADCs), and viral vectors such as adeno-associated viruses (AAV) for cell and gene therapies. For ADCs, Samsung Biologics offers integrated services covering antibody production, linker-payload conjugation, and purification, supported by advanced analytical methods for payload distribution and stability assessment. These capabilities are bolstered by a dedicated ADC facility equipped with single-use and stainless-steel reactors up to 500 liters, enabling high-potency handling at occupational exposure limits (OEL) of 5 ng/m. Viral vector services focus on scalable production for gene therapy applications, integrated within their multi-modal manufacturing framework.
With the operational launch of Plant 5 in April 2025, Samsung Biologics' total biomanufacturing capacity reaches 784,000 liters, utilizing a mix of flexible single-use bioreactors (from 1,000 to 15,000 liters) and stainless-steel systems to accommodate diverse client needs and scales. This hybrid approach allows for rapid process adaptation and reduced contamination risks in single-use setups, alongside the robustness of stainless-steel for high-volume production.
To support clients, Samsung Biologics integrates digital twin technology, which creates virtual replicas of bioprocesses using computational fluid dynamics, mechanistic modeling, and data analytics for real-time simulation and optimization. Introduced in late 2024, this tool enhances transparency in process monitoring, streamlines technology transfers by predicting scale-up challenges, and improves overall efficiency, reducing development timelines.
In 2025, Samsung Biologics enhanced its ADC offerings through an extended collaboration with LigaChem Biosciences, providing dedicated development and manufacturing support for multiple ADC programs at the new facility to accelerate oncology drug pipelines.

History

Establishment and Early Development

Samsung Biologics was established on April 22, 2011, as a joint venture of Samsung Group affiliates holding 90% and Quintiles holding 10%, becoming a wholly owned subsidiary of the Samsung Group in April 2016 following Quintiles' divestment, marking the conglomerate's strategic entry into the biopharmaceutical sector to capitalize on the growing global demand for biologics manufacturing. This move was part of Samsung's broader diversification efforts beyond consumer electronics, leveraging the group's established engineering and manufacturing prowess to address the complex needs of biopharma production. The company was headquartered in Songdo, Incheon, South Korea, where an initial research and development center was set up to support early process development activities.
In its formative phase, Samsung Biologics secured initial capital infusions from Samsung affiliates to fund foundational infrastructure, including the groundbreaking for its first manufacturing plant in May 2011, which aimed to achieve a 30,000-liter capacity for drug substance production. This investment reflected the group's ambition to build a contract development and manufacturing organization (CDMO) capable of competing internationally, with early partnerships such as the February 2011 collaboration with Quintiles for biosimilars development underscoring the focus on rapid capability building. By July 2012, mechanical completion of Plant 1 was achieved, just 25 months after groundbreaking, demonstrating efficient project execution.
Prior to full operational readiness in 2015, Samsung Biologics faced challenges in assembling specialized expertise, recruiting talent from established global biopharma firms to bridge knowledge gaps in biologics processes. The company adapted Samsung's precision manufacturing methodologies from semiconductors to biopharma, fostering an engineering-driven approach to scale production while navigating regulatory hurdles for current good manufacturing practices (cGMP) compliance. These efforts culminated in Plant 1 reaching drug substance cGMP readiness by June 2013 and drug product readiness by August 2013, setting the stage for future expansions.

Major Milestones and Expansions

Samsung Biologics achieved significant early growth with the completion of its first manufacturing facility in 2013, when Plant 1 reached drug substance current good manufacturing practice (cGMP) readiness with a capacity of 30,000 liters, followed by drug product cGMP readiness later that year. In 2014, the company secured its first commercial manufacturing partnership for drug product services with Bristol-Myers Squibb, marking its entry into global biopharmaceutical production. Plant 1 received U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) approval in November 2015, enabling commercial operations, and the facility earned European Medicines Agency (EMA) approval in July 2016. That same year, in November, Samsung Biologics went public with an initial public offering on the Korea Exchange (KOSPI), raising capital to support further expansion.
From 2017 to 2020, the company expanded its infrastructure and global presence. Plant 2 achieved drug substance cGMP readiness in February 2016 with a capacity of 180,000 liters and received FDA approval in September 2017, followed by EMA approval in December 2017. In April 2020, Samsung Biologics partnered with Vir Biotechnology for manufacturing a COVID-19 treatment, highlighting its role in pandemic response efforts. The company opened its San Francisco R&D Center in October 2020 to enhance innovation and client support in the United States. Plant 3 reached cGMP readiness in October 2018 with a capacity of 180,000 liters, further boosting production scale.
Between 2021 and 2023, Samsung Biologics pursued strategic investments and facility advancements. In June 2021, the company published its first annual sustainability report, outlining commitments to environmental, social, and governance practices over the next decade. In 2021, Samsung Biologics, along with Samsung Bioepis and Samsung C&T, established the Samsung Life Science Fund, a KRW 240 billion venture initiative to invest in promising biotech technologies. Plant 4 groundbreaking occurred in November 2020, and the facility achieved cGMP readiness in June 2023 with a capacity of 240,000 liters. In April 2022, Samsung Biologics completed its full acquisition of Samsung Bioepis for approximately $2.3 billion, integrating biosimilar development capabilities. The company opened a sales office in New Jersey in March 2023 to strengthen North American operations.
In 2024 and 2025, Samsung Biologics accelerated its growth through major contracts and infrastructure projects. In October 2024, the company signed a $1.24 billion manufacturing agreement with an Asia-based pharmaceutical firm, contributing to a total contract value exceeding $3.3 billion for the year. In November 2024, Samsung Bioepis received European Commission approval for Opuviz, a biosimilar to aflibercept (marketed as Eylea), expanding its portfolio in ophthalmology treatments. Plant 5, part of Bio Campus II, became operational in April 2025, adding 180,000 liters of capacity. In October 2025, Samsung Biologics introduced the ExellenS manufacturing framework to optimize production efficiency. Culminating these efforts, in November 2025, the company completed the spin-off of Samsung Bioepis, transitioning to a pure-play contract development and manufacturing organization (CDMO) focused solely on biopharmaceutical services.

Facilities and Infrastructure

Manufacturing Plants

Samsung Biologics operates its manufacturing plants at the Bio Campus in Incheon, South Korea, comprising five facilities that collectively provide a total biomanufacturing capacity of 784,000 liters as of 2025. These plants are designed for the production of biologics, including monoclonal antibodies, with scalable bioreactor systems ranging from clinical to commercial scales.
Plant 1, operational since 2015, features a capacity of 30,000 liters using six 5,000-liter stainless-steel bioreactors and focuses on early-stage clinical production to support process development and small-batch manufacturing. Plant 2, completed in 2017, expands capacity to 154,000 liters with ten 15,000-liter bioreactors and four 1,000-liter units, enabling commercial-scale production of monoclonal antibodies and larger-volume drug substances.
Plant 3, which became cGMP-ready in 2018, offers 180,000 liters of capacity through twelve 15,000-liter bioreactors and incorporates single-use systems alongside stainless-steel options to provide flexibility in process customization and reduce cross-contamination risks. Plant 4, fully operational by mid-2023, is the world's largest single biomanufacturing facility at 240,000 liters, utilizing stainless steel bioreactors with N-1 perfusion optimized for high-titer processes that achieve elevated protein yields while maintaining product quality.
The newest facility, Plant 5, commenced operations in April 2025 with a capacity of 180,000 liters across twelve 15,000-liter bioreactors (utilizing the company's single-use and stainless steel options), supporting advanced modalities such as antibody-drug conjugates (ADCs) and viral vectors through integrated process optimization.
All plants adhere to current good manufacturing practices (cGMP) standards, ensuring compliance with regulatory requirements from agencies like the FDA and EMA, and incorporate advanced automation systems to enhance operational efficiency, reduce manual interventions, and accelerate validation timelines. In line with sustainability goals, Samsung Biologics achieved a 24% reduction in greenhouse gas emissions across its facilities by 2024 compared to the prior year, through energy-efficient designs and renewable energy integration.

Global Offices and Expansion

Samsung Biologics has strategically expanded its international footprint to enhance client support and operational efficiency worldwide. In the United States, the company opened its first overseas facility, an R&D center in San Francisco, California, in October 2020 to bolster contract development organization (CDO) services and foster collaborations with West Coast biotech firms. This was followed by the establishment of a sales office in Ridgefield Park, New Jersey, in March 2023, aimed at improving East Coast client engagement, regulatory affairs support, and proximity to major pharmaceutical hubs. Additionally, a Boston, Massachusetts, office was set up to facilitate direct interactions with clients in the region's dense biotech ecosystem.
Beyond North America, Samsung Biologics launched a representative office in Tokyo, Japan, in September 2025 to serve the Asia-Pacific market, enabling better access to regional partners and addressing growing demand in the area. While no dedicated physical office exists in Europe, the company's U.S. operations provide comprehensive support for European clients, including navigation of European Medicines Agency (EMA) requirements.
The offices fulfill a critical strategic function by streamlining technology transfers, aiding clinical trial logistics, and nurturing partnerships across borders. Each location is staffed with local regulatory experts to ensure compliance with key authorities such as the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) and EMA, thereby accelerating project timelines for international clients. In 2025, following the activation of Plant 5, Samsung Biologics bolstered its U.S. teams to manage surging global demand, particularly for antibody-drug conjugates (ADCs), while integrating support for enhanced manufacturing capabilities.

Partnerships and Collaborations

Key Biopharma Partnerships

Samsung Biologics has established numerous strategic partnerships with leading biopharmaceutical companies, focusing on contract development and manufacturing organization (CDMO) services for biologics, biosimilars, vaccines, and advanced therapies. These collaborations leverage Samsung Biologics' expertise in large-scale production to support global supply chains and accelerate drug development. By 2024, the company had secured contracts with 17 of the world's top 20 pharmaceutical companies, demonstrating its position as a preferred partner in the biopharma sector. This figure remained at 17 as of November 2025.
A key partnership is with Pfizer, involving multiple long-term manufacturing agreements for biosimilars targeting oncology and inflammatory diseases. In June 2023, Samsung Biologics signed a $411 million deal to produce Pfizer's multi-product biosimilars portfolio at its Songdo facility in South Korea. Shortly thereafter, in July 2023, the companies expanded their collaboration through two additional agreements valued at a combined $897 million for ongoing biosimilar production. These deals underscore Samsung Biologics' role in supporting Pfizer's biosimilar expansion.
GlaxoSmithKline (GSK) represents another cornerstone partnership, centered on vaccine and monoclonal antibody manufacturing. In May 2020, Samsung Biologics entered an $231 million, eight-year contract with GSK to produce biologic products, including the lupus treatment Benlysta (belimumab). This was followed by a $296 million expansion in October 2022 for large-scale production of an undisclosed antibody, further strengthening their alliance in immunology and infectious diseases.
Samsung Biologics has also partnered with other major players, including Moderna for mRNA-based therapies, Eli Lilly for COVID-19 antibody production, AstraZeneca for monoclonal antibodies, and Bristol Myers Squibb for oncology drug substance manufacturing. The Moderna agreement, announced in May 2021, involved commercial fill-finish services for the mRNA-1273 COVID-19 vaccine at Samsung Biologics' facilities, aiding global distribution starting in the second half of 2021. With Eli Lilly, a 2020 strategic pact focused on scaling up supply for COVID-19 therapeutics. AstraZeneca collaborations have included antibody production, while Bristol Myers Squibb's partnership, initially established in 2013 and expanded in September 2023, covers commercial-scale manufacturing of cancer antibodies.
Recent developments highlight Samsung Biologics' growing emphasis on high-value deals and innovative modalities. In October 2024, the company secured its largest-ever contract worth $1.2 billion with an undisclosed Asia-based pharmaceutical firm for long-term drug substance manufacturing through 2037, bringing the year's total deal value to over $3.3 billion. In 2025, Samsung Biologics continued securing major contracts, including a $1.4 billion agreement with an undisclosed European pharmaceutical company in January, a $514 million deal with a US-based firm in April (running until 2031), and a $1.29 billion contract with another US pharma in September, pushing the 2025 total to over $3.6 billion. In October 2023, Samsung Biologics formed a multi-year strategic alliance with Kurma Partners, Europe's leading life sciences venture capital firm, to provide chemistry, manufacturing, and control (CMC) development services for biologics in Kurma's portfolio companies, streamlining the path from gene to investigational new drug (IND). A January 2024 collaboration with Flagship Pioneering aims to advance translational technologies in biopharmaceutical innovation, including AI-driven protein design through investments in Flagship's portfolio. Most recently, in January 2025, Samsung Biologics extended its partnership with LigaChem Biosciences to support multiple antibody-drug conjugate (ADC) programs at a dedicated ADC facility, building on a 2024 initial agreement for ADC development and manufacturing.
These partnerships have yielded significant outcomes, with Samsung Biologics serving over 130 clients as of 2025 and contributing to key regulatory approvals. Notably, the company supported the development and manufacturing leading to the FDA approval of Opuviz (aflibercept-yszy), an interchangeable biosimilar to Eylea, in May 2024 for treating wet age-related macular degeneration and other retinal conditions; European Commission approval followed in November 2024.

Investment and Venture Initiatives

Samsung Biologics has pursued strategic investments and joint ventures to bolster its position in the biopharmaceutical ecosystem, focusing on biosimilars, innovative therapeutics, and emerging biotechnologies. A key initiative was the establishment of Samsung Bioepis in 2012 as a 50-50 joint venture with Biogen Inc., aimed at developing and commercializing biosimilars. This partnership leveraged Samsung Biologics' manufacturing expertise and Biogen's commercialization capabilities to target global markets for affordable biologic alternatives, with Samsung Bioepis excelling in key therapeutic areas such as oncology and immunology. In 2022, Samsung Biologics acquired Biogen's entire equity stake in Samsung Bioepis for an aggregate consideration of up to $2.3 billion, making it a wholly owned subsidiary and enhancing operational autonomy for biosimilar development and sales growth.
To further drive innovation, Samsung Biologics co-launched the Samsung Life Science Fund in 2021 with affiliates including Samsung C&T Corporation and Samsung Bioepis, committing KRW 240 billion (approximately $180 million) to invest in promising biotech ventures. The fund targets technologies in areas such as AI-driven drug discovery, gene editing, and diagnostics to accelerate pipeline advancements and secure future contract development and manufacturing organization (CDMO) opportunities. As of 2025, the fund's portfolio includes investments in Generate: Biomedicines for AI-enabled protein therapeutics (December 2024), C2N Diagnostics for neurodegenerative disease biomarkers ($10 million, March 2025), and Arbor Biotechnologies for next-generation genetic medicines (October 2025).
In a significant restructuring move completed on November 3, 2025, Samsung Biologics spun off its investment and subsidiary management business unit, including Samsung Bioepis, into a new entity named Samsung Epis Holdings. This spin-off, approved by shareholders in October 2025, allows Samsung Biologics to streamline operations as a pure-play CDMO while enabling Samsung Epis Holdings to independently manage venture investments and biosimilar activities, with the holding company set to list on the Korea Exchange on November 24, 2025. The initiative supports broader objectives of fostering biotech ecosystem growth, mitigating risks through diversified stakes in emerging companies, and positioning Samsung Biologics to capture long-term manufacturing contracts from its investment partners.

Technology and Platforms

Development and Manufacturing Platforms

Samsung Biologics employs proprietary technological platforms to accelerate biologics research and development (R&D) while optimizing manufacturing processes for high efficiency and yield. These platforms address key challenges in cell line engineering, antibody design, and candidate selection, enabling faster progression from discovery to clinical production.
The S-CHOice platform, introduced in 2020, is a Chinese hamster ovary (CHO) cell line development system engineered for superior protein expression yields. It supports rapid cell division cycles of 18-20 hours and, when integrated with technologies like the Berkeley Lights Beacon system, completes cell line development in three monthsreducing timelines by approximately 30% relative to conventional approaches. This enables high-titer production suitable for scalable manufacturing of therapeutic proteins.
Launched in 2022, the S-DUAL platform facilitates bispecific antibody development through an innovative asymmetrical scaffold that achieves a 99% heavy-light chain pairing success rate. By minimizing mispairing impurities and enhancing binding affinity, it reduces chemistry, manufacturing, and controls (CMC) risks while supporting high-purity, high-yield expression for dual-targeting therapies.
The DEVELOPICK platform functions as an integrated assessment tool for selecting optimal cell lines and processes via high-throughput developability screening. Utilizing just 20 mg of material, it evaluates candidates for issues like stability or aggregation within one month, prioritizing viable molecules early to streamline progression toward investigational new drug (IND) and biologics license application (BLA) stages.
These platforms have been applied in numerous client projects, supporting modalities such as monoclonal antibodies (mAbs), antibody-drug conjugates (ADCs), and next-generation formats including bispecific antibodies. For example, S-CHOice underpins mAb and ADC cell line optimization, S-DUAL enables bispecific engineering, and DEVELOPICK informs cross-modality selections to mitigate development risks.
Integration with digital tools further enhances these platforms' effectiveness, incorporating in silico predictive modeling and proprietary algorithms to forecast molecular liabilities like deamidation or aggregation. This hybrid approach combines computational simulations with wet-lab validation, improving process predictability and efficiency across R&D phases.

Innovations and Frameworks

Samsung Biologics introduced ExellenS in October 2025 as a comprehensive manufacturing framework designed to optimize biologics production through integrated plant design, automation, and sustainability measures. This framework standardizes facility designs, equipment, and processes across all manufacturing sites to ensure operational equivalency and accelerate project timelines. By harmonizing systems and digital processes, ExellenS enhances stability, reduces variability in production, and supports efficient global execution of biopharmaceutical projects.
In 2024, Samsung Biologics advanced its use of digital-twin technology for bioprocess simulation and optimization, creating virtual models that mirror physical manufacturing environments to predict outcomes and streamline client collaborations. These digital twins integrate computational fluid dynamics, mechanistic simulations, and real-time data to enable predictive modeling of cell growth, nutrient dynamics, and bioreactor conditions, thereby reducing technology transfer times and de-risking scale-up processes. The technology facilitates virtual testing and iterative improvements, allowing for faster validation and enhanced collaboration with partners by simulating complex bioprocesses before physical implementation.
Building on these efforts, Samsung Biologics enhanced its antibody-drug conjugate (ADC) facility in 2025, establishing a dedicated site for end-to-end ADC development, manufacturing, and conjugation services to address the growing demand for targeted therapies. This enhancement includes expanded capabilities for antibody production and payload integration, with plans for further growth into ADC drug product development. Additionally, the company integrated artificial intelligence (AI) tools for predictive analytics in bioprocessing, leveraging AI alongside digital twins to analyze real-time data, forecast process deviations, and optimize yields for complex biologics. These AI applications improve decision-making in quality control and compliance, adapting to regulatory changes while minimizing production risks.
These innovations contribute to broader benefits, including a 24% reduction in greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions by the end of 2024 compared to 2023, achieved through energy-efficient designs and renewable energy adoption embedded in frameworks like ExellenS. They also enable scalable production for advanced modalities such as ADCs and next-generation biologics, supporting higher throughput and flexibility in meeting global biopharma needs.

Sustainability and ESG

Environmental and Sustainability Efforts

Samsung Biologics has prioritized environmental sustainability through targeted reductions in greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions, achieving a 24% decrease in Scope 1 and 2 emissions by 2024 compared to the baseline year. This progress supports the company's net-zero emissions goal by 2050, aligned with Samsung Electronics' New Environmental Strategy announced in 2022, which emphasizes decarbonization across operations and supply chains. The firm has implemented a multi-scope roadmap, including renewable energy adoption at 28.6% and plans to reduce Scope 1 and 2 emissions by 32% and Scope 3 by 36% by 2030.
The company has earned several prestigious sustainability certifications, reflecting its environmental leadership. Samsung Biologics was included in the Dow Jones Sustainability World Index starting in 2021, recognizing its top 10% performance among over 2,500 global companies in ESG criteria. In 2022, it received the Terra Carta Seal from the Sustainable Markets Initiative for commitments to a climate-positive future and net-zero emissions. Additionally, it achieved a CDP Leadership A- rating for climate action in 2024 and an EcoVadis Platinum ratingthe highest levelin 2024, placing it in the top 1% of assessed companies for sustainable practices.
Key initiatives include the publication of its first ESG report in 2021, with annual reports since then to transparently disclose environmental impacts and strategies. Samsung Biologics has focused on resource efficiency, achieving a 22% water reuse rate through advanced recycling systems and wastewater treatment. Energy management efforts incorporate efficient bioreactors and automation to minimize consumption, contributing to an 89% waste recycling rate. The 2024-2025 ESG plan emphasizes decarbonization, including supplier engagement for Scope 3 reductions and scaling renewable energy via power purchase agreements. Following the November 2025 spin-off of Samsung Bioepis, Samsung Biologics continues to align with Samsung Group's ESG frameworks while pursuing independent net-zero commitments tailored to its CDMO operations.
In plant infrastructure, Samsung Biologics integrates sustainable designs, notably in Plant 5, operational since 2025, which uses low-carbon green concrete over 15,000 square meters to cut emissions by 50% and incorporates 20% renewable energy across operations for a reduced carbon footprint. These measures align with broader goals for eco-friendly biomanufacturing while maintaining high standards in biologics production.

Social Responsibility and Governance Integration

Samsung Biologics maintains a diverse workforce exceeding 4,900 employees, with women comprising 40% of its staff, and has been recognized as a "Best Family-Friendly Management" company for nine consecutive years by South Korea's Ministry of Gender Equality and Family Affairs. The company implements comprehensive training programs, including annual sessions for core talent across occupational fields, VR-based hands-on biomanufacturing simulations, and career mentoring initiatives like the Dream Class for underprivileged students. In community engagement, particularly in Incheon where its headquarters and plants are located, Samsung Biologics supports local health initiatives through employee hospital volunteering and a 2024 charity program partnering with nearby restaurants to donate 5-15% of proceeds to children needing long-term medical or educational aid. For ethical supply chain management, the company joined the Pharmaceutical Supply Chain Initiative (PSCI) in 2024 as a supplier partner, committing to principles in ethics, labor, health and safety, environment, and management systems, while conducting annual ESG assessments of suppliers.
ESG principles are integrated into Samsung Biologics' governance through its annual Corporate Governance Reports, published since 2021, which detail board oversight of sustainability strategies and link social performance metrics, such as diversity and community impact, to decision-making processes. The company aligns with global standards, including the UN Global Compact principles joined in 2023 and contributions to UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) like good health and well-being (SDG 3) and decent work and economic growth (SDG 8), as outlined in its ESG reports adhering to GRI Standards and ISO 26000.
In 2025, following the November spin-off of its biosimilars subsidiary Samsung Bioepis to focus as a pure-play CDMO, Samsung Biologics enhanced its diversity goals by intensifying women-in-STEM recruitment and inclusive training to support CDMO advancements, including antibody-drug conjugates (ADCs). Stakeholder engagement occurs annually through ESG forums and conferences like BIO International 2025, emphasizing social impacts of ADC and biotech innovations for global health access. Achievements include a Platinum rating from EcoVadis in 2024the highest level, placing it in the top 1% globally for social and ethical practices.

Corporate Governance

Board Structure and Directors

Samsung Biologics' Board of Directors consists of seven members, comprising three internal directors and four outside directors, designed to provide balanced oversight and independent perspectives on strategic decisions. The board operates through specialized committees, including those focused on audit, compensation, and strategy, to ensure robust governance in the company's contract development and manufacturing organization (CDMO) operations.
Among the internal directors, John Rim serves as Chairman, Chief Executive Officer, and President, bringing extensive leadership experience in biopharmaceuticals since his appointment in 2020. Seung-Ho Ryu, appointed as a director in 2025, holds the positions of Chief Financial Officer and Executive Vice President, contributing expertise in financial strategy and operations.
The outside directors are independent experts who enhance objectivity; for instance, Ho-Seung Lee was newly appointed in 2025 to bolster committee oversight. These directors play key roles in the audit, compensation, and strategy committees, aligning board activities with regulatory and ethical standards in the biologics sector.
In 2025, following the completion of a corporate spin-off effective November 1 that separated the investment and subsidiary management unit to form Samsung Epis Holdings, the board underwent adjustments to prioritize CDMO-focused governance and operational efficiency. As of November 1, 2025, Samsung Biologics received an ISS Governance QualityScore of 4, reflecting strong performance in audit (score of 1) while identifying areas for improvement in board structure (score of 5).
The company's shareholder structure underscores its ties to the Samsung Group, with Samsung C&T holding 43.1%, Samsung Electronics owning 31.2%, and other investors comprising the remaining 25.7% as of the latest available data.

Executive Leadership and Management

Samsung Biologics' executive leadership is headed by President and CEO John Rim, who has served in the role since December 2020 and also chairs the board of directors. Rim brings over 30 years of experience in the biopharmaceutical industry, having previously held senior positions at Genentech and Roche, where he contributed to global commercialization strategies, as well as at Astellas Pharma. He holds a BA from Columbia University, an MA from Stanford University, and an MBA from Northwestern University's Kellogg School of Management. Under Rim's leadership, the company has expanded its contract development and manufacturing organization (CDMO) capabilities, emphasizing innovation in biomanufacturing to enhance global access to biomedicines.
The executive team comprises several Executive Vice Presidents (EVPs) overseeing critical functions such as operations, finance, research and development, and human resources. Key among them is Seung Ho Ryu, EVP and Chief Financial Officer (CFO), appointed to the board in March 2025, with over 25 years at Samsung Group, including roles in finance at Samsung Electronics. Kun Lo, Ph.D., another board member and EVP serving as Head of Engineering, Procurement, Construction, Validation (EPCV) and Chief Strategy Officer (CSO), joined in 2020 after 28 years with Samsung, holding a Ph.D. in Chemical Engineering from Seoul National University; he drives strategic initiatives in facility expansion and process optimization.
Other prominent executives include Kevin Sharp, EVP and Head of Sales & Operations, who joined in 2017 with 24 years of biopharma sales experience from GlaxoSmithKline (GSK) and holds an MBA from Temple University; James Choi, EVP and Chief Marketing Officer (CMO), with 35 years in healthcare leadership, including as CIO at Beckman Coulter; and Hosung Min, Ph.D., EVP and Head of Cell and Gene Therapy Development Organization (CDO) & Bio R&D, an antibody expert formerly at Amgen, with a Ph.D. from UCLA. These leaders report to the CEO and collaborate on integrating Samsung's technological prowess with biopharma demands, focusing on end-to-end CDMO services from development to commercialization.
The management structure emphasizes cross-functional expertise, with additional EVPs like Diane Black, Ph.D., Chief Quality Officer (CQO) with over 30 years in quality assurance from CSL Behring; Kyuho Lee, Chief Human Resources Officer (CHRO) with 31 years in HR at Samsung; and Pierre Catignol, Head of Operations, drawing from prior roles at Sanofi-Pasteur and Lonza. This team supports the company's growth, evidenced by expansions in manufacturing capacity and partnerships, while aligning with Samsung Group's broader governance principles.
