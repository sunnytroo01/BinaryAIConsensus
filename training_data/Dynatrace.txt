Dynatrace
 Ara Eve Leo Sal 
1x
 Dynatrace, Inc. is an American multinational software technology company that develops and provides an AI-powered unified observability platform designed to monitor, analyze, and secure complex cloud-native applications and digital ecosystems. Founded in 2005 in Linz, Austria, as dynaTrace Software GmbH by Bernd Greifeneder, the company pioneered distributed tracing technology with its PurePath innovation in 2006, enabling code-level analysis of application performance. It was acquired by Compuware Corporation in 2011, rebranded as Dynatrace in 2014 following Thoma Bravo's purchase of Compuware's application performance management business, and went public on the New York Stock Exchange in 2019 under the ticker symbol DT.
The Dynatrace platform integrates observability, automation, and security capabilities into a single SaaS solution, supporting IT operations, development, security teams, and business analytics across infrastructure, applications, digital experiences, and logs. Key features include AI-driven root cause analysis, AIOps for automated remediation, and application security to detect threats in real-time, helping organizations transform IT complexity into actionable business insights. As of September 2025, Dynatrace serves over 4,000 customers worldwide, including many Fortune 500 enterprises, with annual recurring revenue of $1.9 billion and a workforce of more than 5,000 employees across 60 locations.
Dynatrace has earned consistent industry recognition, including being positioned as a Leader in the Gartner Magic Quadrant for Observability Platforms for 15 consecutive years as of 2025, underscoring its role in enabling faster innovation and operational efficiency in the AI era. Headquartered in Boston, Massachusetts, the company continues to expand its cloud-focused offerings, emphasizing explainable AI to automate cloud operations and ensure secure digital interactions.

Overview

Founding and headquarters

Dynatrace was founded in 2005 in Linz, Austria, originally under the name dynaTrace Software GmbH by Bernd Greifeneder and his team of engineers. The company emerged as a startup dedicated to developing innovative solutions for application performance management, addressing the critical need for real-time monitoring and optimization in complex enterprise software environments. This foundational focus on performance diagnostics laid the groundwork for Dynatrace's evolution into a leader in observability and security platforms.
Initially headquartered in Linz, where much of its early innovation took place, Dynatrace has since expanded its global footprint significantly. In 2025, the company relocated its corporate headquarters to Boston, Massachusetts, United States, at Atlantic Wharf (280 Congress Street), marking a strategic move to a central hub for technology and business in North America. Despite this shift, Linz remains a key center for research and development, hosting substantial engineering teams that continue to drive technological advancements. The company also maintains major offices in other Austrian cities, including Vienna, Graz, Klagenfurt, and Innsbruck.
Dynatrace now operates 67 offices across 38 countries, spanning North America, Europe, the Middle East and Africa, Asia Pacific, and Latin America, enabling it to serve a diverse international customer base. As of fiscal year 2025, the company employs approximately 5,200 people worldwide, with a workforce heavily weighted toward engineering, product development, and sales to support its expansive operations and innovation pipeline.

Leadership and governance

Dynatrace's leadership team is led by Chief Executive Officer Rick McConnell, who has served in the role since December 2021. McConnell brings over 30 years of experience in scaling multibillion-dollar software companies, having previously held senior executive positions at Akamai Technologies, where he served as President and General Manager of the Security Technology Group, and at Cisco Systems.
As Chief Technology Officer and co-founder, Bernd Greifeneder has been instrumental in shaping the company's technical direction since its inception in 2005. Greifeneder oversees the global product vision, platform development, and innovations in AI-driven observability, holding over 20 patents including key technologies like PurePath and Smartscape.
The financial strategy is managed by Chief Financial Officer Jim Benson, who joined Dynatrace in November 2022. Benson, with more than 30 years in technology finance, previously served as Executive Vice President and CFO at Akamai Technologies.
Jill Ward has chaired the Board of Directors since January 2021, after joining the board in September 2019. Ward's expertise in cybersecurity stems from prior board roles at Symantec Corporation from 2016 to 2019 and Carbon Black, complemented by executive experience at companies like Fleetmatics and Intuit.
Dynatrace maintains a robust governance structure as a New York Stock Exchange-listed company under the ticker DT, with a focus on environmental, social, and governance (ESG) reporting through annual sustainability reports that emphasize compliance, risk management, and ethical practices. The board and management oversee ESG initiatives via a cross-functional steering committee, ensuring alignment with global standards.

Products and platform

Core observability capabilities

Dynatrace's unified observability platform, introduced following the company's 2014 spin-off from Compuware, integrates full-stack monitoring capabilities across applications, infrastructure, and cloud services to provide comprehensive visibility into IT environments. This platform enables organizations to track performance, detect anomalies, and correlate data from diverse sources in real time, supporting the management of complex, dynamic systems.
At the core of the platform is OneAgent, a single agent that automatically instruments and collects telemetry data from hosts, processes, services, and cloud-native environments without requiring manual configuration. OneAgent discovers running processes, injects monitoring code, and gathers metrics such as CPU usage (collected every 10 seconds for hosts, containers, and Kubernetes pods, with data aggregated into 1-minute intervals for metric reporting, events, and alerting, though entity-based anomaly detection uses the 10-second granularity directly), network traffic, and application traces, ensuring end-to-end coverage from user interfaces to backend databases.
Complementing OneAgent, Smartscape provides dynamic topology mapping by automatically discovering and visualizing dependencies between components in the observed environment. It generates interactive graphs showing relationships across tierslike applications, services, hosts, and data centersupdated in near real-time to reflect changes and highlight impacted areas during incidents.
Data collected by these components is stored and processed in Grail, a purpose-built data lakehouse designed for observability workloads. Grail unifies logs, metrics, traces, and events in a schema-on-read format, eliminating the need for separate silos and enabling contextual queries across petabyte-scale datasets. It supports sub-second query response times through massively parallel processing, facilitating rapid analysis even as data volumes grow.
The platform extends observability to hybrid and multi-cloud setups, with native integrations for services like Amazon Web Services (AWS), Microsoft Azure, and Kubernetes clusters. For instance, it monitors Kubernetes pods, nodes, and workloads on Azure Kubernetes Service (AKS) and Elastic Kubernetes Service (EKS), capturing metrics, logs, and traces to ensure consistent visibility across on-premises, public cloud, and containerized deployments.

AI-driven features and innovations

Dynatrace's Davis AI engine employs causal AI to automate root-cause analysis and anomaly detection across complex IT environments, correlating observability data with topological context to pinpoint issues without manual intervention. Introduced in 2017 as part of the platform's core innovation, Davis uses deterministic causation algorithms to evaluate dependencies, metrics, and events in real time, reducing mean time to resolution by identifying contributing factors with explainable insights.
In fiscal 2025, Dynatrace launched Davis CoPilot, an extension of the Davis engine that integrates generative AI for natural language interaction and automated remediation workflows. Built on large language models such as Azure OpenAI, Davis CoPilot translates user queries into Dynatrace Query Language (DQL) commands, generates dashboards, and proposes remediation actions, enabling broader team access to AI-driven operations without specialized expertise. This enhances AIOps by combining causal and predictive analysis with generative capabilities for proactive issue prevention.
On the security front, Dynatrace leverages AI for runtime vulnerability management through Runtime Vulnerability Analytics, which assesses risks by analyzing execution paths, data access, and production behavior to prioritize exploitable vulnerabilities. Complementing this, AI-powered behavior analytics detect threats by monitoring deviations in user and system patterns, such as anomalous access or risky behaviors, facilitating early incident response and forensics.
Key innovations include Davis's hypermodal AI framework, which fuses predictive, causal, and generative models to forecast system dependencies and simulate impacts, enabling proactive capacity planning and disruption prevention. Additionally, integrations with large language models support advanced AIOps by providing end-to-end observability for AI applications, tracing inputs and outputs to ensure compliance and optimize model performance.
In 2025, Dynatrace introduced further enhancements, including AI-powered log analytics for instant explanations of log content and advanced pattern detection, as well as Cloud Security Posture Management (CSPM) to extend runtime security to cloud configurations. The platform also added developer observability tools, such as the Live Debugger, to provide real-time insights during code development and testing. These updates, showcased at Perform 2025, aim to accelerate innovation and strengthen security in AI-driven environments.

Open source contributions

Key projects and standards

Dynatrace engineers have played a leading role in the Cloud Native Computing Foundation (CNCF) since 2020, particularly in advancing OpenTelemetry as a standard for distributed tracing and observability. As one of the project's largest contributors, with over 63,000 contributions tracked over the past decade, the company participates in the OpenTelemetry Technical Committee, specification development, the Collector component, and language-specific implementations to promote vendor-neutral telemetry collection across cloud-native environments.
The company maintains two dedicated GitHub organizationsdynatrace-oss for projects directly developed and supported by Dynatrace, and dynatrace-oss-contrib for upstream contributions to external repositories. These organizations host a range of tools enhancing observability in modern infrastructures, including the Dynatrace Operator, an open-source Kubernetes operator that automates the lifecycle management of Dynatrace OneAgent and other monitoring components in Kubernetes and OpenShift clusters. Other notable repositories include integrations for CI/CD workflows, such as the Dynatrace GitHub Action for reporting metrics to Dynatrace environments.
Dynatrace actively supports more than 30 open-source projects to foster interoperability in observability ecosystems, with key examples including Prometheus exporter integrations for ingesting metrics from annotated Kubernetes pods and eBPF-based tools for low-overhead system insights. For instance, the company's eBPF Discovery tool, hosted under dynatrace-oss, uses extended Berkeley Packet Filter (eBPF) technology to dynamically discover HTTP endpoints on Linux hosts by analyzing incoming requests, requiring kernel version 4.20 or later. These contributions emphasize seamless data ingestion from standards like Prometheus while extending capabilities through innovative tracing mechanisms.

Community and ecosystem involvement

Dynatrace maintains active membership in the Cloud Native Computing Foundation (CNCF), having joined in 2017 as part of an expansion that brought the organization to over 100 members, including major cloud providers. Through this involvement, the company supports cloud-native technologies and participates in governance, with representatives serving on CNCF's technical oversight committee and funding key projects such as Keptn for continuous delivery observability and OpenFeature for standardized feature flagging.
Several Dynatrace employees hold CNCF Ambassador status, contributing to community leadership, mentorship, and events like KubeCon to promote cloud-native adoption. The company also engages deeply with the OpenTelemetry community, where Dynatrace personnel serve as project maintainers and approvers, helping standardize observability data formats across ecosystems.
Dynatrace fosters ecosystem integrations by developing and certifying plugins for prominent open source tools, including a dedicated data source plugin for Grafana to visualize Dynatrace metrics and logs directly in dashboards. Similar support extends to the ELK Stack through community-driven integrations that enable streaming of Dynatrace data for log analysis and visualization. These efforts connect Dynatrace's platform to over 500 third-party technologies, many open source, enhancing interoperability in DevOps workflows.
To bolster community participation, Dynatrace operates an Open Source Program Office (OSPO) that guides employee contributions to upstream projects, develops internal policies for open source usage, and promotes collaborative innovation without specified time allocations like dedicated hack hours. This structure supports ongoing commits to projects focused on automation and observability, aligning with broader DevOps practices.

History

Origins and early years

dynaTrace Software GmbH was founded in 2005 in Linz, Austria, by Bernd Greifeneder, Sok-kheng Tang, and Hubert Gerstmeyer, with a focus on developing advanced application performance management (APM) software. The company quickly innovated in the field, introducing its patented PurePath technology in 2006, which enabled full end-to-end transaction tracing from the web browser to the database, providing code-level visibility into application performance. Between 2005 and 2010, dynaTrace expanded its platform to support continuous monitoring across development, testing, and production environments, addressing the growing need for detailed diagnostics in complex software stacks.
Initially rooted in the European market, dynaTrace faced challenges in scaling to the United States, where it established a headquarters in suburban Boston to better serve North American customers. By 2011, the company had grown to approximately 180 employees and served over 500 customers worldwide, including leading enterprises such as Zappos, SAS, Macys, BBVA, and Thomson Reuters, but its trailing twelve-month revenue stood at $26 million, reflecting the hurdles of international expansion and competition in the APM space. 
In July 2011, Compuware Corporation acquired dynaTrace for $256 million in cash, integrating its PurePath technology into Compuware's broader portfolio of mainframe monitoring and APM tools to enhance end-to-end visibility for enterprise applications. This acquisition provided resources for further development while positioning dynaTrace within a larger ecosystem focused on legacy and distributed systems.
A key milestone came in 2014, when a small R&D team led by founder Bernd Greifeneder developed Ruxit as a separate SaaS offering, marking a strategic pivot toward cloud-native monitoring and simplifying deployment for modern infrastructures.

Independence and public listing

In 2014, private equity firm Thoma Bravo acquired Compuware Corporation for $2.5 billion and subsequently carved out its application performance management (APM) business unit, establishing it as an independent entity named Dynatrace. This separation allowed the APM division to operate autonomously, free from Compuware's legacy mainframe focus, with Thoma Bravo appointing John Van Siclen as CEO to lead the transition. The carve-out included merging the business with Thoma Bravo's portfolio company Keynote Systems, setting the stage for focused investment in cloud-native monitoring technologies.
From 2015 to 2019, the company underwent significant rebranding and platform development under Thoma Bravo's ownership, unifying its offerings into the Dynatrace SaaS platform launched in 2016. This unification integrated APM capabilities with broader observability features, emphasizing AI-driven analytics to address hybrid and multi-cloud environments, which drove customer adoption and revenue expansion. By March 31, 2019, Dynatrace achieved total annual recurring revenue (ARR) of approximately $403 million, with subscription revenue reaching $350 million, reflecting 36% year-over-year growth amid a shift away from legacy on-premises products.
Dynatrace went public on August 1, 2019, listing on the New York Stock Exchange under the ticker symbol "DT" following an initial public offering that raised $570 million through the sale of 35.6 million shares priced at $16 each. Shares debuted strongly, closing 49% above the IPO price at $23.85 and valuing the company at approximately $6.7 billion on its first trading day. The IPO provided capital for further platform enhancements and global scaling, marking a pivotal transition from private equity backing to public market accountability.
Following the IPO, Dynatrace sustained robust growth, with total ARR expanding to $1.734 billion by the end of fiscal year 2025 (March 31, 2025), representing 15% year-over-year increase driven by demand for its unified observability platform in AI and cloud ecosystems. This period saw continued investment in SaaS expansions and customer retention, evidenced by a dollar-based net expansion rate exceeding 110%, underscoring the company's maturation as a public entity.

Acquisitions and expansions

Dynatrace has pursued a strategy of strategic acquisitions to enhance its observability and security platform, particularly following its independence and public listing in 2019. These moves have focused on integrating advanced technologies for data processing, debugging, compliance, and database management, thereby expanding the company's capabilities in cloud-native environments.
In September 2021, Dynatrace acquired SpectX, a high-speed parsing and query analytics company, to accelerate the convergence of observability and security analytics. This acquisition introduced advanced parsing capabilities to handle logs, events, metrics, and traces at scale, enabling faster data querying and deeper insights into application performance and threats without requiring extensive data transformation. By integrating SpectX's technology, Dynatrace improved the efficiency of its Software Intelligence Platform in processing unstructured data, supporting real-time analytics for large-scale environments.
Dynatrace expanded its developer tools in July 2023 with the acquisition of Rookout, a cloud-native debugging platform based in Tel Aviv. Rookout's non-intrusive debugging solution allows developers to collect runtime data from production applications without redeploying code or restarting services, facilitating always-on troubleshooting in Kubernetes and other cloud environments. This integration enhances Dynatrace's platform by providing contextual code-level insights alongside observability data, reducing debugging time and improving software release velocity while maintaining security.
In January 2024, Dynatrace announced the acquisition of Runecast, a provider of AI-powered security and compliance solutions, which closed later that year. Runecast's technology delivers automated, real-time vulnerability assessments, compliance checks, and security posture management for multi-cloud and hybrid environments, including VMware and Kubernetes workloads. By incorporating Runecast, Dynatrace strengthened its cloud-native application protection platform (CNAPP), enabling proactive risk mitigation and regulatory adherence through AI-driven analytics integrated with existing observability features.
Most recently, in March 2025, Dynatrace acquired Metis, an Israeli startup specializing in AI-driven database observability. Metis's platform empowers developers and site reliability engineers to perform root-cause analysis on database performance issues, optimizing queries and reducing downtime in complex data environments. This acquisition bolsters Dynatrace's database monitoring capabilities, integrating AI-powered troubleshooting directly into its unified platform to address scalability challenges in modern applications.
