Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance
 Ara Eve Leo Sal 
1x
 Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance Co., Ltd. (SFMI) is a leading South Korean non-life insurance provider, specializing in property, casualty, fire, marine, auto, and liability coverage, as well as retirement pension plans. Established on January 26, 1952, as Anbo Fire & Marine Insurance Co., Ltd., the company was renamed Ankuk Fire & Marine Insurance Co., Ltd. in 1963 before adopting its current name on December 6, 1993, following integration into the Samsung Group. As the top-ranked non-life insurer in Korea by market share and financial strength, SFMI serves millions of customers domestically and maintains a global footprint through subsidiaries and offices in key markets.
The company's history reflects steady growth and innovation, beginning with its founding amid post-war reconstruction in South Korea. Key milestones include the launch of auto insurance in October 1983, marking a pivotal expansion into personal lines; the opening of its first overseas office in London on May 25, 1975; and the establishment of its New York branch in April 1990. In 2005, SFMI pioneered as the first foreign insurer to form a wholly owned local subsidiary in China, Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance (China) Co., Ltd., followed by further expansions such as the Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance Company of Europe Ltd. in 2011. As of 2025, under CEO Lee Mun-hwa, SFMI operates with a focus on digital transformation, risk management, and sustainability, aiming for net-zero emissions by 2050 and full renewable energy conversion by 2040 as part of its VISION 2030 strategy.
SFMI's product portfolio encompasses comprehensive solutions like the "Samsung anycar" auto insurance brand (launched in 2002) and innovative safety initiatives, such as the "Firevase" throwable extinguisher distributed to over 100,000 customers since 2018. The company has earned high credit ratings, including an 'AA-' from S&P in 2013the highest for any Korean insurerand maintains robust financial health with audited consolidated statements supporting its position as a trusted safety net for individuals and businesses.

Overview

Company Profile

Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance Co., Ltd. was founded on January 26, 1952, as Anbo Fire & Marine Insurance Co., Ltd. in Busan, South Korea. The company traces its roots to this establishment during the post-Korean War reconstruction period, initially focusing on fire and marine risks. The company acquired Ankuk Fire & Marine Insurance Co., Ltd. in January 1963 and changed its name to Ankuk Fire & Marine Insurance Co., Ltd. in March 1963. The current name, Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance Co., Ltd., was adopted on December 6, 1993, reflecting its full integration into the Samsung conglomerate.
Headquartered in Seoul, South Korea, the company operates as a key affiliate within the Samsung Group, providing non-life insurance services domestically and internationally. As of June 2025, Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance employs 5,533 people. It holds a dominant position in the South Korean market, commanding approximately 22% share in property and casualty insurance as of 2024, making it the largest non-life insurer in the country.
The company is led by CEO Mun Hwa Lee, who assumed the role in recent years, overseeing strategic initiatives in risk management and customer services. Its governance structure includes a board of directors with representatives from Samsung Group affiliates, ensuring alignment with the broader conglomerate's objectives.

Core Operations and Products

Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance specializes in non-life insurance, with its core operations centered on property and casualty (P&C) coverage, including fire, marine, auto, liability, and specialty lines. As the leading non-life insurer in South Korea, the company provides comprehensive risk management solutions tailored to individuals and businesses, emphasizing protection against property damage, transportation risks, and personal liabilities.
Key products include automobile insurance under the "Samsung anycar" brand, launched in 2002 to offer customizable coverage for vehicle owners, and its digital counterpart "my anycar," an online direct service introduced in 2009 for streamlined policy issuance and management. Marine insurance encompasses cargo and hull protection for shipping and logistics, while fire and property damage policies safeguard assets against disasters and accidents. The portfolio also features accident and health insurance for personal injury coverage, including overseas travel insurance that covers risks associated with international trips. Policyholders can extend travel insurance periods through the online direct website or app's contract management menu, with immediate processing for arrival date/time extensions. Extensions may be requested after departure subject to company-specific conditions (such as remaining period and application timing); customers should verify details via the customer center or official website. If an extension is not feasible, purchasing a separate short-term travel insurance policy for the additional period is a common alternative. Alongside these, credit and surety bonds mitigate financial risks in commercial transactions.
Service delivery innovations enhance customer accessibility and efficiency. The Midnight Express Claims Adjustment Center, established in 1989, enables 24/7 processing of claims, particularly for auto incidents, reducing response times significantly. In 2005, the company opened the Samsung Loss Control Center to provide proactive risk prevention consulting and safety assessments for clients. Digital advancements include paperless contracts and mobile e-signatures, allowing customers to complete policies electronically and minimizing environmental impact.
Products are distributed through a multi-channel network, including a large base of tied agents for personalized consultations, robust online platforms for self-service auto policies, and dedicated financial planner centersthe first of which opened in 2008 to offer holistic advisory services. Partnerships with banks and auto dealers further expand reach, integrating insurance sales into broader financial and automotive ecosystems. As of 2024, revenue breakdown reflects the dominance of auto insurance at approximately 50%, long-term insurance (including health and accident) at about 30%, and other P&C lines at around 20%, underscoring the company's balanced yet auto-centric operations.

History

Early Foundation and Mergers (1950s-1960s)

Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance originated in the aftermath of the Korean War, when Anbo Fire & Marine Insurance Co., Ltd. was established on January 26, 1952, in Busan by Koo Jin-hyun to provide essential reinsurance services amid the country's economic recovery efforts. As one of South Korea's early non-life insurance providers, it focused on fire and marine reinsurance to support rebuilding industries devastated by conflict.
In parallel, Ankuk Fire & Marine Insurance Co., Ltd. was founded in June 1956 in Seoul by shareholders from Chosun Life Insurance, under the leadership of Jang Gi-young, targeting direct property and casualty insurance operations. However, Ankuk encountered significant difficulties with sales performance shortly after its inception. In February 1958, the Samsung Group acquired the underperforming company, incorporating it into its expanding business ecosystem and providing the resources needed for stabilization.
To consolidate operations and enhance capital strength, Anbo Fire & Marine Insurance acquired Ankuk on January 31, 1963. The unified entity adopted the name Ankuk Fire & Marine Insurance Co., Ltd. on March 2, 1963, retaining the more familiar brand to maintain customer trust and market presence under Samsung's oversight.
This foundational merger positioned the company for diversification, including the launch of a home insurance division in November 1969, which introduced long-term products and propelled its expansion within the domestic property and casualty sector. By 1970, it had grown to become the second-largest player in South Korea's P&C insurance market.

Domestic Growth and Innovation (1970s-1990s)

In the 1970s, Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance, then operating as Ankuk Fire & Marine Insurance, achieved a significant milestone through its initial public offering on June 1, 1975, on the Korea Stock Exchange, which provided essential capital to support operational modernization and expansion efforts. This listing marked a key step in the company's growth trajectory, building on the stability gained from earlier mergers in the 1950s and 1960s that had consolidated its position in the domestic property and casualty insurance sector. Key early international steps included the opening of its first overseas office in London in May 1973.
The 1980s saw strategic diversification into new product lines, particularly with the launch of auto insurance business on October 1, 1983, which broadened sales channels and positioned the company as a leader in motor insurance by the following year. This initiative capitalized on South Korea's growing automotive market, enabling Ankuk to surpass competitors and strengthen its market share through innovative distribution approaches.
Customer service enhancements further underscored the company's commitment to accessibility during this period. In January 1989, Ankuk opened its Customer Service Center to streamline inquiries and support, followed by the introduction of the Midnight Express Claims Adjustment Center in November, the first such facility in the Korean insurance industry for processing after-hours motor accident claims.
The early 1990s brought internal reforms aligned with broader Samsung Group strategies. In June 1993, the company underwent a management overhaul as part of the group's "New Management" initiative, launched by Chairman Lee Kun-hee to prioritize quality, innovation, and global competitiveness across affiliates. This restructuring emphasized operational efficiency and customer-focused practices, setting the stage for renewed growth. The establishment of the New York branch in April 1990 further supported international outreach.
On October 4, 1993, the company declared its "Second Foundation," a transformative pledge mirroring the group's efforts to refocus on customer-centric reforms, risk management excellence, and long-term sustainability. In December 1993, it officially renamed itself Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance Co., Ltd., reflecting its deeper integration into the Samsung ecosystem and commitment to branding innovation.
To bolster employee development amid these changes, Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance acquired the Yuseong facility from the Hanil Group and opened it as the Yuseong Training Campus in May 1994, establishing a dedicated center for professional training and leadership cultivation. These developments solidified the company's domestic leadership by the end of the decade, fostering a culture of continuous improvement and service excellence.

Global Expansion and Modernization (2000s-Present)

In the 2000s, Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance (SFMI) leveraged its strengthened domestic foundation to pursue aggressive international expansion, establishing key branches and subsidiaries across Asia and beyond to diversify its market presence and enhance global risk management capabilities. This period marked a shift toward modernization through digital innovations and strategic partnerships, positioning the company as a leader in non-life insurance amid rapid economic globalization.
The company's overseas push began in April 2001 with the opening of its Shanghai branch in China, followed shortly by the establishment of the Samsung Traffic Safety Research Institute in July 2001 to advance automotive safety studies and support insurance product development. In April 2002, SFMI launched the "Samsung anycar" brand, revolutionizing auto insurance with customer-centric features tailored for the Korean market while laying groundwork for international adaptations. Building on this momentum, SFMI expanded further in 2003 by opening an office in Qingdao, China, in September and forming a joint venture, Samsung Vina Insurance, with Vietnam National Reinsurance Corporation in August to tap into Southeast Asian growth opportunities.
By 2005, SFMI achieved a milestone as the first foreign insurer to establish a local subsidiary in China, Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance (China), in June, enabling direct operations and regulatory compliance in the world's largest insurance market. Complementing this, the company set up the Samsung Loss Control Center in February 2005 to provide specialized risk assessment and prevention services globally. Expansion continued in 2006 with the July opening of the Beijing office for its Chinese subsidiary, enhancing regional oversight. In January 2007, SFMI earned recognition as one of Forbes' "Asia's Fabulous 50" companies, highlighting its robust performance and strategic vision in the Asia-Pacific region. Further offices followed in December 2007 for Shenzhen, China; July 2008 for Suzhou, China; and December 2008 for Singapore, broadening its footprint in high-growth economies.
Digital transformation gained traction in March 2009 with the launch of "my anycar," an online auto insurance platform that streamlined sales and claims processes, foreshadowing broader tech integration. That year, SFMI opened representative offices in India in April and Brazil in September, marking its entry into emerging markets in South Asia and Latin America. In 2010, the company obtained Business Continuity Management (BCM) certification in June, the first among Korean non-life insurers, underscoring its commitment to resilient operations. The same month, SFMI entered a comprehensive partnership with China's Huatai Insurance to collaborate on product development and risk sharing. Concluding the decade, SFMI established a central office in Dubai in December 2010, facilitating access to Middle Eastern markets and reinsurance opportunities.
From 2011 to 2020, SFMI sustained its global trajectory, with ongoing development in established regions like Indonesia, where its local subsidiaryfounded in 1997expanded operations to support increasing demand for property and casualty coverage. The company further internationalized by establishing a European subsidiary in March 2011 and Singapore Samsung Re in January 2012 for reinsurance activities. In May 2017, SFMI acquired a 20% stake in Vietnam's PJICO to deepen its Southeast Asian presence. A November 2020 joint venture agreement with Tencent for the China subsidiary integrated digital technologies for enhanced customer services. In August 2023, SFMI joined the Taskforce on Nature-related Financial Disclosures (TNFD), aligning its risk frameworks with global sustainability standards.
Recent years have seen strategic consolidations and financial resilience. In June 2025, SFMI increased its stake in Lloyd's underwriter Canopius Group to 40% through a $570 million investment, bolstering its global specialty insurance capabilities. For fiscal year 2024, the company reported net profit of 2.07 trillion won ($1.4 billion), up 14% year-over-year, driven by strong insurance and investment income despite market volatility. In Q2 2025, SFMI achieved net profit of 638.4 billion won, a 4.2% increase from the prior year, alongside a 24.1% revenue surge to 5.75 trillion won, reflecting effective modernization and expansion efforts.

Corporate Structure

In April 2025, Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance was absorbed as a wholly-owned subsidiary of Samsung Life Insurance Co., Ltd., following regulatory approval by the Financial Services Commission.

Subsidiaries and International Operations

Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance maintains a global presence through a network of subsidiaries, branches, and representative offices spanning multiple continents, supporting its expansion into key markets since the 1990s. The company's international operations primarily focus on providing property and casualty insurance, reinsurance, and risk management services tailored to multinational clients, particularly affiliates of the Samsung Group. As of 2025, these operations include six overseas subsidiaries, two branches, and three representative offices across eight countries.
In Asia, Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance has established significant footholds to capitalize on regional growth. The company founded Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance (China) Co., Ltd. in June 2005, marking it as the first local subsidiary established by a foreign insurer in China; it operates offices in Beijing, Shanghai, and Qingdao to offer property, casualty, and marine insurance adapted to local regulations. In Indonesia, PT Asuransi Samsung Tugu was established in November 1996 in Jakarta, providing non-life insurance products including fire, marine, and auto coverage. Samsung Vina Insurance Co., Ltd., a joint venture formed in November 2002 in Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam, focuses on general insurance services such as property and liability. Additional representative offices support operations in Tokyo (opened January 1994), Singapore (December 2008), and India (April 2009), facilitating reinsurance and risk consulting for Samsung Group entities in these markets.
In the Americas, the company has prioritized North and South American markets with early and strategic entries. A branch office was opened in New York in April 1990 to handle marine and specialty insurance for U.S.-based clients. In Brazil, an office was established in September 2009 in So Paulo to address growing demand for industrial and construction insurance. Complementing these, Samsung Fire & Marine Management Corporation was set up in New Jersey in June 2011 as a U.S. subsidiary for insurance agency and consulting services. In June 2025, the company increased its equity stake in the UK-based Lloyd's syndicate Canopius Group from 19% to 40%, enhancing its access to global specialty insurance markets through this London-headquartered entity.
In Europe and the Middle East, operations emphasize specialized coverage for high-value assets. Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance Company of Europe Ltd., established in London in March 2011, primarily insures risks associated with Samsung Group affiliates, including marine cargo and property policies; in 2024, approximately 81% of its gross earned premiums derived from such affiliated clients. A central office in Dubai was opened in December 2010, followed by the formation of Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance Management Middle East Limited in February 2016, which provides agency services for construction, energy, and liability insurance in the region.
The overarching strategy for international operations centers on mitigating risks for Samsung Group affiliates while gradually expanding to third-party clients, with core products like automobile, marine, and property insurance modified to comply with local regulatory frameworks. This affiliate-focused approach has driven steady growth, with overseas activities contributing to diversified revenue streams amid domestic market maturity. Samsung Reinsurance Pte. Ltd., established in Singapore in December 2011, further bolsters this by offering reinsurance solutions across Asia and beyond.

Financial Performance and Market Position

Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance maintains a leading position in South Korea's non-life insurance sector, holding a 22% market share as of 2024. This dominance is supported by the company's robust capital position, evidenced by its solvency ratio of 274.5%, the highest among domestic non-life insurers as of the first half of 2025.
In fiscal year 2024, the company reported a net profit of KRW 2.07 trillion, marking a 14% increase from the previous year, driven by strong underwriting and investment performance. For the first half of 2025, revenue reached KRW 12.96 trillion, reflecting a 17.5% year-on-year growth, while net profit totaled approximately KRW 1.25 trillion, bolstered by gains in operating income of KRW 1.60 trillion. Total assets stood at around KRW 88 trillion by mid-2025, underscoring the company's scale and financial stability. Credit ratings affirm this strength, with AM Best affirming an A++ (Superior) Financial Strength Rating and aa+ Issuer Credit Rating in September 2025. S&P Global Ratings maintained its 'AA-' rating with a stable outlook in July 2025.
The company's investment strategy emphasizes scaling private equity commitments, with six new fund investments made in 2025 featuring larger ticket sizes, representing a strategic push into buyouts that comprise about 25% of its private equity portfolio. Focus areas include renewable energy initiatives aligned with broader sustainability goals and investments in Samsung Group affiliates to leverage synergies. Despite these strengths, Samsung Fire & Marine faces challenges from rising claims related to climate events, which have increased social losses and drawn scrutiny over coverage practices. The outlook remains stable, as affirmed by S&P Global Ratings.

Sustainability and Social Impact

Corporate Social Responsibility Programs

Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance has implemented various corporate social responsibility (CSR) programs since the 1990s, emphasizing community support, education, and safety initiatives aligned with its insurance expertise. These efforts focus on aiding vulnerable populations, particularly those affected by accidents and disabilities, through partnerships with government agencies and foundations. By 2024, the company had committed significant resources to these programs, including over KRW 16.9 billion in total donations for social contributions in 2023 alone.
A key ongoing program is the Scholarship Grant for children of traffic accident victims, established in 1993 in partnership with the National Police Agency and the Kids & Future Foundation. This initiative provides monthly scholarships, school uniforms, birthday presents, holiday gifts, and one-on-one mentoring by company employees to bereaved children or those whose families have lost economic stability due to accidents. By 2024, it had delivered over KRW 100 billion in total aid, supporting emotional and financial recovery for affected families.
In the area of disability support, Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance operates the Guide Dog School, founded in 1993 in collaboration with the Seoul National School for the Blind and other societal partners. The program trains and donates guide dogs to visually impaired individuals at no cost, enhancing their mobility and independence. As of December 2024, it had trained a total of 300 dogs, with 84 actively in service and 12-15 new dogs trained annually. In October 2025, the company collaborated with the Guide Dog School to launch AI-powered manuals for home appliances aimed at visually impaired users. Additionally, to raise awareness of disabilities, the company has produced educational TV dramas since 2009 in association with the Ministry of Education and the Disabled First Action Campaign; notable examples include "Perfect Sense" (2015) and ongoing series like "To You, From Me" (2021). These dramas target youth to foster empathy and understanding. The company also organizes camps and concerts for students with disabilities through Ministry of Education partnerships, promoting inclusion and social integration.
For education and safety, Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance channels donations through the Kids & Future Foundation to support underprivileged youth, including scholarships and programs for traffic accident victims' children. In 2001, it established the Samsung Traffic Safety Research Institute, Korea's first private traffic safety research organization, with the goal of achieving zero traffic accidents through policy development, safety education for vulnerable groups, and public awareness campaigns. The institute collaborates with the National Police Agency and Ministry of Land, Infrastructure, and Transport on initiatives like road improvements in high-risk areas and traffic safety management in communities and universities.
Post-COVID, the company expanded support for underprivileged groups from 2023 to 2025, including enhanced disaster response funds for students affected by pandemics and natural calamities, aiding 818 beneficiaries cumulatively by 2023. In August 2025, the company donated KRW 3 billion to the Korean Red Cross for flood relief efforts. In 2024, it allocated KRW 50 billion specifically for educational grants and initiatives, reinforcing commitments to youth development and financial inclusion for low-income families, single-parent households, and migrant workers. These efforts underscore the company's vision of "Going towards the Future & Enabling People" through targeted social contributions.

Environmental and ESG Initiatives

Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance has integrated environmental, social, and governance (ESG) considerations into its operations through participation in several international frameworks. The company joined the United Nations Environment Programme Finance Initiative (UNEP FI) in 2010 to promote sustainable practices in the financial sector. In 2015, it became the first Korean insurer to sign the Principles for Sustainable Insurance (PSI), committing to embed ESG issues in decision-making, risk management, and product development. Additionally, Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance has been a member of the Carbon Disclosure Project (CDP) since 2010, disclosing its greenhouse gas emissions and climate-related risks annually. The company joined the Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures (TCFD) in July 2021 to enhance transparency on climate risks and opportunities. It participates in the Partnership for Carbon Accounting Financials (PCAF), using its standards to measure and report financed emissions since becoming a member in 2023. In August 2023, Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance joined the Taskforce on Nature-related Financial Disclosures (TNFD) to assess and disclose nature-related risks and impacts. Furthermore, it committed to the RE100 initiative, aiming for 100% renewable electricity by 2040.
To oversee these commitments, the company established a board-level ESG Committee and a Sustainability Management Committee in 2012, comprising seven members including the CEO, CFO, and key executives, which deliberates on ESG strategies and activities. In terms of environmental actions, Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance has set an internal goal of achieving net zero emissions by 2050, aligning with global climate objectives. It promotes paperless operations by implementing mobile and e-signature systems for insurance contracts, reducing paper usage and supporting eco-friendly practices. In 2020, the company adopted the Coal Phase-out Principle as part of its Social Responsibility Regulation, suspending underwriting and investments related to new coal-fired power plants to mitigate climate risks.
On climate efforts, the Global Loss Control Center (GLCC) plays a key role in researching and managing climate-related risks, providing support to customers and communities for disaster prevention and adaptation. This aligns with the company's integrated report goals, including full conversion to renewable energy sources by 2040 to lower operational emissions.
Governance structures further embed ESG principles, with the Social Responsibility Regulation established in 2013 and updated in 2020 to incorporate climate response measures, such as the coal phase-out policy. The company conducts annual double materiality assessments to identify and prioritize economic, environmental, and social factors impacting its business and stakeholders.
In 2024, Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance reported progress on PSI principles through enhanced integration of ESG factors in underwriting and investments, as detailed in its annual PSI report. The 2024 integrated report highlighted reductions in Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions, reflecting ongoing efforts in energy efficiency and renewable transitions across its operations and subsidiaries. For 2025, the company continues to advance these initiatives, with plans to further align with TNFD recommendations for biodiversity disclosures.

Recognition and Sponsorships

Awards and Industry Recognition

Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance has received numerous accolades for its growth, customer service, financial stability, and sustainable practices. In 2007, it was selected as one of "Asia's Fabulous 50 Companies" by Forbes, recognizing its strong performance in growth and innovation across the Asia-Pacific region.
The company has consistently led in customer satisfaction metrics in South Korea. In 2025, Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance maintained its top position in the National Customer Satisfaction Index (NCSI) for auto insurance, marking the 25th consecutive year with a score of 79 out of 100. It has also ranked first in the Korean Customer Satisfaction Index (KCSI) for auto insurance for 28 consecutive years and for long-term insurance for 15 years as of 2025. In the 2023 KCSI survey, scores were 89.8 for auto and 88.4 for long-term insurance. Additionally, it has been designated an "Excellent Call Center" under the Korea Service Quality Index (KSQI) for 20 consecutive years through 2024.
Financial strength has been a hallmark of the company's recognition. AM Best affirmed its A++ (Superior) Financial Strength Rating in September 2025, continuing a streak of 13 consecutive years at this top level, reflecting its robust solvency and market leadership as South Korea's largest non-life insurer with approximately 22% market share.
In risk management, Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance became the first Korean non-life insurer to obtain Business Continuity Management (BCM) certification, specifically BS 25999, in June 2010, underscoring its advanced capabilities in operational resilience.
The company has also been acknowledged for its environmental, social, and governance (ESG) efforts. As a signatory to the United Nations Environment Programme Finance Initiative (UNEP FI) since 2010 and the first Korean insurer to join the Principles for Sustainable Insurance (PSI) in 2015, it has demonstrated leadership in integrating sustainability into insurance practices, including annual PSI-aligned reporting that highlights its ESG governance and commitments such as Net Zero targets by 2050.

Sponsorship Activities

Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance has been a prominent sponsor in the sports sector, particularly through its long-standing support for the game of Baduk (Go). In June 1996, the company initiated the SF&MI Cup World Baduk Open Championships, an annual international tournament that attracts top professional players from around the world and promotes the strategic board game on a global stage. This event, now in its 30th edition as of 2025, features preliminary rounds and main competitions, fostering cultural exchange and talent development in the sport.
In the realm of culture and awareness, the company has funded initiatives to highlight social issues, including the production of TV dramas focused on disabilities during the 2000s. Since 2009, Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance has collaborated with the Ministry of Education and the Disabled First Action Committee to create educational dramas aimed at raising youth awareness about disabilities, with notable productions like "Perfect Sense" previewed on International Day of Persons with Disabilities. Complementing these efforts, the company organizes annual concerts and camps for students with disabilities, such as the "Poco a Poco" music program launched in 2009, which provides music education and performance opportunities to young participants with developmental challenges, culminating in public concerts to showcase their talents.
The company's community sponsorships emphasize public safety, particularly through partnerships with government entities on traffic safety campaigns. Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance collaborates with governmental and civic groups to promote road safety awareness, including joint initiatives to reduce accidents and educate the public on preventive measures. A key component is its sponsorship of traffic safety education via the Samsung Traffic Safety Research Institute, established in July 2001 as Korea's first private institute dedicated to traffic safety research, which conducts studies, campaigns, and programs aimed at achieving zero traffic accidents.
In recent years, from 2024 to 2025, Samsung Fire & Marine Insurance has expanded collaborations with civic groups for ESG-aligned events, such as environmental awareness programs and safety-focused gatherings that integrate sustainability themes. For instance, the company served as the lead sponsor for the Korea International Insurance Conference (KIIC) in June 2025, highlighting innovative insurance solutions for global challenges including environmental risks. These efforts align strategically with the company's corporate social responsibility objectives, tying sponsorships to core insurance products like auto coverage by promoting safety culture and risk prevention to enhance brand engagement and societal impact.
