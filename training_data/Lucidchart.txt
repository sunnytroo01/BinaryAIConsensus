Lucidchart
 Ara Eve Leo Sal 
1x
 Lucidchart is a cloud-based intelligent diagramming and visual collaboration platform that enables teams to create, edit, and share flowcharts, organizational charts, network diagrams, and other visuals in real time, with AI-powered features for automation, data integration, and intelligent diagram generation.
Developed by Lucid Software Inc., the platform supports a wide range of use cases, including process mapping, system architecture design, data visualization, and application planning through diagrams, through drag-and-drop interfaces, thousands of templates, and integrations with popular applications such as Google Workspace, Microsoft Office, Jira, Salesforce, and Slack.
Key features include real-time co-authoring with collaborative cursors and in-editor chat, version history for tracking changes, conditional formatting for dynamic data-linked diagrams, and Lucid AI tools that enable users to generate diagrams from text promptssuch as flowcharts, entity-relationship diagrams (ERDs), mind maps, UML diagrams, sequence diagrams, and other visuals useful for process design, app planning, and web contexts like sitemaps or user flowsbrainstorm and sort ideas, summarize diagram content, apply conditional formatting rules via natural language descriptions, and use AI Prompt Flow to interact with large language models (LLMs) for generating and refining diagrams or automating repetitive tasks. While these AI capabilities support visual workflows and application planning through diagramming, Lucidchart focuses on diagramming and visualization rather than no-code application development or building deployable apps.
Lucidchart emphasizes enterprise-grade security, compliance with standards like GDPR and SOC 2, and scalability for large organizations, making it suitable for both individual users and teams across industries.
As of 2025, Lucidchart serves over 100 million individual users and is adopted by 99% of Fortune 500 companies, operating in more than 180 countries and ranking as the 12th most-used application on identity management platforms like Okta.

History

Founding and early years

Lucidchart was founded in 2010 by Ben Dilts, who serves as the company's Chief Technology Officer, and Karl Sun, the initial CEO and current Board Chair, while Dilts was studying computer science at Brigham Young University in Provo, Utah. The idea originated from Dilts' frustrations with desktop-based diagramming tools like Microsoft Visio, which lacked real-time collaboration and cloud accessibility during his earlier work at Zane Benefits from 2006 to 2009. Motivated by the need for a web-based solution to enable visual collaboration, Dilts began developing a prototype as a side project in the summer of 2008, completing an initial version by December of that year after dedicating over 30 hours weekly outside his studies and job.
Sun, a former Google executive, met Dilts at a pitch competition in Utah and was impressed by the prototype, leading him to invest $200,000 from his network and join as CEO to formalize the venture. Together, they incorporated Lucid Software Inc. in 2010, establishing the company to focus on cloud-based visual tools. Early operations began informally in Dilts' living room near BYU before moving to a rented basement, where the team worked for six months to refine the product. The initial launch of Lucidchart occurred in 2010 as the flagship product, offering basic diagramming capabilities such as flowcharts and UML diagrams to address the limitations of legacy software.
To build a user base rapidly, Lucidchart adopted a freemium model from the outset, providing free access to core features for individual users while charging for advanced functionalities and unlimited storage. This approach proved effective, attracting thousands of free users within 10 days of launch and gaining early media attention from outlets like Lifehacker. By 2011, the company had relocated its first official office to South Jordan, Utah, marking a shift toward scaling operations in the Salt Lake City area while maintaining a focus on real-time web-based diagramming.

Milestones and expansions

In 2012, Lucidchart secured its first enterprise customer, Pearson Education, which represented a pivotal shift from a freemium model to focused enterprise sales efforts.
The company experienced significant funding milestones starting in 2020, when it raised $52 million in a Series D round led by ICONIQ Capital, achieving unicorn status with a $1 billion valuation. In June 2021, Lucidchart completed a $500 million secondary investment round led by Alkeon Capital, elevating its valuation to $3 billion. By 2025, the company had amassed total funding exceeding $166 million across eight rounds from investors including Spectrum Equity, Meritech Capital, and Global Tech Ventures.
Product expansions included the launch of Lucidspark, a virtual whiteboarding tool, in October 2020 to support ideation and real-time collaboration. This was followed by the introduction of Lucidscale in 2021, a cloud architecture visualization platform designed to automate mapping of cloud environments. In April 2025, Lucid Software announced its Work Acceleration Platform for business transformation, alongside the acquisition of airfocus GmbH, a Hamburg-based AI-powered product management and roadmapping platform, to bolster features for product teams in prioritization and execution. These developments contributed to Lucidchart's unicorn recognition and rapid scaling, reaching over 100 million users by 2025.
In 2025, Lucid Software won the Google Workspace Partner of the Year award for Technology Innovation. It also garnered notable awards, including recognition as one of Fast Company's Most Innovative Companies for its advancements in visual collaboration tools. Additionally, it ranked as the 12th most popular app globally in Okta's Businesses at Work 2025 Report, highlighting its strong enterprise adoption. In October 2025, the company announced new AI-powered features and platform enhancements to accelerate productivity and business processes.

Product features

Core diagramming tools

Lucidchart provides robust support for creating a wide array of diagram types, catering to diverse visualization needs in professional and educational settings. Key diagram types include flowcharts for process mapping, organizational charts for hierarchical structures, UML diagrams for software design, entity relationship diagrams (ERDs) for database modeling, and mind maps for brainstorming and idea organization.
The platform features extensive shape libraries that enable users to build diagrams efficiently through a drag-and-drop interface. These libraries offer thousands of customizable shapes tailored to specific industries, such as IT symbols for network diagrams, business process icons, and educational elements like Venn diagrams. Pre-built templates accelerate diagram creation, providing ready-to-use starting points for common scenarios in IT, business analysis, and education, allowing users to modify layouts, colors, and content as needed.
Data linking enhances diagram dynamism by allowing imports from external sources like CSV files, Excel spreadsheets, or Google Sheets to automatically generate and populate diagrams. For instance, users can import organizational data to auto-create org charts or process steps for flowcharts, with shapes updating based on the linked data. Lucidchart's org chart maker supports automatic generation from imported CSV, Excel, or Google Sheets data, with data linking enabling real-time syncing and updates (particularly via linked Google Sheets or Microsoft 365 Excel). Additional features include drag-and-drop editing to add, move, or delete employees; customizable templates such as company, marketing, location-based, cross-functional, geographic, and inverted; interactive elements including employee photos, embedded videos (e.g., via Loom or CloudApp), hyperlinks, customizable colors and shapes; group view using Smart Containers; embedding options; presentation mode; and integrations with Google Workspace, Microsoft Office add-ins (for Excel, Word, and PowerPoint), and Slack. As of October 2025, enhancements include automatic detection of organizational charts after adding shapes with names and titles, prompting a switch to specialized org chart tools, and drag-and-drop support for entire imported databases to auto-generate ERDs with tables and relationships. Conditional formatting further refines visualizations by applying rulessuch as color changes based on data thresholdsto ensure diagrams reflect real-time changes without manual intervention.
Editing tools in Lucidchart facilitate precise control over diagram composition. Users can employ layering to manage overlapping elements, grouping to treat multiple shapes as a single unit for easier manipulation, and alignment/distribution functions to maintain consistent layouts. Formatting options include adjusting fill colors, borders, shadows, and text styles, enabling professional polishing of visuals directly within the intuitive interface.
For distribution, Lucidchart supports exporting diagrams in multiple formats to accommodate various workflows and audiences. Common outputs include PDF for printable documents, PNG or JPEG for high-resolution images, Visio (.vdx or .vsdx) for compatibility with Microsoft tools, and embeddable links or iframes for seamless integration into websites or presentations without requiring a Lucidchart account.
Lucidchart provides robust printing capabilities directly from the editor. Users access printing via the More menu and File > Print, where they can select specific content (such as layers), adjust page size, orientation, and alignment, and enable multi-page tiling for large diagrams by activating page tiling in Document Settings (requiring disabling infinite canvas if necessary). PDF exports include additional controls like "Divide by Page Tiles" to split large diagrams across multiple pages. Limitations include the non-inclusion of comments, tasks, notes, or interactive elements in printed or exported outputs, as well as potential scaling issues on complex or large diagrams that may result in small text. These can often be resolved by adjusting content scale, disabling infinite canvas, enabling page tiling, or copying content to a new document. Overall, printing is effective for most users but may require tweaks for optimal results on detailed diagrams.

Collaboration and advanced capabilities

Lucidchart supports real-time collaboration, enabling multiple users to edit diagrams simultaneously with changes appearing instantly for all participants. This feature includes collaborative cursors to track others' positions on the canvas and in-editor chat for direct communication. Users can add comments to specific shapes or the canvas, mention collaborators by typing "@" followed by their email to notify them, and assign tasks within comment threads to track responsibilities and mark completions. These collaborative features facilitate the joint development and maintenance of organizational charts, supporting real-time co-editing of hierarchical structures and advanced sharing through presentation mode and interactive embedding in platforms such as Slack.
Lucidchart and Lucidspark facilitate design handoff to developers through collaborative and visual tools rather than dedicated design-to-development handoff capabilities. The tools do not provide specialized features such as developer inspect modes, CSS or measurement extraction, or automated specification exports. Instead, they enable effective handoffs by supporting the sharing of detailed diagrams, including wireflows, user journey maps, and process flows, to deliver clear context and minimize miscommunication. Lucidspark aids early developer involvement through real-time collaborative whiteboarding with features like sticky notes, voting, and seamless importing/exporting to Lucidchart. Both products support real-time co-editing, in-editor chat, comments, and integrations with developer tools such as Jira and Confluence to streamline workflows and foster ongoing collaboration. Lucid's official guidance emphasizes best practices including early developer inclusion, use of visuals for clarity, and establishment of structured handoff processes.
The platform provides comprehensive version history, allowing users to view a document's change log, identify who made specific revisions, revert to previous versions, or branch new documents from past states. Revision tracking displays timestamps and modifiers for each update, ensuring accountability in team workflows.
As of October 2025, Lucidchart includes AI-powered features to enhance diagramming efficiency, including the generation of diagrams such as flowcharts, entity-relationship diagrams (ERDs), mind maps, UML class diagrams, sequence diagrams, and others directly from text prompts via Lucid AI. These tools allow iteration on visuals for precision and include automated layout suggestions that organize shapes into containers, adjust spacing, and apply branching structures. Intelligent shape recommendations use AI to map dataset fields automatically, suggesting optimal shapes and properties based on imported data like Excel columns. Recent enhancements to Lucid AI introduce a conversational interface for generating diagrams, brainstorming ideas, sorting content into themes, summarizing diagrams and boards, asking questions about diagram elements, and setting conditional formatting rules. AI Prompt Flow enables users to visually interact with large language models (LLMs) by creating prompt-response flowcharts. The Cloud Accelerator generates up-to-date diagrams from AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud infrastructures, supporting blueprints and data source sharing. These AI capabilities, primarily through Lucid AI in Lucidchart and Lucidspark, also encompass generating and sorting ideas as well as building mind maps. Such features can indirectly support tasks related to software and app planning, such as creating UML and sequence diagrams from text prompts for visual workflows and process design, though Lucidchart does not have a native app builder or no-code application development tool; it focuses on diagramming and visualization rather than building deployable apps. Similarly, these tools can support web design tasks like creating sitemaps, user flows, or wireframes via descriptive text prompts, though Lucidchart does not generate HTML/CSS code or interactive web prototypes.
Automation capabilities extend to conditional logic for interactive diagrams, where AI applies formatting rules based on data conditions to highlight patterns, such as color-coding shapes by value thresholds. Data-driven actions enable dynamic updates to diagrams from linked sources, creating responsive visuals that reflect real-time changes without manual intervention. The Universal Canvas facilitates seamless transitions between diagramming and whiteboarding modes, supporting scripted workflows through the platform's API for programmatic diagram creation and manipulation.
For accessibility and security, Lucidchart employs a role-based permissions system that allows administrators to control document access, including view-only, edit, and ownership transfer restrictions. Revision history further supports auditing by preserving all changes. The platform complies with GDPR through measures like secure user access, document sharing controls, and IP restrictions. Integrations with SSO providers such as SAML-enabled identity providers (e.g., Okta, Microsoft Entra ID) enable centralized authentication and multi-factor support for enterprise users.
Lucidchart offers dedicated mobile apps for iOS and Android, enabling users to view, access, and perform basic editing of diagrams on the go. Changes made in the apps sync automatically upon reconnection to the internet, though full offline editing is limited to desktop browsers and not available on mobile devices. These apps support touch-based interactions for shape manipulation and commenting, integrating with the platform's core shape libraries for consistent diagramming across devices.

Company and ecosystem

Lucid Software overview

Lucid Software, Inc. is a visual collaboration software company headquartered in South Jordan, Utah, with additional offices in Raleigh, North Carolina; Amsterdam, Netherlands; Melbourne, Australia; Hamburg, Germany; and London, United Kingdom, alongside distributed remote teams worldwide. As of 2025, the company employs over 1,300 people globally, supporting its operations across multiple continents.
The company's leadership includes Board Chair and Co-Founder Karl Sun, who has guided strategic direction since transitioning from CEO in 2022; CEO Dave Grow, appointed in 2021 to oversee day-to-day operations; and CTO and Co-Founder Ben Dilts, focusing on engineering innovation within the visual collaboration suite. Under this team, Lucid Software emphasizes expanding its platform to accelerate work through AI-driven tools and productivity enhancements. In October 2025, Lucid Software announced new AI and productivity innovations, advancing its accelerators to improve document workflows and intelligent planning for large-scale initiatives. In April 2025, the company acquired airfocus, an AI-powered product management platform, to bolster its offerings for product teams.
Lucid Software operates on a freemium business model. As of March 2026, Lucidchart (intelligent diagramming) and Lucidspark (virtual whiteboarding) are part of the Lucid Visual Collaboration Suite. A Free plan is available with limited features (e.g., 3 editable boards for Lucidspark). Team and Enterprise plans, with the Enterprise plan including both Lucidchart and Lucidspark along with advanced team features like team hubs, universal canvas, integrations, and security options, require contacting sales for custom pricing.
Positioned as a leader in the visual collaboration software market, Lucid Software competes with tools like Microsoft Visio, Miro, and Draw.io, serving over 100 million users across 180 countries and achieving adoption by 99% of Fortune 500 companies. The company fosters a corporate culture centered on innovation, teamwork, and diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI) through a dedicated committee and employee resource groups, while committing to sustainability via an ESG report and environmental initiatives overseen by a specialized committee. In 2025, Lucid Software was named Google Workspace Partner of the Year for Technology - Innovation, highlighting its strategic alliances in enterprise productivity ecosystems.

Integrations and related products

Lucidchart offers native integrations with a variety of third-party tools to facilitate seamless data flow and collaboration across platforms. These include connections to Google Workspace for embedding diagrams in Google Docs, Sheets, and Slides; Microsoft Office 365 for inserting visuals into Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and Teams; Slack for sharing documents and receiving notifications; Jira for attaching diagrams to issues and embedding in project boards; Salesforce for importing account, opportunity, contact, activity, and task data to create dynamic maps; Confluence for enriching pages with embedded Lucidchart documents; and Zapier for automating workflows by connecting to over 8,000 apps. Additionally, API access enables custom integrations tailored to specific organizational needs.
Within the Lucid Software ecosystem, Lucidchart integrates closely with related products such as Lucidspark, a virtual whiteboarding tool designed for brainstorming and ideation on an infinite canvas that facilitates early developer involvement through real-time collaborative features including sticky notes, voting, commenting, chat, and seamless import/export to Lucidchart for creating detailed diagrams, and Lucidscale, which automates the generation of cloud infrastructure diagrams for platforms including AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud Platform. These products are accessible through the unified Lucid Visual Collaboration Suite, allowing users to switch seamlessly between diagramming, whiteboarding, and cloud visualization in a shared workspace.
The integrations provide key ecosystem benefits by enabling efficient data import and export, as well as enhanced cross-tool collaboration; for instance, diagrams can be embedded directly into Notion pages for interactive viewing, and Salesforce CRM data can be pulled into Lucidchart to update visuals in real time. This connectivity supports workflow automation and alignment across teams, reducing silos and accelerating decision-making without leaving familiar applications.
Lucidchart and Lucidspark do not offer dedicated design handoff features such as developer inspect modes, CSS/measurement extraction, or automated spec exports. Instead, they support effective handoffs through visual collaboration and documentation: Lucidchart enables sharing detailed diagrams (e.g., wireflows, user journey maps, process flows) to provide developers with clear context and reduce miscommunication; Lucidspark facilitates early developer involvement via real-time collaborative whiteboarding, brainstorming, sticky notes, voting, and importing/exporting to Lucidchart; both tools support real-time co-editing, in-editor chat, comments, and integrations with developer tools like Jira and Confluence to streamline workflows and ongoing collaboration. Lucid's official guidance emphasizes best practices such as involving developers early, using visuals for clarity, and establishing structured handoff processes, focusing on visual alignment and communication.
For developers, Lucidchart provides APIs that support embedding Lucid documents into external applications and extending functionality through custom shape libraries and data import/export capabilities, fostering innovative integrations within broader tech stacks.
