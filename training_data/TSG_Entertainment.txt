TSG Entertainment
 Ara Eve Leo Sal 
1x
 TSG Entertainment Finance LLC, commonly known as TSG Entertainment, is an American film financing and production company specializing in funding live-action and animated feature films for major studios. It began its key partnership with 20th Century Fox in 2012, providing slate financing for high-profile projects and establishing itself as a major player in Hollywood's independent financing landscape.
Founded by entertainment financier Chip Seelig and closely tied to The Seelig Group, a New York City-based investment firm focused on media and entertainment, TSG has invested over $3 billion in more than 140 films (as of 2023), many of which have achieved critical and commercial success. Notable productions financed by TSG include Academy Award-winning Best Picture The Shape of Water (2017), Best Actor winner Bohemian Rhapsody (2018), and blockbusters such as Avatar: The Way of Water (2022) and the Deadpool franchise. The company's funding model often involves collaborations with international partners, including a $235 million investment from China's Bona Film Group in 2015 for a slate of Fox tentpoles like The Martian, and a renewed deal in 2024 for franchise titles including upcoming Avatar sequels.
Beyond film, TSG has expanded into live theater and experiential entertainment, spearheading projects like the 2024 London stage production Come Alive! The Greatest Showman Circus Spectacular, inspired by the 2017 Fox musical hit. In recent years, TSG faced legal challenges, including a 2023 lawsuit against Disney (following its 2019 acquisition of Fox) alleging self-dealing and withheld profits on co-financed films, which was settled in January 2024. With majority backing from Magnetar Capital and additional partners like Bona Film Group, TSG continues to support innovative storytelling across global entertainment sectors.

Overview

Founding

TSG Entertainment was established in 2013 by Chip Seelig, a former co-founder and partner at Dune Entertainment, which he had left in May 2011 to pursue new ventures in film financing. Seelig's prior role at Dune, where he helped structure multi-film slate deals with major studios, informed the creation of TSG as a specialized financing entity.
The company emerged as a direct replacement for Dune following the latter's decision not to renew its long-term co-financing agreement with 20th Century Fox, which concluded after the 2012 release of Parental GuidanceDune's final film under the partnership. This transition allowed Fox to maintain its slate financing model without interruption, with TSG stepping in to provide similar support for upcoming productions.
Headquartered in New York City, TSG Entertainment initially concentrated on financing live-action film projects, particularly those produced by Fox, and has funded over 100 such titles. The firm's early operations were backed primarily by The Seelig Group, its parent entity, which secured a landmark $400 million commitment for Fox's film slate, with the majority of that funding sourced from Magnetar Capital.

Ownership and Leadership

TSG Entertainment is a subsidiary of The Seelig Group, a principal investment and advisory firm founded in 2012 by experienced investors and entrepreneurs with a primary focus on media and entertainment projects. The company is backed by major funding from Magnetar Capital, which provided the majority of initial capital for its film financing operations. Bona Film Group has also been a key investor since 2015, forging a strategic partnership that includes co-financing of major Hollywood productions.
Chip Seelig founded TSG Entertainment in 2013 following his involvement in Dune Entertainment's financing arrangements and serves as its managing member and primary leader.
His brother, Robert Seelig, co-leads the company alongside advisory input from seasoned investors associated with its funding partners.
Under the Seelig brothers' direction, TSG has evolved from a specialized film financing entity into a multifaceted investment firm that supports a broad range of media and entertainment initiatives, including slate deals with major studios.
The company is headquartered in New York City at 1441 Broadway and extends its advisory scope globally through international collaborations.

History

Early Development (20132015)

TSG Entertainment was established in 2013 by Chip Seelig, a veteran film financier and former co-founder of Dune Entertainment, to serve as a new co-financing partner for 20th Century Fox after Dune opted not to renew its longstanding agreement with the studio. The company, backed primarily by Magnetar Capital with additional equity from Seelig and other investors, focused on providing slate financing for high-profile live-action projects, emphasizing action and drama genres to align with Fox's production pipeline.
In January 2013, TSG secured a landmark multi-year agreement with 20th Century Fox valued at more than $400 million, committing funds to support nearly all of the studio's theatrical releases over the subsequent five years, including sequels to major franchises like Avatar. This deal marked TSG's entry into Hollywood's competitive financing landscape, positioning the firm to underwrite production and marketing costs for a diverse slate of films. The company's inaugural project under the pact was the action thriller A Good Day to Die Hard, released in February 2013 and presented in association with TSG Entertainment.
Building on this foundation, TSG financed additional early releases, including the historical drama The Book Thief in November 2013 and the adventure film The Secret Life of Walter Mitty in December 2013, both distributed by 20th Century Fox. These projects exemplified TSG's initial strategy of targeting prestige and commercial titles to establish partnerships and demonstrate reliability in the industry. By late 2015, TSG had further bolstered its capacity through a $235 million investment from China's Bona Film Group, enabling expanded participation in Fox's tentpole slate. Overall, the period saw TSG deploy hundreds of millions in commitments, laying the groundwork for long-term studio collaboration while navigating the demands of slate-based risk in Hollywood financing.

Expansion and Key Deals (20162020)

During the period from 2016 to 2020, TSG Entertainment significantly expanded its operations through strategic investments and deepened its long-term co-financing partnership with 20th Century Fox, enabling the funding of high-profile blockbuster projects. In late 2015, Chinese studio Bona Film Group invested $235 million in TSG via a combination of equity and credit facilities, bolstering the company's capacity to support larger slate deals and participate in profit shares from Fox's live-action films. This infusion facilitated TSG's involvement in major franchises, marking a shift toward financing tentpole releases with budgets exceeding $100 million each.
TSG's key deals during this era included co-financing the Marvel superhero films Deadpool (2016), which grossed $783 million worldwide, and Logan (2017), which earned $619 million globally, both distributed by Fox and contributing to the studio's X-Men franchise success. The company also backed the musical biopic Bohemian Rhapsody (2018), a Queen tribute film that achieved $911 million in worldwide box office, highlighting TSG's entry into diverse genres beyond action and sci-fi. Additionally, TSG provided pre-production financing for Avatar: The Way of Water as part of its ongoing Fox slate commitment, supporting development costs for the sequel ahead of its 2022 release. These deals reflected increased investment sizes, with TSG committing tens of millions per project under the expanded Fox arrangement initially established in 2013.
The financed films from this period demonstrated substantial commercial impact, with TSG-backed releases collectively generating over $5 billion in global box office revenue, underscoring the company's role in Fox's output amid rising competition from streaming platforms. This era saw TSG diversify into superhero spectacles, musical dramas, and epic sequels, while maintaining financial stability through its core Fox partnership before the 2019 Disney acquisition of 21st Century Fox. The investments positioned TSG as a key player in Hollywood financing, prioritizing high-return franchises to navigate industry shifts toward serialized content.

Post-Acquisition Era (2021present)

Following the 2019 Disney acquisition of 21st Century Fox, TSG Entertainment maintained its longstanding co-financing arrangement with what became 20th Century Studios, continuing to fund high-profile projects within Disney's portfolio. This included support for blockbuster franchises, such as the 2024 release Kingdom of the Planet of the Apes, directed by Wes Ball, which grossed $398 million worldwide and extended the series' narrative generations after the original Caesar storyline. TSG's role ensured financial backing for ambitious visual effects-driven productions, aligning with Disney's emphasis on established intellectual properties.
In adapting to the rise of streaming platforms, TSG-financed films under 20th Century Studios have incorporated hybrid release strategies, transitioning from theatrical debuts to availability on Disney+ to maximize audience reach and revenue streams. A key example is Avatar: The Way of Water (2022), co-financed by TSG and directed by James Cameron, which earned $2.32 billion globally before streaming on Disney+ on June 7, 2023, approximately 173 days after its December 16, 2022, theatrical release, demonstrating TSG's flexibility in supporting Disney's direct-to-consumer model.
By November 2025, TSG's project slate reflects ongoing activity, with notable releases including 28 Years Later (2025), a post-apocalyptic horror film directed by Danny Boyle and distributed by Sony Pictures, which opened to $30 million in North America and achieved a domestic total of approximately $70 million. Other recent efforts encompass Venom: The Last Dance (2024), co-financed alongside Sony for a final global box office of $450 million.
Strategically, TSG has shifted toward broader media diversification post-merger, expanding beyond Disney through new partnerships such as co-financing with Sony Pictures Motion Picture Group on multiple titles since 2022 and a 2024 investment deal with China's Bona Film Group to back Hollywood sequels like those in the Avatar and Deadpool series. This approach mitigates reliance on single studios, incorporating international capital and cross-studio collaborations to fund a mix of theatrical and streaming content.

Business Model

Financing Approach

TSG Entertainment operates primarily through a slate financing model, under which it provides upfront capital to major studios for a portfolio of multiple films rather than individual projects, in exchange for a share of the backend profits. This approach allows TSG to support a diversified slate of productions, spreading investment across more than 140 films since establishing its partnership with 20th Century Fox in 2013, with total investments exceeding $3 billion.
The company focuses on feature films with strong commercial potential, including live-action and animated projects, to prioritize high-return opportunities in the theatrical market. Typical slate investments range from $200 million to $400 million, as seen in deals funding multi-year film packages, enabling studios like Fox to greenlight ambitious projects without bearing the full financial burden upfront.
To manage risk, TSG structures its agreements to include backend participation in box office revenues, ancillary sales, and licensing, which helps offset potential losses from underperforming titles through successes in the slate. This model also incorporates options for selling interests in film tranches, providing flexibility to adjust exposure based on performance. International rights sales further bolster returns, leveraging global distribution to enhance overall profitability.
Unlike traditional production companies that exert creative control, TSG differentiates itself by concentrating on financial structuring and risk assessment, leaving artistic decisions to studio partners such as Fox and, post-acquisition, Disney. This finance-first strategy positions TSG as a key enabler for studio output without involvement in day-to-day production.

Studio Partnerships

TSG Entertainment established a foundational partnership with 20th Century Fox in 2013 through a multi-year co-financing agreement designed to fund a slate of films, filling the gap left by the departure of previous financier Dune Entertainment. This deal enabled TSG to contribute significant capital to Fox's productions, fostering a collaborative model where TSG shared in financial decisions for select projects.
Following The Walt Disney Company's $71.3 billion acquisition of 21st Century Fox in March 2019, the partnership seamlessly transitioned to Disney, with TSG continuing its co-financing role for films under the rebranded 20th Century Studios. The arrangement was maintained without interruption, allowing TSG to support ongoing productions and adapt to Disney's integrated ecosystem. To enhance international reach, TSG formed co-financing alliances, notably with China's Bona Film Group, which invested $235 million in 2015 to back Fox titles targeted at global markets, including sequels and blockbusters. This collaboration was extended in early 2024, with Bona committing to further investments in high-profile franchises like Avatar and Deadpool through TSG's slate.
TSG's collaborations extended to joint ventures on major franchises, such as the X-Men and Planet of the Apes series, where it co-financed key installments like X-Men: Days of Future Past (2014) and Dawn of the Planet of the Apes (2014), leveraging Fox's intellectual properties for shared risk and revenue. These efforts highlighted TSG's role in scaling ambitious projects through strategic alignments. Post-Disney acquisition, the partnerships evolved to encompass streaming distribution, integrating TSG-financed content onto platforms like Disney+ to capitalize on direct-to-consumer models and expand audience access beyond theatrical releases.

Legal Issues

2023 Disney Lawsuit

In August 2023, TSG Entertainment filed a breach of contract lawsuit against 20th Century Studios in Los Angeles Superior Court, accusing the studio of withholding more than $40 million in profits from backend participation deals on several films. The suit specifically highlighted an independent audit of three financed projects, including the Oscar-winning The Shape of Water (2017), which revealed unrealized revenues totaling at least $40 million that TSG claimed were not properly distributed. TSG alleged that these shortfalls stemmed from "Hollywood accounting" practices, where expenses were inflated and revenues minimized to reduce profit-sharing obligations to financiers like TSG.
TSG further sued The Walt Disney Company for tortious interference with contract, claiming Disney induced the breach through deliberate self-dealing after acquiring 20th Century Fox in 2019. According to the complaint, Disney prioritized boosting Disney+ subscriber numbers and propping up its stock pricealong with executive compensation for CEOs Bob Iger and Bob Chapekby diverting films to streaming platforms prematurely, thereby suppressing theatrical box office earnings that would have triggered higher backend payouts to TSG. Specific accusations included Disney blocking TSG's access to detailed financial records and backend earnings data, as well as forcing 20th Century Studios into unfavorable licensing deals that funneled profits away from traditional revenue streams. The films at the center of the dispute encompassed high-profile titles such as Deadpool (2016), The New Mutants (2020), and the Avatar sequels, including Avatar: The Way of Water (2022), where TSG had invested over $3.3 billion across its partnership portfolio.
Disney responded by filing a counter-suit in the same court, seeking a declaratory judgment that neither it nor 20th Century Studios had breached any agreements with TSG. The company denied the allegations of self-dealing, asserting that its distribution strategies were standard industry practices and that TSG's claims misrepresented the terms of their long-standing financing arrangements. TSG described the case as a "chilling example" of how major studios manipulate accounting to disadvantage independent financiers, potentially setting a precedent for transparency in profit participation.

Settlement and Implications

In January 2024, TSG Entertainment and Disney reached a confidential settlement that resolved the ongoing breach of contract lawsuit filed by TSG against 20th Century Studios and Disney, as well as Disney's countersuit, effectively ending all litigation between the parties.
The settlement enabled TSG to maintain its long-standing partnership with Disney subsidiaries, though it drew increased attention to the complexities of profit-sharing arrangements in film financing deals. This outcome allowed TSG to stabilize its financing operations without disruption from legal proceedings.
On an industry level, the case illuminated persistent challenges in streaming-era accounting practices, where studios prioritize platform subscriptions over traditional revenue streams, straining relationships between financiers and production partners. It served as a reminder of "Hollywood accounting" tactics that can obscure backend profits, echoing similar disputes like Scarlett Johansson's 2021 lawsuit against Disney over Black Widow.
Following the resolution, TSG continued to finance projects with Disney-affiliated studios, including the 2024 anthology film Kinds of Kindness produced in association with Searchlight Pictures, demonstrating restored operational momentum for 20242025 releases.

Filmography

20132017

TSG Entertainment entered the film financing landscape in 2013, establishing an initial co-financing partnership with 20th Century Fox to support a diverse slate spanning action, drama, comedy, and thriller genres.
The company's early releases included A Good Day to Die Hard (February 14, 2013, directed by John Moore), an action thriller in the Die Hard franchise that TSG co-financed with Fox, grossing $304 million worldwide. Later that year, TSG backed The Heat (June 28, 2013, directed by Paul Feig), a buddy cop action comedy starring Sandra Bullock and Melissa McCarthy, which earned $229 million globally and highlighted the firm's support for female-led comedies. Demonstrating portfolio diversity, The Book Thief (November 8, 2013, directed by Brian Percival) was a World War II drama adaptation that TSG co-financed, achieving $77 million in worldwide box office. Rounding out 2013, The Secret Life of Walter Mitty (December 25, 2013, directed by and starring Ben Stiller) blended adventure and comedy in a tale of self-discovery, co-financed by TSG and grossing $187 million internationally.
In 2014, TSG continued its varied investments with The Grand Budapest Hotel (March 7, 2014, directed by Wes Anderson), a whimsical comedy-drama that earned $172 million worldwide and secured four Academy Awards, including for production design and costume design. The firm also co-financed Dawn of the Planet of the Apes (July 11, 2014, directed by Matt Reeves), a sci-fi action sequel that became a major hit with $711 million in global earnings, underscoring TSG's entry into blockbuster franchises.
By 2015, TSG's slate featured Kingsman: The Secret Service (February 13, 2015, directed by Matthew Vaughn), a stylish spy action comedy that grossed $414 million worldwide and spawned a franchise. The Martian (October 2, 2015, directed by Ridley Scott) followed as a sci-fi survival drama co-financed by TSG, achieving $630 million globally and earning seven Oscar nominations, including Best Picture.
The period's superhero breakthroughs came in 2016 with Deadpool (February 12, 2016, directed by Tim Miller), an R-rated action comedy reboot that TSG co-financed, shattering records with $782 million in worldwide box office and revitalizing the genre for mature audiences. Complementing this, X-Men: Apocalypse (May 27, 2016, directed by Bryan Singer) was a superhero ensemble film that earned $544 million internationally.
In 2017, TSG co-financed Logan (March 3, 2017, directed by James Mangold), a gritty superhero Western concluding Hugh Jackman's Wolverine arc, which grossed $619 million worldwide and received an Academy Award nomination for Adapted Screenplay. War for the Planet of the Apes (July 14, 2017, directed by Matt Reeves) extended the franchise with $490 million in global receipts, blending action and emotional depth.
Over 20132017, TSG's financed films collectively surpassed $5 billion in worldwide box office, with standouts like Deadpool and Dawn of the Planet of the Apes driving profitability and awards recognition, including multiple Oscars for titles such as The Grand Budapest Hotel and The Martian. This era marked TSG's shift toward a franchise-heavy portfolio, building on initial diverse investments to fuel larger-scale productions.

20182022

During the 20182022 period, TSG Entertainment continued its role as a key financier for 20th Century Studios (formerly Fox), co-financing a diverse slate of films that spanned biographical dramas, action sci-fi, sports epics, and horror thrillers, achieving significant commercial success while navigating the disruptions of the COVID-19 pandemic. The company's contributions typically involved partial financing, often covering international distribution rights or production costs in association with studios. This era saw TSG-backed projects gross over $4 billion worldwide collectively, with standout performers like Bohemian Rhapsody and Avatar: The Way of Water driving revenue amid shifting release strategies. The COVID-19 pandemic profoundly influenced operations, causing production halts, release delays for films like Free Guy (originally slated for 2020), and the adoption of hybrid models pairing limited theatrical runs with simultaneous streaming on platforms like Disney+ and Hulu to mitigate theater closures.
Key films from this period include:

Bohemian Rhapsody (2018): Released on October 31, 2018, and directed by Bryan Singer, this biographical music drama chronicled the rise of Queen and Freddie Mercury, starring Rami Malek. TSG Entertainment provided co-financing in association with 20th Century Fox and Regency Enterprises. The film earned critical acclaim for its performances and soundtrack, winning four Academy Awards including Best Actor for Malek, and grossed over $910 million worldwide, becoming one of the highest-grossing musical biopics.

The Predator (2018): Directed by Shane Black and released on September 14, 2018, this sci-fi action film revived the franchise with a story of alien hunters invading Earth. TSG co-financed the $88 million production alongside Davis Entertainment and 20th Century Fox. Despite mixed reviews for its chaotic narrative, it achieved a worldwide box office of approximately $160 million.

Ford v Ferrari (2019): Released on November 15, 2019, under director James Mangold, this sports drama depicted the 1960s rivalry between Ford and Ferrari at the Le Mans race, featuring Matt Damon and Christian Bale. TSG Entertainment co-financed in association with Chernin Entertainment and Turnpike Films. Praised for its high-octane racing sequences and historical accuracy, the film received four Oscar nominations and grossed $225 million globally.

Underwater (2020): This sci-fi horror film, directed by William Eubank and released on January 10, 2020, followed a deep-sea crew (led by Kristen Stewart) battling mysterious creatures after an earthquake. TSG provided financing support through Chernin Entertainment. Released just before widespread theater shutdowns, it faced truncated runs due to the pandemic, earning $40.9 million worldwide against an $80 million budget.

Free Guy (2021): Directed by Shawn Levy and released on August 13, 2021, the action-comedy starred Ryan Reynolds as an NPC in a video game world who gains sentience. Delayed from 2020 by COVID-19 production issues, TSG co-financed alongside 21 Laps Entertainment and Maximum Effort, with a hybrid theatrical-streaming rollout on Disney+ and Hulu. It received positive reviews for its humor and effects, grossing $331 million worldwide and highlighting the viability of post-pandemic hybrid releases.

The Menu (2022): Released on November 18, 2022, and directed by Mark Mylod, this black comedy horror starred Anya Taylor-Joy and Ralph Fiennes in a satirical tale of elite dining gone wrong. TSG Entertainment financed in association with Hyperobject Industries and Gary Sanchez Productions for Searchlight Pictures. Acclaimed for its sharp social commentary (88% on Rotten Tomatoes), it earned $79 million worldwide.

Avatar: The Way of Water (2022): James Cameron's epic sci-fi sequel, released on December 16, 2022, continued the story of the Na'vi on Pandora, starring Sam Worthington and Zoe Saldana. Multiple delays from 2021 due to pandemic-related production challenges pushed its release; TSG co-financed the $350460 million project with Lightstorm Entertainment. It garnered praise for visual innovation and grossed $2.32 billion worldwide, becoming the third-highest-grossing film ever and a major post-COVID box office recovery milestone.

2023present

In the post-acquisition era under Disney, TSG Entertainment continued its role as a key financier for 20th Century Studios productions, emphasizing collaborations on high-profile franchises and auteur-driven projects following the January 2024 settlement of its lawsuit against Disney over profit-sharing and streaming distribution practices. This period marked a shift toward integrated financing within Disney's ecosystem, supporting both theatrical blockbusters and prestige films amid evolving release strategies influenced by hybrid streaming models.
Poor Things (2023), directed by Yorgos Lanthimos, is a black comedy science fiction film released on December 8, 2023, with TSG Entertainment serving as a primary financier and producer in association with Searchlight Pictures and Element Pictures. The film grossed $111.9 million worldwide against a $35 million budget, establishing strong commercial performance for an indie-leaning title. It received widespread acclaim, winning four Academy Awards, including Best Actress for Emma Stone and Best Production Design, along with Best Actress at the Golden Globes and BAFTAs.
All of Us Strangers (2023), directed by Andrew Haigh, is a romantic fantasy drama released on December 22, 2023, financed by TSG Entertainment in partnership with Searchlight Pictures and Film4. The limited-release film earned $20.2 million globally, prioritizing critical reception over broad box office appeal with nominations for three BAFTAs, including Outstanding British Film.
A Haunting in Venice (2023), directed by and starring Kenneth Branagh as Hercule Poirot, is a mystery horror film released on September 15, 2023, with TSG Entertainment providing financing alongside 20th Century Studios and The Mark Gordon Company. It achieved $220.6 million worldwide, contributing to the Poirot franchise's success while exploring supernatural themes in a post-World War II setting.
In 2024, TSG's financing supported a mix of franchise expansions and original stories, reinforcing its partnership with Disney post-settlement. Kinds of Kindness (2024), another anthology black comedy directed by Yorgos Lanthimos, premiered on June 21, 2024, with TSG as a co-financier through Searchlight Pictures and Element Pictures. The film grossed $16.4 million worldwide, earning praise for its provocative triptych structure and a Best Actor win for Jesse Plemons at Cannes.
Kingdom of the Planet of the Apes (2024), directed by Wes Ball, is a science fiction action film released on May 10, 2024, financed by TSG Entertainment in collaboration with 20th Century Studios as the fourth installment in the rebooted Planet of the Apes series. It delivered $397 million worldwide, underscoring the franchise's enduring appeal with advanced motion-capture technology.
Deadpool & Wolverine (2024), directed by Shawn Levy, is a superhero action comedy released on July 26, 2024, with TSG Entertainment financing the Marvel Studios production in association with 20th Century Studios and Bona Film Group. The R-rated blockbuster shattered records with $1.34 billion worldwide, becoming the highest-grossing R-rated film ever and revitalizing the MCU's box office momentum.
TSG's pipeline includes Avatar: Fire and Ash (2025), directed by James Cameron, slated for December 19, 2025, with TSG providing financing for the third Avatar installment through 20th Century Studios and Lightstorm Entertainment, building on the series' visual spectacle and global dominance. 28 Years Later (2025), directed by Danny Boyle, is a post-apocalyptic horror film released on June 20, 2025, funded by TSG Entertainment in support of Sony Pictures and DNA Films as the third entry in the 28 Days Later series. It grossed $149.8 million worldwide and received positive reviews (89% on Rotten Tomatoes) for its tense atmosphere and thematic depth. These projects exemplify TSG's strategic focus on high-impact sequels amid Disney's expanded theatrical and streaming ecosystem.
