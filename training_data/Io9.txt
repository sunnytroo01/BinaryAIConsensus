 io9 is an American digital media blog dedicated to science fiction, fantasy, and related pop culture, encompassing coverage of books, comics, movies, television, games, and theme parks through a lens that explores their cultural and futuristic implications. Launched in January 2008 under Gawker Media by editor Annalee Newitz, the site was established on the core principle that science fiction is mainstream entertainment accessible to everyone, rather than a niche interest, and it initially blended reporting on science, technology, and speculative fiction to illustrate how sci-fi concepts manifest in reality. Its tagline, "We Come From the Future," underscores this forward-looking ethos, emphasizing content that anticipates cultural trends and innovations.
Originally part of Gawker Media's network of blogs founded by Nick Denton, io9 quickly gained prominence for fostering an inclusive community around genre storytelling, avoiding insider jargon and focusing on broad appeal. In 2015, as Gawker restructured its properties, io9 merged operations with Gizmodo, consolidating science and space coverage under the latter while sharpening io9's emphasis on fantasy and sci-fi pop culture; Newitz served as Gizmodo's editor-in-chief during this transition, and the site adopted the URL io9.gizmodo.com later that year. Following Gawker Media's bankruptcy and asset sale in 2016, the blog became part of Gizmodo Media Group under Univision Communications, which acquired the portfolio for $135 million (excluding the flagship Gawker.com). Ownership shifted again in 2019 to G/O Media, a private equity-backed entity, and most recently in 2024, Gizmodoincluding io9was sold to Swiss publisher Keleops Media, ensuring its continued operation as a key voice in genre media.
Over its history, io9 has evolved from a broad speculative outlet to a specialized hub for nerd culture analysis, featuring contributions from writers like Charlie Jane Anders and producing influential lists, reviews, and essays that highlight the genre's societal impact. Notable for its anniversary retrospectives, such as the 2018 tenth-anniversary celebration, the site remains active in 2025, delivering timely previews of upcoming releases like films, series, and games while maintaining its commitment to diverse, inclusive perspectives on futuristic narratives.

Background

Founding and Launch

io9 was launched on January 2, 2008, as the fifteenth blog in the Gawker Media network, marking the company's final standalone site debut before shifting strategies. The site was established on the Gawker platform at io9.gawker.com, integrating seamlessly into the broader ecosystem of Gawker's content management and distribution tools.
The blog was co-founded by science journalist Annalee Newitz and author Charlie Jane Anders, with Newitz taking on the role of primary editor and driving its early direction. This collaboration stemmed from discussions initiated months earlier, positioning io9 as a dedicated space for exploring speculative and scientific themes.
Gawker Media founder Nick Denton conceived the idea for io9, drawing from his personal passion for science fictionparticularly the works of Isaac Asimovand aiming to create a platform that merged genre storytelling with forward-looking futurism and real-world science. Denton's vision emphasized inclusivity, seeking to appeal to a wide audience interested in imaginative narratives and technological possibilities.
At launch, io9 operated with a lean team of three full-time writers supplemented by a handful of freelancers, enabling agile content production focused on the site's nascent goals. This modest setup reflected Gawker Media's model of rapid, voice-driven blogging while laying the groundwork for io9's growth within the network.

Original Mission and Scope

io9 launched in January 2008 with a core mission to explore science fiction, fantasy, futurism, science, and technology as interconnected fields that collectively shape contemporary culture and envision possible futures. Founded by Annalee Newitz and Charlie Jane Anders under Gawker Media, the site aimed to provide readers with a vision grounded in scientific reality while embracing speculative narratives, acting as a "visionary watchdog" against overhyped or stagnant predictions of tomorrow. This blended approach treated imaginative genres not as escapist entertainment but as essential thought experiments reflecting and influencing societal progress.
The target audience encompassed fans of speculative fiction, technology enthusiasts, and pop culture followers who desired in-depth analysis rather than superficial trivia or nostalgia-driven content. io9 sought to appeal to neophiles and early adopters interested in provocative explorations of futurism, positioning science fiction as mainstream cultural literacy accessible to broad audiences beyond hardcore genre enthusiasts. By emphasizing conceptual depth, the site encouraged readers to engage with ideas that challenged everyday assumptions about innovation and human potential.
At its inception, io9's content pillars revolved around book reviews of innovative science fiction and fantasy works, previews of upcoming films and television series, interviews with authors, creators, and scientists, and essays dissecting the societal implications of emerging technologies. These elements covered speculative media across books, movies, TV, comics, games, and art, alongside non-fictional advancements in engineering, design, and architecture. Unlike other Gawker Media properties, which often aggregated general news or focused on gadget-centric reviews, io9 distinguished itself through its commitment to thoughtful, speculative coverage of imaginative futures rather than rote reporting or consumer tech rundowns. The site's inaugural manifesto declared this ethos with the bold proclamation that io9 came "from the future," underscoring its role in unveiling shockingly different possibilities.

Organizational Development

Independent Era (20082015)

During its independent era under Gawker Media, io9 experienced rapid growth in readership and influence within the science fiction community from 2008 to 2012, establishing itself as a key voice in speculative fiction discourse. Launched as a standalone blog in early 2008, the site quickly gained traction by blending science journalism with pop culture analysis, attracting fans eager for in-depth coverage of emerging trends. By 2010, io9's U.S. audience had surged 118 percent year-over-year, outpacing many sister Gawker sites and solidifying its role as a hub for sci-fi enthusiasts.
Operationally, io9 functioned as a fully independent blog with its own dedicated URL (io9.gawker.com), generating revenue through Gawker Media's centralized advertising model while relying heavily on freelance contributions to supplement a small core staff. This structure allowed for agile content production, with writers drawing from a network of freelancers to cover niche topics in science and fantasy. Gawker's overall ad revenue reached $45 million in 2014, supporting io9's expansion without direct financial oversight from the parent company beyond shared infrastructure. Freelancers played a crucial role, enabling the site to publish diverse perspectives on speculative genres amid Gawker's emphasis on high-traffic, voice-driven blogging.
Key milestones during this period included io9's extensive coverage of the rising Marvel Cinematic Universe, beginning with the 2008 release of Iron Man and continuing through films like The Avengers in 2012, which helped position the site as a go-to resource for blockbuster sci-fi analysis. The blog also became a prominent platform for discussions around the Hugo Awards, offering previews, nominee breakdowns, and post-ceremony recaps that engaged the awards' dedicated fanbase and amplified debates within the speculative fiction world. These efforts contributed to io9's reputation for bridging mainstream entertainment with literary science fiction.
Annalee Newitz served as editor-in-chief from io9's founding in 2008 until November 2015, overseeing its transformation into a influential outlet for genre news. Charlie Jane Anders, who had been managing editor since the site's early days, then stepped in as editor, maintaining the blog's focus on innovative storytelling and cultural critique through April 2016. Under their leadership, io9 became a go-to source for speculative fiction news, achieving millions of monthly pageviews by 2014 as part of Gawker Media's network, which collectively hit nearly 500 million pageviews that year.

Merger and Integration (2015present)

In January 2015, Gawker Media announced the merger of io9 with Gizmodo to form a unified "Future Initiative" aimed at enhancing collaborative content production between the science fiction-focused io9 and the technology-oriented Gizmodo, addressing overlapping coverage in areas like futurism and innovation. This restructuring combined their editorial teams under io9 editor Annalee Newitz's leadership, while maintaining distinct brand identities, to prioritize deeper reporting and analysis amid intensifying competition in digital media.
Following the merger, io9 transitioned to operating as a subsite at io9.gizmodo.com, sharing resources such as writers, editors, and infrastructure with Gizmodo, yet preserving its editorial autonomy in covering speculative fiction, science, and pop culture. Ownership of the merged entity shifted in August 2016 when Univision Communications acquired Gawker Media's non-Gawker.com assets, including Gizmodo and io9, for approximately $135 million as part of a bankruptcy settlement. In April 2019, Univision sold Gizmodo Media Groupwhich encompassed io9to private equity firm Great Hill Partners, rebranding it as G/O Media to streamline operations and focus on audience growth. Further consolidation occurred in June 2024 when G/O Media divested Gizmodo, including io9, to European publisher Keleops Media for an undisclosed sum, positioning it within a portfolio emphasizing tech and innovation content.
Post-merger, io9 adapted by amplifying its emphasis on pop culture intersections with science and technology, such as reviews of sci-fi media and explorations of emerging tech in entertainment, while integrating more closely with Gizmodo's gadget and innovation reporting to create cross-promotional content ecosystems. This evolution allowed io9 to leverage shared platforms for broader reach without diluting its niche focus on imaginative genres.
As of 2025, io9 remains an active subsite under Keleops Media's ownership of Gizmodo, continuing to contribute original articles, newsletters, and features to the network's portfolio, with ongoing adaptations to digital trends like enhanced social media engagement and event coverage.

Content and Features

Core Topics Covered

io9 primarily addresses science fiction literature, fantasy media, superhero franchises such as Star Wars, Marvel, and DC, and horror genres in its articles. These areas form the backbone of its speculative journalism, with regular roundups of new releases and analyses that highlight emerging trends in genre storytelling.
Originally rooted in science, technology, and futurism, io9's coverage evolved post-2015 toward heavier emphasis on pop culture analysis following its merger with Gizmodo, integrating broader entertainment perspectives while retaining its speculative core. This shift aligned with its foundational mission to make science fiction accessible, expanding to critique cultural phenomena through genre lenses.
Recurring coverage includes book-to-film adaptations, such as previews of upcoming projects from novels like The Bright Sword and Seveneves, alongside in-depth looks at genre awards like the Hugo and Nebula, often featuring winner announcements, eligibility controversies, and tributes to influential works. io9 also provides cultural critiques of speculative narratives, examining how they reflect real-world issues like power structures and societal norms.
A distinctive angle in io9's reporting lies in the intersection of fiction with real-world science, such as discussions on AI ethics drawn from sci-fi tropes like artificial intelligence threats and their societal implications. As of 2025, this focus has intensified on TV and streaming series, anime adaptations, and gaming within speculative contexts, with previews of projects like Mobile Suit Gundam sequels and horror titles such as M3GAN 2.0.

Signature Series and Formats

io9 has established several recurring content formats that emphasize curated selections, original storytelling, and expert insights into science fiction, fantasy, and related genres. Among these, the site's annual "Best of" compilations stand out as a hallmark, aggregating staff and contributor picks to highlight standout works in television, film, and literature from the preceding year or decade. For instance, the 2018 edition of io9's Greatest TV Shows of the Past 10 Years ranked HBO's Westworld at number 8 for its philosophical exploration of consciousness and Hulu's The Handmaid's Tale at number 9 for its dystopian critique of authoritarianism, drawing on a decade of genre evolution to provide readers with a reflective overview. These lists often extend to year-end roundups, such as the comprehensive 2022 Year in Review series, which encompassed categories like best sci-fi books, superhero movies, and fantasy TV episodes to encapsulate trends and achievements.
A key collaborative feature is io9's integration of short fiction from Lightspeed Magazine, presenting one original story each month to blend journalistic coverage with immersive narrative. This series, ongoing since 2012, spotlights emerging and established authors in speculative fiction, such as the October 2025 selection "Drosera regina" by A.L. Goldfuss, a fantasy tale exploring monstrous allure and hidden identities that aligns with io9's focus on eerie, genre-bending prose. By hosting these pieces directly on the site, io9 fosters a direct pipeline for readers to access professional-level short stories, often accompanied by author spotlights that delve into creative processes.
The site's interview series provides in-depth conversations with creators, emphasizing adaptations, world-building, and behind-the-scenes insights that recur across multiple seasons and franchises. Notable examples include io9's 2025 discussion with director Edgar Wright on his adaptation of Stephen King's The Running Man, where he detailed narrative expansions and fidelity to the source material's themes of media spectacle. Similarly, early interviews with Star Trek writers Alex Kurtzman and Roberto Orci in 2009 explored unused scenes and character arcs, such as a potential William Shatner cameo, highlighting io9's role in chronicling franchise developments. These dialogues often recur with key figures, offering longitudinal perspectives on evolving projects.
Chronological guides form another signature format, offering structured timelines to navigate complex multimedia franchises without delving into speculative elements. io9's recurring Star Wars overviews, for example, compile movies and TV series in narrative order, such as the 2024 guide listing entries from The Acolyte (set centuries before The Phantom Menace) to The Mandalorian (post-Return of the Jedi), aiding readers in understanding the saga's expansive chronology. Updated periodically to reflect new releases, these guides prioritize canon alignment and accessibility.
Opinion and analysis essays round out io9's formats, providing thematic deep dives into genre trends through critical essays. Recurring explorations of temporal mechanics in sci-fi television, such as the 2019 analysis of Star Trek: Discovery's time-jump twist, examine how "timey-wimey" narrativesreferencing non-linear plotting and paradoxesenhance emotional stakes and continuity across episodes. These pieces often synthesize broader patterns, like the resurgence of multiverse motifs in shows from Doctor Who to Loki, without exhaustive listings, to illuminate evolving storytelling techniques.

Editorial Team

Founders and Early Editors

io9 was co-founded in 2008 by Annalee Newitz and Charlie Jane Anders under Gawker Media, with Newitz serving as the initial editor-in-chief from 2008 to 2015. Newitz, a science journalist with prior experience as culture editor at the San Francisco Bay Guardian from 2000 to 2004 and recipient of a Knight Science Journalism Fellowship at MIT, brought a focus on blending rigorous science reporting with speculative fiction. In January 2014, she transitioned to editor-in-chief of Gizmodo while maintaining involvement with io9 until her departure in November 2015, following the site's merger with Gizmodo.
Charlie Jane Anders joined as co-founder and the first hire in 2008, becoming editor-in-chief of io9 from 2015 to 2016. With a background in speculative fiction and pop culture criticism, Anders had hosted the monthly spoken-word event Writers With Drinks since 2001, fostering a vibrant community around genre literature. She departed io9 in April 2016 to concentrate on her novel-writing career. Anders's tenure emphasized engaging with science fiction fandom through insightful analysis of media and literature.
Newitz's contributions centered on futurism and science essays, such as explorations of emerging technologies and their societal implications, which helped establish io9 as a hub for thoughtful speculation on real-world advancements. Anders, meanwhile, excelled in book reviews and pop culture critiques, including essays on films like Transformers: Revenge of the Fallen, while building community through interactive features that amplified fan discussions. Their collaborative vision integrated science journalism with genre commentary, creating content that appealed to both experts and enthusiasts.
The early team was a small core group, including contributors like Esther Inglis-Arkell for quirky science pieces and Meredith Woerner for television recaps, operating within Gawker Media's experimental environment. This lean structure prioritized diverse voices in speculative genres, with a gender-balanced staff from the start to promote inclusive conversations among commenters and writers, reflecting io9's commitment to broadening access to science fiction and futurism.

Current Leadership and Staff

As of 2025, io9 operates as a specialized vertical within Gizmodo, with a compact editorial team of approximately four to six core writers who collaborate closely with Gizmodo's broader staff to produce content on science fiction, fantasy, and related pop culture topics. This structure emphasizes a mix of full-time reporters and senior editors, supplemented by freelancers for specialized assignments, allowing for focused coverage while leveraging Gizmodo's resources for multimedia and investigative pieces.
James Whitbrook serves as the Deputy Editor (also referred to as Lead Editor) of io9, a position he has held since 2021 after joining the site as an intern in 2014 and advancing to staff writer. Whitbrook specializes in in-depth coverage of franchises like Star Wars and Star Trek, as well as anime and broader entertainment trends, often contributing analysis on their cultural impact.
Cheryl Eddy has been the News Editor and Senior Writer at io9 since 2014, where she oversees daily news updates and performs copy editing duties. Her work primarily focuses on horror genres, science fiction films, and related breaking developments in speculative media.
Germain Lussier acts as Senior Reporter, a role he has maintained since joining io9 and Gizmodo in 2015, with an emphasis on franchise deep dives, film reviews, and interviews within the entertainment sector.
Sabina Graves functions as Staff Reporter, contributing to io9's coverage of pop culture through interviews, genre film and television analysis, and features on theme parks and immersive experiences.
The team has seen no major personnel changes following the December 2024 appointment of Rose Pastore as Editor-in-Chief of Gizmodo, ensuring continuity in io9's leadership under the site's integrated operations.

Notable Events

Staff Transitions

In early 2015, io9 co-founder Annalee Newitz transitioned to the role of editor-in-chief at Gizmodo, prompting Charlie Jane Anders to assume the position of editor-in-chief at io9. This handover marked a pivotal moment in io9's evolution, allowing Anders to steer the site's focus on science fiction, fantasy, and related pop culture while integrating more closely with Gizmodo's broader tech and science coverage.
Anders' tenure ended in April 2016 when she departed to concentrate on her novel-writing career, leading to the appointment of Rob Bricken as editor, a role he held until 2018. Bricken, previously a senior editor at io9 and founder of the irreverent pop culture site Topless Robot, introduced a humor-infused approach to coverage, infusing articles with witty, sarcastic commentary that amplified the site's engaging tone on genre topics. This shift refined io9's pop culture analysis, making it more accessible and entertaining while preserving its core commitment to speculative fiction.
In July 2018, following Bricken's departure to pursue freelance opportunities, Jill Pantozzi was promoted from managing editor to deputy editor in charge of io9, serving in that capacity through 2021. Drawing from her background at The Mary Suea site dedicated to inclusive coverage of women and underrepresented voices in geek culturePantozzi emphasized diversity and equity in genre discussions, fostering deeper explorations of representation in science fiction and fantasy media. Her leadership further honed io9's pop culture lens, prioritizing thoughtful critiques of inclusivity amid evolving industry trends.
By December 2021, amid ongoing transitions at parent company G/O Mediawhich had acquired Gizmodo Media Group in 2019James Whitbrook was promoted from staff writer to deputy editor, taking direct oversight of io9. Whitbrook, a long-time contributor since 2014, continued to build on prior editorial foundations, maintaining the site's refined emphasis on pop culture while navigating corporate shifts.
In November 2023, G/O Media conducted layoffs affecting 23 editorial staff across its properties, including io9 and Gizmodo. Among those laid off was Jill Pantozzi, who had continued contributing to the site after her 2021 departure from the deputy editor role, and Angely Mercado, the last dedicated staff member for certain io9 sub-sections. These cuts were part of broader cost-saving measures at the company. As of 2025, James Whitbrook remains io9's lead editor. These successive handovers collectively sharpened io9's identity, evolving its direction through distinct editorial voices that enhanced its influence in genre journalism.

2023 AI Content Controversy

In July 2023, io9, a Gizmodo Media Group site under G/O Media, published an AI-generated article titled "A Chronological List of Star Wars Movies & TV Shows" without prior editorial review, which contained significant factual errors including misordered titles, omissions of Disney+ series such as Andor, Obi-Wan Kenobi, and The Book of Boba Fett, and the erroneous placement of The Clone Wars after The Rise of Skywalker. The article, credited to "Gizmodo Bot" and produced using tools like Google's Bard and OpenAI's ChatGPT, was part of G/O Media's experimental push into AI content generation across its properties.
The publication drew immediate backlash from readers, io9 staff, and media outlets for its inaccuracies, lack of human oversight, and undisclosed reliance on AI, with a Variety report highlighting how it undermined the site's credibility on a core topic like Star Wars. io9 deputy editor James Whitbrook publicly denounced the piece on social media as "shoddily written," "embarrassing," and a direct threat to the outlet's "authority and integrity," noting that staff were only informed of its posting about 10 minutes in advance and had no involvement in its creation or editing. The Gizmodo Media Group Union echoed these concerns, labeling the AI use "unethical" and urging readers to skip articles with bot bylines due to risks of misinformation and plagiarism.
In response, io9 quickly removed the article and provided G/O Media with a detailed list of corrections, while the parent company acknowledged the errors and committed to ethical AI guidelines, including mandatory human review for future outputs. G/O Media's editorial director, Merrill Brown, defended the initiative as a "modest test" aimed at efficiency but emphasized gathering staff feedback to refine processes, leading to heightened internal policies requiring top editors to oversee AI-generated content.
The incident, occurring amid broader G/O Media experiments with AI for tasks like content summaries and research, prompted no further major controversies involving undisclosed AI articles on io9 through 2025, though it fueled industry-wide debates on the ethics of automation in journalism, including concerns over job displacement, disinformation, and the balance between cost savings and editorial integrity.
