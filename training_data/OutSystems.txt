OutSystems
 Ara Eve Leo Sal 
1x
 OutSystems is an AI-powered low-code development platform that enables organizations to rapidly build, deploy, and manage enterprise-grade mobile and web applications, as well as custom AI agents, with full software development lifecycle (SDLC) governance. Core AI-assisted development features include OutSystems Mentor, an AI-driven tool that generates full-stack applications from natural language prompts or requirements files using agentic AI architecture to automate the SDLC including ideation, design, development, deployment, and monitoring with real-time code validation, security checks, iterative refinements, and best practice enforcement; and Agent Workbench, a low-code tool for building, orchestrating, and governing custom AI agents with visual drag-and-drop workflows, integration with LLMs and enterprise systems, multi-agent orchestration, observability, and human-in-the-loop controls. Founded in 2001 in Lisbon, Portugal, by Paulo Rosado, Rui Pereira, and Rodrigo Coutinho, the company pioneered the low-code market with a mission to empower every organization to innovate through software by simplifying complex development processes and accelerating delivery up to 10 times faster than traditional coding.
Headquartered in Boston, Massachusetts, with primary operations based in Linda-a-Velha, Portugal, OutSystems employs approximately 2,000 people globally (as of 2025) and serves thousands of customers across 80 countries and 21 industries, supported by over 500 partners and a community of more than 850,000 members. In 2025, the company reported surpassing 500 million in annual revenue and appointed Woodson Martin as its Chief Executive Officer in May, succeeding founder Paulo Rosado, who transitioned to Chairman of the Board, to drive further expansion in AI-driven innovation.
The platform integrates full-stack development capabilities, including visual programming, AI-assisted coding through OutSystems Mentor and Agent Workbench, and robust security features compliant with standards like GDPR, allowing IT teams and business users to create mission-critical applications for use cases ranging from customer portals to internal automation tools. OutSystems has been recognized as a leader in low-code for enterprise application development, earning spots on Forbes' Cloud 100 list for three consecutive years through 2023, named a Leader in the 2025 Forrester Wave for Low-Code Application Platforms, named a Leader in the 2025 Gartner Magic Quadrant for Enterprise Low-Code Application Platforms for the ninth consecutive year with the highest score in Ability to Execute and an emphasis on scalable, AI-driven development for complex enterprise applications and agentic AI, ranked #1 in G2's Grid Report for Enterprise Low-Code Development Platforms in early 2026 with a 99/100 satisfaction score from nearly 1,500 user reviews, and rated 4.5/5 on Gartner Peer Insights from 1,044 reviews with praise for rapid development, ease of use, integration, and scalability alongside some user notes on debugging challenges and performance issues in very large environments, and powering applications that drive tangible business value for clients in sectors like finance, healthcare, and manufacturing.

History

Founding and early development

OutSystems was founded in March 2001 in Lisbon, Portugal, by Paulo Rosado, Rodrigo Coutinho, and Rui Pereira, who sought to revolutionize software development by emphasizing visual tools that would enable rapid, cost-effective creation and modification of applications. As seasoned IT professionals, the founders identified key inefficiencies in traditional coding practices and aimed to democratize development for enterprises through a platform that prioritized speed and simplicity over manual programming.
The company's initial offering, Hub Edition 1.0, launched in 2002 as a web-based application builder focused on simplifying enterprise software, particularly for Portuguese businesses in sectors like telecommunications, with built-in support for mobile (WAP and SMS) and early cloud (ASP) integrations. This product secured its first customer that year, marking the start of organic adoption among local enterprises. In 2003, the first full commercial version, Hub Edition 2.0, was released, introducing essential components such as Service Center for management and Service Studio for visual modeling, which laid the foundation for the platform's proprietary visual programming language and emphasized drag-and-drop interfaces to reduce development complexity.
Throughout the mid-2000s, OutSystems achieved key milestones that supported its growth, including 2006 enhancements for larger development teams via compare-and-merge tools, which facilitated expansion into international markets beyond Portugal as demand grew for scalable low-code solutions. By 2012, the release of Agile Platform 6.0 introduced ready-to-use templates and hybrid mobile app capabilities, solidifying its position in the emerging low-code category. The company faced initial challenges in bootstrapping operations, relying primarily on self-sustained revenue from Portuguese and early global clients supplemented by early-stage funding, while iterating on its visual language to ensure enterprise-grade reliability and governance. During this era, OutSystems began transitioning to a cloud-native model, with cloud deployment options added in 2011 to enhance accessibility.

Growth, funding, and acquisitions

OutSystems' early funding included a seed round of 1 million in March 2001, followed by Series B investments of 2.2 million in March 2005 and $3.2 million in June 2007 led by Armilar Venture Partners, along with a 10 million Series C in 2011, which supported initial product development and local expansion.
The company experienced significant financial growth starting in the mid-2010s, marked by a series of major funding rounds that propelled its expansion. In February 2016, the company secured $55 million in a Series C funding round led by North Bridge Growth Equity (now Guidepost Growth Equity), which supported enhancements to its low-code platform and international scaling. This was followed by a substantial $360 million investment in June 2018 from KKR and Goldman Sachs, valuing OutSystems at over $1 billion and achieving unicorn status. The funds were directed toward accelerating product innovation and global market penetration.
The company's momentum continued with a $150 million Series E round in February 2021, co-led by Abdiel Capital and Tiger Global Management, which elevated its valuation to $9.5 billion. This investment, involving existing backers like KKR and Goldman Sachs, focused on expanding R&D and customer acquisition amid rising demand for low-code solutions. By 2023, subsequent rounds had pushed total funding to over $800 million, though no new major investments were announced through 2025.
In terms of strategic expansion, OutSystems made a key acquisition in November 2022 when it purchased Ionic, a leading open-source platform for cross-platform mobile app development, to integrate advanced mobile capabilities into its ecosystem. The deal, terms undisclosed, aimed to enhance hybrid app development for enterprise users without disrupting Ionic's community-driven model. No further significant acquisitions occurred by late 2025.
OutSystems' operational growth reflected its financial success, with employee numbers reaching 1,875 by August 2023, up from earlier years, supporting expanded operations across engineering, sales, and support. The customer base grew to include over 1,000 enterprise clients by 2023, featuring major organizations such as Bosch, which used the platform for digital factory initiatives, and Heineken, which scaled app development across more than 80 countries. This enterprise adoption underscored OutSystems' role in enabling rapid application delivery for large-scale deployments.

Products and services

OutSystems Platform

The OutSystems Platform is an AI-powered low-code development environment designed for building, deploying, and managing enterprise-grade web and mobile applications. It enables developers and business users to create custom software solutions with minimal hand-coding, focusing on visual modeling to accelerate the entire application lifecycle from design to maintenance. This approach supports mission-critical applications that require scalability, security, and integration with existing enterprise systems.
At its core, the platform features a visual development environment that utilizes drag-and-drop interfaces for designing user interfaces, logic, and data structures. Key components include pre-built templates for common application patterns, such as user authentication or data entry forms, which allow teams to start projects quickly without building from scratch. Additionally, it provides seamless integration capabilities with databases like SQL Server and Oracle, APIs from services such as Salesforce and SAP, and legacy systems through pre-built connectors and web services. These elements ensure that applications can incorporate real-time data and processes from diverse sources, reducing custom coding needs.
Deployment options on the OutSystems Platform are flexible, supporting cloud-native environments on AWS and Azure for rapid setup and global scalability. It also accommodates on-premises and hybrid deployments, allowing organizations to maintain control over sensitive data while leveraging automated scaling to handle varying workloads. Built-in DevOps pipelines automate testing, continuous integration, and deployment, enabling one-click publishing across environments with governance controls.
In practice, the platform excels in use cases involving rapid prototyping of business applications, such as custom CRM extensions that tailor customer interactions or workflow automations that streamline internal processes like approval chains. For instance, organizations use it to develop employee portals or compliance tracking tools that integrate with existing ERP systems. This facilitates up to 10 times faster development compared to traditional coding methods, allowing teams to deliver functional prototypes in days rather than months. The platform's enhancements from AI tools further support this speed, though detailed AI capabilities are covered separately.

AI and mobile extensions

OutSystems Mentor is an AI-driven assistant integrated into the platform to support developers throughout the software development lifecycle (SDLC). It generates full-stack applications from natural language prompts or requirements files in minutes, using an agentic AI architecture to automate the SDLC, including ideation, design, development, deployment, and monitoring. Key capabilities include real-time code validation, security checks, iterative refinements, and enforcement of best practices, accelerating development while maintaining enterprise-grade quality and security. Introduced in October 2024 and generally available as of January 2025, it provides contextual guidance for logic suggestions and model-driven code generation, ensuring applications meet security, performance, and architecture standards without manual coding errors.
Building on this foundation, Agent Workbench enables the creation of custom AI agents for agentic AI workflows through a low-code environment. It supports visual drag-and-drop workflows, integration with LLMs and enterprise systems, multi-agent orchestration, observability (tracking reasoning, quality, costs), and human-in-the-loop controls. Agents can automate complex tasks like workflows, personalization, and operations. Announced at the ONE 2025 conference with general availability in September 2025, it offers a unified environment for agent orchestration, from design and testing to deployment and monitoring, with built-in security and compliance features to prevent uncontrolled AI sprawl.
In mobile development, OutSystems integrated the Ionic framework following its 2022 acquisition, facilitating the building of hybrid mobile applications that deliver native performance. This leverages Capacitor for cross-platform native runtime and Angular for web-based UI components, enabling seamless deployment of web apps to iOS and Android while maintaining enterprise-grade scalability.
These AI-assisted app development features enable up to 10x faster development compared to traditional approaches, bridge talent gaps by empowering existing teams, ensure governance and security, and support scalable enterprise AI adoption. Benefits include rapid prototyping, reduced repetitive work, and reliable production-grade apps. They are backed by case studies (e.g., HEINEKEN, Bosch) and patents, with official claims highlighting strong ROI (e.g., 363%) and practical enterprise use. Recent innovations in AI-powered app generation have significantly reduced development timelines, as demonstrated in Bosch's implementation where generative AI tools integrated with OutSystems accelerated secure app creation under IT governance. This approach ensures enterprise AI deployments include full lifecycle management, from prompt-based generation to monitoring, with up to 50% reductions in development time reported across similar use cases.

Technology

Architecture and infrastructure

OutSystems employs a layered architecture that separates concerns into distinct presentation, business logic, and data layers, enabling developers to build modular, extensible applications while supporting microservices patterns for enhanced scalability and maintainability. The presentation layer handles user interfaces and interactions, the business logic layer manages core processes and rules, and the data layer oversees storage and retrieval, allowing for independent scaling and updates across components without disrupting the overall system. This design promotes reusability and extensibility, as modules can be developed and deployed as independent services, aligning with modern software engineering principles for enterprise-grade applications.
The platform's cloud-native infrastructure is built on Kubernetes for container orchestration, facilitating automated deployment, management, and scaling of applications in dynamic environments. It incorporates auto-scaling capabilities to handle varying workloads by dynamically adjusting resources, such as container instances and database capacity, ensuring high availability across multiple availability zones. Integrated CI/CD pipelines support continuous integration and delivery, automating builds, testing, and deployments to streamline development workflows and reduce time-to-market. OutSystems supports cloud deployments on AWS for its hosted editions and self-managed installations on various clouds, including AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud, to provide flexibility and avoid vendor lock-in.
Security is embedded throughout the architecture, with built-in compliance to standards such as GDPR and SOC 2, ensuring data protection and regulatory adherence. All data is encrypted at rest and in transit using TLS and platform-managed keys, while role-based access control (RBAC) enforces granular permissions at the application and infrastructure levels to prevent unauthorized access.
For extensibility, OutSystems adheres to open standards, including REST APIs for seamless integration with external services and direct SQL access for database operations. Developers can incorporate custom code in languages such as JavaScript for client-side enhancements, C# via .NET extensions, and Java through compatible plugins, enabling hybrid low-code and pro-code development without compromising platform integrity.

Development capabilities

OutSystems provides a visual development environment that enables developers and citizen developers to model applications through drag-and-drop interfaces, significantly accelerating the creation process compared to traditional coding methods. For user interface and experience (UI/UX) design, users can drag widgets, layouts, and blocks onto screens within the Service Studio IDE, allowing for responsive web and mobile layouts without manual coding. Logic flows are constructed using visual action flows and client actions, where developers connect nodes to define business rules, events, and processes in a flowchart-like manner, promoting intuitive workflow orchestration. Data modeling is handled through a visual entity designer that supports drag-and-drop creation of database structures, relationships, and attributes, bridging UI and backend seamlessly. These visual elements automatically generate underlying code in languages such as C# for server-side logic in web applications and JavaScript for client-side interfaces and mobile applications, ensuring production-ready, optimized output while abstracting complexity.
Collaboration is facilitated through integrated version control and team-oriented features that streamline multi-developer workflows. All application versions and modules are stored in a central repository, enabling branching, merging, and conflict resolution to support parallel development without overwriting changes. Team workspaces allow multiple users to collaborate in real-time, with features like shared editing and communication tools integrated into the platform, enhancing productivity across cross-functional teams. The Forge community serves as an open repository of over 8,000 reusable components, including UI elements, connectors, and business logic modules, which developers can download and integrate to reuse proven assets and reduce development time (as of November 2025).
Testing and governance tools are embedded to ensure application quality and compliance throughout the development lifecycle. Built-in unit testing frameworks allow developers to create and automate tests for individual actions and APIs, providing rapid feedback on functionality and enabling continuous validation during iterative builds. Performance monitoring is available via tools like Service Center, which tracks metrics such as exceptions, database queries, and response times in real-time, facilitating proactive optimization and troubleshooting. Lifecycle management encompasses the full spectrum from design to maintenance, with features like AI-driven architectural guidance, generative AI for code assistance, and support for agentic AI workflows, alongside CI/CD pipelines and versioning (incremental or major/minor) to govern deployments, security scans, and updates, ensuring scalable and compliant applications.
OutSystems strikes a balance between low-code, no-code, and pro-code paradigms, supporting hybrid development for enterprise-scale needs. Low-code and no-code users leverage drag-and-drop tools and pre-built templates for rapid prototyping and standard applications, while pro-code extensions allow integration of custom codesuch as .NET or Java snippetsdirectly into visual models for handling complex logic or third-party integrations. This hybrid approach enables citizen developers to build simple apps without coding expertise, while professional developers extend functionality seamlessly, optimizing resource allocation and maintenance for intricate systems.

Company overview

Leadership and operations

OutSystems was founded in 2001 by Paulo Rosado, Rodrigo Coutinho, and Rui Pereira, with Rosado serving as the original CEO until May 2025. Rosado, who led the company through its initial growth and establishment as a low-code leader, transitioned to the role of Chairman of the Board, continuing to provide strategic oversight. Coutinho and Pereira contributed significantly to the early technical direction, shaping the platform's foundational architecture for rapid application development.
In May 2025, OutSystems appointed Woodson Martin as CEO to drive AI integration and global expansion, leveraging his experience from Salesforce in scaling enterprise software solutions. The executive team includes key figures such as Luis Blando, Chief Product and Technology Officer since 2024, who focuses on platform innovation and AI advancements. Other senior leaders, including Andy Pemberton as Chief Revenue Officer and Carlos Alves as Chief Client Partner, support operational efficiency and customer growth.
In 2025, the company reported surpassing 500 million in annual revenue.
The company's operational model emphasizes significant investment in research and development to maintain technological leadership, alongside agile methodologies that enable rapid iteration and deployment. With approximately 2,000 employees worldwide as of 2025, OutSystems fosters an employee-centric culture recognized by Forbes as one of the top cloud companies to work for four consecutive years, prioritizing collaboration and customer success.
OutSystems operates on a subscription-based business model, offering tiered pricing for platform access starting at $36,300 USD per year, scaled by applications, users, and add-ons. Complementary professional services include implementation support and training to facilitate adoption and customization.

Global presence and impact

OutSystems maintains its headquarters in Boston, Massachusetts, with primary operations based in Linda-a-Velha, Portugal, and additional major offices in cities such as London, United Kingdom; Amsterdam, Netherlands; and Singapore. The company operates from over 15 locations worldwide, spanning the Americas, Europe, the Middle East, Africa, and Asia Pacific, enabling localized support for international clients.
The platform serves more than 2,000 enterprise customers across over 80 countries and 21 industries, facilitating rapid application development and digital transformation initiatives. A notable example is HEINEKEN, which leverages OutSystems to scale app development across more than 80 markets through a federated model, aiming to return one million hours to the business via automation and process improvements. This widespread adoption underscores OutSystems' role in enhancing operational efficiency for global organizations.
OutSystems has been recognized as a Leader in the Gartner Magic Quadrant for Enterprise Low-Code Application Platforms for the ninth consecutive year in 2025, a distinction it has held since 2017, reflecting its vision and execution in the low-code space. The company contributes to industry standards by integrating AI-driven tools into low-code development, promoting enterprise-wide AI adoption through platforms that support generative AI apps and custom agents with built-in governance. Such innovations help standardize secure, scalable low-code practices across sectors.
As of 2025, OutSystems holds a significant position in the low-code market, valued at approximately $37 billion and projected to grow to $264 billion by 2032 at a CAGR of 32.2%, with AI agents driving much of the expansion. This growth trajectory positions OutSystems to capitalize on the increasing demand for AI-enhanced low-code solutions in enterprise settings.
