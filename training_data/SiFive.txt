SiFive
 Ara Eve Leo Sal 
1x
 SiFive, Inc. is an American fabless semiconductor company specializing in the design and licensing of customizable RISC-V processor intellectual property (IP) cores, enabling efficient, open-standard silicon solutions for applications ranging from edge computing to data centers. Founded in 2015 by University of California, Berkeley professors Krste Asanovi, Yunsup Lee, and Andrew Watermanwho are the primary inventors of the RISC-V instruction set architecture (ISA) alongside David PattersonSiFive emerged from academic research to commercialize RISC-V as an open, royalty-free alternative to proprietary architectures like ARM and x86. Headquartered in Santa Clara, California, with global offices, the company focuses on delivering scalable, high-performance cores that prioritize power efficiency and customization, avoiding vendor lock-in for chip designers across industries including automotive, aerospace, IoT, and AI.
SiFive's product portfolio includes a range of RISC-V core IP families, such as the Intelligence series for AI acceleration, Performance series for high-end applications, and Essential series for real-time embedded systems, all optimized for minimal area and low power consumption. The company also offers software tools, development boards like the HiFive series, and full system-on-chip (SoC) solutions to support rapid prototyping and deployment. By contributing to the RISC-V International consortium and open-source ecosystems, SiFive has driven widespread adoption of the ISA, with its IP integrated into products from major semiconductor firms and tech giants.
As of 2025, SiFive has achieved over 400 design wins and powered more than 10 billion cores shipped worldwide, with eight of the top ten semiconductor companies and most of the "Magnificent Seven" tech firms incorporating its technology in RISC-V projects. Recent innovations include the launch of the second-generation Intelligence X100 series in September 2025, featuring five new cores tailored for AI workloads at the far edge and in data centers, alongside partnerships like the October 2025 collaboration with Upbeat Technology for ultra-low-power MCUs with AI capabilities and the January 2026 announcement of integrating NVIDIA NVLink Fusion into its high-performance RISC-V compute platforms to enable coherent, high-bandwidth connections for AI data centers. Under leadership including CEO Patrick Little and the co-founders, SiFive continues to expand the RISC-V ecosystem, positioning it as a foundational technology for future computing efficiency and innovation.

Overview

Company profile

SiFive is a fabless semiconductor company specializing in RISC-V-based intellectual property (IP) cores and custom silicon designs. Founded in September 2015 and headquartered in Santa Clara, California, the company focuses on delivering scalable, high-performance processor solutions that leverage the open-source RISC-V instruction set architecture.
Led by CEO Patrick Little since 2020, SiFive employs approximately 500 people as of 2025, with a workforce heavily skewed toward engineering expertise, including over 180 PhDs. The company's leadership also includes co-founders Krste Asanovi as Chief Architect, Yunsup Lee as CTO, and Andrew Waterman as Chief Engineer, all key contributors to the original RISC-V development at UC Berkeley.
SiFive's mission is to democratize chip design through the open-standard RISC-V architecture, fostering innovation and collaboration across industries such as embedded systems, artificial intelligence, and high-performance computing. As a pioneer in the RISC-V ecosystem, SiFive has secured over 400 design wins and powers more than 10 billion cores in devices worldwide by 2025.

Business model

SiFive operates as a fabless semiconductor company, designing RISC-V-based processor intellectual property (IP) cores and outsourcing manufacturing to foundries such as TSMC. This model allows the company to concentrate on innovation in customizable, high-performance IP without the capital-intensive costs of fabrication facilities.
The core of SiFive's revenue strategy revolves around licensing its RISC-V processor IP, which generates income through upfront fees for access to core designs and ongoing royalties tied to the selling price or volume of chips incorporating the IP. Complementary streams include professional services such as custom system-on-chip (SoC) design assistance, engineering support, and technical training to help customers integrate and optimize SiFive's IP into their products. The company also sells development platforms, including the HiFive series of boards, to facilitate prototyping and evaluation.
In response to growing demand, SiFive has emphasized AI-optimized IP families like the Intelligence series, targeting applications in edge computing, datacenters, and Internet of Things (IoT) devices. This strategic pivot positions the company to capture expanding markets for efficient, scalable compute solutions.
For 2023, SiFive recorded revenue of US$38.2 million alongside a net loss of US$113.1 million, reflecting investments in R&D and market expansion. Looking ahead, the company projects a significant uptick in 2024, with licensing revenue expected to reach US$60 million and lifetime royalty deals totaling at least US$180 million, fueled by AI-driven adoption of RISC-V technology.

History

Founding and early development

SiFive was founded in 2015 by University of California, Berkeley researchers Krste Asanovi, Yunsup Lee, and Andrew Waterman, who played pivotal roles in developing the open-source RISC-V instruction set architecture (ISA) during their time at the university.
The company's initial mission centered on commercializing RISC-V to enable accessible, customizable silicon designs, positioning it as an alternative to proprietary ISAs like ARM by democratizing processor IP for a wide range of applications from embedded systems to high-performance computing.
In November 2016, SiFive achieved a key milestone with the release of its first commercial RISC-V system-on-chip (SoC), the Freedom E310, alongside the HiFive1 development board, which provided developers with an affordable, Arduino-compatible platform to prototype RISC-V-based projects and marked the debut of open-source RISC-V hardware in the market.
Early operations were led by the founding team, with Jack Kang contributing as vice president of product and business development to drive initial market outreach and partnerships. In September 2017, industry veteran Naveed Sherwani was appointed CEO to steer the company through scaling and expanded commercialization efforts.
To support these early initiatives, SiFive raised $5 million in Series A funding in 2015, led by Sutter Hill Ventures, which fueled product development and the launch of its initial offerings.

Expansion and key milestones

In 2020, SiFive appointed Patrick Little, a former Qualcomm executive, as its president and CEO to lead the company's expansion in RISC-V processor IP and custom silicon solutions. Later that year, in August, the company secured $61 million in Series E funding led by SK Hynix and Saudi Aramco, bringing total investment to over $190 million and supporting advancements in domain-specific architectures for sectors like aerospace and automotive.
By 2022, SiFive's growth accelerated, achieving a valuation exceeding $2.5 billion following a $175 million Series F round led by Coatue Management, which funded further innovation in high-performance RISC-V IP. That year, the company expanded its presence in the UK by sponsoring Cambridge United F.C. as the first-team training wear partner for the 2022-2024 seasons, aiming to strengthen community ties and talent recruitment in its Cambridge office.
In 2023, amid broader semiconductor market challenges, SiFive laid off approximately 20% of its workforcearound 130 employeesto realign operations and focus on core RISC-V IP development for AI and high-performance computing. Despite these adjustments, the company continued expanding its IP portfolio, announcing the Performance P870 core and Intelligence X390 in October, optimized for generative AI and machine learning workloads with enhanced vector processing capabilities.
Entering 2024 and 2025, SiFive marked its 10-year anniversary in July 2025, celebrating milestones such as over 400 design wins and more than 10 billion cores powered by its technology deployed in the market. In September 2025, the company released its 2nd Generation Intelligence family under the X100 series, introducing the X160 Gen2 and X180 Gen2 cores alongside upgraded X280, X390, and XM designs; these AI-focused processors combine scalar, vector, and matrix compute to support efficient edge IoT devices and datacenter applications, with production chips expected in 2026. In October 2025, SiFive announced a collaboration with Upbeat Technology to develop next-generation ultra-low-power RISC-V microcontrollers with AI acceleration capabilities.

Business development

Funding and growth

SiFive secured seed funding in August 2015, followed by a Series A round later in 2015 ($5 million), Series B in May 2017 ($8.5 million), Series C in April 2018 ($50.6 million), Series D in June 2019 ($65.4 million), Series E in August 2020 ($61 million), and Series F in March 2022 ($175 million), for a total of over $366 million raised. Key investors across these rounds included Sutter Hill Ventures, Spark Capital, Osage University Partners, Qualcomm Ventures, Intel Capital, and Samsung Ventures, providing strategic support for RISC-V development.
The Series F round, led by Coatue Management and Ibex Investors, valued SiFive at over $2.5 billion, reflecting strong investor confidence in its RISC-V IP leadership amid growing demand for open-source processor architectures. This valuation marked a significant milestone, positioning SiFive as a unicorn in the semiconductor IP space.
Employee headcount expanded rapidly from more than 20 in late 2016 to over 550 by the end of 2022, reaching approximately 650 in 2023 to support global operations and R&D efforts. As of 2025, SiFive's employee count is estimated at around 500, following recovery from the 2023 layoffs, though exact figures vary by source. Design wins also surged, exceeding 400 by 2025, driven by adoption in hyperscale data centers, automotive, and IoT applications.
Revenue growth accelerated in subsequent years, with projections for sixfold increase in 2024 fueled by heightened licensing of AI-optimized RISC-V cores to address surging demand in data centers and edge computing. This trajectory continued into 2025, supported by expanded IP portfolios tailored for AI workloads.
Despite robust growth, SiFive faced challenges in 2023, including a net loss attributed to substantial R&D investments and a broader semiconductor market slowdown; the company responded with layoffs affecting about 20% of its workforce, or approximately 140 employees, to align with evolving funding conditions.

Partnerships and collaborations

SiFive has established strategic partnerships with major semiconductor companies to advance RISC-V adoption across various applications. In 2018, Intel Capital invested in SiFive's Series C funding round, supporting the development of RISC-V IP for high-performance computing, while subsequent collaborations in 2021 and 2022 with Intel Foundry Services enabled the integration of SiFive's RISC-V cores into modular platforms optimized for Intel process nodes. Samsung Foundry has partnered with SiFive since 2019 to fabricate RISC-V-based SoCs on advanced nodes like 14nm, extending the collaboration in recent years to accelerate AI and machine learning inference and training systems for mobile and edge devices. Western Digital has integrated SiFive's RISC-V processors into storage solutions, including early prototypes for NAS devices in 2018 and ongoing adoption in SSD controllers for data center efficiency.
In 2025, SiFive collaborated with Red Hat to deliver a developer preview of Red Hat Enterprise Linux 10 optimized for RISC-V platforms, enhancing enterprise infrastructure support, while ecosystem integrations with NVIDIA's software tools have facilitated RISC-V deployment in AI workloads. In January 2026, SiFive announced the adoption and integration of NVIDIA's NVLink Fusion into its high-performance RISC-V compute platforms, enabling coherent, high-bandwidth connections to NVIDIA GPUs and accelerators for AI data centers. This integration aims to reduce latency, improve efficiency, and enhance scalability in AI infrastructure. SiFive CEO Patrick Little stated, "NVLink Fusion unlocks unprecedented performance for RISC-V systems in AI workloads," while NVIDIA CEO Jensen Huang noted, "This collaboration accelerates the RISC-V ecosystem's growth in high-performance computing." Qualcomm Ventures participated in SiFive's 2019 Series D funding round, fostering joint exploration of RISC-V for AI applications in mobile and IoT devices.
SiFive plays a pivotal role in the RISC-V ecosystem as a founding premier member of RISC-V International, contributing technologies like the WorldGuard security model in 2023 to standardize protection across RISC-V implementations. The company partners with leading foundries, notably joining TSMC's IP Alliance Program in 2017 and earning the Open Innovation Platform Partner of the Year award in 2022, enabling seamless manufacturing of RISC-V designs on TSMC's advanced processes.
SiFive's design wins underscore these partnerships' impact, with RISC-V cores adopted in SSD controllers for high-throughput storage in 2025, AI edge devices for efficient inference, and datacenter applications for scalable compute. Beyond technical alliances, SiFive sponsored Cambridge United F.C. as the training wear partner from 2022 to 2024, boosting brand visibility in the UK community.

Products and technologies

Processor IP cores

SiFive's processor IP cores are built on the open RISC-V instruction set architecture (ISA), offering customizable, silicon-proven designs that enable licensees to integrate high-performance, energy-efficient computing into system-on-chips (SoCs) for various markets. These cores support standard RISC-V extensions, including the vector extension (RVV) for parallel processing in AI workloads, and are compliant with the ratified RISC-V specifications to ensure interoperability and ecosystem compatibility. Scalability is a core strength, allowing configurations from single-core setups to multi-core clusters of up to 256 cores for high-end families like the P800 series, depending on the family and application needs.
The Essential family targets embedded and real-time applications with a focus on power efficiency and determinism. The E-series provides ultra-low-power 32-bit cores, such as the E2 and E3 variants, optimized for IoT devices and microcontrollers, delivering up to 2.9 DMIPS/MHz while supporting features like fast I/O access and configurable memory maps for minimal footprint designs. The S-series offers 64-bit real-time processors, like the S7 series, with an 8-stage dual-issue in-order pipeline for general-purpose embedded tasks requiring low latency and safety certifications. Complementing these, the U-series delivers higher-performance 64-bit cores, exemplified by the U8-series (including the legacy U84), which feature an 8-stage dual-issue pipeline suitable for power-sensitive, high-throughput embedded systems; the U84, for instance, achieves 1.5 times better performance per watt than comparable Arm Cortex-A72 cores at 16nm.
For demanding compute workloads, the Performance family includes the P-series, such as the P600 and P800 series (e.g., P870-D), which provide out-of-order execution and high clock speeds up to 3.6 GHz, targeting datacenter infrastructure and automotive applications with up to 2x the area efficiency of prior generations. The Intelligence family specializes in AI and machine learning acceleration, integrating scalar, vector, and matrix processing units. Key offerings include the X100 series (X160 and X180), which support RVV 1.0 for vector operations, and the XM series for matrix extensions enabling efficient tensor computations; these cores scale from edge devices to datacenter accelerators, with the X180 providing 64-bit addressing for larger memory spaces in AI inference tasks.
These IP cores find applications across edge AI for real-time inference in IoT and robotics, automotive systems for safety-critical control (e.g., ASIL B/D certified E-series variants), storage controllers like SSDs where low-power RISC-V cores optimize data path efficiency, and infrastructure for scalable server processing. For example, in October 2025, SiFive partnered with Upbeat Technology to develop ultra-low-power MCUs using Essential cores with AI acceleration for edge devices. In 2025, SiFive launched the second-generation Intelligence family, including the X160 Gen 2, X180 Gen 2, X280 Gen 2, X390 Gen 2, and XM Gen 2, to address surging AI demands by enhancing scalar-vector-matrix compute efficiency for example, the X160 Gen 2 delivers over twice the AI inference speed of equivalent Arm-based designs in far-edge scenarios while reducing power consumption. Technically, all cores adhere to RISC-V ISA versions up to 2024 ratified profiles, with benchmarks showing the Intelligence series achieving up to 2.5x higher energy efficiency in vectorized AI workloads compared to Arm Neoverse equivalents, as measured in standard MLPerf inference tests.

Development platforms and tools

SiFive offers a range of development platforms and tools designed to facilitate RISC-V prototyping, software development, and ecosystem collaboration, emphasizing accessibility for developers from hobbyists to enterprise teams. The HiFive series of hardware boards serves as the cornerstone of these offerings, providing ready-to-use platforms that integrate SiFive's processor IP with peripherals for rapid evaluation and application building.
The HiFive1, launched in 2016, was SiFive's inaugural development board, an Arduino-compatible platform featuring the Freedom E310 SoC to enable initial RISC-V prototyping and embedded development. Subsequent boards expanded capabilities for more demanding workloads; the HiFive Unmatched, introduced in a standard PC form factor, supports high-performance Linux development with the FU740 SoC, making it suitable for desktop-like software testing and multi-core applications. In 2023, the HiFive Premier P550 arrived as a Mini-DTX board powered by the Performance P550 core, delivering the highest-performance RISC-V development platform at the time with features like a 20 TOPS NPU for AI acceleration, enabling prototyping in machine learning, robotics, and edge computing. These boards include comprehensive documentation, SDKs, and toolchain support to streamline hardware-software integration.
Complementing the hardware, SiFive's DesignShare platform, launched in 2017, is an open-source ecosystem for custom chip design and IP sharing, allowing developers to prototype RISC-V-based SoCs by integrating third-party IP in a streamlined process. It has grown to include over 20 partners, facilitating low-cost access to components like memory controllers and security modules for accelerated silicon realization. A key collaboration within DesignShare is with Brite Semiconductor, which provides DDR IP compliant with DDR2/3/4 and LPDDR2/3/4 standards up to 2667 MT/s, optimizing data transfer for power-efficient RISC-V SoCs. This platform fosters community contributions by enabling design sharing and rapid iteration, reducing barriers to custom hardware development.
On the software side, SiFive provides the Freedom SDK suite, which supports bare-metal, RTOS, and Linux environments across its processors, including tools for application development and workload modeling. The Freedom SDK for Linux integrates with upstream distributions, while the Metal variant offers low-level programming for embedded systems. SiFive Freedom Tools deliver pre-built RISC-V toolchains based on GNU standards, simplifying compilation and debugging with multilib support for 32-bit and 64-bit code. These tools extend to enterprise integrations, such as compatibility with Ubuntu on HiFive boards for AI experimentation.
In 2025, SiFive advanced its platforms to support the second-generation Intelligence family of cores, which combine scalar, vector, and matrix compute for AI workloads spanning edge IoT to data centers. HiFive boards like the Premier P550 received updates via software ecosystems, including a developer preview of Red Hat Enterprise Linux 10, to enable seamless AI model deployment and testing on high-performance RISC-V hardware. These enhancements, including improved memory subsystems and configurable caches in the Intelligence cores, prioritize rapid prototyping and community-driven innovation in the RISC-V ecosystem.
