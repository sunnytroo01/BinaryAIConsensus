 Masaki Okada ( , Okada Masaki; born August 15, 1989) is a Japanese actor renowned for his versatile performances in film, television, and voice acting, particularly in dramatic and coming-of-age roles. Standing at 180 cm tall and managed by Stardust Promotion, Okada has built a prominent career since his debut in 2006, earning recognition for portraying complex characters across genres.
Okada was scouted in Harajuku during his second year of junior high school while playing basketball, initially declining offers before committing to acting full-time and dropping out of university in 2009 to pursue it professionally. His breakthrough came with the 2009 film I Give My First Love to You, where he played Takuma Kakunouchi, a role that showcased his emotional depth and led to further acclaim in Confessions (2010) as Yoshiteru Terada, a pivotal character in the psychological thriller. He received the Best New Artist award at the 34th Hochi Film Awards for his early work and was nominated twice for Best Supporting Actor at the Japan Academy Film Prize in 2011.
Throughout the 2010s and 2020s, Okada expanded his repertoire with standout performances in historical dramas like Taira no Kiyomori (2012) and Showa Genroku Rakugo Shinju (20162018), where he depicted a character's life from youth to old age, as well as in live-action adaptations such as Gintama (2017) as Kotar Katsura. Internationally, he gained wider notice for his role as Kji Takatsuki in Ryusuke Hamaguchi's Drive My Car (2021), which earned critical praise at the Cannes Film Festival. More recently, he starred in films like Last Mile (2024) and voiced a lead role in Mamoru Hosoda's anime Scarlet (2025), while appearing in episodes of anthology series such as After the Quake (2025). In November 2024, Okada married actress Mitsuki Takahata, and in 2025, he announced a career hiatus to focus on personal matters, including expecting their first child due in winter 2025, following an intensive schedule.

Early life and education

Family and childhood

Masaki Okada was born on August 15, 1989, in Edogawa, Tokyo, Japan. He measures 182 cm in height and has blood type AB, details that have been part of his public profile since early in his career.
Okada grew up in a typical urban Japanese household in Tokyo during his childhood and early teenage years. Little is publicly known about his family background, with no specific details available regarding siblings or his parents' professions.

Entry into acting and schooling

During his high school years, Masaki Okada experienced a pivotal shift after quitting the basketball team he had been passionate about since middle school. Having been scouted by an agency in his second year of junior high while in Harajuku, he initially declined the offer to focus on academics and sports, but the sense of aimlessness following his departure from basketball in his first year of high school prompted him to revisit the opportunity. Inspired by the desire to find a new passion and challenge himself, Okada contacted the agency on his own initiative, leading to his affiliation with Stardust Promotion in 2005.
This entry into the entertainment industry occurred while Okada was still navigating high school, where he transferred to a school that permitted extracurricular activities like acting to accommodate his burgeoning commitments. His decision was further influenced by a growing curiosity about performance, sparked by the void left after basketball, which had previously dominated his extracurricular life. By proactively reaching out during his high school period, Okada laid the groundwork for his professional transition without immediately disrupting his education.
Parallel to his early acting pursuits, Okada enrolled in Asia University in Tokyo in 2008, motivated in part by a desire to reassure his parents amid his shifting career path. He balanced coursework with initial industry engagements, attending classes while taking on preliminary roles, though the demands of acting soon intensified. Ultimately, Okada withdrew from the university at the end of 2009 to fully dedicate himself to his profession, marking a deliberate choice to prioritize his evolving career over continued formal education.

Career

Debut and early roles (20062009)

Masaki Okada entered the entertainment industry in 2006, beginning with minor television appearances that introduced him to professional acting. His debut came in March of that year with a small role in the TBS drama Tokyo Shojo ~ Nagameru Shojo, followed by a guest spot in episode 2 of the Fuji TV series Mo Hitotsu no Sugar & Spice (also known as Seishun Energy) in September, where he portrayed a high school student navigating youthful challenges. These early parts allowed Okada to build foundational experience while still in high school, reflecting his initial foray into scripted performance without prior training.
By 2007, Okada secured more prominent supporting roles in youth-oriented dramas, which helped solidify his on-screen presence in Japanese media. In Seito Shokun! on TV Asahi (AprilJune 2007), he played Kaoru Kinoshita, a student grappling with school dynamics and personal growth in a story centered on teacher-student conflicts. Later that summer, in the Fuji TV hit Hana Kimi (Hanazakari no Kimitachi e, JulySeptember 2007), Okada portrayed Kyogo Sekime, a bespectacled and earnest member of the school's dormitory, contributing to the series' ensemble of charismatic young characters in a comedic tale of disguised identities and high school rivalries. These roles showcased his ability to embody relatable teenage personas, earning him recognition among viewers of teen dramas.
Okada's shift toward cinema culminated in 2009 with his first significant film role in Gravity's Clowns (Jyuryoku Pierrot), directed by Junichi Mori, where he starred as Haru Okuno, the younger brother in a dysfunctional family unraveling dark secrets after their mother's death. This performance marked a pivotal transition from television supporting parts to more introspective cinematic leads, highlighting his range in dramatic narratives. Throughout 20062009, Okada managed the demands of his rising career alongside enrollment at a university in Tokyo, often juggling shoots and classes until he dropped out at the end of 2009 to focus exclusively on acting.

Breakthrough and major films (20102015)

Okada's breakthrough arrived in 2010 with his supporting roles in the crime drama Villain, directed by Lee Sang-il, and the psychological thriller Confessions, directed by Tetsuya Nakashima. In Villain, he portrayed Keigo Masuo, a university student suspected in a young woman's murder, delivering a nuanced performance that captured the character's inner turmoil and moral ambiguity. The film, praised for its exploration of guilt and redemption, highlighted Okada's ability to convey emotional depth in intense dramatic scenarios.
Similarly, in Confessions, Okada played Yoshiteru Terada, an idealistic new teacher unwittingly drawn into a web of revenge following a child's death, showcasing his skill in portraying vulnerability amid escalating tension. The film's commercial success, grossing over 4 billion yen at the Japanese box office, and its critical reception for innovative storytelling amplified Okada's visibility, with both roles earning him dual nominations for Best Supporting Actor at the 34th Japan Academy Film Prize.
The momentum from his lead role as Takuma Kakinouchi in the 2009 romantic drama I Give My First Love to You carried into 2010, where his sensitive depiction of a terminally ill teenager navigating first love received widespread praise and secured him the Newcomer of the Year award at the 33rd Japan Academy Film Prize. This performance, blending heartfelt romance with poignant tragedy, marked a pivotal step in demonstrating his range beyond teen idols.
Building on this foundation, Okada took on diverse leading roles in subsequent projects, including the inspirational drama Space Brothers (2012), where he starred as aspiring astronaut Hibito Nanba alongside Shun Oguri, contributing to the film's status as a box-office hit that grossed approximately 1.6 billion yen and resonated with audiences through its themes of perseverance and brotherhood. His turn in the popular legal comedy series Legal High season 2 (2013), as the ambitious prosecutor-turned-lawyer Haruki Hanyu, further bridged his film work with television, allowing him to flex comedic and adversarial skills opposite veteran lead Masato Sakai in a ratings powerhouse that averaged 18% viewership.
From 2010 to 2015, these roles across romance, intense drama, thriller, and lighthearted comedy solidified Okada's reputation as a versatile actor in his mid-20s, propelling him from promising newcomer to a sought-after talent in Japan's film industry.

Established career and international work (2016present)

Following his breakthrough roles in the early 2010s, Masaki Okada solidified his position as a leading actor in Japanese cinema and television from 2016 onward, taking on more nuanced characters that showcased his range in dramatic and introspective narratives. A pivotal moment came with his portrayal of Koji Takatsuki, a charismatic yet troubled young actor, in Ryusuke Hamaguchi's Drive My Car (2021), adapted from Haruki Murakami's short story. Okada's performance, which involved multilingual scenes in Japanese, Korean, and English within the film's theater production framework, contributed to the movie's critical acclaim and its win for Best International Feature Film at the 94th Academy Awards. This role marked a significant elevation in his international visibility, introducing him to global audiences through festival screenings and awards circuits.
In the years following, Okada continued to diversify his portfolio with projects that blended thriller, drama, and relational dynamics. He starred as team manager Ko Nashimoto in the crime thriller Last Mile (2024), directed by Ayuko Tsukahara, where he navigated a high-stakes crisis involving exploding delivery packages amid Japan's logistics network. The film, set in a interconnected universe with prior TBS dramas, highlighted his ability to anchor ensemble casts in tense, contemporary settings. Similarly, in the Amazon Prime Video series 1122: For a Happy Marriage (2024), Okada played Otoya Aihara, a husband in a sexless marriage who explores extramarital connections, delivering a layered depiction of emotional intimacy and personal boundaries. These works exemplified his shift toward mature, psychologically complex roles that resonate with modern societal themes.
In 2025, Okada provided the voice for a lead role in Mamoru Hosoda's anime film Scarlet and starred as Komura in episodes of the anthology series After the Quake. Later that year, in April 2025, he announced a career hiatus to focus on personal matters following an intensive schedule.
Throughout this period, Okada has expanded into varied genres while maintaining selectivity in his projects, prioritizing scripts that allow for deep character exploration over commercial volume. He remains affiliated with Stardust Promotion, the agency that has managed his career since his debut, providing stability for his transition to more auteur-driven works. In a 2023 interview, Okada reflected on his post-2016 approach, emphasizing his passion for versatile acting that challenges him to embody multifaceted personas, from arrogant prodigies to introspective professionals, as a means to evolve beyond early typecasting. This deliberate curation has sustained his reputation as one of Japan's most adaptable performers.

Personal life

Pre-marriage relationships

Throughout his career, Masaki Okada has maintained a strong commitment to privacy regarding his personal relationships, rarely addressing romantic matters in public interviews or appearances. This approach allowed him to focus on his professional growth amid rising fame, with limited details emerging about his private life until media reports in the early 2020s.
The most notable publicly reported relationship prior to his 2024 marriage was with Fuji Television announcer Yui Suzuki, which reportedly began around summer 2020. The pair was first spotted together in April 2021, when Okada picked her up from her apartment for a drive and shopping outing in Tokyo, later returning to his residence. Subsequent sightings in May 2021 and June 2022 confirmed their ongoing dates, including train rides and casual outings, as documented by Japanese media. The relationship ended by late 2022, according to reports.
As Okada's prominence grew through major film roles in the 2010s, the demands of his schedule and public scrutiny posed challenges to maintaining personal balance, prompting him to limit disclosures about his off-screen life up to 2023. No other relationships were publicly confirmed or widely reported during this period.

Marriage and family

On November 19, 2024, Japanese actor Masaki Okada and actress Mitsuki Takahata announced their marriage through identical posts on their respective Instagram accounts. In the statements, they expressed feelings of happiness and nervousness about the decision, pledging to support each other in their professional endeavors while striving to grow as individuals and contribute positively to society. The couple had informed their families, close friends, and agencies prior to the public reveal, with plans to complete their marriage registration by the end of 2024.
Okada and Takahata first met while co-starring as an on-screen married couple in the 2023 Prime Video series 1122: For a Happy Marriage, adapted from a manga by Yi Neun. Their collaboration during filming fostered a close relationship, leading them to begin dating in early 2024.
In April 2025, Okada announced he would take a break from acting that year to focus on personal matters, including enriching himself, reflecting on his nearly 20-year career, prioritizing health through exercise and diet, and adjusting to life after his marriage. This decision followed an intensive schedule in 2024 and aimed to support his well-being amid family changes.
On July 16, 2025, the couple announced that Takahata was pregnant with their first child, expected to arrive toward the end of 2025. In their joint statement, they described the news as a blessing with a new life, noting that Takahata intended to continue her acting work while prioritizing her health under medical guidance. As of November 2025, Takahata remains pregnant and has publicly affirmed her commitment to balancing her career and family, including participating in stage performances during this period. The couple has expressed ongoing gratitude to fans and supporters, requesting warm encouragement as they prepare for parenthood.

Television dramas

Masaki Okada's television career began with minor roles in the mid-2000s, evolving into prominent parts across genres such as romance, mystery, and historical drama. His early work often featured youthful characters in school settings, transitioning to more complex leads in legal and medical series.
One of his breakthrough roles was Sekime Kyogo, a supporting member of the Dorm 2 group, in the 2007 Fuji TV series Hana-Kimi (), a 12-episode adaptation of the manga that highlighted his comedic timing in an all-boys school comedy. In 2009, he starred as the lead Masamune Asuka in Otomen (), a 12-episode Fuji TV romantic comedy exploring gender stereotypes, marking his first main role in a full season.
Okada's versatility shone in investigative dramas during the 2010s, including the supporting role of assistant investigator Kudo Suguru in the 9-episode NTV series Ogon no Buta (- -) in 2010. He took a main role as Shiba Kengo in the 2012 TV Asahi thriller Seinaru Kaibutsutachi (), an 8-episode corporate conspiracy story. In Legal High 2 (2013), Okada portrayed Hanyu Haruki as a key supporting lawyer in the 10-episode Fuji TV legal comedy, contributing to its satirical take on the justice system.
Subsequent leads included detective Yurine Tomohisa in the 10-episode NTV procedural ST: Akai Shgen (ST ) in 2014, handyman Takeyama Jun in the 12-episode TV Tokyo series Fuben na Benriya () in 2015, and the amnesiac investigator Kakutaka Yakumo in The Memorandum of Kyoko Okitegami (), a 10-episode NTV mystery in 2015. He headlined the satirical office comedy Yutori desu ga Nani ka? () as Sakama Masakazu in its 10-episode NTV run in 2016.
In historical and ensemble works, Okada played the young Minamoto no Yoritomo (narrator) in the 50-episode NHK taiga drama Taira no Kiyomori () in 2012, and the eighth-generation Yuurakutei Yakumo in the 10-episode NHK adaptation Showa Genroku Rakugo Shinju () in 2018, earning praise for his nuanced portrayal of a rakugo performer's emotional journey. His extended supporting role as Okuhara Saitaro, the protagonist's brother, spanned 156 episodes in the NHK morning serial Natsuzora () in 2019.
Recent contributions include the dual lead Kuroiwa Kensuke in the 7-episode NHK revenge thriller Talio ( 2) in 2020, and the husband Shinshin Nakamura in My Dear Exes (), a 10-episode Kansai TV/Fuji TV series in 2021 that explored post-divorce dynamics. Okada starred as traveling nurse Nasuda Ayumi in the 8-episode TV Asahi medical drama The Travel Nurse () in 2022, reprising the role in its 9-episode second season in 2024. In 2024, he led as Aihara Otoya in the 7-episode Netflix series 1122: For a Happy Marriage (1122 ), portraying a man in a troubled marriage; the production also marked his on-set meeting with actress Mitsuki Takahata. In 2025, Okada appeared as Komura in episode 1 of the NHK anthology miniseries Jishin no Ato de (After the Earthquake), a 4-episode drama spanning 19952025 exploring disaster impacts and societal recovery.

Awards and nominations

Wins

Masaki Okada received his first major award recognition in 2009 for his breakout performances in multiple films. At the 34th Hochi Film Awards, he won Best New Artist for his role in Honokaa Boy. Later that year, at the 22nd Nikkan Sports Film Awards, Okada was honored with the Best Newcomer award for his work in Honokaa Boy, I Give My First Love to You, and Gravity's Clowns.
In 2010, Okada's rising prominence led to several newcomer accolades across prestigious ceremonies. He received the Best New Talent award at the Yokohama Film Festival for Honokaa Boy, Halfway, Gravity's Clowns, and I Give My First Love to You. The 52nd Blue Ribbon Awards also awarded him Best New Talent for the same ensemble of roles. Additionally, at the 34th Elan d'or Awards, he was named Newcomer of the Year. His performances in Honokaa Boy, I Give My First Love to You, and Gravity's Clowns earned him the Newcomer of the Year at the 33rd Japan Academy Prize.
Okada's television work was recognized in 2014 when he won Best Supporting Actor at the 79th Television Drama Academy Awards for his role in Legal High 2. No further individual wins have been recorded through 2025.
