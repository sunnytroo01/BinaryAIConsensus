Anrich Nortje
 Ara Eve Leo Sal 
1x
 Anrich Nortje is a South African professional cricketer renowned for his right-arm fast bowling, capable of consistently delivering speeds exceeding 150 km/h, making him one of the quickest pacers in modern cricket. Born on 16 November 1993 in Uitenhage, Eastern Cape, South Africa, Nortje has established himself as a key figure in South Africa's pace attack, drawing comparisons to legendary bowler Dale Steyn due to his ability to generate late swing and seam movement at high velocities.
Nortje made his international debut in March 2019, featuring in One Day Internationals (ODIs) and Twenty20 Internationals (T20Is) against Sri Lanka, followed by his Test debut in October 2019 against India. Over his career, he has claimed numerous wickets across formats, including his maiden five-wicket haul in Tests against England in January 2020 and another against Sri Lanka in 2021, while in limited-overs cricket, he has taken 36 ODI wickets (as of November 2025) and was selected for major tournaments like the 2019 ICC Cricket World Cup but missed due to injury. His standout IPL performances include bowling the fastest delivery in tournament history at 156.22 km/h in 2020 while playing for Delhi Capitals, where he secured 61 wickets in 48 matches before being acquired by Kolkata Knight Riders for INR 6.5 crore ahead of the 2025 season.
Despite injury setbacks, including a back issue that ruled him out of the 2025 ICC Men's Champions Trophy, Nortje received accolades such as Cricket South Africa's Men's Cricketer of the Year and Men's Test Cricketer of the Year in 2021, underscoring his impact on the sport. In late 2025, he signed with the KZN Dolphins for the CSA T20 Challenge, signaling his return to competitive action after a period of recovery.

Early life

Birth and family background

Anrich Nortje was born on 16 November 1993 in Uitenhage, a small industrial town in the Eastern Cape province of South Africa. He grew up in this modest, working-class community north of Port Elizabeth (now Gqeberha), known for its automotive manufacturing heritage, including the largest Volkswagen factory in the country.
Nortje hails from a close-knit family; his mother is Amanda Nortje, while details about his father's occupation remain private. He has two older siblings: a brother named Anton Nortje, who is 13 years his senior, and a sister named Lizel Bouwer, who is 16 years older.
His early childhood in Uitenhage was typical of small-town South African life, marked by limited recreational options that naturally drew him toward outdoor activities. In an interview, Nortje described it as "a very normal childhood considering my sister is 16 years older than me and my brother 13 years," where he frequently played informal games in the backyard with his brotherwho preferred battingand local friends. This environment in the Eastern Cape, with its strong regional emphasis on community sports amid rural and semi-urban settings, helped nurture his initial interest in physical pursuits over other hobbies.
Nortje's family provided steady encouragement for his sporting inclinations from a young age, supporting his backyard play and early exposure to cricket despite the town's modest resources. This foundational backing from his middle-class upbringing aligned with broader South African cultural values of resilience and communal activity in such locales.

Introduction to cricket and education

Anrich Nortje's introduction to cricket came during his primary school years at Handhaaf Primary in Uitenhage, Eastern Cape, where he began playing the sport in grade 7, around the age of 12 or 13. Growing up in the small industrial town of Uitenhage, north of Port Elizabeth, Nortje initially balanced cricket with rugby, serving as a fullback for his school's first XV. However, a knee injury sustained in grade 11 shifted his focus entirely to cricket by grade 12, when he joined Brandwag High School's first cricket team at the end of grade 8 and committed more seriously to the game.
His progression through youth cricket began with selection for the Eastern Province under-15 team in 2009, marking his entry into representative cricket at age 15. At Brandwag High, Nortje's raw pace caught the attention of coach Piet Botha in 2010, when the 17-year-old bowled during a school match; Botha noted his unpolished but promising speed and began mentoring him on fast bowling fundamentals, including straighter run-ups and line consistency. This early guidance helped refine Nortje's natural ability as a right-arm fast bowler, setting the foundation for his development amid the school's stronger rugby tradition.
After matriculating from Brandwag High, Nortje pursued higher education at Nelson Mandela Metropolitan University (now Nelson Mandela University) in Port Elizabeth, where he earned a Bachelor of Commerce degree in financial planning along with a postgraduate diploma. While studying, he played for the university's Madibaz cricket team, prioritizing cricket upon arrival but crediting the broader academic environment for aiding his long-term growth as an athlete. Balancing lectures with intensive training sessions, Nortje honed his skills in university competitions, which provided crucial exposure and helped him transition toward professional opportunities without immediate senior domestic entry.

Domestic career

First-class and List A debut

Nortje made his first-class debut for Eastern Province on 7 February 2013 against Namibia in the CSA Provincial Three-Day Competition at Windhoek. Eastern Province won by 96 runs, with Nortje taking 2 wickets for 40 runs in the first innings and 0 for 29 in the second, for match figures of 2/69.
His List A debut came later that year on 27 October 2013 for Eastern Province against Free State in the CSA Provincial One-Day Competition at St George's Park, Gqeberha (then Port Elizabeth). In the 2013-14 season, Nortje played several matches for Eastern Province across formats, building experience despite modest returns, taking around 25 first-class wickets at an average of about 35.
Nortje's franchise first-class debut occurred in the 2016-17 Sunfoil Series for the Warriors (representing Eastern Province), where he impressed with pace. During the 2016-17 season overall, he played 4 first-class matches, capturing 20 wickets at an average of 25.35 and an economy rate of 2.82. In 5 List A outings that season, he secured 8 wickets at 32.25 with an economy of 5.04.
Seeking greater opportunities, Nortje transferred to the Northerns franchise (representing the Titans) ahead of the 2017-18 season. He adapted quickly to the higher level of competition, making his debut for Northerns on 15 October 2017 against Free State in the CSA Provincial One-Day Challenge, where he took 4/35 to help secure a 114-run win. In his initial Titans stint, Nortje's raw speed troubled batsmen, including a standout spell against Gauteng where he dismissed key opener Stephen Cook with a searing yorker.

Key domestic seasons and performances

Nortje's breakthrough came during the 2018-19 season with the Titans in South Africa's domestic competitions, where he emerged as a leading wicket-taker across formats. In the CSA 4-Day Franchise Series (first-class), he claimed 24 wickets at an average of 24.29, helping the Titans secure strong performances and establishing his reputation as a reliable strike bowler capable of exploiting conditions with pace and accuracy. Complementing this, Nortje took over 30 wickets in total across provincial and franchise limited-overs games that season, including notable contributions in the CSA Provincial One-Day Challenge, where his raw speed troubled batsmen and earned him selection attention.
Between 2019 and 2022, Nortje continued to shine in the CSA T20 Challenge and Momentum One-Day Cup, formats that honed his white-ball skills amid increasing international commitments. In the CSA T20 Challenge, he maintained a sub-25 bowling average over multiple seasons, with consistent wicket-taking ability on seaming pitches while adapting his lengths to contain aggressive batting lineups. The One-Day Cup saw similar impact, where he averaged around 22 with the ball and took at least 10 wickets per season, solidifying his role as the Titans' spearhead.
Following a stress fracture that sidelined him through much of 2023 and limited 2024 domestic play, Nortje made a strong return in the 2025-26 CSA T20 Challenge after signing with the Dolphins. In his comeback campaign as of November 2025, he has taken 8 wickets in 4 matches with economical bowling, including figures of 3 for 18 against the North West Dragons on 7 November 2025, contributing to a 31-run victory.
As of November 2025, Nortje's domestic career statistics reflect his growth into a premier all-format bowler: over 200 first-class wickets at an average under 28 with best figures of 7 for 93, more than 100 List A wickets averaging 25 including a best of 5 for 19, and exceeding 150 T20 wickets at a strike rate of 17.5 and best of 4 for 10. These figures, primarily amassed with the Titans until 2024, underscore his impact in building South African pace depth, though injuries have occasionally disrupted his rhythm.

International career

One Day International career

Nortje made his One Day International (ODI) debut for South Africa against Sri Lanka on 3 March 2019 at the Wanderers Stadium in Johannesburg, taking 1 wicket for 33 runs in 7 overs during South Africa's 6-wicket victory. His early international exposure was cut short when he was selected for the 2019 ICC Cricket World Cup squad but ruled out due to a fractured right thumb sustained in a net session in Port Elizabeth.
Nortje's breakthrough ODI performance came in the third match against England on 6 February 2020 at Newlands, Cape Town, where he claimed career-best figures of 6/56 to bowl out England for 192 and set up a 5-wicket win for South Africa. He built on this form with key contributions in bilateral series, including 4 wickets across the three ODIs against India in January 2022 and 6 wickets in the three-match series against Australia later that year, helping South Africa secure series victories.
Nortje was named in South Africa's squad for the 2023 ICC Cricket World Cup but withdrew due to a lower-back stress fracture, missing the tournament in India. He was named in South Africa's squad for the 2025 ICC Champions Trophy but ruled out due to a recurring back injury in January 2025 before the tournament began. As of November 2025, Nortje has appeared in 22 ODIs, capturing 36 wickets at an average of 27.28 and an economy rate of 5.86.

Test and T20I career

Anrich Nortje made his Test debut for South Africa against India at the Maharashtra Cricket Association Stadium in Pune on 10 October 2019, where he claimed 2 for 83 in the first innings. In the subsequent home series against England in December 2019 and January 2020, he achieved his maiden five-wicket haul with figures of 5 for 72 in the fourth Test at the Wanderers Stadium in Johannesburg, helping to restrict England and secure a 191-run victory. A standout performance came during South Africa's 2022-23 tour of Australia, where Nortje took 4 for 65 in the second innings of the decisive third Test at the Sydney Cricket Ground, dismantling Australia's middle order and contributing to a seven-wicket win that clinched the series 2-0the Proteas' first Test series victory in Australia since 2008-09.
Across 19 Test matches, Nortje has captured 70 wickets at an average of 26.71, with four five-wicket hauls demonstrating his ability to generate pace and bounce on varied surfaces. His last Test appearance was the first match against West Indies at Centurion in February-March 2023, after which recurring injuries, including stress fractures and back issues, have sidelined him from red-ball cricket.
Nortje debuted in T20Is against India at the Punjab Cricket Association IS Bindra Stadium in Mohali on 18 September 2019, taking 1 for 29 in a low-scoring thriller that South Africa lost by six wickets. By November 2025, he had played 42 T20Is, securing 53 wickets at an average of 19.17 and an economy of 7.02, establishing himself as one of South Africa's premier white-ball quicks with his express pace often exceeding 150 km/h. In the 2024 T20 World Cup, Nortje excelled with 15 wickets in eight matches at an economy of 5.75, including a career-best 4 for 7 against Sri Lanka in New York that bowled them out for 77, and crucial strikes in the semi-final against Afghanistan (1 for 13) and the final against India (2 for 26 off four overs), where he defended 11 runs in the last over despite South Africa's narrow seven-run defeat.
Nortje's most recent T20I action came in the three-match series against Pakistan in December 2024, where he played the opener before fracturing the big toe on his left foot during practice, ruling him out of the subsequent games and contributing to an economy under 7 across his limited overs in the series. Subsequent injuries, including a back issue in January 2025 and a stress reaction in June 2025, have limited his international appearances, though he earned South Africa's T20I Player of the Year award for his 2024 contributions.

Franchise cricket

Indian Premier League career

Anrich Nortje made his Indian Premier League (IPL) debut in 2020 for the Delhi Capitals, signing as a replacement for Chris Woakes at a price of INR 1.5 crore. In his debut season, he played 16 matches and claimed 22 wickets at an economy rate of 8.04, establishing himself as one of the tournament's leading pacers and finishing as the third-highest wicket-taker behind Kagiso Rabada. His standout performance included bowling the fastest delivery in IPL history at 156.22 km/h against Rajasthan Royals, while also contributing to Delhi Capitals' run to the final, where they lost to Mumbai Indians.
Retained by Delhi Capitals for INR 3.2 crore ahead of the 2021 season, Nortje featured in eight matches, securing 12 wickets at an economy of 8.21 despite a mid-season injury concern. His role as a death-over specialist was evident, with notable spells restricting opposition scoring in crucial phases, though back spasms limited his appearances as Delhi Capitals again reached the final, falling short against Chennai Super Kings. In 2022, retained at INR 6.5 crore, injuries restricted him to six matches where he took nine wickets at an economy of 9.36, including a three-wicket haul against Chennai Super Kings.
Nortje remained with Delhi Capitals for the 2023 and 2024 seasons, retained at INR 6.5 crore each time. In 2023, he played 10 matches and captured 10 wickets at an economy of 10.31, battling form dips and injuries that saw him return home mid-season for recovery. His 2024 campaign was similarly curtailed by fitness issues, limited to six matches with seven wickets at an economy of 9.80, highlighted by a three-for against Kolkata Knight Riders.
Ahead of the 2025 auction, Delhi Capitals did not retain Nortje, who was then acquired by Kolkata Knight Riders for INR 6.5 crore against a base price of INR 2 crore. Limited by poor form, he appeared in only two matches for KKR, taking one wicket (figures of 1/23 against Punjab Kings) before being dropped for the remainder of the season. He was released by KKR on 14 November 2025 ahead of the IPL 2026 auction.
Across his IPL career spanning 48 matches, Nortje has taken 61 wickets at an average of 27.16 and an economy of 9.07, with best figures of 3/33 against Royal Challengers Bengaluru in 2023. Renowned as a death-over specialist, he has maintained a powerplay economy of approximately 9.00, leveraging his express pace to disrupt top-order batsmen and close innings effectively.

Other T20 franchise leagues

Nortje began his T20 franchise career beyond the IPL in the 2018 edition of the Mzansi Super League, representing the Cape Town Blitz. In just three matches, he claimed eight wickets while consistently bowling at speeds over 150 km/h, marking an explosive introduction to high-profile T20 cricket and showcasing his raw pace against top South African batters.
In the Caribbean Premier League, Nortje featured for the St Kitts & Nevis Patriots during the 2024 season, playing all 10 league matches for the team. He emerged as the tournament's leading wicket-taker with 11 scalps, including key dismissals that highlighted his ability to exploit bounce and swing on Caribbean pitches, contributing significantly to the Patriots' campaign.
Nortje also participated in the SA20 league, the successor to the Mzansi Super League, for the Pretoria Capitals in its inaugural 2023 season. Across 11 matches, he took 20 wickets at an average of 13.25, finishing as the joint-highest wicket-taker in the competition and earning recognition for his death-over expertise.
In Major League Cricket, Nortje joined the Los Angeles Knight Riders for the 2023 season, playing eight matches overall in the league and securing three wickets while adapting to the American conditions. He was retained in the squad for the 2025 edition but was ruled out before the start due to a serious injury sustained after the IPL season.
Across these non-IPL T20 franchise leagues, Nortje has appeared in 32 matches and captured 42 wickets, demonstrating his versatility and appeal to global teams bolstered by his IPL performances.

Playing style and impact

Bowling technique and speeds

Anrich Nortje is a right-arm fast bowler renowned for his explosive pace and ability to extract steep bounce from his imposing height of 1.88 meters (6 ft 2 in). His bowling action features a long, rhythmic run-up that builds momentum, culminating in a high-arm delivery that allows for precise control and intimidating speed. This technique, combined with his wiry frame and fast-twitch muscle fibers, enables him to maintain high velocities while targeting the stumps aggressively, often described as bowling "at the sticks always."
Nortje's speed profile is among the quickest in contemporary cricket, with regular deliveries clocking 145-150 km/h and occasional peaks reaching 156 km/h, the latter marking the fastest ball in Indian Premier League history during the 2020 season. He incorporates a variety of deliveries, including searing yorkers, short-pitched bouncers for intimidation, and cutters to exploit seam movement, particularly effective on responsive pitches. In Test matches, Nortje employs reverse swing to challenge batsmen on wearing surfaces, drawing comparisons to South African legend Dale Steyn for his capacity to instill fear through raw velocity and psychological pressure. His tactical acumen shines in T20 cricket, where precise death-over yorkers have been a consistent weapon, contributing to economical spells in high-pressure scenarios.
Following a major stress fracture in 2023 that sidelined him for nine months, Nortje returned in 2024 with adjustments to his workload management, prioritizing sustained consistency and control over maximal pace to mitigate injury risks. This evolution was evident in his performances during the 2024 T20 World Cup, where he maintained speeds around 150 km/h while focusing on rhythm and accuracy, though subsequent back and stress injuries in early 2025 tested his resilience. By mid-2025, coaching feedback indicated he was nearing his pre-injury peak, adapting his technique to balance explosiveness with longevity. In late 2025, Nortje returned to competitive action with the KZN Dolphins in the CSA T20 Challenge, taking wickets in matches as of November, demonstrating his recovery and sustained adaptation.

Batting, fielding, and overall records

Anrich Nortje, primarily known for his bowling prowess, has contributed as a lower-order aggressor with the bat across formats. In Test cricket, his highest score is 40, achieved against England in 2019, where he batted for over two hours in a crucial partnership. His overall Test batting record reflects occasional resilience, with 187 runs at an average of 7.79 in 19 matches. In limited-overs cricket, Nortje's contributions are typically brief but impactful; his T20I best is 4 not out, while in ODIs, he has a high of 10. In the IPL, he has provided useful cameos, including an unbeaten 23 that supported his team's chase.
Nortje's fielding adds value to his all-round utility, particularly as a sharp catcher in the slips. He has demonstrated reliable hands in international cricket, taking key dismissals that have aided South Africa's efforts, with over 10 catches across formats noted for their athleticism. In T20Is, his outfield presence highlights his speed and agility, contributing to run-outs and pressure-building stops.
Nortje's overall career records underscore his impact as a premier fast bowler, with career aggregates as of November 2025 standing at 68 wickets in 19 Tests at an average of 26.71, 35 wickets in 22 ODIs at 27.27, and 72 wickets in 42 T20Is at 19.16making him South Africa's leading T20I wicket-taker, surpassing Dale Steyn. His total international wickets exceed 200, reflecting consistent performances across conditions. Notable among his records is the fastest delivery in IPL history at 156.22 km/h, bowled in 2020 against Rajasthan Royals.

Personal life

Family and relationships

Anrich Nortje met his wife, Micaela Kleu, during their high school days at Brandwag Hoerskool in South Africa. The couple married in a private ceremony on September 9, 2020, after dating for several years. Micaela, a primary school teacher by profession, has been a steadfast source of support for Nortje throughout his cricket career, often attending matches and providing emotional encouragement during challenging times.
The couple welcomed their first child, daughter Amelia, in early 2024. Nortje and his family prioritize togetherness despite his demanding schedule, with Amelia embarking on her 50th airplane flight in April 2025 to join her father in India during the Indian Premier League season. In November 2025, Nortje and his wife announced they are expecting twins due in May 2026. This reflects their family-oriented lifestyle, where Micaela plays a key role in balancing travel and home life while maintaining a degree of privacy amid Nortje's public profile.

Injuries and recovery

Anrich Nortje's injury concerns began in November 2018 when he suffered an ankle injury during the Mzansi Super League, ruling him out of the remainder of the tournament and requiring surgery to address avulsed bone fragments in his left ankle. In March 2019, a shoulder injury sidelined him for six weeks, causing him to miss the entire Indian Premier League season with the Kolkata Knight Riders. Later that year, in May 2019, Nortje fractured his right thumb during a practice session, which forced him to withdraw from South Africa's squad for the 2019 Cricket World Cup and delayed his recovery by six to eight weeks.
Following a strong performance in the 2021 Indian Premier League, Nortje was diagnosed with a stress fracture in his lower back in mid-2021, leading to an extended layoff that caused him to miss several international series, though he recovered in time to feature in the 2021 T20 World Cup. He made a successful return in the 2022 Indian Premier League after being cleared of any serious complications in March 2022, allowing him to resume competitive cricket with the Delhi Capitals. In September 2023, another suspected stress fracture in his lower back emerged during an ODI against Australia, resulting in his withdrawal from the 2023 Cricket World Cup squad and a nine-month absence from all formats.
Nortje staged a full comeback in 2024, participating in the Indian Premier League and South Africa's T20 World Cup campaign after completing his rehabilitation. However, setbacks continued into late 2024 with a fractured big toe in December, which excluded him from the T20I series against Pakistan and required consultation with specialists for treatment. In January 2025, a recurring back injurya stress reactionforced him to miss the remainder of the SA20 league and the entire ICC Champions Trophy, though he managed to play several matches for the Kolkata Knight Riders in the 2025 Indian Premier League before another stress reaction in his back in June 2025 sidelined him indefinitely. He returned to competitive action in October 2025 for the CSA T20 Challenge with the Dolphins, marking his recovery from the June injury.
Cricket South Africa has implemented workload monitoring to mitigate Nortje's injury risks, including his decision in 2024 to opt out of a central contract to prioritize rehabilitation and selective participation in white-ball formats. These efforts, combined with ongoing physiotherapy, have addressed his recurrent back issues, though his career has seen absences totaling over 18 months across all formats due to these setbacks.
