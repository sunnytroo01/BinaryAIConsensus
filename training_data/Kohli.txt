 Virat Kohli (born 5 November 1988) is an Indian professional cricketer who has represented the national team across Test, (/page/One Day International) (ODI), and (/page/Twenty20 International) (T20I) formats as a right-handed top-order batsman and occasional right-arm medium-pace bowler.
Kohli captained India to the 2008 ICC Under-19 Cricket World Cup title and later led the senior side to significant successes, including the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup and the (/page/2013 ICC Champions Trophy), while amassing a Test captaincy record of 40 wins in 68 matches before retiring from the format in May 2025. His batting has yielded over 14,000 ODI runs at an average exceeding 57, including a record 50 centuries that surpassed Sachin Tendulkar's previous mark of 49 in 2023.
In the Indian Premier League, Kohli has scored the highest single-season tally of 973 runs in 2016 for Royal Challengers Bengaluru, where he continues to play, and his aggressive style and fitness regimen have influenced modern cricket's emphasis on athleticism and chasing targets, evidenced by his milestone as the first player to record 70 fifty-plus scores in ODI chases as of October 2025; in January 2026, he scored 93 runs to lead India to a 4-wicket victory over New Zealand in the first ODI in Vadodara, earning Player of the Match, marking his fifth consecutive fifty-plus ODI score, becoming the fastest player to reach 28,000 international runs, and reclaiming the No. 1 position in the ICC Men's ODI Batting Rankings after 1,403 days. While praised for record-breaking consistency, Kohli's on-field intensity has occasionally sparked debates over sportsmanship, though empirical performance data underscores his dominance in high-pressure scenarios.

Etymology and origins

Indian origins

The surname Kohli originates in the Punjab region of northern India, where it denotes a specific clan within the Khatri community, a mercantile and professional caste historically involved in trade, administration, and military roles. The Khatri caste, predominant in Punjab's Majha and Malwa subregions, traces its members to urban centers like Lahore and Amritsar before the 1947 Partition of India, with many families relocating to Indian Punjab and Delhi post-independence. Kohli is one of the clans grouped under the Khukhrain confederation, comprising eight interrelated Khatri sub-clans that emphasize shared mercantile heritage and endogamous marriage practices.
The etymology of Kohli remains largely unexplained in primary surname dictionaries, though some accounts propose a link to "kohla," a term for a furnace or kiln in regional dialects, potentially indicating ancestral involvement in metalworking or smithing occupations among early clan members. This occupational derivation aligns with broader patterns in Indian surnames but lacks definitive linguistic corroboration, as no ancient texts or inscriptions directly attest to it. The name appears among both Hindu and Sikh Khatris, reflecting Punjab's religious syncretism prior to the 20th century, with Sikh adoption often tied to conversions during the Sikh Gurus' era in the 15th18th centuries.
Demographically, Kohli bearers constitute a notable portion of Punjab's Khatri population, with genetic ancestry studies showing over 68% Northern Indian and Pakistani heritage among global carriers, underscoring its Punjabi roots. Historical migrations, including those during Mughal rule and British colonial partitions, dispersed the clan to urban diaspora hubs like Mumbai and overseas Punjabi communities, yet the core association remains with Punjab's Khatri networks.

Swiss-German origins

The Swiss-German surname Kohli, commonly rendered as Khli with the umlaut in native orthography, originates as a pet form or diminutive of an archaic personal name constructed from the Proto-Germanic element Colo-, cognate with Old Norse kollir denoting "helmet". This etymological root aligns with early medieval Germanic naming conventions, where such diminutivesoften ending in -i or -liemerged from hypocoristics (affectionate shortenings) of compound names emphasizing protective attributes like headgear, reflecting warrior or status symbolism in tribal societies.
Historical records trace the surname's adoption in fixed form to the late Middle Ages in Alemannic-speaking regions of Switzerland and southwestern Germany, where surnames solidified between the 13th and 15th centuries amid feudal documentation and parish registers. The variant Khli predominates in cantons such as Zurich, Bern, and Aargau, as well as in Baden-Wrttemberg, comprising nearly all European instances of the name and underscoring its localization to Germanic Europe (81% of bearers). Unlike occupational derivations (e.g., from Kohler for "charcoal burner" in some Low German contexts), the helmet-derived pet form prevails in Swiss-German contexts, distinguishing it from broader German variants.
Genealogical distributions indicate Khli bearers number in the thousands across Switzerland (over 2,000 as of recent estimates) and adjacent German areas, with migration patterns limited until 19th-century industrialization, which dispersed families to urban centers like Basel and Stuttgart. This contrasts with the unrelated Indian Kohli, highlighting independent evolution: the European form tied to onomastic traditions rather than geographic or caste descriptors. Primary evidence derives from church and civil records digitized in platforms like FamilySearch, confirming continuity from baptismal entries in the 16th century onward without evidence of phonetic shifts from non-Germanic sources.

Historical and demographic context

Historical usage

The surname Kohli has been historically employed among the Khatri community in Punjab, north-western India, as a clan identifier within the Khukhrain sub-group, comprising eight specific septs originating from regions including the Salt Range, Bhera, and Gujrat district in present-day Pakistan. These clans were associated with mercantile occupations, facilitating trade networks across Punjab, with documented migrations to inland towns such as Nurmahal and Jamsher in the Doaba region, reflecting economic adaptations during pre-colonial and colonial periods.
In Swiss-German contexts, the variant Khli (and anglicized Kohli) denoted a hereditary surname derived from a diminutive form of medieval personal names incorporating the element Colo-, akin to Old Norse kollir signifying "helmet," with earliest verifiable records appearing in Bern canton's parish registers by the late 17th century, such as the 1669 baptism of Barbara Khli in Guggisberg. Subsequent 19th-century instances, including baptisms in 1841 and births around 1811 in Canton Bern, indicate established familial lineages tied to rural Swiss communities before transatlantic emigration.
Transoceanic dissemination of the name is evidenced by U.S. census data from 1880, enumerating 41 Kohli households, predominantly among early immigrants from European and South Asian backgrounds, marking the onset of its broader demographic footprint beyond indigenous locales.

Global distribution and prevalence

The surname Kohli is the 11,872nd most common globally, borne by approximately 47,341 individuals, or about 1 in 153,937 people. Its distribution is heavily skewed toward Asia, where 81% of bearers reside, with 75% in South Asia and 62% in Indo-South Asia, reflecting its primary Indian origins among Khatri communities in northern India. In India, 34,259 individuals carry the name, concentrated in Punjab, Haryana, Himachal Pradesh, Delhi, and Jammu & Kashmir, at a frequency of 1 in 22,390.
Significant diaspora populations have emerged due to migration, particularly to Western countries and Gulf states for economic opportunities. In the United States, 3,265 bearers were recorded as of recent estimates, comprising 57.3% Asian or Pacific Islander ethnicity, largely of Northern Indian & Pakistani ancestry. Other notable concentrations include England (1,367), Canada (911), the United Arab Emirates (945), and Kuwait (627), often linked to professional and labor migration from India.

Notable individuals

In academia

Atul Kohli serves as the David K. E. Bruce Professor of International Affairs and Professor of Politics at Princeton University, where his research centers on the political economy of development, state-building, and democracy in developing countries. His work examines how state structures influence economic growth and political stability, drawing on comparative case studies from Asia, Latin America, and Africa; notable publications include State-Directed Development: Political Power and Industrialization in the Global Periphery (2004), which argues that cohesive authoritarian states have historically outperformed fragmented democracies in fostering industrialization. Kohli, an Indian-born scholar who holds Canadian and U.S. citizenship, earned his Ph.D. from the University of California, Berkeley in 1983 and has held visiting positions at institutions like the University of Toronto and the London School of Economics.
Martin Kohli is Emeritus Professor of Sociology at the European University Institute in Fiesole, Italy, specializing in life course sociology, aging, and social policy in Europe. Born in Switzerland in 1942, he has contributed to understanding generational contracts and welfare state sustainability, with key works such as co-editing Time for Retirement: Comparative Studies of Early Exit from the Labour Force (1991), which analyzes labor market exits using longitudinal data from multiple European countries. Kohli's research employs empirical methods to assess intergenerational equity, influencing policy discussions on pension reforms amid demographic shifts like population aging.
Other academics bearing the surname include Nidhi Kohli, Professor of Quantitative Methods in Education at the University of Minnesota, whose expertise lies in psychometric modeling and educational assessment, including Bayesian approaches to item response theory applied to large-scale testing data. Punit Kohli, Professor of Chemistry at Southern Illinois University Carbondale, focuses on nanoscale materials functionalized for biosensing and drug delivery, with publications on nanopore fabrication techniques yielding devices sensitive to single-molecule detection since joining the faculty around 2005. These scholars represent diverse fields, from political science and sociology to quantitative education and physical sciences, underscoring the surname's presence across academic disciplines primarily in North American and European institutions.

In administration

Dharamnath Prasad Kohli served as the founding Director of India's Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) from April 1, 1963, to May 31, 1968, establishing the agency's foundational structure and operational framework. He was awarded the Padma Bhushan in 1967 for his contributions to building the CBI on robust principles.
Vinay Kohli, an Indian Administrative Service (IAS) officer of the 1966 batch from the Assam-Meghalaya cadre, held key positions including Secretary in the Ministry of Information Technology and later Secretary in the Department of Chemicals and Petrochemicals in 2001. He also served as the first Chairperson of the Meghalaya State Electricity Regulatory Commission from 2008 until demitting office in January 2009.
Sanjiv Kohli, a 1988-batch Indian Foreign Service (IFS) officer, has undertaken multiple diplomatic assignments, including as High Commissioner to New Zealand from 2016 to 2019, High Commissioner to Tanzania from 2019 to 2021, and Ambassador to Serbia from 2021 to 2023. Prior to joining the IFS, he worked in industry and the Indian Railways as an electronics engineer.
S. S. Kohli, an IAS officer, served as Chairman and Managing Director of India Infrastructure Finance Company Limited (IIFCL), a government-owned entity focused on infrastructure financing, with over four decades in the financial sector. He has also held roles such as Independent Director at PTC India Financial Services Ltd.

In armed forces

Admiral Sourendra Nath Kohli (19161997) commanded the Indian Fleet as Rear Admiral from 1967 to 1969 before serving as the Chief of the Naval Staff from March 1973 to February 1976, overseeing naval operations during a period of modernization following the 1971 Indo-Pakistani War. He was awarded the Param Vishisht Seva Medal for distinguished service.
Captain Mohan Singh Kohli (19312025), an officer in the Indian Navy, led the 1965 Indian expedition to Mount Everest, achieving the first successful Indian ascent with nine summiteers, a record unbroken for 17 years. His military career intersected with mountaineering efforts supported by the armed forces, later transitioning to roles in the Indo-Tibetan Border Police.
Brigadier Rajendra Shamsher Singh Kohli (19192019) served in the British Indian Army during World War II, participating in campaigns where he demonstrated leadership but declined the Military Cross award, citing personal reservations about its implications. Born in Benares to a police chief, he continued service post-independence in the Indian Army.
Lieutenant General Nitin Kohli PVSM, AVSM, VSM, from the 1975 batch of the Corps of Signals, served as Signal Officer-in-Chief from September 2013 to July 2016, managing the Indian Army's telecommunications, IT infrastructure, and cyber defense systems. He also held the role of Director General of Manpower Planning and later commented publicly on strategic military matters post-retirement.

In entertainment

Armaan Kohli (born 23 March 1972) is an Indian actor known for his work in Bollywood films and television. He began his career as a child artist in films such as Badle Ki Aag (1982) and Tilak (1984), both directed by his father, filmmaker Rajkumar Kohli. His lead debut came with Virodhi (1992), followed by roles in Jaani Dushman: Ek Anokhi Kahani (2002), and a supporting part in Salman Khan's Prem Ratan Dhan Payo (2015). He gained additional visibility through reality shows like Bigg Boss 7 (2013).
Purab Kohli (born 23 February 1979) is an Indian actor, model, and former video jockey who transitioned from television to film. He rose to prominence as a VJ on Channel V and starred in the popular youth series Hip Hip Hurray (19982001). His film credits include Rock On!! (2008), My Name Is Khan (2010), Airlift (2016), and international projects like Netflix's Sense8 (2015).
Gurdeep Kohli (born 30 January 1980), also known as Gurdeep Punj, is an Indian television actress who started as a model in advertisements. She debuted on screen in the medical drama Sanjivani (20022005), portraying Dr. Juhi Singh, which established her in Hindi TV. Subsequent roles include Hemani Singh in Best of Luck Nikki (20132016).
Rochak Kohli is a Bollywood music director and composer whose debut song "Pani Da Rang" from Vicky Donor (2012) marked his entry into the industry. He has composed for films including Sonu Ke Titu Ki Sweety (2018), M.S. Dhoni: The Untold Story (2016), and Bad Newz (2024), earning recognition for hits like "Dil Chori" from Sonu Ke Titu Ki Sweety. 
Rahul Kohli (born 13 November 1985) is a British actor of Indian descent known for roles in American television. He gained acclaim as Ravi Chakrabarti in iZombie (20152019) and Sheriff Hassan in Netflix's Midnight Mass (2021), praised for portraying a complex Muslim law enforcement character. 
Dev Kumar (born Chaman Lal Kohli, 1 January 1930 1990) was a veteran character actor specializing in antagonistic roles in Hindi cinema from the 1960s to 1980s. Debuting in Shahi Raqsa (1965), he appeared in over 100 films, including Namak Halaal (1982), often cast as imposing villains due to his physical presence. 

In judiciary

Hima Kohli (born September 2, 1959) served as a judge of the Supreme Court of India from August 2021 until her retirement on September 1, 2024. Prior to her appointment to the Supreme Court, she was elevated as a judge of the Delhi High Court in 2006 after practicing as an advocate since 1984, specializing in civil, arbitration, and constitutional matters. In January 2021, she became the first woman Chief Justice of the Telangana High Court, a position she held until her transfer to the Supreme Court later that year.
During her three-year tenure at the Supreme Court, Kohli authored 40 judgments and participated in benches for 208 cases, often addressing issues in constitutional law, family matters, and women's rights. Her notable decisions included dissenting in the marriage equality case Supriyo v. Union of India (2023), where she upheld the constitutional right to marry for queer couples but emphasized legislative action over judicial overreach, and contributing to a split verdict on abortion rights in X v. Principal Secretary, Health and Family Welfare Department (2022), allowing termination beyond 24 weeks in certain exceptional circumstances. She also authored opinions reinforcing traditional family structures in adoption and surrogacy rulings while advocating for procedural fairness in criminal appeals.
Kohli's judicial philosophy balanced progressive interpretations with adherence to statutory limits, as evidenced in her emphasis on evidence-based reasoning in property disputes and environmental litigation during her High Court years. Post-retirement, she has engaged in legal education and advisory roles, including joining the board of the Princeton Foundation in April 2025 to promote ethical governance. As one of only 11 women judges in the Supreme Court's history up to her time, her career highlighted the underrepresentation of women in India's higher judiciary, where she noted the need for resilience amid institutional biases.

In politics

Om Prakash Kohli (19352023) was an Indian politician affiliated with the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP), serving as Governor of Gujarat from July 2014 to 2019. He previously held positions including membership in the Rajya Sabha, India's upper house of Parliament, and leadership roles within the BJP's Delhi unit.
Nalin Kohli serves as a national spokesperson for the BJP, India's ruling party as of 2025, and practices as a senior advocate at the Supreme Court of India. In this capacity, he frequently comments on national policy, electoral matters, and opposition activities in media appearances and press conferences.
Krishna Kumari Kohli, a member of Pakistan's Pakistan Peoples Party (PPP), was elected to the Senate in March 2018, becoming the first Hindu woman from the Dalit community to serve in Pakistan's upper house of Parliament. Representing Sindh province, she has advocated for minority rights, girls' education, and measures against honor killings and forced conversions.
While the Kohli surname has Swiss-German roots, no prominent elected politicians bearing it have been identified in Switzerland or Germany; diplomatic figures such as Natalie Kohli, Swiss Ambassador to Cameroon, Central African Republic, and Equatorial Guinea since 2023, represent related public service roles.

In science and medicine

Pushmeet Kohli is a British computer scientist of Indian descent specializing in artificial intelligence, machine learning, and their applications to scientific challenges. As Vice President of research at Google DeepMind, he leads the AI for Science project, which employs AI to address complex problems in fields like protein folding and materials discovery, and heads the Responsible and Reliable AI team to mitigate risks in AI deployment. Prior to DeepMind, Kohli directed research at Microsoft Research Cambridge, where his work advanced techniques in computer vision, probabilistic programming, and optimization algorithms, earning recognition for contributions to ethical AI frameworks.
Rahul Kohli is an American physician-scientist focusing on the biochemistry of genome modification and its implications for infectious diseases and medicine. He holds a tenured position as associate professor of medicine and associate professor of biochemistry and biophysics at the University of Pennsylvania Perelman School of Medicine, where his research examines enzymatic base editing in bacterial genomes to uncover mechanisms of pathogen evolution and antibiotic resistance. Kohli's lab integrates structural biology, chemical biology, and computational modeling to inform therapeutic strategies, with publications in high-impact journals on topics like cytidine deaminase evolution. His work has been supported by the Rita Allen Foundation and emphasizes causal links between genomic changes and disease outcomes.
Anil Kohli was an Indian dental surgeon who served as president of the Dental Council of India from 2004 to 2010, overseeing regulatory standards for dental education and practice nationwide. A graduate of Government Dental College in Nagpur, he specialized in oral health policy and advanced dental techniques, contributing to the establishment of postgraduate programs and ethical guidelines in Indian dentistry. Kohli's tenure focused on elevating professional accreditation amid growing demand for oral care in India's expanding healthcare system.
Rohit Kohli is an Indian-American pediatric gastroenterologist specializing in liver diseases and nutrition. He earned his MBBS from Armed Forces Medical College in India in 1999 and an MS in clinical investigation from Northwestern University in 2008, before joining Children's Hospital Los Angeles as division chief of gastroenterology, hepatology, and nutrition. Kohli's research centers on non-alcoholic fatty liver disease in children, with clinical trials exploring metabolic interventions; he has published over 100 peer-reviewed articles on pediatric hepatology and holds leadership roles in the North American Society for Pediatric Gastroenterology, Hepatology, and Nutrition.

In sports

Virat Kohli, born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India, is a right-handed batsman and occasional right-arm medium-pace bowler who has represented the Indian national cricket team across all formats. He made his international debut in August 2008 during the Twenty20 Quadrangular Series in Sri Lanka, scoring 12 runs in his first innings. Kohli rose to prominence during the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup, where India won the tournament, and he contributed 282 runs at an average of 70.50.
As captain of the Indian team from 2014 to 2021 in limited-overs formats and until 2022 in Tests, Kohli led India to the number one ranking in Test cricket and secured multiple bilateral series victories, including a historic Test series win in Australia in 2018-19. He received the ICC ODI Cricketer of the Year award in 2012 after topping the run-scoring charts in ODIs that year with 1,501 runs. In the Indian Premier League (IPL), playing primarily for Royal Challengers Bengaluru, Kohli holds records for the most runs (over 8,000 as of 2025), most centuries (8), and most fifties (63).
Kohli's international career statistics include 14,255 Test runs at an average of 57.71 before his retirement from the format in May 2025, along with 5 wickets. In ODIs, he has amassed runs at a strike rate of 93.26, opening the batting in 7 innings and topping the scores in 79. His T20I record features 125 matches with a focus on aggressive batting. Post-retirement from Tests, where he scored 9,230 runs at 46.85, Kohli continues in white-ball cricket. In the first ODI against New Zealand on 11 January 2026 at Vadodara, he scored 93 runs off 91 balls to guide India to victory, earning the Player of the Match award, and became the fastest player to reach 28,000 international runs in 557 innings, surpassing Kumar Sangakkara.
