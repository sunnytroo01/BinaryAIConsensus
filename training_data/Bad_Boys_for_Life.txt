Bad Boys for Life
 Ara Eve Leo Sal 
1x
 Bad Boys for Life is a 2020 American buddy cop action comedy film directed by Adil El Arbi and Bilall Fallah, serving as the third installment in the Bad Boys franchise and a direct sequel to Bad Boys II (2003). The story follows Miami Police Department detectives Mike Lowrey (Will Smith) and Marcus Burnett (Martin Lawrence), who reunite to confront a ruthless mother-son duo of drug lords seeking vengeance after past events, blending high-octane action with the duo's signature humor and banter. Produced by Columbia Pictures and distributed by Sony Pictures Releasing, the film was released theatrically in the United States on January 17, 2020.
The supporting cast includes Vanessa Hudgens as Kelly, Mike's former partner and leader of an elite task force; Alexander Ludwig as Dorn, a tech-savvy officer; and Joe Pantoliano reprising his role as Captain Conrad Howard. Notable villains are portrayed by Kate del Castillo as Isabel Aretas, the vengeful mother, and Jacob Scipio as her son Armando Aretas. The screenplay was written by Chris Bremner, with story credits to Peter Craig and Joe Carnahan, building on the franchise created by George Gallo. Filming took place primarily in Miami, Florida, capturing the vibrant urban setting central to the series.
Upon release, Bad Boys for Life achieved significant commercial success, earning $206.3 million domestically and $220.2 million internationally for a worldwide gross of $426.5 million against a production budget of $90 million, making it the highest-grossing film of the franchise at the time. It opened at number one at the North American box office with $62.5 million over its debut weekend, including the Martin Luther King Jr. Day holiday. Critically, the film received generally positive reviews, holding a 76% approval rating on Rotten Tomatoes based on 270 reviews, with praise for its energetic direction, explosive action, and the enduring chemistry between Smith and Lawrence, though some noted familiar plotting.
The movie garnered several accolades, including a win for Outstanding Motion Picture at the 52nd NAACP Image Awards, along with nominations for Best Actor for both Smith and Lawrence. It also received three Saturn Award nominations, including Best Action or Adventure Film, highlighting its impact in the action genre. Bad Boys for Life revitalized the franchise after a 17-year hiatus, paving the way for the subsequent entry, Bad Boys: Ride or Die (2024).

Synopsis

Plot

The film opens with Miami Police Department detectives Mike Lowrey and his partner Marcus Burnett, a family-oriented officer nearing retirement, pursuing a suspect in a high-speed chase through the city, highlighting their contrasting approaches to life and workMike as the ambitious, single-minded lawman and Marcus as the devoted family man seeking stability. Following the birth of Marcus's grandson, the duo celebrates at the hospital, but their joy is short-lived when a mysterious assassin shoots Mike in a targeted drive-by attack ordered by Isabel Aretas, a powerful Mexican drug cartel leader seeking vengeance against those who imprisoned her husband years earlier. Mike falls into a coma, prompting Marcus to question his impending retirement as he vows to uncover the attacker.
Six months later, Mike miraculously recovers and, against medical advice, rejoins the force, determined to track down his assailant, identified as Armando Aretas, Isabel's ruthless son and enforcer leading the Aretas cartel's operations. Marcus, pulled back into the fray despite his family's pleas for him to step away, partners with Mike once more as they integrate into the department's elite Advanced Miami Metro Operations (AMMO) task force, comprising tech-savvy officers including captain Rita Secada, who shares a past romantic history with Mike, and specialists Kelly, Dorn, and Rafe. The team investigates a series of cartel-orchestrated hits on witnesses and officials linked to the original case against Isabel's husband, including the murder of their former captain Conrad Howard, escalating the personal stakes for Mike and Marcus as the attacks draw closer to their loved ones.
As the AMMO unit pursues leads across Miami, they dismantle parts of the Aretas network, but the investigation reveals deeper family dynamics: Marcus grapples with his role as a grandfather and husband, urging Mike to confront his isolated lifestyle, while Mike's relentless drive uncovers that Armando is his biological son from a youthful undercover affair with Isabel during a prior operation. This twist intensifies Mike's internal conflict, blending paternal instinct with duty as he hesitates to kill the young man. The team traces Isabel and Armando to Mexico City, where they confront the cartel in a multi-location assault involving chases through crowded markets and a fortified compound.
In the climactic showdown, Armando, torn by his newly revealed heritage, turns against Isabel to protect Mike from her final attempt on his life, allowing Rita to fatally shoot Isabel. Though wounded, Mike survives, and Armando is arrested, but not before Mike promises to help redeem him. Back in Miami, Marcus abandons his retirement plans to continue partnering with Mike, reaffirming their brotherhood, while the film closes with Mike visiting Armando in prison, subtly hinting at potential future alliances in their ongoing fight against crime.

Cast

Bad Boys for Life features Will Smith reprising his role as Detective Michael "Mike" Lowrey, the high-energy detective who joins the elite AMMO task force and pursues high-risk operations despite personal vulnerabilities. Martin Lawrence returns as Detective Marcus Miles Burnett, Mike's longtime partner, now a family man who reluctantly rejoins the action after a life-threatening event. Vanessa Hudgens portrays Kelly Soto, a skilled marksman and member of the AMMO team.
In supporting roles, Alexander Ludwig plays Dorn, the tech-savvy drone operator and analyst for AMMO who provides crucial surveillance support. Charles Melton appears as Rafe, the athletic AMMO operative and Marcus's son-in-law through his marriage to Theresa. Paola Nez stars as Captain Rita Secada, the no-nonsense head of the AMMO unit and Mike's former flame. Joe Pantoliano reprises Captain Conrad Howard, the exasperated Miami PD captain overseeing Mike and Marcus. Theresa Randle returns as Theresa Burnett, Marcus's devoted wife and a voice of reason in their family dynamic.
Additional notable performances include DJ Khaled as Manny, a boisterous Miami butcher entangled in the criminal underworld who aids the protagonists in their investigation. Happy Anderson plays Picante Jenkins, a jittery informant connected to the city's illicit operations. The ensemble underscores the film's blend of veteran chemistry and fresh action-oriented characters driving the narrative of redemption and partnership.

Production

Development

Following the success of Bad Boys II in 2003, development on a third installment began in 2008, with director Michael Bay expressing interest in returning but highlighting high costs and the scheduling conflicts of stars Will Smith and Martin Lawrence as major hurdles. Early efforts included hiring screenwriter Peter Craig in 2009 to pen a script, but the project stalled amid ongoing script revisions and actor availability issues. By 2015, momentum briefly returned when Sony Pictures announced plans for both Bad Boys 3 and a fourth film, enlisting Joe Carnahan to direct and co-write alongside David Guggenheim's storyline contributions; however, persistent script dissatisfaction, particularly from Smith who insisted on refinements, led to further delays.
Carnahan departed in 2017 due to creative differences with Smith, prompting Bay to decline a return and opening the door for new directors. In 2018, Belgian filmmakers Adil El Arbi and Bilall Fallah were hired to helm the project, bringing a fresh action-comedy sensibility. That same year, Smith and Lawrence recommitted to reprising their roles as Mike Lowrey and Marcus Burnett, respectively, signaling a renewed push forward.
The script, ultimately credited to Chris Bremner with story elements from Peter Craig and Joe Carnahan, underwent revisions to align with the leads' vision. Sony greenlit production in October 2018 with a $90 million budget, announcing a start date for early 2019 and setting the film's release for January 2020. This approval marked the end of over a decade of development challenges, transforming the long-gestating sequel into a viable production.

Casting

The principal casting for Bad Boys for Life centered on reuniting the franchise's core duo while integrating a younger ensemble to support the established buddy-cop dynamic. In November 2018, Will Smith and Martin Lawrence officially confirmed their return as Detectives Mike Lowrey and Marcus Burnett, respectively, marking the project's greenlight after years of development hurdles.
Supporting roles were filled rapidly in late 2018 to align with the film's accelerated production timeline. Vanessa Hudgens was cast as Kelly, the leader of the AMMO task force, in December 2018, bringing her action credentials from projects like The Princess Switch series to the ensemble. Shortly thereafter, Alexander Ludwig joined as Dorn, with Charles Melton rounding out the new additions as Rafe, both announced as key supporting players to inject fresh energy into the narrative.
Paola Nez was selected for the role of Rita Secada in December 2018, contributing her bilingual versatility from Mexican cinema to portray a pivotal operative. For continuity, Joe Pantoliano reprised his role as Captain Conrad Howard, confirmed in early December 2018, ensuring the franchise's authoritative presence amid the expanded cast. Similarly, Theresa Randle returned as Theresa Burnett, Marcus's wife, in a supporting capacity announced in March 2019 to maintain familial ties from prior installments.

Filming

Principal photography for Bad Boys for Life commenced on January 14, 2019, in Atlanta, Georgia, directed by the Belgian filmmaking duo Adil El Arbi and Bilall Fallah. The production spanned nearly five months, capturing the high-energy action-comedy sequences that define the franchise. Filming concluded on June 7, 2019, as confirmed by star Will Smith in a social media post celebrating the wrap.
The bulk of the shoot occurred in Atlanta, which served as a stand-in for Miami due to its versatile urban landscapes and production incentives, allowing crews to recreate the sun-soaked Florida vibe efficiently. To ensure authenticity, additional scenes were filmed on location in Miami, Florida, capturing the city's iconic architecture and streets for key establishing shots. Action-heavy portions, including intense confrontations, were lensed in Mexico City, Mexico, providing dynamic backdrops for the film's international elements.
The production encountered logistical hurdles in executing the film's elaborate action set pieces, particularly the choreography of high-speed car chases and explosive shootouts, which demanded precise coordination among stunt teams, vehicles, and pyrotechnics to maintain safety and visual impact. Atlanta's winter weather, marked by cold temperatures and overcast skies, tested the crew's adaptability during outdoor sequences but did not significantly disrupt the schedule. Will Smith contributed to the authenticity by performing several of his own stunts in demanding scenes.

Post-production

The post-production of Bad Boys for Life was overseen by editors Dan Lebental and Peter McNulty, who collaborated to refine the film's pacing by balancing high-energy action sequences with comedic elements through a dedicated "comedy pass" in the editing process. They employed stylized techniques such as speed ramping, intraframe editing, and kinetic transitions to enhance the dynamic flow of chase scenes and confrontations, drawing from Lebental's background in music video editing to maintain a rhythmic intensity. The final runtime was established at 124 minutes, incorporating multiple alternate endings that were tested before selecting one that aligned with the directors' vision for a conclusive tone.
Visual effects work was supervised by Gregory D. Liegey at Mammal Studios, which delivered over 300 shots to augment key action elements including explosions, vehicle pursuits, and environmental enhancements. Additional contributions came from Soho VFX for compositing and effects integration, as well as The Third Floor for previsualization of complex stunt sequences to guide post-production refinements.
The film's score was composed by Lorne Balfe, who incorporated motifs from Mark Mancina's original themes in the Bad Boys series to evoke continuity while introducing fresh electronic and orchestral elements suited to the action-comedy hybrid. The soundtrack album featured hip-hop and Latin tracks, prominently including "RITMO (Bad Boys For Life)" by Black Eyed Peas and J Balvin, which was integrated into opening and climactic scenes to amplify the film's energetic vibe. Sound design, handled by Sony Pictures Post Production Services, emphasized immersive audio for gunfights and vehicular stunts to heighten the sensory impact of the edited sequences.

Release

Marketing

Sony Pictures launched the marketing campaign for Bad Boys for Life in early 2019, beginning with teaser images shared by Will Smith on Instagram in late January, featuring himself and Martin Lawrence in character as detectives Mike Lowrey and Marcus Burnett. These initial visuals generated buzz by evoking nostalgia for the franchise's high-octane buddy cop dynamic from the 1995 and 2003 originals.
The first official trailer debuted on September 4, 2019, via Sony's YouTube channel, emphasizing the revival of the series' signature blend of explosive action, humor, and the duo's longstanding partnership, with taglines like "Always bad. Forever bad." A second trailer followed on November 5, 2019, further showcasing high-stakes chases and comedic banter while teasing plot elements such as Lowrey's brush with death and the pair's team-up with a new elite unit.
Promotional strategies included a high-profile TV spot aired during Super Bowl LIV on February 2, 2020, capitalizing on the event's massive audience to highlight the film's adrenaline-fueled return. Sony also leveraged social media extensively, with Smith and Lawrence posting behind-the-scenes videos, joint interviews, and nostalgic content on platforms like Instagram and Twitter to underscore their real-life friendship and on-screen chemistry, amassing millions of views and interactions. Tie-in merchandise featured apparel and accessories branded with film motifs, distributed through retailers to extend the campaign's reach.
The campaign culminated in premieres that connected to the story's settings, including the world premiere at the TCL Chinese Theatre in Los Angeles on January 14, 2020, followed by the premiere in Miami on January 12. These events helped build global anticipation by linking the hype to authentic production sites.

Theatrical

Bad Boys for Life had its European premiere in Berlin, Germany, on January 7, 2020, at the Zoo Palast theater, attended by stars Will Smith and Martin Lawrence. The film then held a high-profile U.S. premiere on January 14, 2020, at the TCL Chinese Theatre in Hollywood, California, drawing celebrities and marking a key promotional event ahead of its wide release. A separate premiere took place in Miami Beach, Florida, on January 12, 2020, at the Regal South Beach Theater, tying into the film's Miami setting.
The film received a wide theatrical release in the United States and Canada on January 17, 2020, distributed by Sony Pictures Releasing under the Columbia Pictures label, opening in 3,775 theaters. Internationally, the rollout began simultaneously with the domestic release in over 50 countries, including the United Kingdom on January 17, 2020, and expanded progressively, with a later debut in China on August 14, 2020. Marketing trailers played a key role in building anticipation for this global launch.
Bad Boys for Life was certified for premium large-format screenings, including IMAX theaters for an enhanced visual experience starting January 17, 2020. It also featured 4DX presentations in select locations, incorporating motion seats, environmental effects, and sensory elements to immerse audiences in the action sequences. The Motion Picture Association rated the film R for strong bloody violence, language throughout, sexual references, and brief drug use.

Home media

Bad Boys for Life was released on digital platforms on March 31, 2020, by Sony Pictures Home Entertainment, allowing early access amid the COVID-19 pandemic theater closures. The physical home media versions, including 4K Ultra HD, Blu-ray, and DVD, followed on April 21, 2020, also distributed by Sony Pictures Home Entertainment. These releases capitalized on the film's theatrical success, which had grossed over $200 million domestically, driving demand for home viewing options.
The film became available for streaming on various services post-theatrical run. It joined Netflix in select international markets in 2020. It also streamed on HBO Max in the United States. As of November 2025, Bad Boys for Life is available to stream on fuboTV and YouTube TV in the United States, and for purchase or rental on Amazon Prime Video.
Home media editions include a range of special features enhancing viewer engagement. The Blu-ray and digital versions offer behind-the-scenes documentaries such as "Ride or Die: Making Bad Boys for Life" and "Partners in Crime: Behind the Scenes with Mike & Marcus," along with deleted and alternate scenes, outtakes, bloopers, and an audio commentary track featuring directors Adil El Arbi and Bilall Fallah, alongside stars Will Smith and Martin Lawrence, and producer Jerry Bruckheimer. These extras provide insights into the production process and the franchise's legacy.

Reception

Box office

Bad Boys for Life earned $62.5 million at the domestic box office during its three-day opening weekend from January 17 to 19, 2020, setting a record for the largest January debut for an R-rated film. Including the Martin Luther King Jr. Day holiday, the four-day domestic opening reached $73.0 million, while the worldwide opening totaled approximately $101 million. The film went on to gross $206.3 million domestically and $220.2 million internationally, for a worldwide total of $426.5 million against a $90 million production budget.
Key milestones included crossing the $100 million domestic threshold during its second weekend, reaching $120.7 million by day 10. It became the highest-grossing installment in the Bad Boys franchise, surpassing the inflation-adjusted domestic earnings of Bad Boys II ($138.6 million in 2003, equivalent to approximately $215 million in 2020 dollars). Internationally, performance was robust in Europe, led by the United Kingdom ($20.1 million), Germany ($17.8 million), and France ($13.6 million).
The film's theatrical run concluded amid the COVID-19 pandemic, with U.S. and international theater closures accelerating from mid-March 2020, limiting further earnings potential after two months in release. Despite this, it ranked as the top-grossing domestic release of 2020 for several months.

Critical response

Bad Boys for Life received generally positive reviews from critics, with a 76% approval rating on Rotten Tomatoes based on 270 reviews and an average rating of 6.7/10. On Metacritic, the film holds a score of 59 out of 100, indicating "mixed or average" reviews from 46 critics. Audiences polled by CinemaScore gave the film a rare "A" grade, reflecting strong word-of-mouth that contributed to its box office performance.
Critics widely praised the chemistry between Will Smith and Martin Lawrence, noting how their banter and camaraderie revitalized the buddy cop dynamic after a 17-year hiatus. The action sequences were highlighted for their energetic execution, blending high-octane chases and shootouts with modern flair under directors Adil El Arbi and Bilall Fallah. Many reviewers appreciated the film's fresh take on the genre, viewing it as a reinvigorating entry that balanced nostalgia with emotional depth, particularly in exploring the characters' aging and personal stakes.
However, some criticisms focused on the formulaic plot, which relied on predictable twists and over-the-top villainy reminiscent of earlier installments. Uneven pacing was noted in the middle act, where comedic detours occasionally disrupted the momentum. Reviewers also pointed to an over-reliance on nostalgia, including dated humor like Viagra gags, which risked making the film feel like a cash-grab sequel rather than a bold evolution.
In a 2.5 out of 4 star review, RogerEbert.com's Odie Henderson called it "the best of the three films," commending its action and humor while acknowledging its conventional structure. Variety's Owen Gleiberman offered a positive assessment, praising the leads' "A game" performances and the directors' ability to infuse the franchise with "fury and flair," though he critiqued its formulaic extravagance. Overall, the consensus positioned Bad Boys for Life as a crowd-pleasing return that succeeded more through star power and spectacle than narrative innovation.

Accolades

Bad Boys for Life received several nominations and wins across various awards ceremonies, particularly recognizing its action sequences, performances, and overall entertainment value. The film earned acclaim in fan-voted and genre-specific awards, highlighting the chemistry between leads Will Smith and Martin Lawrence, as well as its technical achievements.
The film was nominated for Best Action or Adventure Film at the 46th Saturn Awards but did not win.
At the 2020 People's Choice Awards, Bad Boys for Life won The Movie of 2020 and Will Smith received the Favorite Male Movie Star award.
It was nominated for Best Movie at the 2020 BET Awards.
The film secured a win for Outstanding Motion Picture at the 52nd NAACP Image Awards in 2021.

Legacy

Sequel

Following the commercial success of Bad Boys for Life, Sony Pictures confirmed development of a fourth installment in the franchise in February 2020. The project, eventually titled Bad Boys: Ride or Die, retained directors Adil El Arbi and Bilall Fallah from the previous film, with screenwriter Chris Bremner returning to pen the script alongside Will Beall. This continuation directly builds on the ending of Bad Boys for Life, where Captain Conrad Howard is critically wounded, setting up the sequel's central conflict.
Production faced delays due to the 2023 Writers Guild of America strike, with principal photography ultimately commencing in April 2023 in Atlanta, Georgia, before shifting to locations in Florida, including Miami, to capture the franchise's signature setting. The film carried a reported production budget of $100 million. Bad Boys: Ride or Die was released theatrically on June 7, 2024, by Sony Pictures Releasing.
The film grossed $404.5 million worldwide, falling short of its predecessor both domestically ($193.6 million vs. $206.3 million) and internationally ($211.0 million vs. $220.2 million) amid a competitive summer market. It earned a 97% audience score on Rotten Tomatoes based on over 10,000 verified ratings, reflecting strong fan approval for its action-comedy blend. The plot follows detectives Mike Lowrey and Marcus Burnett as they evade authorities and uncover a conspiracy to frame their late captain, Howard, for corruption tied to drug cartels.

Cultural impact

Bad Boys for Life is credited with revitalizing the buddy cop action-comedy genre by successfully resurrecting a dormant franchise after a 17-year hiatus, blending nostalgic elements with contemporary storytelling that emphasized character development over pure spectacle. The film's release demonstrated sustained audience interest in high-energy pairings of charismatic leads, influencing subsequent entries in similar franchises by prioritizing emotional depth alongside explosive action sequences.
The movie advanced representation in Hollywood action films through its diverse ensemble, featuring prominent Latinx performers such as Paola Nez (Mexican) as the seasoned Captain Rita Secada and Kate del Castillo (Mexican) in a supporting role, alongside Vanessa Hudgens (Filipino-Irish-Native American) as tech-savvy officer Kelly. This casting reflected Miami's multicultural landscape and challenged stereotypes by portraying women of color in empowered, non-tokenistic positions, while also addressing themes of aging action heroes transitioning into mentorship roles and the importance of family bonds. Actor Charles Melton, who plays the young officer Ramos, highlighted the film's inclusive approach, noting how it allowed audiences from various backgroundsincluding his own Korean heritageto see themselves in the narrative.
Post-release, the film's signature banter between Will Smith and Martin Lawrence spawned widespread memes celebrating their comedic chemistry, contributing to its enduring online popularity. During the COVID-19 pandemic, Bad Boys for Life gained traction on streaming platforms as one of the last major pre-lockdown theatrical successes, offering escapist entertainment that resonated with viewers confined at home. By 2025, retrospectives have positioned the film as a pivotal entry in Sony's action slate, underscoring its role in sustaining the franchise's relevance amid evolving audience preferences for diverse, character-focused blockbusters. This cultural resonance directly informed the 2024 sequel Bad Boys: Ride or Die.
