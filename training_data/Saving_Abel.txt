Saving Abel
 Ara Eve Leo Sal 
1x
 Saving Abel is an American hard rock band formed in 2004 in Corinth, Mississippi, by vocalist Jared Weeks and guitarist Jason Null, drawing its name from the biblical story of Cain and Abel with a twist on the phrase "there was no saving Abel." The group is known for its post-grunge and southern rock influences, achieving mainstream success with their self-titled debut album in 2008, which featured the multi-platinum single "Addicted" that peaked at number 20 on the Billboard Hot 100 and number 2 on the Mainstream Rock chart.
The band's debut album, Saving Abel, released via Virgin Records, peaked at number 49 on the Billboard 200 and was certified platinum by the RIAA, selling over one million copies, while follow-up singles "18 Days" and "Drowning (Face Down)" also charted on rock radio, reaching number 6 and number 3 on the Mainstream Rock chart, respectively. Subsequent releases included Miss America (2010), featuring the single "The Sex Is Good"; Bringing Down the Giant (2012), with tracks like the title song; and Blood Stained Revolution (2014), the final album fronted by Scott Austin before Weeks' return. These efforts solidified Saving Abel's presence in the post-grunge scene, with millions of streams and sales accumulated across platforms.
Lineup changes have defined the band's trajectory since the early 2010s: Weeks left in 2013 to pursue solo work, replaced by Scott Austin, who fronted the group for Blood Stained Revolution and until 2021, when Weeks rejoined for tours and promotions. However, Weeks departed again in January 2024, leading to an amicable split where he continues touring under the Saving Abel name with a separate lineup at events like bike rallies and festivals, while founding members Jason Null (guitar), Scott Bartlett (guitar), and Scott Wilson (bass) plan independent performances in 2025. In 2025, Weeks' version of the band released new singles such as "Keep Swinging". Despite these shifts, the band remains active, with ongoing VIP tour packages and a legacy of high-energy rock anthems.

History

Formation and early career (20042007)

Saving Abel was formed in 2004 in Corinth, Mississippi, by lead vocalist Jared Weeks and lead guitarist Jason Null, who drew inspiration from the biblical story of Cain and Abel for the band's name. The duo met at a friend's house that year, where Null was rehearsing with a rival band while Weeks was learning guitar; they quickly bonded over shared musical interests and began writing acoustic songs together the following day. Their early sound was rooted in Southern rock influences, blending raw emotion with driving guitar riffs.
By early 2005, Weeks and Null had expanded the lineup to include rhythm guitarist Scott Bartlett, bassist Eric Taylor, and drummer Blake Dixon, solidifying the band's core electric formation. That same year, the group caught the attention of producer Skidd Mills, who invited them to record demos at his 747 Studios in Memphis, Tennessee. These sessions resulted in a self-titled independent EP featuring tracks like "Addicted" and "18 Days," which served as demos for label outreach and were distributed locally to build interest.
From 2005 to 2007, Saving Abel honed their live sound through performances in small venues across the Mississippi and Tennessee region, including bars, clubs, and a pivotal show at a VFW hall in Jackson, Tennessee, where industry scouts took notice. The band supplemented these gigs with online promotion via platforms like MySpace, gradually cultivating a regional fanbase while members balanced day jobs, such as Weeks working as a phlebotomist. Their efforts culminated in 2007 when a demo copy of "Addicted" reached Virgin Records executive Jason Flom during a casual encounter, leading to the band's signing with the label after a showcase performance.

Debut and mainstream success (20082010)

Saving Abel released their self-titled debut album on March 11, 2008, through Virgin Records, with production handled by Skidd Mills at 747 Studios in Memphis, Tennessee. The record featured a mix of post-grunge and hard rock elements, building on the band's earlier independent EP of the same name, and marked their entry into the major label scene after signing with Virgin in 2007. The album debuted at number 49 on the Billboard 200 chart and quickly gained traction through radio airplay and music video exposure.
The lead single, "Addicted," propelled the band to mainstream attention upon its release in early 2008, peaking at number 20 on the Billboard Hot 100 and reaching number 2 on the Mainstream Rock chart, where it held for 11 weeks as one of the year's most-played active rock tracks. The song's provocative lyrics and energetic riff-driven sound earned it heavy rotation on MTV, further boosting the album's visibility and helping it achieve Platinum certification by the RIAA. Follow-up singles "18 Days," released later in 2008, climbed to number 6 on the Mainstream Rock chart, while "Drowning (Face Down)" followed in 2009, peaking at number 3 on the Mainstream Rock chart and adding to the album's momentum. The album itself was certified Gold by the RIAA on March 6, 2009, and later Platinum, signifying shipments of over 1,000,000 units in the United States.
From 2008 to 2010, Saving Abel embarked on extensive touring to capitalize on their rising profile, supporting major acts such as Nickelback on their Dark Horse Tour in 2009 and Three Days Grace during various arena dates. They also performed at prominent festivals, including multiple appearances at Rock on the Range in Columbus, Ohio, in 2008 and 2009, where they shared stages with bands like Slipknot and Disturbed. By 2010, the album had surpassed 1,000,000 shipments, solidifying Saving Abel's position as a notable post-grunge act in the rock landscape during a period dominated by similar radio-friendly hard rock bands.

Subsequent albums and lineup changes (20112014)

Following the success of their debut album, Saving Abel released their second studio album, Miss America, on June 8, 2010, through Virgin Records, which had recently been affected by the merger of its parent company EMI with other labels under Universal Music Group. The album featured singles such as "The Sex Is Good," which reached No. 1 on the Billboard Mainstream Rock chart in 2010, and "Miss America," released in 2011 to promote ongoing touring. Despite these efforts, Miss America debuted at No. 24 on the Billboard 200 but experienced declining commercial performance compared to their debut, ultimately signaling challenges in maintaining major-label momentum.
In 2011, the band underwent its first significant lineup change when founding drummer Blake Dixon departed, citing personal reasons; he was replaced by Michael McManus for live performances and recording sessions. This shift coincided with preparations for their next release, as the group transitioned away from Virgin Records to the independent label eOne Music. Their third studio album, Bringing Down the Giant, arrived on July 17, 2012, showcasing a harder-edged post-grunge sound with tracks emphasizing resilience and introspection. The title track served as the lead single, peaking at No. 6 on the Billboard Mainstream Rock chart, though the album itself charted lower at No. 74 on the Billboard 200, reflecting the band's move toward independent distribution and reduced radio play.
Tensions within the band escalated in late 2013, culminating in the departure of lead vocalist and co-founder Jared Weeks on December 26, announced via a personal statement on the band's social media channels. Weeks cited a desire to pursue a solo career amid struggles with alcoholism and internal conflicts that had strained relationships during years of relentless touring. Shortly thereafter, the band introduced Scotty Austin as their new frontman, a move that allowed them to continue without interruption; Austin's energetic style helped stabilize the lineup for upcoming projects. Drummer Michael McManus also left around this time, with session players filling in as needed.
Under Austin's leadership, Saving Abel released their fourth studio album, Blood Stained Revolution, on November 11, 2014, through the independent Tennessippi Whiskey Records, coinciding with Veterans Day to honor themes of struggle and perseverance in the lyrics. The album explored motifs of personal redemption and overcoming adversity, drawing from the band's recent upheavals, with the title track as the lead single reaching No. 31 on the Billboard Mainstream Rock chart. This release marked a period of adaptation for the group, as they navigated independent status and rebuilt their identity amid ongoing commercial challenges.

Hiatus and revival (2015present)

Following the release of their major-label album Bringing Down the Giant in 2012, Saving Abel independently issued Blood Stained Revolution on November 11, 2014, marking the debut of new lead vocalist Scotty Austin after the 2013 departure of Jared Weeks, which had initiated a period of lineup flux. The album, also featuring new drummer Steven Pulley, represented the band's shift away from label support and culminated in a full hiatus by early 2015, with no subsequent studio recordings until 2025.
From 2015 to 2024, Saving Abel's activity remained sporadic, limited primarily to occasional tours without any new music releases. Austin continued as frontman until his departure in summer 2021 to pursue a solo career, after which the band operated with interim vocal arrangements. In 2017, experienced bassist Scott Wilson joined the lineup, replacing Eric Taylor and providing continuity amid the reduced schedule. This era of dormancy reflected ongoing challenges in maintaining momentum post-label era, though the core membersguitarists Jason Null and Scott Bartlettremained committed to the band's legacy.
The band's revival gained momentum in 2025 amid internal disputes. In May, Null, Bartlett, and Wilson released a joint statement clarifying their disassociation from unauthorized performances by Weeks, who had been touring separately using the Saving Abel name without their involvement. Later that June, Null announced plans for a fall tour, reigniting interest in the group's future.
August 2025 marked a pivotal shift with the addition of Randy Arthur Webb as lead vocalist, drawn from the Southern rock scene and bringing fresh energy to the lineup alongside Null, Bartlett, Wilson, and new drummer Garfield Redden. This change, announced via official channels, positioned the band for a creative resurgence.
By September, media outlets covered the development as the dawn of a "new era" for Saving Abel, emphasizing upcoming fall performances that promised a revitalized Southern rock sound rooted in the band's Mississippi origins while appealing to longtime fans. The tour, kicking off in October across the U.S., featured a setlist blending classics like "Addicted" with recent singles such as "Stars" and "Dodged a Bullet," underscoring the group's renewed focus on live energy and evolution. As of November 2025, the fall tour concluded successfully, featuring performances across the U.S. and highlighting the new lineup's chemistry.

Former members

Jared Weeks served as the lead vocalist for Saving Abel from its formation in 2004 until 2013, when he departed amid personal struggles, including substance abuse issues that impacted his mental health and career decisions. He rejoined the band in 2021 until departing again in January 2024. His contributions were central to the band's early breakthrough, particularly on their self-titled debut album.
Scott Austin joined as lead vocalist in 2013 following Weeks' exit and remained with the band until 2021, leaving to focus on his solo project.
Eric Taylor played bass from 2007 to 2017, departing as part of the broader lineup shifts that occurred after Weeks' initial departure.
Blake Dixon was the original drummer, performing with the band from 2004 to 2011 before exiting ahead of subsequent major changes in the group's direction.

Musical style and influences

Musical style

Saving Abel's music is primarily rooted in post-grunge and hard rock, infused with distinctive Southern rock elements that manifest through gritty guitar riffs and anthemic choruses designed for high-energy live performances and radio play. The band's sound emphasizes raw, riff-heavy arrangements that blend aggressive instrumentation with melodic hooks, creating a style that appeals to fans of mainstream rock acts like Nickelback or Shinedown.
Central to their signature aesthetic are heavy guitar-driven tracks paired with emotional lyrics delving into themes of tumultuous relationships and personal redemption, as vividly captured in the platinum-certified single "Addicted," which portrays an intense, addictive romantic dependency. The dual guitar attack from Jason Null and Scott Bartlett forms the backbone of this approach, layering intricate riffs and solos over driving rhythms to build tension and release in each song. Vocalist Jared Weeks (20042013) delivered dynamic performances that transitioned seamlessly from soaring, melodic verses to aggressive, shouted choruses, enhancing the emotional intensity.
Over time, Saving Abel's style evolved from the polished, radio-friendly post-grunge of their self-titled debut (2008) and Miss America (2010), which prioritized catchy, accessible anthems, to a heavier, more raw edge in later independent releases like Bringing Down the Giant (2012) and Blood Stained Revolution (2014). This shift incorporated deeper Southern rock influences, with intensified heaviness in the riffs and production that favored unpolished authenticity over commercial sheen. Subsequent works, including their 2021 EP Shade of Grace Twenty Year Songs and 2025 revival efforts with new vocalist Randy Webb, maintained this rugged evolution while revisiting early melodic strengths, as heard in singles like "Keep Swinging," blending raw intensity with melodic elements.

Influences

Saving Abel's music draws heavily from Southern rock traditions, shaped by the band's origins in Corinth, Mississippi. Founding members Jason Null and Jared Weeks have cited classic Southern acts like Lynyrd Skynyrd and the Allman Brothers Band as key influences, particularly for their guitar-driven tones and narrative-driven lyrics that emphasize personal struggle and resilience. The regional blues heritage of the Mississippi Delta further infuses their sound with heartfelt, emotive themes, reflecting the area's storied musical legacy of raw expression and storytelling.
Broader rock influences extend to heavy riffing from classic hard rock bands, with Null highlighting AC/DC and Poison as formative for their energetic, anthemic structures. Null's background in metal, inspired by acts like Metallica, adds intensity to the band's songwriting, blending aggressive elements with melodic hooks. In contrast, post-grunge contemporaries such as Nickelback inform their accessible, radio-friendly song structures, though Saving Abel maintains a distinct Southern edge.
Member contributions further diversify these influences. The band's emotive delivery incorporates country-tinged inflections drawn from artists like Willie Nelson and Glen Campbell. Null's songwriting pulls from 1990s alternative rock, including bands like Candlebox and Helmet, which shape the introspective and dynamic layers in their compositions. These elements converge in their debut album, manifesting as a blend of gritty riffs and vulnerable lyrics.

Discography

Studio albums

Saving Abel's debut studio album, titled Saving Abel, was released on March 11, 2008, through Virgin Records. The record consists of 11 tracks, with the lead single "Addicted" becoming a major hit that propelled the band's early success. It debuted and peaked at number 49 on the Billboard 200 chart and received a gold certification from the RIAA on March 10, 2009, for shipments of 500,000 units.
The band's second studio album, Miss America, followed on June 8, 2010, also via Virgin Records. Produced by Skidd Mills, the 11-track effort marked a more mature evolution in their sound while retaining post-grunge roots. It achieved a higher chart position than the debut, peaking at number 24 on the Billboard 200.
Bringing Down the Giant, the third studio album, came out on July 17, 2012, under Entertainment One Music. This 11-track release was recorded with the original lineup including vocalist Jared Weeks. The album peaked at number 74 on the Billboard 200, reflecting a shift toward independent distribution channels.
The fourth and most recent studio album, Blood Stained Revolution, was independently released on November 11, 2014, through Tennessippi Whiskey Records, featuring vocalist Scott Austin, who had replaced Jared Weeks in late 2013. Comprising 10 tracks, it adopted a heavier, more aggressive sound and received limited distribution primarily through digital platforms and direct sales. Unlike prior releases, it did not chart on the Billboard 200.

Singles

Saving Abel's debut single, "Addicted", released in February 2008 from their self-titled album, became their breakthrough hit, peaking at number 20 on the Billboard Hot 100 and number 2 on the Mainstream Rock chart. The song received significant radio airplay and a music video directed by Anthony Honn, which featured the band performing in a stylized, high-energy setting.
The follow-up single, "18 Days", also from the 2008 album, was released to rock radio in September 2008 and peaked at number 6 on the Mainstream Rock chart, while reaching number 104 on the Hot 100. A music video accompanied the release, emphasizing the song's emotional narrative about regret and loss. An acoustic version appeared on the band's 18 Days Tour EP in April 2009.
In March 2009, "Drowning (Face Down)" served as the third single from the debut album, achieving a peak of number 3 on the Mainstream Rock chart. The track's intense post-grunge sound contributed to its strong rock radio performance, and a music video highlighted themes of personal struggle.
From the 2010 album Miss America, "The Sex Is Good" was released in September 2010 as the lead single, topping the Mainstream Rock chart for one week and marking the band's sole number-one hit on that ranking. It garnered moderate success on alternative rock formats, peaking at number 22 on the Alternative Songs chart. "Out of the Dark", another single from Miss America issued in 2011, received rock radio play but did not achieve significant chart prominence.
The band released "Blood Stained Revolution" in 2014 as a promotional single from their album Blood Stained Revolution, focusing on themes of rebellion and resilience amid their shift to self-production.
In 2023, Saving Abel released "Baptize Me" in April and "Fire" in August, marking their return to new music after nearly a decade.
In 2025, the band issued "Dodged A Bullet", "Keep Swinging" in August, and "Stars" in October, with accompanying music videos. These singles did not chart significantly but continued the band's post-grunge style.
