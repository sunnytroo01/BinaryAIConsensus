Bent Knee
 Ara Eve Leo Sal 
1x
 Bent Knee is an American experimental art rock band formed in 2009 in Boston, Massachusetts, by students at the Berklee College of Music. The group is known for its genre-blending style that fuses progressive rock, pop, and avant-garde elements, featuring intricate compositions, powerful lead vocals by Courtney Swain, and a democratic approach to songwriting where all members contribute equally.
Originally a five-piece ensemble including co-founders Swain (vocals and keyboards) and Ben Levin (guitar and vocals), violinist Chris Baum, bassist Jessica Kion, and drummer Gavin Wallace-Ailsworth, the band debuted with a self-titled album in 2011 that showcased their raw, eclectic sound. Their music often explores themes of emotion, relationships, and introspection through dynamic shifts in tempo, unconventional structures, and lush instrumentation, earning praise for live performances that emphasize energy and precision. Following lineup changes, including the departure of Levin and Kion after a 2022 tour, the current quartet consists of Swain, Baum, Wallace-Ailsworth, and guitarist Vince Welch, who joined to refresh the band's direction.
Bent Knee has released seven studio albums, with Shiny Eyed Babies (2014) marking their breakthrough in the progressive and art rock scenes through its violent yet melodic fusion of jazz, rock, and classical influences. Subsequent works like Say So (2016), Land Animal (2017), You Know What They Mean (2019), and Frosting (2021) refined their signature complexity, incorporating guest musicians and experimental production to heighten emotional depth. Their latest album, Twenty Pills Without Water (2024), reflects a return to concise, vocal-driven songcraft amid personal transitions, solidifying their reputation as innovative boundary-pushers in modern rock. The band's evolution highlights their commitment to artistic growth, with tours across North America and Europe building a dedicated following in the indie and prog communities.

Formation and Early Years

Origins at Berklee College of Music

Bent Knee was formed in Boston, Massachusetts, in 2009 by Ben Levin and Courtney Swain, who were students at the Berklee College of Music. The duo initially collaborated as a laptop-based project, trading sound files to develop their early material, before expanding into a full live ensemble.
The initial lineup came together through connections at Berklee, incorporating Gavin Wallace-Ailsworth on drums, Jessica Kion on bass and vocals, and Chris Baum on violin, alongside Levin on guitar and backing vocals and Swain on vocals and keyboards. This assembly reflected the school's diverse musical environment, where members drew from various genres to build a cohesive yet boundary-pushing group. Multi-instrumentalism was inherent from the start, with several members contributing across roles to enhance the band's dynamic texture.
The early songwriting process was highly collaborative, beginning with individual core ideas from members that were then refined collectively during rehearsals. Emphasis was placed on experimental elements, such as blending unconventional structures and timbres, to create a sound that defied traditional pop and rock conventions while maintaining accessibility. This democratic approach ensured consensus on arrangements, often involving iterative testing and revision to capture the group's shared vision.
Bent Knee's first informal performances and rehearsals occurred within Boston's local music scene around 2009-2010, where the band hashed out material like "Funeral" in small venues and practice spaces. These early outings, even before a complete lineup was solidified, allowed the group to experiment live and build their foundational creative dynamics amid creative debates and refinements.

Debut Releases and Initial Performances

Bent Knee self-released their eponymous debut album on September 27, 2011, marking the band's initial foray into recording as a collective formed at Berklee College of Music. The album was recorded at Mercenary Audio, Big T Productions, and The Record Co in the Boston area, with production and mixing handled by Vince Welch, engineering by Adam Brass and Chris Ray, and mastering by Randy Roos at Squam Sound. Featuring tracks like "Funeral" and "Nave," the release showcased experimental compositions characterized by intricate arrangements, sultry vocals from Courtney Swain, and a blend of art rock with diverse instrumentation including violin and keyboards. This DIY effort highlighted the band's democratic approach to songwriting, emphasizing emotional depth and boundary-pushing structures in the progressive and experimental rock genres.
Building on their initial output, Bent Knee self-released their second album, Shiny Eyed Babies, on November 11, 2014, further evolving their sound through self-production in the Boston indie scene. Recorded at Woolly Mammoth Sound and The Record Co, the album was again produced and mixed by Vince Welch, with engineering by Welch and Adam Brass, and mastered by Ray Jeffery. Key tracks such as "Way Too Long" and "Skin" incorporated art rock elements like complex compositions, soulful vocals, and expanded instrumentation including trumpet and saxophone, reflecting a more synergistic and emotionally intricate style compared to their debut. As a DIY release, it served as a bold statement of the band's maturation, prioritizing unconventional arrangements over mainstream accessibility.
During this period from 2011 to 2014, Bent Knee focused on live performances in the Northeast U.S., particularly in the Boston area, to cultivate a grassroots following. They played intimate venues like the Davis Square Theatre in Somerville, Massachusetts, where a full concert was captured in July 2014, showcasing their dynamic stage presence and experimental material. Early shows at local Boston spots and small regional festivals helped build word-of-mouth support within the indie and progressive music communities, emphasizing their homegrown ethos amid the early 2010s DIY landscape. Operating independently, the band navigated funding and distribution hurdles typical of self-released acts, relying on platforms like Bandcamp for direct sales and personal networks for promotion.

Career Milestones

Label Signings and Album Releases

In late 2015, Bent Knee signed with the independent label Cuneiform Records, marking their entry into a more established progressive music distribution network following self-released efforts. This deal facilitated the production and release of their third studio album, Say So, on May 20, 2016. The album was produced and mixed by band member Vince Welch, with engineering by Matt Beaudoin at Q Division Studios in Somerville, Massachusetts, and additional recording at The Record Company and Converse Rubber Tracks Boston; it was mastered by Randy Roos at Squam Sound.
By April 2017, the band transitioned to Inside Out Music, a Sony-affiliated progressive rock imprint, for their fourth album, Land Animal, released on June 23, 2017. This signing expanded their reach in the prog and art rock scenes. Production again fell to Vince Welch, with engineering at Q Division Studios and Big Nice Studio in Rhode Island, emphasizing a polished yet eclectic sound through professional facilities that enhanced the album's dynamic instrumentation.
Bent Knee's fifth album, You Know What They Mean, followed on Inside Out Music on October 11, 2019, continuing the partnership without a noted shift to independence. Welch handled production, building on prior collaborations to integrate rock, pop, and avant-garde elements seamlessly.
In 2021, Bent Knee signed with Take This To Heart Records, an indie label focused on alternative and emo-adjacent acts, for their sixth album, Frosting, released on November 5, 2021. This move allowed greater creative autonomy, with the album produced and mixed by Welch, engineered across home studios, Big Nice Studio, The Record Company, and Tiny Telephone in San Francisco, and mastered by Roos.
The band's seventh album, Twenty Pills Without Water, arrived on Take This To Heart Records on August 30, 2024, amid themes of anxiety, depression, and personal upheaval following recent lineup changes. Welch produced, engineered, mixed, and mastered the record, with sessions at Big Nice Studio, The Record Company, and Tiny Telephone, incorporating influences from Phoebe Bridgers and Radiohead to explore coping mechanisms and transience.

Lineup Changes and Transitions

In 2022, Bent Knee underwent a pivotal lineup transition when founding members Ben Levin and Jessica Kion departed after 13 years, marking the end of the original formation established at Berklee College of Music. Levin, who served as lead guitarist and backing vocalist, and Kion, the bassist and backing vocalist, who are a couple, cited the demanding lifestyle of touring and personal life changessparked by a "pandemic epiphany"as the primary reasons for leaving, with no reported animosity among the members. The departures were highlighted during the band's summer farewell tour, providing a bittersweet close to their tenure. Specifically, Levin shifted focus to his longstanding solo project, the Ben Levin Group, known for its concept albums exploring themes like death. Kion, meanwhile, directed her energies toward other creative endeavors outside the band, including her project Justice Cow.
To stabilize the group, longtime collaborator Vince Welch was integrated as a full band member following the 2022 changes, contributing on synthesizers, guitar, bass, and production alongside remaining core members Courtney Swain, Chris Baum, and Gavin Wallace-Ailsworth. This adjustment formed a leaner four-piece configuration, emphasizing Welch's production expertise to maintain the band's intricate sound.
The personnel shifts notably affected Bent Knee's creative direction, resulting in a more streamlined approach to songwriting and performance. On their 2024 album Twenty Pills Without Waterthe first recorded without Levin and Kionthe material reflects themes of upheaval and adaptation, with a reduced emphasis on complex guitar work and greater reliance on violin textures from Baum and bass lines handled by Swain on select tracks. Live shows were similarly adapted for the smaller lineup, with some pieces from the original configuration retired due to instrumental challenges, while the band supported the new release with a tour that highlighted their evolved, groove-oriented dynamic.

Musical Style and Influences

Genre and Sound Characteristics

Bent Knee is primarily classified as an art rock band, incorporating elements of avant-garde pop and progressive rock to create a borderless sound that defies easy categorization. Their music blends influences from jazz, electronic, and classical traditions, resulting in a multi-genre fusion characterized by unconventional song structures and seamless integration of disparate styles such as pop, minimalist, and avant-garde.
A hallmark of Bent Knee's sonic identity is the versatile vocals of lead singer Courtney Swain, who employs a range from intimate whispers to powerful, emotive deliveries often processed through effects pedals for textured harmonies. This is complemented by intricate instrumentation, featuring violin lines from Chris Baum that add classical-tinged expressiveness and Swain's keyboard work that weaves electronic and ambient layers into the mix. The band's compositions exhibit dynamic shifts, with abrupt tempo changes and multifaceted forms that build tension through rhythmic complexities before resolving into melodic releases.
Over time, Bent Knee's sound has evolved from the raw, experimental edge of their early releases to a more polished approach emphasizing thematic storytelling and synthetic production. Their 2021 album Frosting exemplifies this maturation, incorporating heavily processed dream-pop elements, auto-tuned vocals, and soundscapey textures for quirky, immersive narratives that lean further into pop accessibility while retaining avant-garde unpredictability. Following lineup changes, their 2024 album Twenty Pills Without Water continues this evolution with a shift toward more concise, vocal-driven songcraft and a grounded art rock sound that highlights melody, introspection, and dynamic string arrangements. Live performances enhance this fusion through improvisation, allowing the group to expand on studio arrangements with spontaneous energy drawn from jazz and theater traditions.

Key Influences and Collaborations

Bent Knee's music draws heavily from experimental rock and progressive traditions, with band members frequently citing Radiohead as a unanimous influence for their innovative song structures and atmospheric depth. Guitarist Ben Levin has highlighted Radiohead's impact alongside artists like Sufjan Stevens and Kendrick Lamar, emphasizing how these shaped the band's blend of emotional introspection and rhythmic complexity. Similarly, Bjrk's influence is evident in early compositions, such as the track "Urban Circus," which was directly inspired by her song "Pagan Poetry" for its unconventional vocal and structural elements. King Crimson's progressive experimentation also resonates, particularly through drummer Gavin Wallace-Ailsworth's admiration for Bill Bruford's playing, which informed Bent Knee's polyrhythmic and dynamic shifts.
Jazz elements contribute to the band's improvisational flair, integrating complex harmonies and spontaneous interplay, as seen in tracks like "Way Too Long" that fuse jazz phrasing with heavier rock textures. Violinist Chris Baum's classical training adds layered, multi-string-like textures, drawing on folk traditions for earthy, narrative-driven lines that enhance the group's avant-garde leanings. Electronic influences appear through sound designer Vince Welch's loops and effects, creating seamless integrations of minimalist and pop elements across their recordings.
Collaborations have expanded Bent Knee's reach within progressive circles, including joint North American tours with Haken and Leprous in 2018, where shared bills amplified their genre-fusing sound. They also supported VOLA alongside Wheel in 2023, fostering cross-pollination among modern prog acts. Earlier tours with The Dillinger Escape Plan, Cult Leader, and O'Brother further honed their high-energy performances and validated their eclectic approach.
Guitarist Ben Levin's side projects, such as the Ben Levin Group and collaborations with Justice Cow, have influenced Bent Knee's sound by exploring jazz-inflected improvisation and experimental compositions prior to the band's 2022 activities. Vocalist Courtney Swain's lyrics often stem from personal introspection and literary inspirations, drawing on poetry and emotional narratives to infuse themes of vulnerability and human experience, as in her references to cold-weather motifs and inner reflection.

Band Members

Current Members

Bent Knee's current lineup as of 2025 consists of four core members who have shaped the band's evolution into a streamlined art-rock ensemble following the departures of key contributors in 2022.
Courtney Swain serves as lead vocalist, keyboardist, and bassist, functioning as the primary songwriter and frontwoman since the band's formation in 2009 at Berklee College of Music. Her multifaceted role drives the group's lyrical depth and melodic structures, with her versatile voice anchoring the avant-pop aesthetic across albums.
Gavin Wallace-Ailsworth has provided drums since the band's early years around 2010, delivering a rhythmic foundation that balances power and nuance while incorporating percussion experimentation to support the music's intricate dynamics. His contributions emphasize textural variety, enhancing live performances and recordings with energetic precision.
Chris Baum handles violin, backing vocals, and guitar, bringing string arrangements and multi-instrumental layers that enrich the band's orchestral textures since joining in the band's formative period around 20092011. In recent years, he has expanded to prominent guitar duties, weaving melodic and atmospheric elements into the core sound.
Vince Welch plays synthesizers, guitar, production, and bass, managing electronic elements and studio production; he integrated as a full band member in the mid-2010s and has assumed broader instrumental responsibilities post-2022 lineup transitions. His work fuses analog and digital sounds, contributing to the band's polished, genre-defying productions.

Former Members

Bent Knee's original lineup included additional members who contributed to the band's early development and recordings.
Ben Levin was a co-founder, guitarist, and vocalist from 2009 until his departure in 2022 following the band's tour.
Jessica Kion played bass and provided vocals from around 2011 until leaving in 2022.
Michael O'Brien served as a multi-instrumentalist (woodwinds, keyboards, guitar) during the band's formation in 2009, contributing to the 2011 self-titled debut before departing in the early 2010s.

Discography

Studio Albums

Bent Knee's eponymous debut studio album, released on September 27, 2011, via self-release, features 10 tracks including "Urban Circus" and "I Don't Love You Anymore." Produced by Vince Welch, the record was largely home-recorded and marked the band's entry into experimental rock with raw, unconventional compositions drawing comparisons to avant-garde influences like Mr. Bungle.)
The band's second album, Shiny Eyed Babies, arrived on November 11, 2014, also self-released, comprising 11 tracks such as "Way Too Long" and "Dry." This effort introduced a more polished production compared to their debut, blending art rock accessibility with experimental elements while amplifying melodic structures.
Say So, Bent Knee's third studio album, was issued on May 20, 2016, by Cuneiform Records and includes 10 tracks like "Black Tar Water" and "Leak Water." Representing their first release on a professional label, it featured refined production that unified themes of emotional complexity and adventurous songcraft across its avant-garde rock framework.
In 2017, Land Animal was released on June 23 via InsideOut Music, containing 10 tracks including the title track and "These Hands." The album's production emphasized inventive arrangements, with an early version of "These Hands" featured in the video game Rock Band 4 Rivals as a free download, highlighting the band's growing reach in multimedia contexts.
You Know What They Mean, self-released on October 4, 2019, consists of 9 tracks such as "Faith" and "Give Us the Gold." This album shifted toward a more introspective tone, capturing raw live energy and exploring themes of hope and personal resolve through primal, fun-driven rock.
The sixth studio album, Frosting, came out on November 5, 2021, through Take This to Heart Records, with 11 tracks including "Frosting" and "Leopard." Recorded amid the COVID-19 pandemic, it embraced collaborative experimentation in hyperpop and synth-heavy styles, mixing lighthearted and intense elements to reflect post-isolation creativity.
Bent Knee's most recent album, Twenty Pills Without Water, was released on August 30, 2024, by Take This to Heart Records, featuring 10 tracks like "Enter" and "Forest." The record addresses mental health struggles, channeling grief from lineup changes and mid-life reflections into a return to the band's core avant-pop-prog sound.

Other Releases

Bent Knee has issued a number of singles to promote their albums and expand their reach. The track "Being Human," from their 2014 album Shiny Eyed Babies, was released as a single that year, highlighting vocalist Courtney Swain's emotive delivery and the band's intricate arrangements. In 2017, "Land Animal" served as the lead single for their album Land Animal of the same name, accompanied by an official music video that showcased the band's dynamic live energy and visual storytelling. An early mix of "These Hands," also from the 2017 album, appeared as downloadable content for the video game Rock Band 4 in March of that year, part of the free tracks for the Rock Band Rivals expansion promoting local Boston artists.
More recently, the band has focused on digital singles tied to their evolving sound. In 2023, "Lawnmower" was released as a standalone single, emphasizing themes of introspection with layered vocals and rhythmic complexity. For their 2024 album Twenty Pills Without Water, "Enter" debuted as a pre-release single, introducing the record's exploration of anxiety and personal transformation through atmospheric production and Swain's soaring melodies. Additional 2024 singles include "Illiterate" and "Never Coming Home," both capturing the album's blend of prog-rock experimentation and pop accessibility.
In terms of EPs and live recordings, Bent Knee released Bent Knee on Audiotree Live in 2022, a four-track session featuring stripped-down performances of songs like "Set It Off" and "Being Human," recorded during a Chicago visit to highlight their improvisational prowess. While no full live albums exist, the band has contributed tracks to progressive music compilations and samplers, such as selections appearing in Prog Archives-curated collections that underscore their crossover appeal in the genre.

Critical Reception and Legacy

Album Reviews and Critical Response

Bent Knee's early albums, the self-titled debut released in 2011 and Shiny Eyed Babies in 2014, earned positive notices from indie and progressive music outlets for their innovative fusion of art rock, jazz, and theatrical elements, despite receiving limited mainstream exposure. Shiny Eyed Babies was highlighted for its schmaltzy show-tune opener and exploration of love's darker sides in a review by The Progressive Aspect, which described it as a daring thematic record. Similarly, Progarchy praised the album's top-tier musicianship across violin, drums, and vocals, calling it a "wonderful" product of the band's collective talent.
The band's 2016 release Say So garnered broader acclaim within progressive circles for its bold vocal performances and emotional intensity, marking a significant leap in production and accessibility. Under the Gun Review rated it 9/10, commending its risk-taking creativity and perspective-shifting tracks. Louder Sound emphasized Courtney Swain's astonishing vocal range and emotive delivery, noting the album's cathartic choral arrangements and darkly perverse themes as standout features.
Land Animal (2017) built on this momentum, receiving praise for its more immediate, hook-driven sound that balanced the band's eccentricity with pop sensibilities. AllMusic aggregated a user rating of 8.1/10 from 35 reviews, reflecting its appeal as an engaging entry point. Sonic Perspectives described the album as "juicy and immediate," highlighting Swain's swaggering vocals and the band's unconventional rhythms as key to its rhythmic wanderlust.
You Know What They Mean (2019) further impressed critics with its raw, primal energy and focus on fun, genre-blending songcraft, earning a 9.5/10 from Sonic Perspectives for capturing the band's live essence. Prog Report praised its groovy tracks and edgy instrumentation.
Subsequent albums Frosting (2021) and Twenty Pills Without Water (2024) continued to impress critics with the band's adaptability amid lineup shifts, including the departure of two founding members, while delving into themes of personal vulnerability and change. Scene Point Blank awarded Frosting an 82% score, lauding its wild, refreshing energy and high-octane punch. For Twenty Pills Without Water, The Prog Mind celebrated its grounded alternative rock elements, mature choruses, and haunting atmospheric introspection as evidence of the band's strengthened focus post-transition.
Across their discography, Bent Knee has sustained strong critical favor in niche progressive and art rock communities, with average ratings hovering around 4 stars on aggregator sites like Album of the Year, where albums like Say So and Land Animal score in the 70s for their genre-blending ingenuity, though the band's uncompromising style has kept mainstream breakthrough elusive.

Notable Appearances and Impact

Bent Knee's early live performances from 2011 to 2014 were concentrated in the Northeast United States, building a grassroots following through regional club shows and local venues in Boston and surrounding areas. These runs established the band's reputation for dynamic, genre-blending sets that blended art rock with progressive elements.
Following the release of their 2016 album Say So, Bent Knee expanded their touring scope with extensive U.S. and European dates from 2016 to 2019. Notable U.S. appearances included supporting The Dillinger Escape Plan at The Granada Theatre in Lawrence, Kansas, on October 21, 2016, and co-headlining with Thank You Scientist in Lawrence on April 23, 2017. In Europe and North America, they joined Haken and Leprous for shows like the November 1, 2018, performance at L'Astral in Montreal, Quebec, and supported Haken's Vector tour alongside Vola in 2019, including a sold-out UK debut.
Adapting to lineup changes after departing from InsideOutMusic, Bent Knee resumed touring as a quartet in 20222024, focusing on U.S. dates to promote You Know What They Mean and later releases. Key 2023 shows included performances in Seattle, Washington, on September 18, and Austin, Texas, on September 27, alongside VOLA and Wheel. The 2024 tour featured stops at Empire Control Room & Garage in Austin on August 31, Tulips FTW in Fort Worth, Texas, on September 12, and Kilby Court in Salt Lake City, Utah, on September 15, showcasing their evolved, streamlined sound.
In media, Bent Knee gained visibility through video game integration when an early mix of "These Hands" from Land Animal was released as free downloadable content for Rock Band 4 Rivals on March 16, 2017, exposing their music to rhythm game audiences. The band's innovative style also earned praise in mainstream outlets; a 2016 Wall Street Journal review of Say So by Jim Fusilli described their sound as "equal parts ingenuity and deliciousness," highlighting their boundary-pushing art rock.
Bent Knee's contributions to the art rock and progressive scenes have emphasized experimental songcraft, influencing perceptions of genre fusion in modern indie and prog circles. Their avant-indie approach, blending intricate arrangements with emotional depth, has been credited with expanding the palette for contemporary experimental bands.
As of 2025, Bent Knee remains active, with their 2024 self-released album Twenty Pills Without Water fostering discussions on mental health within the progressive community by exploring themes of anxiety, depression, and personal upheaval. This release, alongside ongoing U.S. tours, underscores their enduring impact on listeners seeking introspective, boundary-defying music.
