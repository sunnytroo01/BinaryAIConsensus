 In mathematics, a mock modular form of weight kkk is a holomorphic function on the upper half-plane that fails to transform like a modular form under the action of the modular group SL2(Z)\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})SL2(Z) by a certain error term, but becomes a modular form of weight kkk after adding a non-holomorphic correction involving its shadow, which is a cusp form of weight 2k2-k2k. These forms arise as the holomorphic components of harmonic weak Maass forms, which are smooth functions annihilated by a modified Laplacian operator and transforming under the modular group. The concept generalizes classical modular forms and includes Ramanujan's mock theta functions as prominent examples of weight 1/21/21/2.
The origins of mock modular forms trace back to Srinivasa Ramanujan's introduction of mock theta functions in his 1920 letter to G. H. Hardy, where he described 17 q-series that mimic the modular properties of theta functions but exhibit asymptotic behaviors near roots of unity. Early studies by G. N. Watson in the 1930s and George Andrews in the 1970s explored their analytic properties, but a rigorous framework emerged only with Sander Zwegers' 2002 Ph.D. thesis, which showed that mock theta functions can be completed using indefinite theta series and Lerch sums to yield real-analytic modular forms of weight 1/21/21/2. Don Zagier formalized the general notion of mock modular forms in 2007, establishing them within the space of harmonic Maass forms via an exact sequence linking weakly holomorphic modular forms to their shadows.
Mock modular forms have since played pivotal roles across number theory, combinatorics, and mathematical physics. In partition theory, they generate functions for Dyson's rank and crank, resolving conjectures on their distribution and asymptotics, such as the Andrews-Dragonette conjecture via Rademacher-type formulas. Applications extend to traces of singular moduli, L-functions of elliptic curves, and Hurwitz class numbers, providing algebraic insights into Diophantine problems. In physics, they appear in string theory models for black hole degeneracies and confirmations of the Vafa-Witten invariants on Donaldson polynomials, while connections to monstrous moonshine and umbral moonshine link them to finite group representations and vertex operator algebras.

Introduction and History

Overview

Mock modular forms are holomorphic functions on the upper half-plane that fail to transform like modular forms under the action of the full modular group SL(2,) by a certain error term, but become modular after adding a non-holomorphic correction involving their shadow. This partial modularity arises from their construction, where the functions can be adjusted to transform correctly under the full group by incorporating non-holomorphic terms.
The earliest examples of such functions were provided by Srinivasa Ramanujan in his 1920 letter to G. H. Hardy, where he introduced 17 "mock theta functions" that mimic the asymptotic behaviors of theta functions near roots of unity but lack complete modularity. These functions, such as the order-3 mock theta function $ H(q) = \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{q^{n^2}}{(q;q^2)_{n+1}} $ with $ q = e^{2\pi i \tau} $, served as the initial motivation for the broader theory, highlighting patterns in partition generating functions that suggested deeper modular connections.
Mock modular forms are significant because their non-holomorphic completions transform as harmonic weak Maass forms under the full modular group, thereby linking holomorphic modular forms with the broader class of non-holomorphic Maass forms and enabling applications in number theory, such as exact formulas for partition functions. In general, a mock modular form $ f(\tau) $ of weight $ k $ takes the form

Historical Development

The origins of mock modular forms lie in the groundbreaking yet enigmatic contributions of Srinivasa Ramanujan, who introduced the concept of mock theta functions in a letter to G. H. Hardy dated January 12, 1920. In this correspondence, Ramanujan presented 17 specific examples of these functions, organized into orders 3, 5, and 7, and described their asymptotic expansions near roots of unity as mimicking those of classical theta functions, though he provided no explicit modular transformation law or general definition. These functions puzzled mathematicians for decades, with early partial analyses by G. N. Watson in the 1930s verifying some identities and George E. Andrews in the 1980s uncovering additional examples from Ramanujan's lost notebook, yet a comprehensive modular framework remained elusive.
The pivotal breakthrough came with Sander Zwegers' 2002 PhD thesis, which showed that Ramanujan's mock theta functions are the holomorphic parts of harmonic weak Maass forms, completed using non-holomorphic terms like indefinite theta series to achieve full modular invariance under the modular group. George Andrews had earlier explored their identities and additional examples from Ramanujan's notebooks.
Building on this foundation, Jan Hendrik Bruinier and Jens Funke advanced the theory in 2006 by exploring harmonic Maass forms through geometric theta lifts, elucidating their connections to Borcherds products and meromorphic modular forms of infinite product type, which enriched applications in number theory and geometry.
In the 2010s, researchers extended mock modular forms to p-adic settings, with Bringmann and Guerzhoy showing in 2010 how certain mock forms can be corrected to yield p-adic modular forms, highlighting arithmetic properties like congruences in their Fourier coefficients. More recently, up to 2025, key developments include explicit constructions of mock modular forms with integral Fourier coefficients, as in 2022 work establishing their modular invariance via regularized Petersson inner products with theta shadows, alongside growing links to quantum modular forms since 2015, where mock forms underpin radial limits and invariants in quantum q-series. Continuing into 20232025, extensions include mixed mock modularity in quantum topology (2023), Kleinian mock forms (2024), and JacobiWeierstrass constructions (2025), further broadening applications.

Applications

In Combinatorics and Number Theory

Mock modular forms play a significant role in partition theory, particularly in establishing congruences for restricted partition functions that echo Ramanujan's classical congruences for the unrestricted partition function p(n). For example, Andrews introduced the spt(n) function in 2007, which enumerates the total number of appearances of the smallest part across all partitions of n, and its generating function was later identified as a mock theta function satisfying identities analogous to those of Ramanujan's mock thetas. This connection has led to the discovery of infinite families of Ramanujan-type congruences for spt(n) modulo primes, such as spt(5n+4) 0 mod 5, mirroring p(5n+4) 0 mod 5. More generally, mock theta functions serve as generating functions for partitions into distinct parts or those with parity restrictions, enabling combinatorial interpretations and arithmetic progressions in their coefficients.
In number theory, harmonic Maass forms, the broader class encompassing mock modular forms, appear as logarithms of Borcherds products, which are modular generating functions whose coefficients encode arithmetic data like discriminants or class numbers. Specifically, Bruinier and Ono showed that generalized Borcherds products constructed from the Fourier expansions of harmonic Maass forms yield meromorphic modular forms of weight zero, with logarithmic singularities determined by the principal parts of the input forms. These products provide modular parametrizations for generating functions of partition coefficients or traces of singular moduli, linking mock modular forms to deeper structures in the theory of modular forms and L-functions.
Mock modular L-series, defined via the Fourier coefficients of harmonic Maass forms, exhibit functional equations and connections to classical L-functions in number theory. Diamantis, Lee, Raji, and Rolen introduced such L-series for harmonic Maass forms and proved their functional equations using Rankin-Selberg integrals, establishing converse theorems that characterize the forms via their L-series properties. These L-series relate to derivatives of L-functions attached to cusp forms, particularly through the shadows of mock modular forms; for instance, special values of mock L-series at the center of the critical strip can involve logarithmic derivatives of cusp form L-functions, providing analytic continuations and growth estimates for mock coefficients.
Recent advancements include constructions of mock modular forms with integer Fourier coefficients, addressing long-standing questions on integrality in indefinite settings. In 2022, Li and Schwagenscheidt explicitly constructed such forms using regularized Petersson inner products of harmonic Maass forms with cusp forms, proving modularity for specific indefinite theta series that arise as holomorphic projections of weakly harmonic Maass forms. These results confirm the existence of integral mock forms beyond classical examples and extend modularity proofs to series with quadratic growth at cusps.
A seminal application in combinatorics and number theory stems from Zwegers' completion of Ramanujan's mock theta functions, resolving entry 17 in his last letter to Hardy. Zwegers demonstrated that the third-order mock theta function (q), appearing in entry 17, is the holomorphic part of a harmonic Maass form whose non-holomorphic completion involves integrals of indefinite theta series over certain cycles, thereby establishing its modular transformation properties. This framework links mock thetas to indefinite theta integrals, providing generating functions for restricted partitions and resolving asymptotic behaviors tied to Ramanujan's original observations.

In Physics and Geometry

In black hole physics, mock modular forms arise in the counting of 1/4 BPS dyonic black holes in four-dimensional N=4 super Yang-Mills theories and their string theory realizations. The degeneracies of these single-centered black holes are captured by the Fourier coefficients of a weight-1/2 mock Jacobi theta function, whose non-holomorphic completion restores modular invariance consistent with AdS3/CFT2 holography. This structure was first identified in the context of wall-crossing phenomena, where the jump in the index across walls of marginal stability is governed by contributions from multi-centered configurations, leading to a refined formula for the full dyon partition function. Ashoke Sen's entropy function approach in 2007 provided the initial framework for exact counting of these states, revealing discrepancies that were later resolved by incorporating mock modular corrections.
Extensions in the 2010s by Atish Dabholkar, Sameer Murthy, and Don Zagier formalized the mock Jacobi form as the generating function for primitive 1/4 BPS states, with the shadow component given by an Appell-Lerch sum that encodes the rational invariants from wall-crossing. The mock Jacobi form 0,1(,z)\Phi_{0,1}(\tau, z)0,1(,z) associated with 1/2 BPS states in related contexts has a Fourier expansion c(m,n,r)qmyn\sum c(m,n,r) q^m y^nc(m,n,r)qmyn, where the coefficients c(m,n,r)c(m,n,r)c(m,n,r) encode the rational invariants from wall-crossing, with q=e2iq = e^{2\pi i \tau}q=e2i and y=e2izy = e^{2\pi i z}y=e2iz; its shadow is the Appell-Lerch sum D(,z,w)\mathcal{D}(\tau, z, w)D(,z,w), ensuring the completion ^=+iD\widehat{\Phi} = \Phi + i \overline{\partial_\tau} \int \mathcal{D}=+iD transforms as a Jacobi form of weight 3/2. This framework has been applied to heterotic string theory on T^6 and type II on K3 x T^2, predicting exact agreements between microscopic and macroscopic entropies.
In string theory, harmonic Maass forms, of which mock modular forms are the holomorphic parts, appear in the twisted partition functions of heterotic strings compactified on tori, particularly in computing genus-one free energies with twisted boundary conditions. These functions encode the contributions from twisted sectors under the torus mapping class group, providing non-perturbative insights into the spectrum of BPS states and resolving anomalies in the modular transformation properties.
In geometric contexts, mock modular forms relate to the periods of Calabi-Yau threefolds through indefinite theta series, which generate the BPS indices via solutions to the holomorphic anomaly equations. Recent developments in the 2020s have shown that generating functions for these indices are higher depth mock modular forms for arbitrary charge vectors, with solutions to the modular anomaly equation involving indefinite theta series. This connection facilitates exact computations of D3-instanton corrections to the hypermultiplet moduli space, where the mock modular structure ensures consistency under SL(2,Z) duality.
Up to 2025, mock modular forms have found applications in quantum gravity, particularly in deriving boundary conditions for topological string amplitudes from D4-D2-D0 indices, which exhibit mock modularity. For instance, in 2025, mock modular forms were applied to explore black hole configurations in quantum gravity, demonstrating their role in "black holes in a forest" scenarios. These advances underscore the role of mock forms in bridging discrete state counting with continuous gravitational dynamics.
