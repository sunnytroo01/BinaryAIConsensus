 CFO$ were an American songwriting and production duo consisting of John Paul Alicastro and Michael Conrad "Mike" Lauri. Originating from Long Island, New York, they were best known for creating entrance themes and other music for WWE, beginning with their debut on the Raw 1000 episode in 2012 and continuing until their departure in 2020. Among their notable works are the iTunes number-one singles "The Rising Sun" for Shinsuke Nakamura and "Glorious Domination" for Bobby Roode. The duo disbanded following a contract dispute with WWE in August 2020, after which they were replaced by def rebel. As of 2025, no significant post-WWE activities have been reported for the duo.

Formation and Members

Duo Members

CFO$ is a songwriting and production duo composed of John Paul Alicastro and Michael Conrad "Mike" Lauri, both natives of Long Island, New York. Alicastro serves as the primary songwriter and producer within the duo, drawing from his early experiences with music composition during high school, where he developed skills as a multi-instrumentalist and honed recording techniques alongside limited collaborators. Lauri, functioning as co-producer and occasional performer, shares a similar origin, having grown up playing guitar and idolizing rock legends such as Jimi Hendrix and Eddie Van Halen, which sparked his interest in music production from a young age.
The two met as longtime best friends and schoolmates at high school on Long Island in the early 2000s, where they first began collaborating on music projects, initially under the name Kromestatik before formalizing as CFO$ in 2012. Prior to their joint endeavors, Alicastro had experimented with local music scenes through informal band involvement, while Lauri explored foundational production elements influenced by rock and emerging genres. Their combined efforts later contributed significantly to WWE's music landscape, producing iconic entrance themes that enhanced the promotion's programming.

Early Influences and Beginnings

CFO$, the production duo of John Alicastro and Mike Lauri, first began collaborating on music during their high school years on Long Island in the early 2000s, where they bonded over shared creative interests as longtime best friends. Lauri, who grew up playing guitar, drew early inspiration from rock legends such as Jimi Hendrix and Eddie Van Halen, laying the groundwork for their experimental approach to sound. Their high school collaborations often involved producing demo tracks for local artists, fostering a hands-on learning process in songwriting and production amid the vibrant Long Island music scene.
After high school and college graduation around 2008, the duo continued their partnership, with Alicastro working as an engineer at Wind-Up Records in New York City from December 2008 to April 2012. There, they utilized after-hours studio access to create original material, self-funding their efforts and gradually building a portfolio of tracks across genres like rock, nu metal, and emerging pop elements influenced by their high school immersion in the nu metal era, including bands like Deftones and Rage Against the Machine. This period of independent production around 2010-2012 saw them releasing unsigned singles and self-produced demos, though much of their early work remained underground as they navigated the challenges of the indie scene, such as limited resources and the need to compile a competitive catalog to attract opportunities.
These foundational years honed their versatile production style, blending rock roots with broader rhythmic explorations that would later define their sound. The duo's persistence in self-funding and portfolio development ultimately positioned them for a pivotal transition to professional opportunities in major music production.

Career with WWE

Entry into WWE Music Production

In 2012, CFO$, the production duo of John Alicastro and Mike Lauri, caught the attention of WWE through a submission of demo tracks to Wind-Up Records, where Alicastro worked as an engineer. Their catalog impressed label executives, leading to an introduction to WWE via Neil Lawi, a key figure in the company's music division. This connection marked the beginning of their integration into WWE's music production ecosystem, transitioning from independent songwriting to high-profile sports entertainment compositions.
Their debut project came swiftly with the creation of "The Night," selected as the official theme for the WWE Raw 1000 episode, which aired on July 23, 2012, at the Scottrade Center in St. Louis, Missouri. This track, produced under their early alias Kromestatik, showcased their ability to craft energetic, arena-ready music tailored to WWE's dramatic programming needs and served as their entry point into the company's audio landscape. Following this success, CFO$ signed a production agreement with the WWE Music Group, formalizing their role in generating original content for events and talent entrances. To support ongoing production demands, they established a dedicated workflow from their New York City studio in Times Square, enabling frequent collaboration without a full physical relocation while leveraging proximity to WWE's Stamford headquarters for iterative feedback sessions.
As their partnership deepened, CFO$ began integrating with WWE's creative team around 2014-2015, aligning with the buildup to the company's "New Era" rebranding in 2016. This early workflow involved brainstorming sessions with executives like Triple H, where they developed custom themes based on character briefs, wrestler input, and narrative arcsoften demoing multiple instrumental variations to refine mood and intensity. Their process emphasized quick turnarounds and adaptability, laying the foundation for a prolific output that defined WWE's evolving sound during this transitional period.

Key Productions and Themes

CFO$, the music production duo consisting of Mike Lauri and John Alicastro, produced over 100 entrance themes for WWE superstars between 2012 and 2020, significantly shaping the auditory landscape of professional wrestling events. Among their most acclaimed works were "The Rising Sun" for Shinsuke Nakamura, released in 2016, which debuted at #1 on the iTunes Soundtrack chart and became a staple for the wrestler's enigmatic persona with its fusion of orchestral strings and electronic beats. Similarly, "Glorious Domination" for Bobby Roode, also from 2016, topped the iTunes Soundtrack chart, featuring bombastic vocals and harmonies inspired by classic rock acts like Queen, underscoring Roode's arrogant heel character.
Their creative approach emphasized blending hip-hop, rock, and electronic elements to tailor each theme to the individual wrestler's persona, ensuring the music amplified their on-screen presence. For high-energy performers like AJ Styles, they crafted upbeat, aggressive tracks such as "Phenomenal," incorporating pop-rock riffs and rhythmic intensity to match his athletic style. This genre fusion extended to notable series of themes, including upbeat hip-hop anthems for The New Day's festive tag-team dynamic, rhythmic R&B-infused tracks for The Usos' family-oriented energy, and polished pop-hip-hop hybrids like Sasha Banks' "Skys The Limit" that highlighted her "Boss" attitude in the women's division.
Behind the scenes, CFO$ employed iterative feedback loops with WWE performers and executives, such as Triple H, to refine demosoften starting with rough ideas and adjusting elements like tempo or arrangement based on input to better align with the talent's vision. They frequently incorporated live instrumentation for authenticity, notably using real violins performed by session musician Earl Maneein in "The Rising Sun" to evoke Nakamura's artistic flair, adding organic depth to otherwise electronic-heavy compositions. This hands-on method, combined with tight production timelines ranging from days to months, allowed them to deliver high-impact themes that resonated with audiences and elevated wrestler entrances.

Media Appearances

Television Roles

CFO$ made their debut contribution to WWE television programming on the July 23, 2012, episode of Monday Night Raw, which celebrated the show's 1,000th installment, by composing and producing "The Night" as the official main theme. This track set the tone for the landmark broadcast and marked the duo's entry into WWE's musical landscape, blending electronic and rock elements to energize the audience during key segments.
The duo expanded their television impact by creating show-specific themes for SmackDown. In 2014, they composed "This Life," featuring Dylan Owen, as the official theme, which aired at the start of episodes to highlight the program's high-stakes action. By 2016, following the brand's rebranding to SmackDown Live, CFO$ delivered "Take A Chance" as the new opening theme, incorporating upbeat synths and driving rhythms to reflect the show's live energy and competitive spirit.
Beyond full show openings, CFO$ contributed to transitional elements like bumpers for WWE's weekly broadcasts from 2015 to 2018. Tracks such as "Unstoppable" served as audio cues for SmackDown Live segments, providing short, intense bursts of music to punctuate commercial breaks and wrestler vignettes, enhancing the fast-paced flow of episodes. Similarly, "Dreams That I Have" functioned as a bumper theme for Raw during the 2016 "New Era," underscoring highlight reels and in-ring recaps with motivational lyrics and pulsating beats.
CFO$ also shaped pay-per-view television events through custom compositions. For SummerSlam 2016, their track "Big Summer" played as a secondary intro theme, building hype during the Barclays Center broadcast with its summery, anthemic vibe that complemented the event's high-energy matches. At WrestleMania 32 in 2016, they remixed Sasha Banks' entrance theme "Sky's the Limit" to feature Snoop Dogg, which aired live during her match against Charlotte Flair, adding a celebrity rap verse that amplified the WrestleMania spotlight on the women's division.
On WWE Network specials, CFO$ provided musical support for behind-the-scenes content, including contributions to shows like Ride Along through custom scoring that integrated with wrestler interactions in episodes such as the 2018 "Ballad of Elias and Blor Club," where their production style helped underscore comedic road trip segments. These efforts extended their influence across WWE's televised ecosystem, blending on-air audio design with narrative enhancement.

Online and Web Presence

CFO$ cultivated a significant digital footprint through WWE's online platforms, offering fans exclusive glimpses into their music production workflow. In August 2016, WWE.com featured an in-depth article where the duo detailed the creation of entrance themes for Superstars, highlighting their collaborative approach with wrestlers to capture character essences. This was complemented by a dedicated video playlist on the site, showcasing behind-the-scenes footage of theme development for talents including Finn Blor and Jason Jordan.
On YouTube, CFO$ contributed to WWE's official channel with collaborative content from 2016 to 2019, such as introductory profiles and theme dissections that explained musical choices for stars like Finn Blor. These videos emphasized production techniques, from initial brainstorming to final mixes, distinct from broadcast programming. Additionally, they participated in web-based interviews, including a 2017 appearance on the Sam Roberts Wrestling Podcast, where they shared tips on crafting WWE themes like Shinsuke Nakamura's "The Rising Sun."
The duo maintained active Twitter (@CFOSofficial) and Instagram (@cfos_official) accounts focused on WWE projects from 2012 to 2020, such as studio sessions and fan interactions with Superstars. This content helped foster a dedicated online community around their work, with posts often cross-promoting WWE's digital exclusives. CFO$'s WWE-related media appearances and online activities concluded in 2020 following their departure from the company.

Discography

WWE Entrance Themes

CFO$ began producing WWE entrance themes in 2012 with their debut track "The Night," which served as the main theme for the Raw 1000 episode. Over the next eight years, they created over 100 original compositions and remixes for superstars across WWE's main roster, NXT, and pay-per-view events, establishing a signature electronic and hip-hop-infused style that emphasized wrestler personas. Their work peaked in the mid-2010s, coinciding with WWE's shift toward digital releases on platforms like iTunes, where select themes achieved commercial success, including two number-one hits on the Soundtrack Top Songs chart: "The Rising Sun" for Shinsuke Nakamura in 2016 and "Glorious Domination" for Bobby Roode in 2017. By 2020, their final major contribution was "Limitless" for Keith Lee, marking the end of their exclusive WWE partnership amid licensing transitions.
CFO$'s themes were categorized by wrestler type, with singles receiving personalized anthems to highlight individual traits, tag teams featuring collaborative energy, and groups incorporating orchestral or remix elements for collective impact. Production often involved wrestler input, such as lyric contributions or persona alignments, alongside technical details like sample integrations for instance, incorporating real violin recordings for Nakamura's track or remixing existing motifs for group reunions. Digital metrics underscored their popularity; AJ Styles' "Phenomenal" (2016) amassed over 26 million Spotify streams by 2025, reflecting enduring fan engagement.

Singles Themes

CFO$ produced numerous singles themes starting in 2013, focusing on high-energy builds to match solo competitors' narratives. Early examples include "Patriot" for Jack Swagger in 2013, evoking American bravado with rock elements, and "Stars in the Night" for Paige in 2014, which debuted during her NXT Women's Championship win and featured ethereal synths cleared for orchestral samples. By 2015, they crafted "Hellfire" for Sheamus, a Celtic rock fusion that topped iTunes briefly upon release. Mid-decade highlights encompassed "Phenomenal" for AJ Styles (2016), blending trap beats with a triumphant chorus; "Fight" for Kevin Owens (2015), a gritty hip-hop track emphasizing his brawler style; and "Celtic Invasion" for Becky Lynch (2014), incorporating Irish folk samples for her underdog persona. Later singles like "The Future" for Asuka (2015) and "Catch Your Breath" for Finn Blor (2014) included custom drops, such as an orchestral addition suggested by Triple H for Blor's dual human-demon character. Their final singles work, "Limitless" for Keith Lee in 2019, featured vocal samples from Lee himself, released as a digital single on March 1, 2019.

Tag Teams Themes

Tag team themes by CFO$ emphasized synergy and catchphrases, often with hip-hop bases from 2014 onward. A standout was "SAWFT is a Sin" for Enzo Amore & Big Cass in 2016, a remix incorporating Enzo's provided lyrics and an Italian mandolin sample for their streetwise dynamic. Other notable productions included "Battlescars" for The B-Team (2018), a punchy rock-rap hybrid cleared for arena playback, and themes for The Usos that evolved through remixes by 2017, integrating Polynesian rhythmic samples.

Groups Themes

For factions, CFO$ specialized in remixes and expansive soundscapes, beginning with group-adjacent work in 2012. The Shield's original theme "Special Op" (2012) was composed by Jim Johnston, a militaristic electronic track with police siren samples; a 2017 variation titled "The Truth Reigns" for the group's reunion was also by Johnston, featuring enhanced bass drops. In NXT, "Undisputed" for Undisputed Era (2017) used gang vocals similar to Roode's style, while broader group themes like "Controlled Chaos" for Sanity (2016) incorporated horror-themed samples cleared from sound libraries.

Independent and Other Releases

Their early independent output included original hip-hop singles from 2012 to 2014, shared through digital platforms, reflecting their self-taught skills in recording and multi-instrumental production. These tracks showcased a blend of hip-hop and electronic influences, distinct from their later wrestling-themed compositions.
During their WWE tenure, CFO$ contributed to non-wrestling projects, including mixing and production for established artists. They served as pioneering mixers for Goo Goo Dolls and Train albums, applying their production expertise to rock and pop tracks. Additionally, they co-wrote the rock single "New Day Coming" for Scott Stapp's 2013 solo album Proof of Life, collaborating with Stapp and Todd Clark on the composition. In 2018, they featured on an R&B-influenced guest production for a media project outside wrestling, highlighting their versatility across genres. Their work also extended to other media, such as the track "Combative," originally an entrance theme but adopted as the official goal song for the NHL's Washington Capitals from 2017 to 2019.
Their WWE partnership ended in 2020 amid a licensing dispute, after which the duo disbanded and pursued separate endeavors, with limited joint releases. John Alicastro contributed to the hip-hop track "Wily's Revenge" by MegaRan in 2020, under the alias JohnFetu$. Compilations of their non-WWE work, including early indie tracks and collaborations, became available on streaming platforms like Apple Music and Spotify, allowing broader access to their independent catalog. These releases emphasize conceptual hip-hop and production elements, separate from wrestling music.

Disbandment and Aftermath

Departure from WWE

In mid-2019, CFO$ was quietly dropped as WWE's primary music production team amid escalating contract disputes with their publisher, Arcade Songs, over royalties and publishing rights for tracks created under WWE Music Group. The duo, who had signed with Arcade in 2016, faced ongoing issues including canceled commercial releases as early as 2017, but tensions boiled over in late 2019 when Arcade fired them in December, citing disagreements on financial splits where WWE owned 50% of the tracks and Arcade/Spirit Music Group controlled the rest. CFO$ pushed for WWE to buy out their publishing deal to resolve the matter, a proposal WWE supported, but Arcade rejected it, leaving the producers with substantial monetary losses from owed payments.
